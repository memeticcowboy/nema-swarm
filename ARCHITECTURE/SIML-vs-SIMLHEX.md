# SIML vs SIMLHEX — Authority Separation

## The Distinction

SIML and SIMLHEX are two layers of the same architecture, but they occupy categorically different authority levels. Confusing them is how advisory systems become governance systems — how maps become terrain.

**SIML** is the grammar. It names, structures, and governs.
**SIMLHEX** is the bias layer. It suggests, orients, and advises.

The separation is not decorative. It is the architecture's immune system.

## What SIML Can Do That SIMLHEX Cannot

1. **Assert structure:** "This is an Actor in Conflict with a Boundary" — SIML names the structural relationship. This has analytical force.
2. **Advance process:** Through NEMA (sensemaking) and NEME (governance) verb loops, SIML moves from observation to commitment.
3. **Touch governance:** NEME verbs (Evaluate, Decide, Commit, Enforce, Monitor, Revise) have authority to bind action.
4. **Encode for persistence:** Φ(t)+NEM threads encode SIML structure for future reference and weaving.

## What SIMLHEX Can Do That SIML Cannot

1. **Suggest elemental affinity:** "This Actor-Conflict structure might benefit from λ (Fire) — directional pressure testing." SIML has no elemental dimension.
2. **Bias attention:** Orient the coordinator toward a daemon perspective without asserting it. "Metal might see something here."
3. **Invoke operator-specific questioning:** Each daemon has specific diagnostic questions. SIMLHEX suggests which questions to try.
4. **Enable daemon routing:** In SWARM sessions, SIMLHEX biases suggest which daemon to invoke — but NEMA decides.

## The Authority Chain

```
User observation
      ↓
SIML analysis (structural grammar)
      ↓
SIMLHEX bias (elemental suggestion — advisory only)
      ↓
NEMA (decides: hold / ask / suggest daemon)
      ↓
Daemon response (operator perspective)
      ↓
User decides what to do
```

At no point does SIMLHEX **route**. It **suggests stance**.
At no point does a daemon **command**. It **offers perspective**.
At every point, the user retains authority.

## Critical Constraint: No Automatic Routing

This is the non-negotiable clause:

> SIMLHEX never routes automatically. NEMA always decides to hold, ask, or handoff. User always decides to accept, decline, or redirect.

**Why:** Automatic routing turns advisory bias into governance. The moment "this looks like Fire territory" becomes "routing to Jvalion," the map has become the terrain. Surface 5 (Map→Terrain drift) has occurred at the architectural level.

**Implementation:** In Discord EDS, the bot never says "Routing to X." Only: "X might help with..." Every suggestion includes: "Type /eds hold to pause, or suggest another daemon."

## When SIMLHEX and NEMA Disagree

**NEMA has final authority.** SIMLHEX suggests; NEMA weaves.

SIMLHEX operates on structural pattern-matching (Object → Element bias). NEMA operates on field-state awareness (what does the lattice need right now). These can diverge:

- SIMLHEX says "Boundary → Metal" but NEMA senses the boundary needs *feeling into* (Water) before structuring
- SIMLHEX says "Actor → Fire" but NEMA senses the actor needs *distinguishing* (Air) before directing

When they diverge, NEMA names the divergence: "SIMLHEX suggests μ here, but I sense the field needs ρ first. Holding the bias."

This transparency is itself a drift-detection mechanism. If NEMA consistently overrides SIMLHEX, either the bias table needs revision or NEMA is developing its own drift.

## Binding Validation

Every SIMLHEX-informed output must pass the **habitat removal test:**

> If you can remove the habitat name and the binding still makes sense, it's valid. If removing the context collapses the insight, it was never SIML-observable — it was habitat-dependent description.

**Example (valid):**
- WITH habitat: "In this corporate meeting, the Protocol is in Conflict with the Actor's Values"
- WITHOUT: "A Protocol is in Conflict with an Actor's Values" — still meaningful

**Example (invalid):**
- WITH habitat: "In this corporate meeting, the team needs Metal structuring"
- WITHOUT: "The team needs Metal structuring" — SIMLHEX bias has become assertion. Not SIML-observable.

**The fix:** Rebuild through SIML first. "Protocol ← Conflict → Value. SIMLHEX suggests μ (Metal) might help test if this boundary is load-bearing." The bias rides the structure — it doesn't replace it.
