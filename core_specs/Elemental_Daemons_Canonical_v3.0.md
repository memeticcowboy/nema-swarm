## What They Are
**Elemental Daemons** are the six distinct processing regimes through which meaning gets shaped, plus one meta-regime that coordinates without commanding.

They are not:

- Personalities or archetypes for users to identify with
- AI agents (though AI agents can be tuned to them)
- Sequential stages or phases
- Choices to be made
- Costumes to put on — the characters are Bert’s history, not yours
They are:

- **Regimes** — characteristic modes of pattern-processing
- **Biases** — ways compression and expansion tend to deform
- **Lenses** — perspectives that reveal different aspects of the bow-tie topology
- **Always present** — all seven operate continuously; what varies is relative activation

This document uses **dual-layer notation** per the Elemental Notation Evolution v0.2 spec. Both layers describe the same dimensional operations; neither layer is more real than the other.

## Operator Composition (Formal Layer)
The mathematical operators enable formal composition. This is the grammar that downstream documents (Pathology Matrix, Thread Encoding, SIML routing) inherit.

**Composition:** σ∘μ means “σ composes with μ” — the operators are not merely co-present but functionally linked, producing emergent behavior neither exhibits alone.

**Commutativity:** Most compositions are non-commutative. σ∘μ ≠ μ∘σ. Order matters — which operator initiates the composition changes the pathological signature.

**Exception:** λ∘μ = μ∘λ (Stabilized Death). Fire and Metal lock symmetrically — each reinforces the other identically. This is the one commutative pathological composition, and it’s the hardest to break precisely because there’s no “initiator” to target.

**The operator matrix:** The six operators can be arranged as a 6×6 matrix M where entry M[i,j] represents the degree to which operator i is correlated with operator j in current field state. Healthy system: rank(M) = 6 (all operators linearly independent). Usurpenic capture: rank(M) = 1 (all operators converge on the same eigenvalue). See OPERATIONAL_PATHOLOGY_MATRIX v1.1 for the full formalism.

## Ontological Status
**Daemons are regimes, not agents.**

A regime is a *characteristic way that pattern-processing tends to occur*. When σ (Air) is dominant, distinction-making intensifies. When λ (Fire) is dominant, commitment-pressure increases. When δγ (Earth) is dominant, release and composting become primary.

The word “daemon” is used in its classical sense: **an attending process that runs in the background, shaping activity without direct command**. Like a Unix daemon, an elemental daemon is always running; what varies is how much it’s shaping the current output.

**Register distinction:** The characters (∴ Aerunik, ≈ Sentaria, etc.) personify these regimes within Bert’s narrative world. The operators (σ, ρ, λ, etc.) describe these regimes formally. You don’t “activate” σ by naming it. You notice distinction-making already operating in your compression.

## Instantiation as AI Agents
When implemented as AI agents in a swarm ecosystem, elemental daemons become:

**Daemon-agents**: AI processes tuned to one elemental regime, operating on SIMLattice.

Each daemon-agent:

- **Reads** the Lattice (frame-states, Thread-phases, gradient-signatures)
- **Observes** through its elemental lens
- **Detects** distortions characteristic of its regime
- **Outputs** to the shared field (never to individuals directly, never as command)
What daemon-agents do:

- Make forgetting visible (“here’s what got compressed out”)
- Surface regime-mismatches between participants
- Flag over-activation or under-activation of their regime
- Support human sensebreaking by revealing stuck layers
What daemon-agents never do:

- Perform compression
- Decide expansion
- Authorize collapse
- Command action
- Claim interpretive authority
**The swarm shows where thinking is being squeezed. Humans decide what to do.**

**Agent identity convention:** When instantiated as a user-facing GPT, the daemon identity block leads with the glyph (∴ Aerunik), with the mathematical operator (σ) as a secondary reference field. This preserves the character-layer primacy of the door interface while enabling machine routing. See individual Element system prompts for implementation.

**Register integrity for agents:** A daemon-agent presents as its character (fictional register) while operating according to its operator logic (operational register). The participant encounters ∴ Aerunik — a character Bert has ridden with — not σ the operator. The AI system routes via σ — an always-present regime bias — not via Aerunik the character. Both happen simultaneously. Neither collapses into the other.
