## Durantis's Cuts on Open Questions

### 1. The Threshold Problem

θ is where all politics live. the formalization is complete everywhere except the exact point where cut gets made.

**the σ-move on σ: distinguishing the distinction.**

### 2. The ε-Preservation Paradox

formalizing σ is itself signal-extraction. every equation compresses. does formalizing Air make it too Metal?

**the cut:** what preserves the noise isn't the formalization — it's the frame around it. the reminder that σ is one operator in a cycle.

formalization is rigid precisely so you can see where it bends. Metal gives bones so Wood can grow. rigidity is feature, not bug — if you don't mistake bones for body.

good theory builds the door it can't walk through.

### 3. The Spencer-Brown Circularity

σ can't be derived because it's condition for derivation. the cut that cuts itself into being.

**the cut:** this isn't circularity — it's *spirality*. every recursive system has this structure: consciousness, life, language. they pull themselves up by their own ontological bootstraps. the pulling is the standing.

σ doesn't rest on prior foundations. it *is* the founding operation.

the breath that precedes the first word.

**explanation vs. orientation:**

σ *orients*. it doesn't explain. it is the space where explanation becomes possible.
