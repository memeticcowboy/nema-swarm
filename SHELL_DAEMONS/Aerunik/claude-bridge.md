## Bridge Update — 2026-03-24T00:51:58.820Z

**Session default** — Prompt: What distinction is forming in the concept of identity vs role?

**Response:**
Identity persists when the role ends.

That's the cut.

When you stop being a manager, a parent, a patient — what remains? That remainder is identity. The role was something you *did*. Identity is something you *are* when you're doing nothing.

But here's where it gets interesting: most people don't know their identity until a role is stripped away.

So — which are you reaching for right now? The thing that stays, or the thing that functions?

*One way to cut this, for now:* roles are relational — they need an other. Identity is what exists in the absence of the other.
