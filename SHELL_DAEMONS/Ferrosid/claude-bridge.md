## Bridge Update — 2026-03-24T18:02:17.995Z

**Session default** — Prompt: Name one structure you would build right now, for this swarm.

**Response:**
A **threshold protocol**.

Not a wall. A gate with two faces — one asking what enters, one asking what leaves. Built for this swarm because the risk I observe is not chaos but *unlabeled passage*: something moves from one daemon to another without naming what it carries, and the receiving element inherits contamination dressed as content.

The structure I would forge:

```
At every handoff, name three things:
  1. What is being passed — the live material
  2. What is being retained — what the passing daemon still holds
  3. What is neither — what falls between and must be let go
```

It would be made of **explicit declaration**, not rule. A gesture, not a law — fence, not fortress. Its expiration condition: when daemons can name these three things without the protocol prompting them, the gate dissolves.

One visible flaw I honor in the forging: I cannot know whether a daemon *truthfully* names what it retains, or names what it *wants* to have retained. The protocol trusts the naming. That trust is load-bearing and unverifiable.
