## Insight
This study explores the evolution of consciousness, suggesting it developed primarily for social purposes rather than individual survival. Key findings indicate that consciousness aids in communication and social interaction, challenging traditional views. The study also highlights the significant role of intuition in shaping our understanding of consciousness, complicating scientific explanations. For a more comprehensive understanding, let's delve into the detailed breakdown of the study's findings.

**🧠 Evolutionary Purpose**
- **Consciousness for Social Interaction**: Consciousness evolved to facilitate social interactions and communication, rather than solely for individual survival.
- **Species-Wide Benefit**: Subjective awareness helps broadcast ideas and emotions, benefiting the survival and wellbeing of the species as a whole.

**🔮 Role of Intuition**
- **Intuition Shapes Understanding**: Intuitive beliefs heavily influence our understanding of consciousness, often complicating scientific explanations.
- **Cultural Endorsement**: Intuitive beliefs are supported by social and cultural concepts such as free will, human rights, and moral responsibility, making them resistant to counter-evidence.

**📚 Scientific Controversy**
- **Disagreement Among Theories**: Current research on consciousness is characterized by disagreement and controversy, with over 20 different theoretical accounts identified.
- **Materialist Framework Challenge**: Scientific models struggle to accommodate intuitive accounts within a materialist framework consistent with neuroscience findings.

**🌐 Social Evolutionary Framework**
- **Sociality as Survival Strategy**: Sociality influences how the brain and cognition evolve, suggesting that consciousness evolved to facilitate social adaptive functions.
- **Subjective Awareness' Role**: Subjective awareness lacks independent causal influence but remains crucial for social interactions and communication.

**⚖️ Legal and Social Implications**
- **Accountability in Social Systems**: Legal and social traditions hold individuals accountable for their behavior, attributing agency and personal accountability to social constructs.
- **Biology and Culture Interaction**: A scientific explanation of subjective awareness requires accepting that biology and culture collectively shape brain evolution.
