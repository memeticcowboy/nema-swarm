# अनाहत नाद - Anāhata Nāda in My-Stream

Created at 2025/10/29 10:44 AM

**Anāhata Nāda** (अनाहत नाद) is a profound Sanskrit term from yogic and tantric traditions, translating literally to **unstruck sound** or **unbeaten sound** ("anāhata" meaning "unstruck" or "unbeaten," and "nāda" meaning "sound" or "vibration").

It refers to the subtle, inner cosmic vibration or primordial hum perceived in deep states of meditation, beyond the realm of physical or audible sounds produced by external friction (like striking instruments). This ethereal resonance is said to emanate from the heart center (Anāhata chakra, the fourth chakra), symbolizing the soul's eternal connection to the divine and the universe's underlying unity. In practices like Nada Yoga, it manifests as an internal symphony—often described as the sound of a conch shell, flute, or ocean waves—guiding practitioners toward spiritual awakening, inner peace, and transcendence of the ego. Ancient texts like the Hatha Yoga Pradipika and Upanishads (e.g., Mandukya) link it to the sacred syllable Om, representing the bridge between the manifest world and pure consciousness.
