# Exploring the Extended Mind and Morphic Resonance

Created at 2024/10/10 9:53 PM

Rupert Sheldrake, a biologist and author, proposes that the mind extends beyond the brain, and that the universe operates not through fixed laws but through a process of "morphic resonance." He suggests that all things, including crystals, organisms, societies, and even galaxies, have “morphic fields” which contain the memory of past events and influence future behavior. This "morphic resonance" explains telepathy, premonitions, and other psychic phenomena that appear inexplicable from a purely materialistic perspective. Sheldrake provides evidence for these claims through experiments on rats, birds, insects, and human subjects, and suggests ways to test the theory further through research on cell biology and the effect of being stared at.
