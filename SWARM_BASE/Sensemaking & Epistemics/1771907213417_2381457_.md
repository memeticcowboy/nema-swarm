**👁️‍🗨️ Human Magnetoreception?**


Recent studies suggest humans may have subconscious magnetosensitivity, even if we don’t navigate by it consciously:

- 2019 Caltech study showed EEG changes (alpha band disruption) in response to geomagnetic field rotations—indicating a subconscious neural response.
- Iron deposits in the ethmoid bone have been examined as a possible substrate for human magnetoreception (ferromagnetic hypothesis).
- Human cryptochromes (Cry1, Cry2) are still functional and may be light- and spin-sensitive—especially relevant in blue light conditions.

Key Metals:

- Iron (Fe): Primary actor (magnetite-based navigation).

- Copper (Cu): Possible cryptochrome modulation.

- Zinc (Zn): Structural/photoreceptor roles.

- Manganese (Mn), Lithium (Li), Nickel (Ni): Secondary, speculative roles.

Likely Mechanisms:

- Magnetite crystals embedded in tissue (ferromagnetic).

- Cryptochrome-mediated quantum spin resonance (radical pair).

Human Status:

- Subconscious magnetosensitivity supported by EEG evidence.

- No confirmed conscious magnetoreception.
