**Magnetoreception** is the ability to detect Earth’s magnetic field, well-documented in animals like birds and bees for navigation. Dr. Joseph Kirschvink, a geophysicist at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), has conducted pioneering research suggesting humans may also possess this ability. His studies, published in *eNeuro* in 2019, found that human brains exhibit alpha-band activity in response to changes in magnetic fields, particularly downward-pointing fields, indicating a subconscious magnetic sense ([Science News for Students](https://www.snexplores.org/article/people-sense-magnetic-fields-brain-wave-study)). This could explain why some people have better senses of direction, though it is not consciously perceived.
