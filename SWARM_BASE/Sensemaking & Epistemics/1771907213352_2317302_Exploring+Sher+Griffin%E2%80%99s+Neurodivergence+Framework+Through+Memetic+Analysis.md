# Exploring Sher Griffin’s Neurodivergence Framework Through Memetic Analysis

Created at 2025/07/13 12:47 PM

📌 Memetic Analysis Summary: [Sher Griffin’s Neurodivergence Framework](https://substack.com/@thecognitiveecologist?r=5a5yhr&utm_medium=ios&utm_source=profile)

Focus: Identification of key memes, their emotional hooks, and their adjacent memeplexes in Sher Griffin’s poststructural and ecological reframing of neurodivergence.




**🧠** 

**Key Memes Identified**





**1. “Cognition is an Ecosystem”**


- Core Claim: Thought, emotion, and identity are emergent from relational, embodied, and environmental dynamics—not internal deficits.
- Emotional Hook: Relief, belonging, resonance with systems-thinking, freedom from isolation
- Memetic Signature: Challenges the Cartesian model of the isolated mind
- Adjacent Memeplexes:<br>
    - Enactivism & 4E Cognition (Embodied, Embedded, Enactive, Extended)
    - Indigenous knowledge systems (holistic relationality)
    - Ecological psychology
    - Somatic trauma theory (e.g., Peter Levine, Bessel van der Kolk)





**2. “Diagnosis Is a Weaponized Narrative”**


- Core Claim: Diagnostic categories are not neutral descriptors but power-laden narratives enforcing normativity and control.
- Emotional Hook: Righteous anger, liberation from stigma, reclaiming narrative agency
- Memetic Signature: Reframes psychiatric classification as a modern mythology of social control
- Adjacent Memeplexes:<br>
    - Foucault’s critique of power/knowledge
    - Mad Pride / Psych survivor movements
    - Disability justice
    - Postcolonial psychiatry and critiques of DSM imperialism





**3. “Trauma is Ecological, Not Just Psychological”**


- Core Claim: Trauma is not only internal; it is shaped by systemic pressures, environmental mismatches, and historical violence.
- Emotional Hook: Recognition, release from blame, validation of context
- Memetic Signature: Links trauma theory to social ecology and systems change
- Adjacent Memeplexes:<br>
    - Polyvagal Theory and co-regulation
    - Environmental justice
    - Intersectional feminist trauma theory (e.g., bell hooks, Sara Ahmed)
    - Complex PTSD and community-based healing





**4. “Neurodivergent Wisdom”**


- Core Claim: Divergent cognition carries unique epistemic value—intuition, pattern recognition, nonlinear insight—not pathology.
- Emotional Hook: Pride, mystical resonance, epistemic empowerment
- Memetic Signature: Epistemology-as-liberation; positions divergence as a new form of intelligence
- Adjacent Memeplexes:<br>
    - Giftedness / Overexcitability Theory (Dabrowski)
    - Mysticism & shamanism (neuroatypicality as sacred function)
    - Metamodernism (integrating premodern intuition, modern science, postmodern critique)





**5. “From Accommodation to Ecological Redesign”**


- Core Claim: The goal is not to fit divergent people into broken systems, but to co-create systems that support diverse minds from the ground up.
- Emotional Hook: Empowerment, agency, collective purpose
- Memetic Signature: “Rebuild the world, don’t retrofit the prison.”
- Adjacent Memeplexes:<br>
    - Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
    - Design justice
    - Regenerative systems theory
    - Peer-led mutual aid networks





**6. “Systemic Resistance Is Self-Love”**


- Core Claim: Resisting pathologization is not selfish—it’s an act of care for the community and future generations.
- Emotional Hook: Righteous solidarity, healing, moral clarity
- Memetic Signature: Ties personal healing to political liberation
- Adjacent Memeplexes:<br>
    - Black feminist self-care as resistance (Audre Lorde)
    - Anti-surveillance discourse (abolitionist tech critiques)
    - Anti-capitalist disability politics





**7. “Internalized Ableism Is a Colonized Voice”**


- Core Claim: The inner critic saying “you’re too much” or “you should cope better” is the voice of the colonizer system inside you.
- Emotional Hook: Relief, rage, awakening
- Memetic Signature: Reframes self-blame as internalized systemic oppression
- Adjacent Memeplexes:<br>
    - Internalized oppression theory
    - Postcolonial trauma discourse
    - Queer theory and identity masking
    - Gestalt/poststructural psychology





**🧬** 

**Memetic Cluster Dynamics**


These memes form interlocking, recursive systems rather than isolated slogans. They function as:

- Disruptors: Challenge dominant (medicalized, individualizing) memetic structures
- Connectors: Link psychological experience to ecological, social, and political conditions
- Seed Memes: Spawn alternative social imaginaries (e.g., neurodivergent-led governance, post-diagnostic epistemologies)
- Moral Inversions: Recast pathology as insight, compliance as colonization, and diagnosis as a form of gaslighting
