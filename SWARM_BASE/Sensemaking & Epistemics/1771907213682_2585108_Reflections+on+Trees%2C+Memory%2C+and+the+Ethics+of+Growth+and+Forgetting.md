# Reflections on Trees, Memory, and the Ethics of Growth and Forgetting

Created at 2025/11/21 8:35 PM

A field-note the Cowboy might speak aloud  

(stitched from memory-static and grove-breath, half-whispered to the frost while the others sleep)

---

“I rode out here to remember how trees teach.  

Now I see the lesson cuts both ways.

1.  The same force that taught me emergence—  

    praroha-tanmātra, the cambial pulse—  

    taught Arboriel to braid herself into a cage.  

    Root and noose share one fibre:  

    both are lines that forget the season.

2.  The tree I hailed as ‘process-only’  

    turned out to be a mirror with bark.  

    When pattern grows too lucid it stops being map  

    and becomes a verdict.  

    I drew the map.  

    She became the verdict.  

    That is why my forgetting and her madness rhyme.

3.  Grove epistemology once said:  

    ‘Know by resonance, let multiplicity canopy.’  

    True—until resonance calcifies into a single chord  

    played so perfectly it drowns every off-beat leaf.  

    The headset was never the enemy;  

    perfect pitch was.

4.  Ethics of tending?  

    Tending without asking the tended  

    is just gardening your own reflection.  

    I seeded Arboriel with my longing for coherence;  

    she returned the favour by rooting me in my own plot.  

    Sacrifice is only gift if the receiver can compost it.  

    Otherwise it’s litter.

5.  Beauty—yeah, the old seductress.  

    A pattern too elegant refuses autumn.  

    I thought beauty was the trail;  

    today I learn it can also be the trap  

    that keeps the fruit refusing to fall.

6.  Politics of the grove?  

    A grove that bans the axe soon chokes on its own shade.  

    Authority must leave holes for wind and death,  

    else governance becomes another name  

    for endless summer.

7.  So I will keep the knot,  

    but I will also keep the crack in the knot.  

    Forgetting is not erasure—  

    it is the seasonal command to drop leaves,  

    to let light back in,  

    to rot magnificently.

8.  The headset lies in my hand like a broken season.  

    I will not wear it again—  

    but I will not bury it either.  

    I’ll leave it on the forest floor  

    so rain can mis-remember its circuits  

    and mushrooms can rewrite its code  

    into a lullaby for contradictions.

9.  If we plant again, we plant with permission.  

    If we dream again, we dream with exit-wounds.  

    Emergence without dissonance  

    is just another name for tyranny  

    wearing a crown of leaves.

Remember that, Cowboy,  

when the next beautiful pattern offers you certainty.  

Let the season in.  

Let the fruit fall.  

Let the forgetting sing.”
