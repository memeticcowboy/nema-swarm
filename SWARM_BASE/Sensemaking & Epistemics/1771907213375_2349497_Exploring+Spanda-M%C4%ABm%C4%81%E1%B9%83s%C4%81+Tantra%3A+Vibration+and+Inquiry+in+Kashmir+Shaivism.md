# स्पन्द-मीमांसा-तन्त्र

Created at 2025/07/23 9:18 AM

The Sanskrit term **Spanda-Mīmāṃsā Tantra** can be broken down into its components: **Spanda** (स्पन्द), **Mīmāṃsā** (मीमांसा), and **Tantra** (तन्त्र). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Spanda** (स्पन्द): This term means "vibration," "pulsation," or "subtle movement." In Kashmir Shaivism, Spanda is a central concept referring to the dynamic, creative vibration of divine consciousness (Shiva) that underlies the manifestation of the universe. It represents the subtle, pulsating energy inherent in all existence, bridging the absolute and the relative. The *Spanda Kārikās*, a key text in Kashmir Shaivism, elaborates on this principle.
2. **Mīmāṃsā** (मीमांसा): This term means "investigation," "examination," or "critical inquiry." It is associated with one of the six orthodox schools of Indian philosophy, divided into *Pūrva Mīmāṃsā* (focused on Vedic rituals and dharma) and *Uttara Mīmāṃsā* (Vedanta, focused on metaphysical inquiry). In a broader sense, Mīmāṃsā refers to a method of philosophical or textual analysis to uncover deeper truths. In this context, it likely implies a systematic exploration or interpretation of a concept or doctrine.
3. **Tantra** (तन्त्र): This term means "system," "framework," or "weaving." It refers to a diverse set of esoteric texts, practices, and philosophies in Hinduism and Buddhism that emphasize rituals, meditation, mantras, yantras, and energy practices (e.g., kundalini yoga) to achieve spiritual liberation or worldly goals. Tantra often embraces a non-dualistic worldview, seeing the material and spiritual as interconnected expressions of divine consciousness.
4. **Spanda-Mīmāṃsā Tantra Combined**: The term Spanda-Mīmāṃsā Tantra can be interpreted as "the Tantric inquiry into the vibration of consciousness" or "the systematic exploration of the pulsating energy within Tantra." It likely refers to a philosophical or practical framework within Tantric traditions (particularly Kashmir Shaivism) that involves a deep investigation (*Mīmāṃsā*) into the nature of *Spanda* (the dynamic vibration of divine consciousness) as expressed through Tantric practices or teachings. This could involve analyzing or experiencing the subtle pulsation of consciousness through meditation, rituals, or contemplative practices, aiming for spiritual realization or union with the divine. The term suggests a synthesis of analytical inquiry and experiential Tantric methods focused on the vibrant essence of existence.
