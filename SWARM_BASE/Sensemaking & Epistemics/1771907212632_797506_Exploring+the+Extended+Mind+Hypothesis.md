- **Mental states are influenced by social systems.**: The integration of social structures into cognitive processes suggests that our interactions shape our mental states.
- **Research implications for psychology.**: If cognition is extended, traditional psychological models may need to be revised to account for external influences on mental processes.

**🔍 Critique of Functionalism**
- **Functionalism inadequately defines mental boundaries.**: The article argues that functionalism, which equates mental states with functional roles, fails to address where mental processes truly begin and end.
- **Need for non-functionalist perspectives.**: A comprehensive understanding of extended cognition requires exploring theories beyond functionalism to address boundary issues.
- **Challenges traditional cognitive science.**: The critique suggests that if minds extend, cognitive neuroscience's focus on neural processes may be fundamentally flawed.
