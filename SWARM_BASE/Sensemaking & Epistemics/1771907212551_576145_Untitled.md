# OODA Sensemaking: IT governance through the COBIT5 framework

Created at 2024/09/08 12:16 PM

This context of balancing innovation and ethics in IT governance through the COBIT5 framework aligns closely with **sensemaking** and the **OODA loop (Observe-Orient-Decide-Act)**, especially in dynamic, complex environments like IT.

Here's how it relates:

### 1. **Sensemaking**:

- **Definition**: Sensemaking is the process by which people interpret complex or ambiguous situations to make decisions. In IT governance, leaders must continuously interpret new technological trends, security risks, and ethical concerns to create meaningful action.
- **Relation to IT Governance**: When applying COBIT5, **sensemaking** is crucial in identifying how innovation aligns with the organization's ethical standards and objectives. For example, in a rapidly changing digital landscape, a governance framework helps clarify what is happening (innovation trends) and how to interpret it through an ethical lens (ensuring data integrity, compliance, etc.). This helps guide decision-makers toward more informed governance practices.

### 2. **OODA Loop**:

- **Definition**: The OODA loop (Observe-Orient-Decide-Act) is a decision-making model designed for environments where rapid and informed decisions are critical.
- **Relation to IT Governance**: In the context of COBIT5 and IT governance:
    - **Observe**: Continuous monitoring of technological developments, risks, and ethical challenges.
    - **Orient**: Interpreting these observations through the lens of IT governance, frameworks, and ethics. For instance, orienting your strategy around how innovations like AI or blockchain impact governance.
    - **Decide**: Making governance decisions that balance innovation with ethical integrity, using COBIT5 as a tool for aligning decisions with both business goals and compliance requirements.
    - **Act**: Implementing these governance decisions in a manner that fosters innovation while maintaining strong ethical and risk management controls.

### **Connection**:

- In both **sensemaking** and **OODA loops**, the goal is to make well-informed, adaptive decisions in complex or fast-moving situations. COBIT5, by providing a structured approach to governance, acts as a framework for both **interpreting** (sensemaking) and **acting upon** (OODA loop) the fast-evolving IT landscape. It enables IT leaders to **orient** their strategies towards ethical innovation while continuously **observing** and **adjusting** (looping back) as new risks and opportunities arise.

### Example:

If a company is deciding whether to adopt a new AI system, the **sensemaking** process helps them understand the ethical implications of the technology (such as data privacy or bias) and its alignment with business goals. The **OODA loop** guides the company in continuously monitoring the system's performance and risks, making ongoing adjustments to governance policies to maintain a balance between innovation and ethics.

In short, the COBIT5 framework supports **sensemaking** by helping interpret the IT environment, and integrates well into the **OODA loop** by providing the tools to continuously adapt governance decisions to evolving innovations and risks.
