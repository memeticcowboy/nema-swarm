# Exploring Systems Empathy

Created at 2025/11/04 10:12 PM

## Conversation Theory
- Pask's conversation theory views learning as a dynamic dialogue.
- Knowledge emerges through mutual exchange, allowing both human and system to adapt.
- Focus is on co-creating meaning rather than just a one-way transmission of information.
- Feedback loops and misunderstandings are essential to deepen learning and empathy.

## Personal Evolution in Empathy
- Noticed a shift from viewing empathy as merely being nice to understanding it as attuning to whole systems.
- This involves recognizing tensions, patterns, and quieter voices that influence outcomes.
- Comparison made to tuning an orchestra, rather than focusing on just one instrument.

## Resources for Deepening Understanding
- Gordon Pask’s work on Conversation Theory.
- Ranulph Glanville’s writings on second-order cybernetics and ethics in design.
- Ezio Manzini’s “Design, When Everybody Designs” for participatory, systemic perspectives.
- John Wood’s research on metadesign and team consciousness.
- Tony Fry’s “Redirective Practice” for ethical, regenerative design.
- Consider case studies on AI-mediated dialogue in healthcare or community design for practical insights.
