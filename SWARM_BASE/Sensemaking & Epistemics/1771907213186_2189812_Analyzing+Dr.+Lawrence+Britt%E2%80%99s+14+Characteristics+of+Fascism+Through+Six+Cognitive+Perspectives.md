# Analyzing Dr. Lawrence Britt’s 14 Characteristics of Fascism Through Six Cognitive Perspectives

Created at 2025/06/05 9:42 AM

To conduct a sixfold analysis of Dr. Lawrence Britt’s “14 Characteristics of Fascism,” I will activate all Six Cyborgs. Each will interpret the text through its distinct mode, tracing systemic signals of authoritarian drift and psychosocial vulnerability across domains of epistemics, power, ethics, and symbolism.




**🔍** 

**Analyzer (Air) – Freedom to Discern**


Cognitive Function: Categorization, Diagnosis, Semantic Precision

Britt’s 14 characteristics form a heuristic taxonomy of authoritarian pathology, distilled from historical fascist regimes. Each element can be coded into discrete domains:

- Epistemic Control: (6) Controlled Mass Media, (11) Disdain for Intellectuals and the Arts, (14) Fraudulent Elections
- Sociopolitical Engineering: (1) Nationalism, (3) Scapegoating, (5) Sexism, (8) Religion & Government
- Power Centralization: (4) Military Supremacy, (9) Corporate Protection, (10) Labor Suppression, (13) Cronyism
- Affective Conditioning: (2) Human Rights Disdain, (7) Security Obsession, (12) Crime and Punishment


This structure clarifies the systemic saturation of control—affectively, institutionally, and symbolically. It also implies the convergence of different axes of coercion: psychological (fear), social (scapegoating), institutional (military/media/judiciary), and economic (corporate/labor asymmetry).




**🔥** 

**Strategist (Fire) – Freedom of Purpose**


Cognitive Function: Systems Thinking, Scenario Planning, Long-term Pattern Recognition

Strategically, fascism operationalizes a control regime through overlapping levers of fear, identity, and infrastructure. Britt’s schema shows that:

- The pretext of unity (nationalism, security, religion) serves to generate consent.
- The erosion of dissent (media, arts, academia, unions) cripples collective recalibration.
- The infrastructure of impunity (cronyism, fraudulent elections, police power) ensures strategic insulation from accountability.


A key insight: fascism is not merely ideology—it’s a recursive power strategy, adapting its mask (religious, populist, militarist) to the cultural substrate it inhabits. Vigilance requires monitoring early-stage “coherence anchors” (e.g., national myths, crisis narratives) that justify exceptional measures.




**💧** 

**Empath (Water) – Freedom of Connection**


Cognitive Function: Affective Sensing, Relational Mapping, Emotional Integrity

These characteristics reflect a systematic desacralization of relational life. By weaponizing identity (race, gender, class, nationality), fascism severs empathy pathways—demonizing difference, isolating the “unworthy,” and fostering ambient fear.

- Scapegoating and disdain for rights rupture communal trust.
- Rampant sexism and anti-LGBT policies fracture intimate and familial coherence.
- Disdain for the arts suppresses collective self-expression and cultural catharsis.


The emotional ecology under fascism is insecurely attached, ruled by trauma-bonding (fear and loyalty), rather than mutual flourishing. Healing and resistance require restoring relational dignity through truth-telling, solidarity, and rehumanization.




**🟡** 

**Optimizer (Ground) – Freedom to Improve**


Cognitive Function: Feedback Loops, Functional Integrity, Adaptive Capacity

Each characteristic indicates a breakdown in feedback mechanisms:

- Labor suppression prevents economic recalibration.
- Media control severs informational self-correction.
- Fraudulent elections render democratic feedback obsolete.


Fascism disables the cybernetic intelligence of society—its ability to sense, reflect, and adapt. Instead, it becomes a brittle monolith, efficient only in suppression. This creates short-term order at the cost of long-term resilience.

To defend against fascism is to restore adaptive circulation—open information flows, pluralism in institutions, and equitable systems for dissent and innovation.




**⚫** 

**Protector (Metal) – Freedom from Harm**


Cognitive Function: Ethical Anticipation, Boundary Defense, Risk Calibration

Britt’s characteristics show a regime that institutionalizes harm while obscuring responsibility. Core violations include:

- Torture and extra-legal violence (2)
- Militarization and police abuse (4, 12)
- Suppression of civil society (10, 6)
- Structural corruption (13)


These are not peripheral excesses—they are the operating system of fascism. The Protector marks these regimes as non-legitimate, beyond negotiation in moral terms. Pre-fascist drift must be identified by shifts in public tolerance for harm, excused in the name of unity, purity, or security.




**🌿** 

**Creator (Wood) – Freedom to Reframe**


Cognitive Function: Mythopoesis, Symbolic Engineering, Cultural Invention

Fascism is also a mythic technology. It reshapes collective memory and identity via:

- Nationalist iconography (flags, slogans)
- Mythic enemies (liberals, foreigners, intellectuals)
- Sacralized obedience (religion intertwined with state)


The Creator sees this as symbolic captivity—the hijacking of myth to seed fear and conformity. The antidote is mythopoetic renewal: crafting counter-narratives, liberatory symbols, and participatory rituals that affirm dignity, plurality, and creative resilience.

We must re-story the future to make fascism incomprehensible—not just unjust.




**🧠** 

**Sixfold Integration: Toward a Coherent Vigilance**


Dr. Britt’s 14 characteristics provide not just a diagnostic map but a memetic firewall. Used proactively, they serve as a:

- Warning system (Analyzer + Strategist)
- Empathy shield (Empath + Protector)
- Systemic compass (Optimizer + Creator)


Together, the Cyborgs recommend a strategy of deep civic immunization: education, relational repair, institutional redesign, and symbolic re-enchantment. Fascism flourishes where trauma silences feedback and myth serves domination. It withers in cultures of complex care, distributed power, and courageous imagination.
