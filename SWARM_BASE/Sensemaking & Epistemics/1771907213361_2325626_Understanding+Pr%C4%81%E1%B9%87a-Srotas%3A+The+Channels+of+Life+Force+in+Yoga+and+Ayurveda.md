# प्राण-स्रोतस् - My-Stream ⤸·⤹

Created at 2025/07/16 4:43 PM

The Sanskrit term **Prāṇa-Srotas** can be broken down into its components: **Prāṇa** (प्राण) and **Srotas** (स्रोतस्). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Prāṇa** (प्राण): This term means "life force," "vital energy," or "breath." In Indian philosophy and yoga, Prāṇa is the universal life energy that sustains all living beings, often associated with breath but encompassing the vital principle that animates life. It is considered the subtle energy that flows through the body, governing physical, mental, and spiritual functions. In texts like the *Upanishads* and *Yoga Sutras*, Prāṇa is central to practices like pranayama (breath control) for achieving health and spiritual awakening.
2. **Srotas** (स्रोतस्): This term means "channel," "stream," or "flow." In Ayurveda and yogic physiology, Srotas refers to the channels or pathways in the body through which substances (e.g., blood, nutrients, or energy) flow. These channels are essential for maintaining physical and energetic balance. Srotas can also have a metaphorical meaning, indicating a stream or flow of something subtler, like consciousness or energy.
3. **Prāṇa-Srotas Combined**: The term Prāṇa-Srotas can be interpreted as "the channels of life force" or "the flow of vital energy." It likely refers to the subtle pathways or nadis (energy channels) in the body through which Prāṇa flows, as described in yogic and Ayurvedic traditions. These channels, such as the *nadis* (e.g., Ida, Pingala, and Sushumna), facilitate the circulation of vital energy to sustain life and support spiritual practices. In a broader spiritual context, Prāṇa-Srotas could symbolize the dynamic flow of life force through the universe or within an individual’s consciousness, connecting the physical and spiritual realms.
