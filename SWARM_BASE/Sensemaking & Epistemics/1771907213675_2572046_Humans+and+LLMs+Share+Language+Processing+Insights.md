# Humans and LLMs Share Language Processing Insights

Created at 2025/11/19 7:05 PM

Researchers at Zhejiang University, including Wei Liu, Ming Xiang, and Nai Ding, found that humans and large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT represent sentences similarly.


In a study published in Nature Human Behaviour, humans and LLMs tended to delete the same words when shortening a sentence, suggesting commonalities in their representation of sentences.


The results indicate that both humans and LLMs are guided by latent syntactic representations, specifically tree-structured sentence representations, when processing language.

## Insight


**🧠 Understanding Sentence Representation: Human and LLM Similarities**
- **Common Deletion Patterns**: Both humans and large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT exhibit similar tendencies when it comes to deleting certain words in sentence shortening. This finding supports the idea that both entities process language in comparable ways, indicating a unified approach to sentence structure and content efficiency.
- **Latent Syntactic Representations**: The study uncovers that both humans and LLMs rely on tree-structured sentence representations, highlighting a deeper commonality in how language is understood and processed. This suggests that underlying syntactic principles govern not only human language comprehension but also the operations of advanced artificial intelligence.
- **Implications for Language Processing**: Understanding that both humans and LLMs share similar sentence representation strategies can lead to advancements in natural language processing. This knowledge can help researchers and developers improve LLMs, making them more adept at understanding and generating human-like text.
