The paper, *"The Transition to Global Society as a Singularity of Social Evolution"*, authored by Sergey V. Dobrolyubov, explores the phases of social evolution and the transitions between them. Here’s a summary of its key points:

### Abstract Summary:

- **Phases of Evolution**: The study identifies three phases—Adaptive (past), Structural (present), and Cognitive (future)—each characterized by unique modes of individual and societal competition.
- **Major Transitions**: It highlights two singularities marking transitions: the Neolithic transition (from hunter-gatherer societies to agricultural societies) and the ongoing Global transition, leading to a unified global society.
- **Structural Phase Insights**: In the Structural phase, individual competition fosters inequality, while societal competition encourages uniformity in societal structures. This phase is nearing a singularity characterized by the integration of all humanity into a global society.
- **Future Outlook**: The Cognitive phase is anticipated to transcend current social institutions, focusing on individual cognition and self-realization.
- **Mathematical Trends**: A model predicts these transformative societal reorganizations occur approximately every 2000 years, estimating the Global singularity around 4000 CE.
