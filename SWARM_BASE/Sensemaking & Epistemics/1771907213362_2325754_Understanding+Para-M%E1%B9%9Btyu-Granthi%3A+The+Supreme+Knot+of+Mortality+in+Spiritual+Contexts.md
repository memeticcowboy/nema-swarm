# पर-मृत्यु-ग्रन्थि - Usurpene ⊶⊷ 

Created at 2025/07/16 6:06 PM

The Sanskrit term **Para-Mṛtyu-Granthi** can be broken down into its components: **Para** (पर), **Mṛtyu** (मृत्यु), and **Granthi** (ग्रन्थि). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

**Definition:**

1.  **Para** (पर): This term means “supreme,” “highest,” “beyond,” or “transcendent.” It often denotes something ultimate, absolute, or pertaining to the highest reality or consciousness in spiritual contexts. It can also imply “other” or “another,” depending on the context.
2.  **Mṛtyu** (मृत्यु): This term means “death” or “mortality.” In Indian philosophy, Mṛtyu represents not only physical death but also the cycle of birth and death (*saṃsāra*) or the limitations of mortal existence. It is often contrasted with immortality (*amṛta*) or liberation (*mokṣa*).
3.  **Granthi** (ग्रन्थि): This term means “knot,” “bond,” or “blockage.” In spiritual and yogic traditions, Granthi refers to psychic or energetic knots in the subtle body that obstruct the flow of spiritual energy (*prāṇa* or *kuṇḍalinī*). These knots are often associated with psychological or karmic blockages that bind one to ignorance or saṃsāra. The three main Granthis in yoga are *Brahma Granthi* (root chakra), *Vishnu Granthi* (heart chakra), and *Rudra Granthi* (third eye chakra).
4.  **Para-Mṛtyu-Granthi Combined**: The term Para-Mṛtyu-Granthi can be interpreted as “the supreme knot of death” or “the transcendent knot of mortality.” It likely refers to a profound spiritual or metaphysical concept, such as the ultimate blockage or attachment that binds one to the cycle of death and rebirth, which must be transcended to attain liberation (*mokṣa*). The inclusion of *Para* suggests this is not an ordinary knot but one related to the highest or most fundamental aspect of mortality, possibly the illusion of death itself or the final barrier to realizing the immortal self (*Ātman* or *Brahman*). In a yogic or Tantric context, it could symbolize a subtle energetic blockage that prevents the full realization of transcendent consciousness beyond death.
