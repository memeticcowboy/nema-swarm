# Follow your bliss

Created at 2025/04/23 10:35 AM

[YouTube: Joseph Campbell Foundation](https://youtu.be/s28rwnz18j4?si=Z_bdtTTzbKMN0P-4)
In a captivating discussion with Bill Moyers, Joseph Campbell explores the profound concept of “bliss,” rooted in Sanskrit and spiritual wisdom. He introduces three key Sanskrit terms: *ananda* (rapture), *sat* (being), and *chit* (full consciousness). Campbell candidly shares his personal journey, noting his uncertainty about consciousness and being but expressing confidence in his experience of rapture.
Campbell recounts how his connection to bliss began through Indian culture, which sparked a deeper exploration of mythology and the arts. He highlights the transformative role of dance, particularly through the influence of a key figure, Jean, who shaped his artistic path.
Drawing from his time teaching at Sarah Lawrence College, Campbell emphasizes the value of one-on-one student conferences. These moments often led to breakthroughs, where a student’s spark of realization signaled a new passion. He urges students to nurture this joy, seeing it as a vital life opportunity.
Offering practical guidance, Campbell encourages embracing one’s “spring of eternal life” by recognizing moments of joy and intuition. He believes these moments guide us toward our true path, supported by “hidden hands” that mysteriously open doors for those who follow their bliss.
With compassion, Campbell reflects on those without this guiding force, expressing pity for individuals unaware of the accessible joys around them. He concludes by affirming that the “waters of eternal life” are always within reach, urging everyone to pursue their bliss to unlock life’s joy and vitality.
The core message resonates deeply: aligning with our passions brings support, fulfillment, and joy in every moment.
