# Epistemic Games and Sensemaking Frameworks

Created at 2025/06/29 5:21 PM

The **epistemic games framework** represents one of the most promising approaches to meta-epistemological education[15](https://lcsherry.org/publications/e_games.html)[16](https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevPhysEducRes.14.020122)[17](https://journals.aps.org/prper/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevPhysEducRes.14.020122). Originally developed for physics education, these **reflective, knowledge-generating activities** teach students **how to construct and organize their own knowledge** rather than simply absorbing information[15](https://lcsherry.org/publications/e_games.html). The framework helps students develop **epistemic fluency**—familiarity with multiple ways of viewing and organizing information—which is **essential in multicultural, complex societies**[15](https://lcsherry.org/publications/e_games.html).

Research from the University of California shows that **epistemic games enhance metacognitive skills** by making students **aware of relationships among things and different ways of looking at them**[15](https://lcsherry.org/publications/e_games.html). Students who engage with these games develop **pattern recognition skills associated with expert behavior and creativity**[15](https://lcsherry.org/publications/e_games.html). The **sensemaking epistemic game model** demonstrates how learners **iteratively construct explanations in response to perceived gaps or inconsistencies in knowledge**[16](https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevPhysEducRes.14.020122)[17](https://journals.aps.org/prper/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevPhysEducRes.14.020122). This approach could be scaled to help citizens develop **resistance to algorithmic manipulation** by teaching them to **recognize and question their own knowledge construction processes**.
