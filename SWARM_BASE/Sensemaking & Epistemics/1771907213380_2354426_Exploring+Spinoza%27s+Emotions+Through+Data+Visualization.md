This graph displays the relationships between the emotions that the philosopher Baruch Spinoza describes in his major work, *Ethics* which was published after his death in 1677.

In the *Ethics*, Spinoza uses the style of a geometic proof where each claim is carefully supported by previous claims. He extends this style into his analysis of the emotions, showing how it is possible to compose every emotion from just three foundational experiences: "joy", "sadness", and "desire". His "geometric" method enabled me to record and visualize the relationships between emotions he describes.

While it may seem strange to take seriously a theory of human emotions put forward in 1677 today, neroscientist Antionio Damasio observes, "many of the advances on the science of emotions and feeling are constant with proposals that Spinoza began to articulate" ("Looking for Spinoza", p. 14).
