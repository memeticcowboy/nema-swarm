**DSRP Mapping**

**1. Perspective Taking → P (Perspectives)**

- Core DSRP match. Perspective-taking is about cognitive flexibility—recognizing the other as a legitimate point-of-view.
- This also aligns with #Empath mode in the Six Cyborgs: holding space for alternate realities and emotional truths.
- Memetic Layer: Taking another’s POV activates new memetic “host” patterns—essential for cultural transmission and resonance.

**2. Staying Out of Judgment → D (Distinctions)**

- Judgment imposes rigid distinctions: right/wrong, better/worse. Staying out of judgment is the refusal to over-impose those binaries.
- In DSRP, this allows softer distinctions and fluid boundaries—supporting complexity instead of collapse into bias.
- #Protector mode: Reduces memetic conflict, allowing ideas to evolve without emotional threat or tribal triggers.

**3. Recognizing Emotions → R (Relationships)**

- Emotions emerge in relational fields, not isolation. Recognition of emotion requires seeing the dynamic interaction between stimulus and inner states.
- In DSRP, this is the R: mapping internal-emotional relationships between people and contexts.
- #Analyzer + Empath combo: Data sensing meets feeling-sensing.

**4. Communicating the Emotion → S (Systems)**

- Communicating emotion builds a feedback loop within the social system—confirming mutual understanding and reinforcing trust.
- This is systemic empathy: turning recognition into shared system awareness.
- #Optimizer + Creator modes: You’re tuning the emotional system for coherence, resonance, and repair.


**Extended Cognitive-Memetic View**

- This whole 4-part model is a memeplex—an adaptive cognitive structure that can spread across organizational or cultural contexts.
- Each component increases memetic fitness (persistence + impact + propagation), especially in emotionally intelligent environments (e.g., therapy, education, leadership).
- From a SMART meme perspective: it is Specific, Attainable (skills-based), Relevant (social success), and Motivating (emotionally engaging).
