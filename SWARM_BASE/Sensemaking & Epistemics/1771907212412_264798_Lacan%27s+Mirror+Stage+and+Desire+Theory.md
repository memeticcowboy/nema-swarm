## Lacan: Mirror, mirror, who am I?

Here are short explanations for each idea in this section of the outline:

2. Formation of the Subject: Lacan's theory on how individuals develop a sense of self and subjectivity.

2.1 Mirror Stage:
- 2.1.1: The developmental phase when infants first recognize themselves in a mirror.
- 2.1.2: The child mistakenly identifies with their mirror image as a complete, unified self.
- 2.1.3: This misidentification becomes the foundation for ongoing psychological struggles.

2.2 Desire and Lack:
- 2.2.1: Most of our desires operate below the level of conscious awareness.
- 2.2.2: Our desires are shaped by societal expectations and relationships with others.
- 2.2.3: Desire always seeks fulfillment in something external to the self.
- 2.2.4: This constant seeking creates a persistent feeling of incompleteness in the subject.
