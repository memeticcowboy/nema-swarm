## Insight
Let's delve into the structure and unpacking of beliefs as illustrated in the provided images.

- The structure of a belief is depicted using three shapes: a triangle for 'Cause', a square for 'Effect', and a circle for 'Meaning'. This visual representation helps in understanding how beliefs are formed and interconnected.
- Unpacking a belief involves breaking it down into its components: 'if' (Cause), 'then' (Effect), and 'so' (Meaning). This process aids in analyzing and questioning the belief.
- An example provided is: 'if it’s too fast, then it won’t last, so it’s not worth doing'. This illustrates how a belief can be constructed and the logical flow from cause to effect to meaning.
- To challenge a belief, questions such as 'How do you know that?', 'What makes it that way?', 'What does this mean?', and 'How does it affect your life?' are essential. These questions help in critically evaluating and potentially altering the belief.

Consider applying this structure to your own beliefs to understand their origins and impacts. Questioning and unpacking beliefs can lead to personal growth and better decision-making.
