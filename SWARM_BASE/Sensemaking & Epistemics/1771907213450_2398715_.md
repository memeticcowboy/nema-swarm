# तनु-संरक्षण

Created at 2025/08/17 12:57 PM

The Sanskrit term **Thanu Samrakshana** can be broken down into its components: **Thanu** (तनु) and **Samrakshana** (संरक्षण). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Thanu** (तनु): This term means "body," "form," or "physical self." In Indian philosophy and literature, Thanu refers to the physical body or the material form of a being, often contrasted with the subtle or spiritual self (*Ātman*). It can also carry a poetic sense, indicating a delicate or slender form.
2. **Samrakshana** (संरक्षण): Derived from the prefix **sam-** (together, complete) and the root **rakṣ** (to protect or preserve), Samrakshana means "protection," "preservation," or "safeguarding." It implies the act of guarding, maintaining, or nurturing something to ensure its well-being or continuity.
3. **Thanu Samrakshana Combined**: The term Thanu Samrakshana can be interpreted as "the protection of the body" or "the preservation of the physical form." It likely refers to practices or principles aimed at safeguarding the physical body, such as through health, diet, exercise, or spiritual disciplines like yoga and Ayurveda, which emphasize maintaining bodily vitality to support spiritual growth. In a broader context, it could also suggest nurturing the physical form as a vessel for the soul or consciousness, ensuring its well-being for the pursuit of higher goals like liberation (*mokṣa*).
