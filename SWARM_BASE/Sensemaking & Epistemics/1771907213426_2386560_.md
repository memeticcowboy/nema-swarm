Whitehead’s “process God” in one sentence

A companion-God who grows with the world, not an almighty Creator.

Picture three short steps

1.  Two sides of the same God

•  Primordial side: a timeless “menu” of every possible value (truth, beauty, etc.).

•  Consequent side: the living memory of everything that has actually happened—every joy, sorrow and choice.

2.  How it works

Each new event in the universe “reads” the menu (primordial) for what is worth aiming at, then adds its own tiny decision. That new moment is then absorbed into God’s memory (consequent), so God grows richer as the universe unfolds.

3.  What this means for us

•  God is not a distant boss; God is the “fellow-sufferer who understands”.

•  The future is genuinely open—God knows the goal (more beauty, more harmony) but not the exact path.

•  We literally complete God: without the world’s becoming, God’s own life-story would be unfinished.

So, in plain words:

“Whitehead’s God is the world’s memory and its compass, learning and loving with every step we take.”
