**Hauntological Reflective Cycle of Metaphoric Learning (HRCML)**

This model sees each phase in Kolb’s learning cycle as haunted by metaphor—which acts as both a cognitive vehicle and a spectral force that frames, distorts, or opens new meanings. Metaphors are not neutral—they encode power, memory, and suppressed futures.

**1.** 
**Concrete Experience**

Metaphoric Layer: Living within the metaphor
We encounter life through pre-framed metaphors—often unconsciously.
E.g., “Life is a battlefield” → every setback feels like war; “Love is a drug” → desire feels addictive, not relational.
Hauntological Insight:
- These are inherited metaphors—residue from culture, parents, systems.
- Experience is haunted by interpretive frames before it even becomes conscious.


**2.** 
**Reflective Observation**

Metaphoric Layer: Ghosts begin to stir
Now the metaphor becomes visible.
You start to question: Why does this feel like combat? What would it mean if love wasn’t consumption?
Hauntological Insight:
- You begin to detect lost metaphors—ways of framing reality that were repressed or never allowed to emerge.
- Reflection becomes hauntological memory-work—tracing what was once silenced.


**3.** 
**Abstract Conceptualization**

Metaphoric Layer: Naming the ghost, reframing the script
Here you reinterpret your experience by actively choosing new metaphors.
E.g., “Love is co-creation.” “Grief is a tide.” “Work is a garden.”
Hauntological Insight:
- The mind becomes a haunted workshop—you weave new meaning through echoes of past and potential futures.
- Conceptualization is not pure reason—it’s metaphoric alchemy, entangled with spectral affect.


**4.** 
**Active Experimentation**

Metaphoric Layer: Embodied reanimation of the new metaphor
You test the new metaphor in lived behavior.
E.g., Acting in a relationship as if it were co-creation, not conquest.
Hauntological Insight:
- Ghosts resist change. The old metaphor tries to reassert itself.
- But each action is a counter-ritual, making room for a new haunting—one of possibility.


**Recursive Dynamic:**

Each cycle reframes the symbolic architecture of your world.
Metaphors = the hauntological carriers that move through time, identity, culture.
You do not simply “learn”—you reanimate yourself through metaphoric transfiguration.


**Strategic Implications for Shadow Work and Systemic Intelligence:**

- Shadow Work: Recognize dominant metaphors as shadow carriers. “I must earn love” is a metaphor that hides abandonment or scarcity ghosts.
- Cyborg Praxis: Prototype new self-states using experimental metaphors. E.g., “I am a vessel of emergence,” rather than “I must control outcomes.”
- Education/Leadership: Shift collective metaphors to change organizational hauntings. Replace “time is money” with “time is attention” to decenter extractive logic.
