### Translation and Definition:

    - This term means "atom," "minute," "subtle," or "smallest particle." In Indian philosophy, particularly in the Vaiśeṣika school, *Anu* refers to the smallest indivisible unit of matter, akin to an atom. In a broader spiritual or philosophical context, it can denote something extremely subtle, minute, or fundamental, often symbolizing the essence or core of something.
2. **Sūtra** (सूत्र):
    - This term means "thread," "aphorism," or "rule." In Indian traditions, a Sūtra is a concise, aphoristic text that encapsulates teachings or principles, often used in philosophical, spiritual, or technical contexts (e.g., Patañjali’s *Yoga Sūtras* or Brahma Sūtras). It implies a guiding thread that weaves together knowledge or practice.
3. **Veda** (वेद):
    - This term means "knowledge," "wisdom," or "sacred scripture." The Vedas are the ancient sacred texts of Hinduism (*Rigveda*, *Yajurveda*, *Samaveda*, *Atharvaveda*), considered the foundational scriptures of Vedic knowledge. In a broader sense, Veda signifies authoritative or divine knowledge.
