# धारण-तन्मात्र - ⛨ Metal Boundary-Only-Ness

The Sanskrit term **Dhāraṇa-Tanmātra** can be broken down into its components: **Dhāraṇa** (धारण) and **Tanmātra** (तन्मात्र). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Dhāraṇa** (धारण): This term means "concentration," "holding," or "retention." In the context of yoga, particularly in Patañjali’s *Yoga Sutras*, Dhāraṇa is the sixth limb of Ashtanga Yoga, referring to the practice of focused concentration, where the mind is fixed on a single point, object, or thought. It is a state of mental steadiness that precedes deeper meditative states like *dhyāna* (meditation) and *samādhi* (absorption).
2. **Tanmātra** (तन्मात्र): This term means "subtle element" or "essence of an element." In Sāṃkhya philosophy, Tanmātras are the five subtle elements (sound, touch, form, taste, and smell) that are the precursors to the five gross elements (*pañca-mahābhūta*: space, air, fire, water, earth). They represent the subtle, non-physical essences of sensory perception that give rise to the material world.
3. **Dhāraṇa-Tanmātra Combined**: The term Dhāraṇa-Tanmātra can be interpreted as "the concentration on subtle elements" or "the focus on the essences of perception." It likely refers to a meditative or yogic practice where the practitioner concentrates (*Dhāraṇa*) on the subtle elements (*Tanmātra*)—sound, touch, form, taste, or smell—in their purest, non-material form. This practice could involve focusing the mind on these subtle essences to transcend sensory perception and move toward deeper spiritual awareness. In a Sāṃkhya or yogic context, it may indicate a meditative technique to understand the subtle building blocks of reality, facilitating insight into the nature of existence and consciousness.
