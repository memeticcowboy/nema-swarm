# David Chapman: Postrationalist Theorist Merging Buddhism, Cognitive Science, and Modern Meaning-Making

Created at 2025/12/24 9:43 PM

David Chapman<br><br>David Chapman is an American writer and theorist associated with the “postrationalist” and “metamodern” communities. He is known for his online writings that synthesize Buddhist philosophy, cognitive science, and critiques of rationalism, especially through his website Meaningness and related essays. His work explores how people make sense of meaning and ethics in a pluralistic, uncertain world.<br><br>Key facts<br>	•	Primary website: Meaningness.com<br>	•	Focus areas: Meaning, ethics, sensemaking, modernity, Buddhism, rationalism<br>	•	Notable concepts: Fluidity of meaning, meta-systematicity, stance theory<br>	•	Influence: Postrationalist and metamodernist online communities<br><br>Intellectual background<br><br>Chapman studied and worked in artificial intelligence and cognitive science at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) before turning his attention to philosophical writing. His technical training informs his interdisciplinary approach, blending scientific thinking with phenomenological and cultural analysis. He frequently critiques the limitations of strict rationalism, arguing that human meaning-making cannot be reduced to algorithmic reasoning or belief systems.<br><br>Meaningness and postrationalism<br><br>His major project, Meaningness, is a long-form online book exploring how meaning operates as a fluid process rather than a fixed property. Chapman proposes that people adopt “stances” toward meaning—patterns of engagement that can be constructive or confused. The work critiques both nihilism and eternalism, aiming for a dynamic “complete stance” that acknowledges uncertainty while remaining participatory in life’s meaning-making.<br><br>Engagement with Buddhism and culture<br><br>Chapman draws extensively from Buddhist philosophy, particularly Madhyamaka thought, adapting it for secular and modern contexts. His writing demystifies Buddhist concepts such as emptiness, emphasizing their practical implications for personal development and ethics. Beyond religion, his essays engage topics like subculture evolution, social transformation, and the future of knowledge communities.<br><br>Influence and reception<br><br>Within the rationalist diaspora, Chapman is regarded as a leading postrationalist voice, bridging the gap between scientific rationality and existential philosophy. His essays circulate widely in online intellectual circles focused on sensemaking, meta-modernity, and cultural evolution. Readers value his combination of analytical clarity, humor, and philosophical depth.
