## Insight


* The image presents a 2x2 matrix categorizing individuals based on their levels of "Consciousness" (vertical axis) and "Agency" (horizontal axis). This framework is used to define four archetypes: "Spiritual Bypassers," "Agentic Sages," "NPCs," and "Agentic Fools."
* "Spiritual Bypassers" are characterized by high consciousness but low agency, implying they possess insight but lack the ability to translate it into action, living in potential rather than impact. This concept is related to the psychological term "spiritual bypassing," which refers to the tendency to use spiritual ideas and practices to avoid dealing with painful feelings, unresolved wounds, and basic needs.
* "Agentic Sages" represent the ideal, possessing both high consciousness and high agency, described as "ideal-aligned, awake, and effective." This archetype embodies the integration of wisdom and the capacity to act on it, aligning with the philosophical concept of "practical wisdom" or "phronesis," which emphasizes the importance of both knowledge and action.
* "NPCs" (Non-Player Characters) are defined by low consciousness and low agency, described as "running default programs, compliant, unexamined lives." This term, borrowed from video game terminology, suggests a lack of autonomy and critical thinking, passively following societal norms and expectations.
* "Agentic Fools" are characterized by low consciousness but high agency, described as "hustle hard but lack wisdom; dangerous in leadership." This archetype highlights the potential dangers of action without insight, where individuals may be driven and effective but lack the ethical or strategic understanding to guide their actions wisely.
