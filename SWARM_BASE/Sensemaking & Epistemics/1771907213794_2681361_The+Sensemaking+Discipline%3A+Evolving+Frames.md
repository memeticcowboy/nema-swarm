## Insight


* The concept of **sensemaking as a discipline** underscores the importance of adaptable frameworks in navigating complex situations. Rather than prescriptive processes, it promotes a fluid approach centered on understanding the evolving dynamics of a situation.

* **Stage 1: Confusion** acts as a critical entry point, where embracing confusion provides a foundation for honest exploration. This aligns with theories in cognitive psychology that advocate for accepting uncertainty as a precursor to deeper insight, rather than rushing to conclusions.

* **Stage 2: Working Frame** emphasizes the idea of coherence as a temporary tool. This reflects the philosophical concept of "epistemic humility," which acknowledges the limitations of one's current understanding while still allowing for action. The "seams" mentioned can be tied to ideas of cognitive flexibility where acknowledging gaps enhances adaptability.

* **Stage 3: Action (Acting 'As If')** shifts focus to a proactive stance where action is taken without full certainty of outcomes. This can relate to adaptive leadership theories that encourage taking calculated risks while remaining open to feedback and revision, thus mitigating the risk of becoming overly rigid in one's approach.

* **Stage 4: The Mandatory Return** highlights the necessity of revisiting and reevaluating frameworks to prevent them from solidifying into dogmas. This can be connected to the concept of "double-loop learning," which emphasizes learning from experiences and being willing to change underlying beliefs and frameworks based on new evidence.

* The notion of **Systemic Health** as a quiet discipline champions the ability to treat coherence as a scaffolding structure rather than a finality. This supports modern organizational theories that promote continuous improvement and adaptability as critical success factors in complex environments.

* Finally, the inquiry aspect triggers essential discussions about how insights derived from experiences can lead to more robust sensemaking processes, aiding organizations and individuals alike in staying responsive to shifting realities. The focus on revisability without shame speaks to fostering a culture of psychological safety, which is vital for innovative environments.
