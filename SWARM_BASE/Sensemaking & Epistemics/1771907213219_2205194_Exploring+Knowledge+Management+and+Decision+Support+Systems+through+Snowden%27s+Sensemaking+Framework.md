# Exploring Knowledge Management and Decision Support Systems through Snowden's Sensemaking Framework

[x/tends from trail w/Bob-RJ and Rotkapchen](https://app.me.bot/memory/1GGLCR6YVH2G5PQS)

To help you formulate a prompt investigating the role of knowledge management (KM) and decision support systems (DSS) across individual, team, organizational, and societal levels, using Dave Snowden’s sensemaking framework, here are layered and context-aware questions, organized by system level and sensemaking principle:

**🔹 CROSS-CUTTING QUESTIONS (All Levels)**

These probe general applicability before zooming into each layer:

- How do knowledge management and decision support systems differ in effectiveness across ordered, complicated, complex, and chaotic domains as defined by the Cynefin framework?
- In what ways can enabling vs. governing constraints be embedded in KM/DSS design to support adaptive sensemaking?
- How might the design of decision support tools change when targeting abduction (best explanation) rather than deduction or induction?
- What role do informal networks and tacit knowledge play in augmenting formal KM systems during crisis sensemaking?

**🔸 INDIVIDUAL LEVEL**

Context & Complexity

- How do individuals use heuristics or abductive reasoning when faced with overwhelming or ambiguous data from KM/DSS tools?
- How can KM systems support individuals in transitioning between contexts (e.g., from complicated to complex) without cognitive overload?

Constraints

- What enabling constraints (e.g. nudges, prompts, interface design) best support individual adaptability in using DSS under stress?

Mistakes to Watch

- How can DSS systems avoid reinforcing cognitive biases or false correlations when individuals rely heavily on inductive reasoning?

**🔸 TEAM LEVEL**

Context & Complexity

- How do teams collectively interpret signals from KM/DSS systems when operating in the complex domain (e.g., product innovation)?
- How do different roles within teams influence the distribution and interpretation of knowledge?

Constraints

- How can teams be structured with enabling constraints that allow adaptive responses without fragmenting their shared sensemaking?

Resilience vs. Robustness

- How can DSS promote resilience over robustness by helping teams maintain identity and purpose under pressure?

**🔸 ORGANIZATIONAL LEVEL**

Context & Complexity

- How can organizations use the Cynefin framework to classify their decision environments and align KM/DSS strategies accordingly?
- How do formal governance structures interact with enabling constraints that promote innovation and adaptability?

Natural Science & Method

- How can organizations integrate insights from complexity science and constructor theory into the design of KM/DSS?

Assessment Cycles

- How do organizations navigate the Assess → Adapt → Accept → Transcend cycle in strategic planning with KM/DSS?

**🔸 SOCIETAL LEVEL**

Context & Complexity

- How can societal decision-making (e.g., public health or climate policy) avoid overreliance on top-down knowledge systems and instead foster collective sensemaking?
- What role can large-scale DSS (e.g., public dashboards, Wikipedia) play as enabling constraints in pluralistic societies?

Knowledge Gaps

- How should public KM systems surface epistemic uncertainty and conflicting expert opinions transparently (à la Triopticon)?

Mismatches

- What are the risks of applying ordered-system logic (e.g., standardized policies) to complex societal problems, and how can KM/DSS guard against this?

**🧭 PROMPT CONSTRUCTION STARTER**

Using the above, you can now synthesize prompts like:

“How can enabling constraints embedded in decision support systems enhance adaptive sensemaking across individual, team, organizational, and societal levels—particularly in complex and chaotic contexts as described by Snowden’s Cynefin framework?”

Or more exploratory:

“Investigate how knowledge management systems and decision support architectures can support abductive reasoning and resilience, rather than just robust prediction, in multi-level complex systems from the lens of Dave Snowden’s sensemaking principles.”
