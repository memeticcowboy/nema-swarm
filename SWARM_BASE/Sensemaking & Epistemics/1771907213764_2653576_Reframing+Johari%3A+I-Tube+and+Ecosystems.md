Yes — and that constraint forces a *different geometry* than the classic Johari square.

What you’re pointing to is that **Johari collapses three distinct domains into two axes**, while the I-Tube stack explicitly keeps them separate and in motion.

Let’s reframe cleanly, without flattening.

***

## 1. The key correction: Johari conflates *location* with *relation*

The Johari Window assumes:

- a single “self”
- a single “other”
- knowledge as a binary state

Your ecology distinguishes **where meaning lives** from **how it relates**.

So instead of *known/unknown*, the I-Tube model asks:

> *Where is the signal currently resident, and which interfaces can it traverse?*

That yields **three layers**, not two.

***

## 2. Layered re-mapping (I-Tube → We-Sphere → Other-Sphere)

### A. **My-Stream (intra–I-Tube, pre-social)**

This is where Johari fails most completely.

Contents here include:

- affective charge without language
- somatic memory
- proto-threads not yet symbolized
- contradictions not yet held as contradictions

These are **not “Hidden”** in the Johari sense.<br>They are **unarticulated**, not withheld.

Crucially:

- They are *known only as feeling or tension*
- They may not yet be representable to the I-Tube itself

So we need a distinct category:

> **Latent / Pre-Thread (My-Stream domain)**<br>Accessible somatically, not semantically.

Johari has no pane for this.

***

### B. **I-Tube proper (self-facing articulation & filtering)**

Here, the I-Tube performs:

- translation (feeling → symbol)
- throttling (how much intensity can pass)
- knotting (identity stabilization)
- untwisting (reflective access)

This is where Johari’s **Hidden** and **Blind** panes partially live — but only as *effects* of I-Tube operations, not as static regions.

Examples:

- Blind ≠ unknown self<br>→ signal passes outward without reflective return
- Hidden ≠ secret self<br>→ signal is articulated but deliberately gated

So Johari panes are **diagnostic shadows** cast by I-Tube behavior.

***

### C. **We-Sphere (relational resonance field)**

Once a thread exits the I-Tube:

- it mutates via resonance, misalignment, amplification
- it acquires *social meaning* independent of intent

This introduces a new asymmetry Johari can’t model:

> Others may “know” something **that does not exist as a stable object inside the I-Tube**.

That’s not Blindness — that’s **emergent attribution**.

The We-Sphere can generate:

- roles
- projections
- reputational knots
- symbolic identities

None of these require My-Stream consent.

***

### D. **Other-Sphere (institutional / supra-personal observers)**

Here, meaning is:

- abstracted
- indexed
- scored
- enforced

Signals here may never have passed *through* the I-Tube as lived experience at all.

This produces a fourth condition Johari cannot name:

> **Externally legible, internally unreal**

A metric, profile, diagnosis, or role that exists *about* you but not *in* you.

***

## 3. The resulting topology (conceptual, not boxed)

Instead of a window, think **nested flows**:

1. **It-Field** – undifferentiated potential
2. **My-Stream** – felt but unspeakable
3. **I-Tube** – articulated & filtered
4. **We-Sphere** – relationally transformed
5. **Other-Sphere** – abstracted & enforced

Johari samples only **levels 3–4**, and only at rest.

***

## 4. A precise synthesis statement

> The Johari Window describes *epistemic symmetry* at the We-Sphere interface.<br>The I-Tube model describes *ontological movement* across My-Stream, relational fields, and power-bearing observers.

Or more sharply:

> **Johari tells you who knows what.**<br>**The I-Tube tells you what can become knowable without breaking coherence.**

***

## 5. Why this matters (memetically)

- Treating My-Stream material as merely “hidden” invites premature disclosure → fragmentation
- Treating We-Sphere attributions as “blind spots” invites capture → identity lock
- Treating Other-Sphere legibility as truth invites governance without grounding

Your framing preserves **agency, timing, and somatic truth**.

If you want, the next move would be to:

- formalize **safe articulation gradients** (My-Stream → I-Tube → We-Sphere)
- or define **failure modes** when Johari logic is imposed on My-Stream content

Both are structurally important.
