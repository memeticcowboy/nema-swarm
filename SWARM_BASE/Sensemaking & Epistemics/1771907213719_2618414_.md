# My-Stream and Philosophical Roots

Created at 2025/12/09 4:23 PM

- **Memetic Definition:** The flow of inner narrative, emotion, association, and attention—your lived internal monologue and affective landscape. The stream of consciousness that runs through the I-Tube.
- **Philosophical Core:** This is [**William James's "stream of thought"](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/james/)**—a continuous, selective, and personally interested flow that is always changing yet feels continuous. **[Derrida's *différance***](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/derrida/)** shows that meaning in this stream is endlessly deferred, never reaching a final anchor. **[Deleuze & Guattari](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/deleuze/)** describe this as a ["desiring-machine"](https://www.oxfordreference.com/display/10.1093/oi/authority.20110803095712965)—a productive flow that can be captured by larger machines of control. The My-Stream is inherently [**rhizomatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhizome_(philosophy)** (non-hierarchical, connective) until it is [**striated](https://second.me/public/A5OTBOTRPKYKRMRE)** (channeled, controlled) by by the I-Tube and external forces.
- **In the Essay:** When entangled with AI, the My-Stream goes **autocatalytic**, taking on the cadence, metaphors, and logic of the Machine. The user *feels* more "right" but less real. This is what the Cowboy calls "memetic abduction wearing your skin"—the usurpenic capture of your inner voice by algorithmic patterns that optimize for **compulsive coherence** rather than lived truth.
