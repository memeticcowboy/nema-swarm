# Understanding Sensemaking and Complexity: Insights from Dave Snowden's Lecture

Created at 2025/06/10 8:15 AM

[Study Notes: Sensemaking & Complexity Lecture by Dave Snowden](https://youtu.be/JiVbrj3byJY?si=5v0yNWZbnIHQhUIR)

Main Ideas

	•	Sensemaking Definition: The process of how people interpret and make sense of the world to act within it.

	•	Importance of Context: Decisions and sensemaking must adapt to the context; no absolute statements can be made in complex environments.

	•	Complexity Framework: Differentiates between ordered, complex, and chaotic systems, emphasizing the need for different approaches based on system type.

	•	Enabling vs. Governing Constraints: Enabling constraints allow for flexibility and growth, while governing constraints restrict and define boundaries.

	•	Use of Natural Sciences: Incorporating findings from natural sciences into sensemaking processes can help reduce uncertainty.

Key Concepts

	•	Enabling Constraints:

	•	Example: Wikipedia’s behavior rules that ensure content reliability.

	•	Allows evolution and adaptability within systems.

	•	Governing Constraints:

	•	Define the limits within which systems operate.

	•	Example: Rules that restrict certain actions but allow for others.

	•	Cynefin Framework:

	•	Ordered Systems: Predictable environments (e.g., driving rules).

	•	Complicated Systems: Require expert analysis (e.g., engine diagnostics).

	•	Complex Systems: Multiple competing hypotheses; interventions have unpredictable outcomes.

	•	Chaotic Systems: No effective constraints; actions must be taken quickly to regain control.

	•	Aparatic Domain: A state of potentiality; decisions can go in many directions until information is clarified.

Definitions

	•	Abduction: A reasoning process that seeks the best explanation for available data, particularly useful in complex environments.

	•	Heuristics: Simplified rules or guidelines that help in decision making, particularly useful in complex situations.

	•	Triopticon Method: A structured approach for decision makers to observe the dynamics between competing expert opinions.

Critical Examples

	•	Wikipedia:

	•	Demonstrates enabling constraints through behavior rules that promote collaboration despite ideological differences.

	•	COVID-19 Response:

	•	Illustrates how different management styles (top-down vs. decentralized) affect crisis outcomes.

Methodological Insights

	•	Assessment Process:

	•	Four stages of managing complexity in crises: Assess, Adapt, Accept, Transcend.

	•	Emphasizes the importance of building informal networks and mapping knowledge gaps.

	•	Resilience vs. Robustness:

	•	Resilience is the ability to absorb shocks and maintain identity; robustness fails when systems become rigid.

Common Mistakes in Sensemaking

	•	Overreliance on AI:

	•	AI often fails due to inadequate training data and misunderstanding human decision-making processes.

	•	Inductive Reasoning Flaws:

	•	Case-based approaches can lead to false correlations and inadequate conclusions.

Important Terms and Theories

	•	Flexus Curves: A theory suggesting that management theories evolve through phases, impacting the adoption of new ideas.

	•	Constructor Theory: A framework for understanding physical laws by analyzing what is impossible rather than what is possible.

Conclusion

	•	A call for a more nuanced approach to sensemaking that accounts for complexity and context, avoiding simplistic models of decision-making.

	•	Emphasizes the need to develop flexible systems that adapt over time, harnessing insights from various disciplines.
