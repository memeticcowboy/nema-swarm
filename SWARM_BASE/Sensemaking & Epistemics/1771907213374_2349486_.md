The Sanskrit term **Spanda** (स्पन्द) is a single word with significant meaning in Indian philosophy, particularly in Kashmir Shaivism. Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

**Spanda** (स्पन्द):

- **Spanda** means "vibration," "pulsation," or "subtle movement." In the context of Kashmir Shaivism, it is a central concept referring to the dynamic, creative vibration of divine consciousness (often associated with Shiva) that underlies the manifestation of the universe. This vibration is the subtle, pulsating energy that animates all existence, bridging the absolute (transcendent consciousness) and the relative (manifested world).
- **Philosophical Context**: Spanda is elaborated in texts like the *Spanda Kārikās*, where it represents the spontaneous, rhythmic pulsation of the divine, through which the universe is created, sustained, and dissolved. It is not merely physical movement but the inherent dynamism of consciousness itself.
- **Yogic and Tantric Context**: In spiritual practice, Spanda can be experienced through meditation, mantra, or awareness of the subtle vibrations within the body or consciousness, leading to realization of one’s unity with the divine. It reflects the interplay between stillness and movement, unity and diversity.
- **Symbolic Context**: Spanda symbolizes the living, pulsating essence of reality, where even apparent stillness contains subtle vibration. It is the dynamic expression of Shiva’s creative power (*Shakti*).
