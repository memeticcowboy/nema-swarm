# How the Brain Fills in the Blanks

Created at 2024/07/09 6:43 AM

## Resources
- https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/what-is-a-human/202312/how-the-brain-fills-in-the-blanks

## Insight
This article from Psychology Today explores how our brains construct parts of our experiences, often filling in gaps and creating illusions. The key outcomes highlight how our perception of reality is a construct of our brain, which can be easily fooled. Understanding these mechanisms can help us better navigate our sensory experiences and be more aware of the limitations and biases in our perception. Let's dive deeper into the fascinating ways our brain shapes our reality.

**🧠 Brain's Constructive Nature**
- **Brains construct experiences**: Our brains actively construct parts of our experiences rather than passively receiving information from the world.
- **Perception is subjective**: We do not see the world as it is; we see it as our brain constructs it, influenced by our needs and experiences.

**👁️ Visual Perception**
- **Contours are brain-created**: Contours we see are not present in the light hitting our retina but are added by our brain during the visual process.
- **Field of vision limitations**: Our field of vision is limited to 180° in width and 135° in height, unlike many animals with nearly 360° vision.

**🔍 Illusions and Gaps**
- **Sharpness illusion**: Only a small part of our field of vision is sharp, but our brain creates the illusion that everything we see is sharp.
- **Blind spot**: There is a part of the retina with no receptors, creating a blind spot, yet we do not perceive any gaps in our vision.

**🎭 Sensory Adaptations**
- **Rocking world experiment**: Pressing the side of one eyeball creates the illusion of a rocking world, showing how our brain constructs our visual experience.
- **Two noses illusion**: Crossing fingers and touching the nose creates the illusion of having two noses due to unusual sensory feedback.

**🦾 Phantom Limbs**
- **Phantom limb phenomenon**: After an amputation, some people feel the presence of a limb that is no longer there, a construct of the brain's body model.
- **Mirror box therapy**: Using a mirror box, patients can regain control over a 'paralyzed' phantom limb by creating the illusion of movement.

**🌍 Reality vs. Perception**
- **Nature's true form**: Without a brain to perceive it, nature is colorless, soundless, and scentless; our senses give it content.
- **Active interpretation**: The brain actively looks for patterns and interprets the world, rather than passively receiving sensory input.
