# Transhumanism and Buddhism: A Journey Towards Universal Well-Being

Created at 2024/09/24 8:57 AM

Via [David Pearce on Quora](https://www.quora.com/Is-transhumanism-compatible-with-Buddhism)

"We advocate the well-being of all sentience, including humans, non-human animals, and any future artificial intellects, modified life forms, or other intelligences to which technological and scientific advance may give rise."
(The Transhumanist Declaration (1998, 2009)

“May all that hath life be delivered from suffering.”
(Gautama Buddha)

Yes. Buddhists and transhumanists want to end suffering. But how can noble words best be translated into effective action?

Gautama Buddha couldn’t urge recalibrating the hedonic treadmill or the use of CRISPR genome-editing to reprogram the biosphere (cf. Genetically designing a happy biosphere). Someone born around 2500 years ago couldn’t promote the development of sentience-friendly superintelligence or biotechnology to phase out aging. Yet everything we know about the historical Buddha suggests he was a pragmatist, interested in what works empirically, not a dogmatic metaphysician. Today we know suffering exists because nasty experiences have been genetically fitness-enhancing for biological organisms. Mastery of our reward circuitry and genetic source code will soon make misery and malaise entirely optional throughout the living world. Would Buddha born today reproach transhumanists for not understanding the true meaning of Buddhism? Or welcome the tools of biotechnology to create post-Darwinian life based on gradients of intelligent bliss – nirvana, so to speak, or something close enough?

## Insight
Imagine a world where suffering is optional, and happiness is a universal right! The intersection of transhumanism and Buddhism opens up fascinating discussions about well-being and the future of sentience.

- Transhumanism and Buddhism both share a core goal: the alleviation of suffering. While Buddhism emphasizes mindfulness and inner peace, transhumanism advocates for technological advancements to enhance well-being. This synergy suggests that we can use modern tools to achieve ancient wisdom, creating a future where happiness is not just a fleeting moment but a sustained state of being.
- The idea of 'reprogramming' our biology through biotechnology resonates with the Buddhist concept of overcoming ignorance and suffering. Just as meditation helps us rewire our minds, advancements like CRISPR could allow us to edit our genetic predispositions towards happiness. This raises intriguing questions about the ethics of such interventions and whether they align with the essence of Buddhist teachings.
- Buddha's pragmatism suggests he would likely embrace the tools of modern science if they lead to the end of suffering. Imagine him advocating for a world where aging and disease are relics of the past, allowing sentient beings to thrive. This perspective challenges us to consider how we can responsibly harness technology to create a more compassionate world.
- The concept of a 'happy biosphere' aligns with both transhumanist and Buddhist ideals, promoting a harmonious existence for all sentient beings. By designing environments that foster well-being, we can create ecosystems where joy and peace flourish. This vision encourages us to think beyond ourselves and consider the collective happiness of all life forms.
- As we explore the potential of artificial intelligence, the question arises: can machines experience suffering or joy? If we create sentient AI, how do we ensure their well-being? This dilemma invites us to reflect on our responsibilities towards all forms of intelligence, echoing the Buddhist principle of compassion for all living beings.
