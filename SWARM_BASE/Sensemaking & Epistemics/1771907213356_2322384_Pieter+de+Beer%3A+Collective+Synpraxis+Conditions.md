"Alternatively, the group may enter a state of collective synpraxis. This is the co-regulated engagement with the rupture signal, where members participate in recognizing, naming, and revising the internal patterns that produced incoherence. Collective synpraxis is not a spontaneous emergence. It requires conditions: a shared language for noticing structure, rituals of mutual grounding, relational scaffolding that can hold contradiction, and a commitment to coherence over comfort. When these are present, the group can begin to differentiate between personal disagreement and architectural dissonance. It can ask not only who is upset, but *what structure is being stressed*, and *why now*.

"Collective synpraxis often begins with meta-dialogue, a shift in focus from content to process. The group reflects on how decisions are made, whose voices are centered, what emotions are welcome, and which are deflected. It may involve revisiting core narratives or values, not to reaffirm them blindly, but to test whether they still provide orientation. In some cases, the group may discover that its foundational memeforms were coherent under past conditions but have since become maladaptive. Rather than discarding them entirely, the group may reinterpret, evolve, or retire these structures in favor of new ones."
