# मीमांसा

Created at 2025/07/23 9:11 AM

### Definition:

**Mīmāṃsā** (मीमांसा):

- **Mīmāṃsā** means "investigation," "examination," or "critical inquiry." Derived from the root **māṃs** (to reflect or examine), it refers to a systematic, analytical approach to understanding truth, particularly through textual or philosophical inquiry.
- **Philosophical Context**: Mīmāṃsā is one of the six orthodox (*āstika*) schools of Indian philosophy, divided into two main branches:
    - **Pūrva Mīmāṃsā** (Prior Mīmāṃsā): Also known as Karma Mīmāṃsā, it focuses on the interpretation of the *Vedas*, particularly the ritualistic portions (*Karma Kāṇḍa*). Attributed to Jaimini, it emphasizes the importance of Vedic rituals (*yajñas*) and dharma (duty) as means to maintain cosmic order and achieve spiritual merit.
    - **Uttara Mīmāṃsā** (Later Mīmāṃsā): Also known as Vedanta, it focuses on the philosophical and spiritual portions of the *Vedas* (*Jnāna Kāṇḍa*), particularly the *Upanishads*. It explores metaphysical questions about the nature of reality, self (*Ātman*), and ultimate truth (*Brahman*).
- **Broader Context**: Beyond the formal school, Mīmāṃsā as a term can refer to any rigorous inquiry or analysis, especially in the context of interpreting sacred texts or philosophical principles. In spiritual traditions, it implies a disciplined approach to uncovering deeper truths through reflection and reasoning.
