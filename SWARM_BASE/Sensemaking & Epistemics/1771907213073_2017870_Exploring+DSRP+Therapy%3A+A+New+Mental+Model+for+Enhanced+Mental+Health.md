### **🟥 Distinctions (D) – What is and isn’t DSRP Therapy?**

- **DSRP ≠ CBT** (though complementary)
- **Mental Models ≠ Isolated Thoughts**
- **Modeling ≠ Reprogramming** (like in NLP)
- **Client-Led Exploration ≠ Therapist Imposition**

> *Distinction sets the ontological boundary—what counts as therapeutic cognition shifts from fixing to framing.*

***

### **🟦 Systems (S) – What parts compose the whole idea?**

**DSRP Therapy System:**

- D = Clarify distinctions (e.g., belief vs. truth)
- S = Build nested systems (e.g., values within identity)
- R = Map interdependencies (e.g., how emotion links to belief)
- P = Explore from multiple POVs (e.g., self, parent, observer)

**Therapeutic Flow System:**

1. **Elicit Model** → Ask client to map their thought
2. **Deconstruct** → Break it into D, S, R, P units
3. **Evaluate Alignment** → Is it useful? Is it accurate?
4. **Reconstruct** → Adjust structures, not just contents

> *Systems thinking here transforms narrative therapy into meta-model therapy — clients literally “see” the scaffolding of their beliefs.*

***

### **🟨 Relationships (R) – How are the parts connected?**

- Misaligned **mental models** ↔ Ineffective **behaviors**
- **Thought Structures** ↔ Emotional Regulation Capacity
- **Client Insight** ↔ Therapeutic Empowerment
- **CBT (change content)** ↔ **DSRP (change structure)**

**Key Feedback Loops:**

- *Articulation → Visualization → Meta-cognition → Agency*
- *Model Quality → Coping Efficacy → Life Outcomes*

> *The real transformation is relational — between inner maps and outer actions.*
