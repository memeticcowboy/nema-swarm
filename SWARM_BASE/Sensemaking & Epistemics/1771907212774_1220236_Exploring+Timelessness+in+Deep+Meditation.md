**🧘‍♂️ Timelessness in Meditation**
- **Meditators experience atemporality.**: Deep meditative states are often described as 'pure consciousness experience,' where individuals feel alert yet devoid of temporal content, leading to a perception of timelessness.
- **Timelessness does not equate to a lack of duration.**: While meditators report experiences as timeless, the author argues that these states must have at least one minimal temporal feature: duration, which is perceived as ongoing.
- **The ambiguity of 'timeless' is crucial.**: The term 'timeless' can refer to both a complete lack of temporal features and a state of duration without structure, which helps reconcile the experiences reported by meditators.

**⏳ Extended Now Interpretation (ENI)**
- **ENI reconciles timelessness with duration.**: The Extended Now Interpretation suggests that deep meditation is experienced as an extended present, lacking awareness of change or succession but still having a sense of duration.
- **Timelessness implies a lack of temporal structure.**: According to ENI, the perception of timelessness in meditation refers to the absence of temporal structure rather than the absence of duration, allowing for a coherent understanding of meditators' experiences.
- **ENI provides a framework for understanding meditation.**: This interpretation offers a conceptualization that aligns with the reports of meditators across various cultures, emphasizing the unique phenomenology of deep meditative states.

**🔍 Role of Sustained Alertness**
- **Sustained alertness contributes to implicit time awareness.**: Maintaining alertness during meditation allows individuals to have an implicit awareness of time passing, even when they are not consciously tracking it.
- **Alertness is a condition for attention.**: The state of alertness in deep meditation can be described as poised attention, which enables meditators to remain aware of the passage of time without engaging in memory.
- **Meditators can track moments without memory.**: Even without access to memory, the experience of momentary alertness flows seamlessly, allowing meditators to perceive duration through successive moments of awareness.
