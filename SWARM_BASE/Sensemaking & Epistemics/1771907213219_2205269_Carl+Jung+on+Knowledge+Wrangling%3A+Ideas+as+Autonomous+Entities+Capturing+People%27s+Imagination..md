To differentiate between "knowledge management" and "knowledge wrangling" (in the context of the Carl Jung quote), we can consider the following perspectives:

**Knowledge Management:**

1. The focus is on capturing, organizing, and distributing knowledge within an organization or system.
2. It views knowledge as a resource that can be managed and controlled, typically by a centralized authority.
3. In this view, people are seen as the possessors or carriers of knowledge, and the goal of management is to ensure that this knowledge is used effectively.

**Knowledge Wrangling (in the context of the quote):**

1. The focus is on understanding and harnessing the power of ideas as autonomous entities that capture people's attention and imagination.
2. It views knowledge as a dynamic and organic process, where ideas emerge and evolve through collaboration and interaction.
3. In this view, ideas are seen as the driving force behind innovation and progress, and the goal of wrangling is to facilitate and support the growth and development of these ideas.

The distinction between these two concepts can be understood as a difference in perspective and focus. Knowledge management is more concerned with managing and controlling knowledge as a resource, while knowledge wrangling is more concerned with understanding and supporting the dynamic and collaborative nature of ideas and innovation.
