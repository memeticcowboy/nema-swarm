# सर्वं विश्वहिताय

Created at 2025/07/16 4:19 PM

The Sanskrit phrase **Sarvaṁ Viśvahitāya** (सर्वं विश्वहिताय) can be broken down into its components: **Sarvaṁ** (सर्वं) and **Viśvahitāya** (विश्वहिताय). Below is the definition and explanation.

### Definition:

1. **Sarvaṁ** (सर्वं): This term means "all," "everything," or "the whole." It is a pronoun in Sanskrit that denotes universality, encompassing all beings, things, or existence.
2. **Viśvahitāya** (विश्वहिताय): This is a compound word:
    - **Viśva** (विश्व): Means "universe," "all beings," or "the entire world." It signifies the collective totality of existence.
    - **Hitāya** (हिताय): A dative form of *Hita*, meaning "benefit," "welfare," "good," or "well-being." The dative case indicates purpose or intention, so *Hitāya* translates to "for the benefit of" or "for the welfare of."
    - Together, **Viśvahitāya** means "for the benefit of all beings," "for the welfare of the universe," or "for the good of all."
3. **Sarvaṁ Viśvahitāya Combined**: The phrase translates to "all for the benefit of the universe" or "everything for the welfare of all beings." It conveys the idea that all actions, intentions, or existence are directed toward the universal good or the well-being of all. In a spiritual or philosophical context, it reflects a selfless orientation toward universal welfare, often associated with dharma (righteousness) or the altruistic principles found in Indian traditions like Vedanta, Yoga, or Buddhism.
