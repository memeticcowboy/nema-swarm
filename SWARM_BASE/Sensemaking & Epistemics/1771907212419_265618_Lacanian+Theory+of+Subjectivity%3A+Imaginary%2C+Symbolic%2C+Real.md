### The Imaginary
- Based on images and identities
- Least mediated register of experience
- Includes fantasies and idealized self-images
- Utilized by advertisers to appeal to basic desires

### The Symbolic
- Realm of language, signs, and codes
- Establishes social norms and propriety
- Using "I" in language forces one to play a subject

### The Real
- Non-linguistic, unmediatable aspect of experience
- Source of anxiety and neurotic adaptations
- Often avoided or repressed

## Formation of the Subject

### Mirror Stage
- Moment when a child recognizes their reflection
- Fundamental misidentification of self with image
- Root of lifelong anxiety and desire

### Desire and Lack
- Desire is largely unconscious
- Structured by others and society
- Always points to something outside oneself
- Creates a sense of incompleteness or lack

## Social Media and the Ideal Self

### Digital Self-Representation
- Concretizes the imaginary crowd through likes and views
- Attempts to create an ideal "I" that can never be fully realized
- Reflects the desire to be seen and validated by others

### Paradox of Subjectivity
- Subjects see themselves as objects but can never truly be objects
- Desire cannot be captured in images or language
- Constant struggle to reconcile internal experience with external representation
