**DD:** What other concepts, potentially unrelated to trust, could be factored into this model and applied to trust and mistrust dynamics and communication and coordination?

**MC:** Here is a curated constellation of **concepts from diverse disciplines**—many not directly about trust—that can **augment a nuanced model of trust/mistrust dynamics**, especially in **communication, coordination, and collective sensemaking**:

### 🧠 Cognitive & Epistemic Tools

1. **Cognitive Load Theory** (Sweller)

    - Trust breaks under excessive cognitive burden. Simplify interfaces or narratives to restore functional coordination.

2. **Bayesian Updating**

    - Trust becomes a function of updating priors with new signals. Allows for dynamic recalibration over time.

3. **Signal-to-Noise Ratio**

    - In high-noise environments, trust requires noise filtration. Emphasizes the Analyzer and Optimizer roles.

4. **Tacit Knowledge** (Polanyi)

    - Many trust signals are non-verbal or embodied. Coordination often depends on things we "know but cannot tell."

5. **Error-Correction Codes** (from Info Theory)

    - Treat trust as redundancy in communication—fragile systems rely on repeated signals to verify consistency.

### 🌀 Philosophical & Existential Concepts

6. **Heideggerian “Thrownness” (Geworfenheit)**

    - We’re always already in situations we didn’t choose. Trust forms from the necessity of engaging *while uncertain*.

7. **Hyperobjects** (Morton)

    - Institutions or dynamics too vast to comprehend—climate change, capitalism—demand proxy trust structures.

8. **Anamnesis** (Platonic remembrance)

    - Some trust is pre-conscious; ancestral, affective. Might explain “unreasonable” trust or mistrust rooted in pattern memory.

9. **Linguistic Relativity** (Sapir-Whorf)

    - Trust is shaped by what our language allows us to perceive. Naming, framing, and metaphor become trust determinants.

10. **Wabi-Sabi** (Japanese aesthetic)

- Embrace imperfection and impermanence. Trust does not mean flawlessness—perhaps trust grows through vulnerability.

### 🧬 Biological & Ecological Metaphors

11. **Mycorrhizal Networks**

- Trust as decentralized information-sharing. Plants “trust” fungi with their nutrients because of co-evolutionary interdependence.

12. **Homeostasis**

- Trust isn’t always about change—it’s about maintaining internal balance amid external shifts.

13. **Symbiosis**

- Trust emerges not from sameness but *mutual difference*. High-trust systems often balance autonomy and interdependence.

14. **Immune System Priming**

- Mistrust isn’t pathology—it’s *necessary vigilance*. Trust evolves when thresholds are tuned over time.

### 🧘‍♀️ Psychological & Relational Dynamics

15. **Attachment Styles**

- Secure vs. anxious/avoidant styles radically shape how trust is given or withheld in personal or organizational systems.

16. **Polyvagal Theory** (Porges)

- Trust depends on physiological state—people in dorsal vagal shutdown can’t trust *even if they want to*.

17. **Projection / Transference**

- We often “trust” or “mistrust” based on prior templates. Recognizing this can break loops of misplaced suspicion.

18. **Resonance**

- More than agreement—shared emotional tone. Systems that *feel* right gain trust faster, even without full comprehension.

### 🧩 Social & Cultural Mechanisms

19. **Stigmergy**

- Coordination without communication. Ants trust signals in the environment. Could we design systems where trust is ambient?

20. **Game Theory – Iterated Prisoner’s Dilemma**

- Trust dynamics depend on *shadow of the future*. Repeat interactions foster cooperation over defection.

21. **Narrative Coherence**

- Trust grows when the story makes sense. When narratives fracture, trust erodes—even if “facts” remain constant.

22. **Cultural Scripts**

- Societies encode “when” to trust. In high-context cultures, trust is pre-loaded into relationships—not easily reset.

### 🌌 Abstract Systems and Meta-Models

23. **Metastability** (complex systems)

- Trust operates best near edges—stable enough to rely on, unstable enough to adapt. Total stability = stagnation.

24. **Simulacra (Baudrillard)**

- Sometimes we trust representations *instead* of reality (e.g. ratings, likes, badges). Trust becomes symbolic performance.

25. **Actor-Network Theory (Latour)**

- Trust isn’t just between people—it’s between people and things (tools, documents, AIs). Tech is part of trust ecology.
