# Understanding Reality Through Science

Created at 2024/06/21 3:08 PM

How do we know what is real? https://youtu.be/dOZDwH_4A4A?si=Yxwh6e6VeIwM8MYS

This transcript discusses the question "How do we know what is real?" from a scientific perspective. The speakers argue that science is not just for academics but a practical, everyday approach to understanding reality. They explain that being scientific involves constantly refining our thinking through observation, questioning, experimentation, and synthesis of information. This process, which they call the "IO Loop" (Information-Organization Loop), helps us align our mental models with reality. The speakers emphasize that reality is always communicating with us, and it's our job to listen and adapt our understanding accordingly. They warn against confirmation bias and stress the importance of being open to new information. The discussion concludes by asserting that science is democratic, accessible to everyone, and the most useful tool we have for understanding the world around us.
