# Imagination: The Cosmic Architect of New Realities

Created at 2025/01/06 8:16 AM

The sources describe imagination as a **constructive form of thought** that precedes action. [1, 2] It is the **"light" by which people can explore new ideas and experiences. [2] Every inventor and discoverer used imagination to move from precedent to experience. [3] Precedent states that things can’t be done, while experience proves that they have been done. [3]

Imagination is a **plastic power** that shapes the things of the senses into new forms and ideals. [4] However, the sources distinguish imagination from “fancy” or daydreaming. [4] Daydreaming is a form of mental dissipation, while imagination is constructive. [4, 5] **To cultivate imagination, it must be exercised and supplied with nourishment. [3]**

The sources provide the following examples of the power of imagination:

- A builder uses an architect’s plan to build a structure, and the architect uses imagination to create those plans. [3]
- A captain of industry uses imagination to plan a corporation. [3]

**Objects in the material world are like clay in the potter’s hand. The real creation happens in the “Master Mind,” which uses imagination to do its work.**
