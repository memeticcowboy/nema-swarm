You're referring to the famous quote from the ancient Greek philosopher Heraclitus, who said, "No man ever steps in the same river twice, for it's not the same river and he's not the same man." This quote highlights the ever-changing nature of both the world around us and ourselves. 

In this metaphor, reality is like a flowing river, constantly moving and evolving. Just as you can't step into the same river twice, because the water has already moved on and the river has changed, you also can't step out of the river of reality, because you are an integral part of it. 

This idea of impermanence and constant flux is a central concept in many Eastern philosophies, including Buddhism and Taoism. It encourages us to embrace change and live in the present moment, rather than clinging to the past or worrying about the future.
