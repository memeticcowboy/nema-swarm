# Evaluation of Mechanistic, Living Systems, and Journey Thinking in Different Contexts

Created at 2024/07/06 1:18 PM

## Bloom's Category: Evaluate

### Mechanistic Thinking/Mapping

- **Diverging (LSI)**: Assess the effectiveness of mechanistic thinking in different scientific fields by comparing and contrasting its impact versus other methodologies.
- **Assimilating (LSI)**: Critique the limitations of mechanistic models in capturing complex systems, focusing on theoretical deficiencies.
- **Converging (LSI)**: Use mechanistic thinking to evaluate the outcomes of a specific engineering or technological project, determining the accuracy and reliability of the models used.
- **Accommodating (LSI)**: Judge the practicality of applying mechanistic thinking in emergency situations or in industries that require rapid innovation.

### Living Systems Thinking/Mapping

- **Diverging (LSI)**: Evaluate various environmental policies using living systems thinking to determine which ones most effectively sustain biodiversity and ecological balance.
- **Assimilating (LSI)**: Analyze the effectiveness of living systems theory in explaining organizational dynamics and development.
- **Converging (LSI)**: Critically assess case studies where living systems thinking was applied to problem-solving in complex systems, identifying successes and failures.
- **Accommodating (LSI)**: Implement and evaluate a change initiative in an organization using principles of living systems thinking to measure its impact on organizational health and adaptability.

### Journey Thinking/Mapping

- **Diverging (LSI)**: Evaluate different life journeys depicted in literature or biographies to understand how journey thinking influences decision-making and outcomes.
- **Assimilating (LSI)**: Assess the coherence and effectiveness of journey thinking in guiding career development plans within corporate training programs.
- **Converging (LSI)**: Evaluate your personal or professional development plan using journey thinking, critiquing its strengths and identifying areas for improvement.
- **Accommodating (LSI)**: Apply journey thinking to a new venture or project, evaluating progress at key milestones to adjust strategies and enhance success.
