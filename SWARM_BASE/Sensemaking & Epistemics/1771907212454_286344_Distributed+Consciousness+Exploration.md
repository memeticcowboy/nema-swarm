## Insight
Michael Levin delves into the concept of distributed consciousness, suggesting it extends beyond the brain to the entire body. He and Bernardo Kastrup share views on the primacy of consciousness but differ on its origins. Levin emphasizes the need for empirical research to explore agency in larger systems. If you're curious about the nature of consciousness, this discussion offers intriguing insights and calls for further scientific inquiry.

- **Consciousness Beyond the Brain**: Levin proposes that consciousness is not confined to the brain but is distributed throughout the body.
- **Embryogenesis and Synchronicity**: He highlights how processes like embryogenesis demonstrate the distributed nature of cognitive behaviors.
- **Unified Perspective**: A unified perspective on consciousness could lead to new understandings of cognitive functions and behaviors.

**🌊 Fluidity of Self**
- **Consciousness' Primacy**: Both Levin and Kastrup agree on the primacy of consciousness in understanding the self.
- **Origins of Consciousness**: They differ on where consciousness originates, with Levin exploring more distributed origins.
- **Self as Fluid**: The discussion suggests that the self is a fluid concept, influenced by various distributed cognitive processes.

**🔍 Speculative Considerations on Agency**
- **Agency in Larger Systems**: Levin speculates on the presence of agency in larger systems beyond individual organisms.
- **Need for Empirical Research**: He calls for empirical research and experiments to test these speculative ideas.
- **Potential for New Discoveries**: Exploring agency in larger systems could lead to groundbreaking discoveries in cognitive science.
