## Hierarchy of the Outline

Given the broad and abstract nature of the concepts involved, we'll establish a hierarchy that allows for a detailed exploration and practical application of each element. Here's an explanation of the structure used in the contents above:

1. **Highest Level (First Level): Bloom's Taxonomy**
   - Bloom's categories span from basic recall to creative application, making it a suitable framework to serve as the overarching structure. Each category can act as a lens through which to view the thinking/mapping metaphors and Kolb's Learning Styles.

2. **Second Level: Thinking/Mapping Metaphors**
   - Nested within each Bloom's category, the metaphors (mechanistic, living systems, and journey thinking) can be explored to see how they apply, are understood, analyzed, etc.

3. **Third Level: Kolb's Learning Style Inventory (LSI)**
   - Each metaphor under a Bloom's category can be further dissected through the lens of Kolb's learning styles, offering insights into how different individuals might grasp and apply the concepts based on their learning preferences.
