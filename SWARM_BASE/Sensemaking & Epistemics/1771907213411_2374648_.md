### **Phenomenological Self-Study**

- **Definition:** A research style focusing on the subjective, lived experience of the researcher as primary data.
- **Key Feature:** Emphasizes *first-person phenomenology*—capturing the essence of experience as it is lived.
- **Use Case:** Often used in philosophy of mind, consciousness studies, or experiential psychology.
- **Example:** Immersing oneself in a silent meditation retreat and recording reflections to understand altered states of perception.
