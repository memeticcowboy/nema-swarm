**🔬 Exploring Goal-Directed Behavior in Collective Entities**

**🤝 Interdisciplinary Collaboration**  
- **Philosophers and Mathematicians Unite**: The research team comprises three philosophers and a mathematician, showcasing a unique interdisciplinary approach to studying complex biological systems. This collaboration aims to integrate philosophical insights with mathematical modeling to better understand collective behaviors.
- **Diverse Expertise**: The team includes Derek Skillings (UNC Greensboro), Benjamin Allen (Emmanuel College), Patrick Forber (Tufts University), and Rory Smead (Northeastern University), each bringing distinct perspectives that enhance the research's depth and breadth.
- **Innovative Methodologies**: By combining philosophical inquiry with mathematical frameworks, the project seeks to develop new methodologies for analyzing goal-directed behaviors in biological entities.

**🌱 Focus on Holobionts**  
- **Understanding Superorganisms**: The research centers on holobionts, defined as collective entities that include host organisms and their symbiotic partners. This concept challenges traditional views of individual organisms by emphasizing the interconnectedness of life forms.
- **Examples of Holobionts**: Coral reefs and lichens serve as primary examples of holobionts, illustrating how diverse species can function as a single ecological unit with shared goals and behaviors.
- **Broader Implications**: The study posits that many organisms, including humans, may be better understood as holobionts, prompting a reevaluation of individual versus collective behavior in biology.

**📈 Evolutionary Trajectories**  
- **Investigating Behavioral Evolution**: The project aims to explore how goal-directed behaviors evolve within collective entities, providing insights into the adaptive significance of these behaviors in changing environments.
- **Long-Term Research Goals**: Over the three-year duration of the grant, the research will track evolutionary patterns and the emergence of complex behaviors in holobionts, contributing to the broader field of evolutionary biology.
- **Potential for New Theories**: Findings may lead to the development of new theories regarding the evolution of cooperation and competition among species within holobionts.
