# अन्तर्-निधि

Created at 2025/07/16 4:33 PM

The Sanskrit term **Antar-Nidhi** can be broken down into its components: **Antar** (अन्तर्) and **Nidhi** (निधि). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Antar** (अन्तर्): This term means "inner," "internal," or "within." It is often used to denote something internal, such as the inner self, mind, or heart. In philosophical and spiritual contexts, Antar can refer to the inner essence or consciousness, as opposed to external phenomena.
2. **Nidhi** (निधि): This term means "treasure," "wealth," or "repository." It can refer to material wealth, spiritual treasures, or a storehouse of valuable qualities, knowledge, or divine attributes. In mythological contexts, Nidhi is associated with treasures guarded by deities like Kubera, the god of wealth.
3. **Antar-Nidhi Combined**: The term Antar-Nidhi can be interpreted as "the inner treasure" or "the treasure within." It likely refers to the spiritual or intrinsic wealth that resides within an individual, such as wisdom, consciousness, or divine qualities. In a philosophical context, it may point to the hidden treasure of the self (Ātman) or the reservoir of spiritual potential within one's heart or consciousness. In a poetic or mystical sense, it could describe the inner riches of virtues, knowledge, or divine grace that one discovers through introspection or spiritual practice.
