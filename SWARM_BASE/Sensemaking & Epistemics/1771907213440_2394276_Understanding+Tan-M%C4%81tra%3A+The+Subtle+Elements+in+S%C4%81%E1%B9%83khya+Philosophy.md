# तन्मात्र

Created at 2025/08/14 11:07 AM

The Sanskrit term **Tan-Mātra** (तन्मात्र) is a compound word with significant meaning in Indian philosophy, particularly in the Sāṃkhya system. Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Tan** (तन्): This is a pronominal base meaning "that" or "it," often used in compounds to refer to something specific. In this context, it serves as a connector to indicate the subtle essence or quality of a sensory perception.
2. **Mātra** (मात्र): This term means "measure," "element," or "essence." It denotes the minimal or fundamental unit of something, often used to describe the subtlest form of a quality or substance.
3. **Tan-Mātra Combined**: The term Tan-Mātra translates to "subtle element" or "essence of perception." In Sāṃkhya philosophy, Tanmātras are the five subtle elements—sound (*śabda*), touch (*sparśa*), form (*rūpa*), taste (*rasa*), and smell (*gandha*)—that represent the primordial essences of sensory perception. These subtle elements are the precursors to the five gross elements (*pañca-mahābhūta*: space, air, fire, water, earth) and arise from the interaction of *Prakṛti* (primordial matter) through the influence of the *tamas* and *sattva* gunas. Each Tanmātra corresponds to a specific sensory organ and gross element:<br>In yogic or Tantric contexts, the Tanmātras are contemplated or meditated upon to transcend sensory experience and understand the subtle nature of reality, leading to deeper spiritual awareness.
    - *Śabda-Tanmātra* (sound) → Space (*ākāśa*)
    - *Sparśa-Tanmātra* (touch) → Air (*vāyu*)
    - *Rūpa-Tanmātra* (form) → Fire (*agni*)
    - *Rasa-Tanmātra* (taste) → Water (*jala*)
    - *Gandha-Tanmātra* (smell) → Earth (*pṛthivī*)
