“You are not really the waker. You are not really the dreamer. You are not the deep sleeper … Waking, dreaming, and deep sleep keep coming and going throughout our days, but behind it is one common consciousness, one common awareness.”

## Insight


**🌌 Illuminating the Nature of Consciousness: Insights from Swami Sarvapriyananda**

---

**🧘‍♂️ The Essence of Advaita Vedanta**  
- **Absolute Reality Above Individuality**: Advaita Vedanta posits a higher reality known as Brahman, transcending individual identity and the physical world. It emphasizes that personal existence is rooted in Sat-Chit-Ananda (existence, consciousness, bliss).
  - The concept of Brahman denotes the ultimate truth beyond perceptions of God, individual selves, and the universe.
  
- **Two Approaches to Spirituality**: Spiritual inquiry can be either God-centered or self-inquiry based, focusing on understanding one's own existence.
  - The God-centered approach often hinges on faith while the self-inquiry methods emphasize direct personal questioning, leading to profound spiritual insights.
  
- **Nature of Suffering and Liberation**: The promise of all spiritual paths is to transcend suffering and attain peace, aligning with the core teachings of Advaita Vedanta.
  - This tradition advocates for self-realization as the means to liberation, emphasizing that recognizing our true nature alleviates suffering.

---

**🔍 Understanding Appearances vs. Reality**  
- **World as an Appearance**: Sarvapriyananda asserts that while the world reflects an apparent reality, it is ultimately a manifestation of a deeper truth, with consciousness being the foundation.
  - Realizing this distinction leads to liberation from the burdens of worldly attachments and misperceptions.

- **The Illusion of Separation**: The distinction between the observer (you) and the observed (the world) dissolves as one recognizes that all experiences occur within consciousness.
  - This perspective asserts that everything seen and felt is not separate from your essence.

- **Ultimate Reality and Self-Identity**: Our real identity is not confined to the transient experiences of individual existence, but rather, we are the embodied consciousness that transcends all forms.
  - The ultimate realization is that “You are that” which is no different from the absolute reality.

---

**🧠 Subject-Object Dynamics in Experience**  
- **Conscious Experience Structures**: The nature of human experience involves a subject experiencing an object, which in Vedanta translates to the exploration of consciousness itself.
  - Recognition of this relationship allows for a deeper understanding of both self and universe within a non-dual framework.

- **Distinguishing Mind from Consciousness**: The mind is merely an object appearing to consciousness, and understanding this helps shed the illusion of being entrapped by one's thoughts.
  - Awareness is the constant, unaffected by the fluctuations of the mind.

- **Non-Dual Reality**: The essence of Advaita Vedanta is that both subject and object exist not as separate entities but as aspects of one unified consciousness.
  - This realization promotes harmony and reduces the perceived conflicts within individual experiences.

---

**✨ Realizing the Witnessing Self and Turiya**  
- **The Four States of Consciousness**: Swami Sarvapriyananda explains that the self is the witness of the three states: waking, dreaming, and deep sleep, with Turiya as the underlying constant.
  - Understanding these states highlights that our essence, Turiya, is unchanging and not bound to any transient experience.

- **The Role of Turiya**: Turiya, or the 'fourth state,' is described as the witness of all experiences, underscoring the awareness beyond the surface-level experiences of life.
  - By identifying with Turiya rather than the fluctuating states, one transcends suffering and enjoys a state of bliss.

- **Real Self Beyond the Common Identities**: The true self is not the fluctuating identities of the waker, dreamer, or sleeper but the unchanging consciousness that encompasses all.
  - This realization brings about a profound understanding of peace and eternal existence beyond duality.

---

**🔑 Insights on Freedom and Free Will**  
- **True Freedom vs. Illusory Individuality**: Swami Sarvapriyananda argues that while individuals seek freedom, recognizing one’s essence as the all-pervading consciousness is the key to true liberation.
  - Freedom does not emerge from the person but from realizing one’s unity with all existence.

- **The Concept of Free Will**: From an Advaitic perspective, free will is seen as an illusion; true freedom lies beyond the causative nature of will and actions.
  - As the absolute, one is free and inherently unbound, as individual actions are manifestations of a larger divine will.

- **Surrendering to Higher Will**: The wisest approach to the illusion of free will is to surrender to the divine, recognizing that this interplay of choice and surrender illuminates true freedom and purpose.
  - Accepting the flow of existence allows a harmonious existence without the burdens of individual struggles.

---

**🌈 Spiritualization of Everyday Life**  
- **Living in Awareness**: Swami Sarvapriyananda encourages integrating spiritual practices into daily existence, emphasizing that recognizing one's divine nature leads to a fulfilling life.
  - The true essence of spirituality is not found in renunciation but in manifesting divinity through service and love in everyday interactions.

- **Radical Shift in Perspective**: Prosecuting a path of inquiry into who you are fosters not just self-awareness but a deeper connection with all beings, seen as expressions of the same divine reality.
  - The call to self-exploration is also a call to connect deeply with the shared reality of existence.

- **Experiencing Peace and Bliss**: By understanding the non-dual nature of reality and recognizing oneself as Turiya, one finds inherent peace and joy that does not depend on external circumstances.
  - This shift enriches life, making the mundane sacred through conscious awareness and love.

--- 

By internalizing these profound teachings from Swami Sarvapriyananda, one can navigate the complexities of life with a deeper understanding of consciousness, identity, and interconnectedness, ultimately leading towards a more fulfilling and peaceful existence.
