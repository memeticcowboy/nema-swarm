# अपस्-तारणे

Created at 2025/07/27 8:02 PM

The Sanskrit term **Apas-Tāraṇe** can be broken down into its components: **Apas** (अपस्) and **Tāraṇe** (तारणे). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Apas** (अपस्): This term means "water" or "waters." In Indian philosophy, Apas is one of the five great elements (*pañca-mahābhūta*), representing the fluid, cohesive, and nurturing aspect of existence. It is associated with the quality of taste (*rasa*), flow, and life-sustaining properties. In Vedic texts, Apas is also personified as a divine force or deity, symbolizing purification, fertility, and the essence of life.
2. **Tāraṇe** (तारणे): Derived from the root **tṛ** (to cross, pass over, or liberate), with the suffix **-aṇe** indicating an action or process in the locative case, Tāraṇe means "in the act of crossing," "liberation," or "deliverance." It can imply the process of crossing over (e.g., a river or obstacle) or achieving liberation, often in a spiritual sense, such as transcending worldly limitations or saṃsāra.
3. **Apas-Tāraṇe Combined**: The term Apas-Tāraṇe can be interpreted as "the crossing of waters" or "liberation through water." It likely refers to a spiritual or metaphorical process involving water as a medium for transcendence or purification. In a Vedic context, it could evoke the idea of crossing the cosmic waters (a common motif symbolizing the transition from chaos to order or from ignorance to liberation). In a yogic or Tantric context, it might suggest the purification or flow of vital energy through the water element to achieve spiritual liberation. Alternatively, it could poetically describe water’s role in facilitating a transformative journey or deliverance.
