# The Clash of Rationalism and Mythic Tradition in French Calendar Reform (1793–1805)

**Historical Contest Site Selection**



**🔥** 

**Clash Site**

**:** 

**French Revolutionary Calendar Reform (1793–1805)**


Why this site?

This moment captures a literal attempt by rationalist technocrats (inspired by Enlightenment ideals) to overwrite organic, mythically-rooted timekeeping with a hyper-rational system—sparking massive symbolic and civic resistance.


**Memetic Conflict Dissection**



**🎭** 

**Key Figures**


Technocratic Saviors Side:

- Jacques-Louis David – painter of revolutionary rationalism.
- Fabre d’Églantine – architect of the French Republican Calendar.
- Robespierre – technocrat-moralist hybrid enforcing virtue via rationalism.


Mythic-Civic Organicism Side:

- Rural Catholics – resisting calendar reform via symbolic fidelity to saints’ days.
- Royalists – defenders of king-as-mythic-axis.
- Pope Pius VI – rejected secularization of time and liturgy.


**Dominant Mediums & Institutions**


Technocratic Saviors:

- Revolutionary tribunals
- Metric system
- State calendar, schools, and civil festivals
- Newspapers and posters


Mythic-Civic Organicists:

- Church bells and liturgical calendars
- Oral storytelling and ritual
- Village feasts and saint days
- Underground religious networks





**🎨** 

**Narrative & Symbolic Tools**


Technocratic Saviors:

- Symbols: Phrygian cap, decimal time clocks, new month names (e.g., Thermidor, Fructidor)
- Narrative Tropes: “Light of reason,” “liberation from superstition,” “nature as math”
- Metaphors: Time as grid; humans as perfectible mechanisms


Mythic-Civic Organicism:

- Symbols: Cross, saints’ relics, seasonal festivals
- Narrative Tropes: “Eternal order,” “moral decay of secular rule,” “God’s rhythm vs man’s folly”
- Metaphors: Time as sacred spiral; society as divine order





**📈** 

**Outcome & Legacy**


- Short-term Outcome: The Republican Calendar was eventually abolished by Napoleon in 1806—unable to overcome symbolic illegibility and civic rejection.
- Long-Term Residues:<br>
    - Technocratic side persists in modern metrics-driven governance, central banking, algorithmic scheduling, and datafication.
    - Mythic-organic side resurfaces in neo-traditionalism, slow movements, ritual revival, and new civic mythology building.
- 
- Cultural Scars: The idea of time as battleground—between sacred and secular—remains active today in debates over AI timelines, spiritual calendars, and digital Sabbath practices.
