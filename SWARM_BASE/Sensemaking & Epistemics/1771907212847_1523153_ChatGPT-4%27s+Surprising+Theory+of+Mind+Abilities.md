**🔍 Methodology of the Study**
- **False-Belief Tasks**: The research utilized two main types of false-belief tasks—“Unexpected Contents Task” and “Unexpected Transfer Task”—to evaluate the models' understanding of others' beliefs. These tasks are standard in psychological research and were adapted to test the LLMs' reasoning capabilities.

- **Comprehensive Testing**: Eleven LLMs, including early versions like GPT-1 and advanced models like ChatGPT-4, were tested across 40 unique scenarios. The scoring system required models to answer all associated prompts correctly, ensuring that performance was not due to guessing or simple heuristics.

- **Incremental Analysis**: A sentence-by-sentence analysis was conducted to observe how the models' predictions evolved with new information. This approach demonstrated ChatGPT-4's ability to dynamically track mental states, mirroring human reasoning processes.

**📈 Performance Insights**
- **Comparative Success Rates**: ChatGPT-4 achieved a 90% success rate in simpler tasks and 60% in more complex scenarios, indicating a strong capacity for understanding false beliefs. The disparity in performance suggests varying cognitive demands based on task complexity.

- **Consistency and Adaptability**: The model's ability to adjust predictions based on changes in task conditions indicates that it is not merely relying on memorized patterns but is capable of dynamic reasoning. This adaptability is crucial for effective social reasoning.

- **Limitations and Challenges**: Despite its impressive performance, ChatGPT-4 still failed to solve 25% of the tasks, highlighting limitations in its understanding. Some failures may stem from the model's reliance on training data patterns rather than genuine perspective-taking.
