# विवेक-प्राण

Created at 2025/07/21 12:21 PM

The Sanskrit term **Viveka-Prāṇa** can be broken down into its components: **Viveka** (विवेक) and **Prāṇa** (प्राण). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Viveka** (विवेक): This term means "discrimination," "discernment," or "wisdom." In Indian philosophy, particularly in Sāṃkhya, Vedanta, and Yoga, Viveka refers to the ability to distinguish between the eternal and the transient, the self (*Ātman* or *Puruṣa*) and the non-self (*Prakṛti* or material world). It is a key faculty for attaining liberation (*mokṣa*), as it enables one to separate truth from illusion (*māyā*).
2. **Prāṇa** (प्राण): This term means "life force," "vital energy," or "breath." In yogic and Ayurvedic traditions, Prāṇa is the universal energy that sustains life, flowing through the body via subtle channels (*nāḍīs*). It is associated with breath, vitality, and the dynamic force that animates living beings. In spiritual practices like *pranayama*, controlling Prāṇa is a means to achieve physical health and spiritual awakening.
3. **Viveka-Prāṇa Combined**: The term Viveka-Prāṇa can be interpreted as "the life force of discernment" or "the vital energy of wisdom." It likely refers to the integration of discriminative wisdom (*Viveka*) with the vital energy (*Prāṇa*) that animates life. In a spiritual or yogic context, it could denote the practice of channeling life force through focused discernment to achieve clarity of consciousness or spiritual awakening. Alternatively, it might symbolize the dynamic energy that arises from discriminative wisdom, empowering one to live in alignment with truth and higher consciousness. In a philosophical sense, it could suggest the vital role of discernment in harnessing or directing the life force toward liberation.
