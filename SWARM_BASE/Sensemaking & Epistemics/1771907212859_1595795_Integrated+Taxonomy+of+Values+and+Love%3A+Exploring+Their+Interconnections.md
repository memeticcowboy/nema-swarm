#### 2. **Forms of Love**

- **Empathy**

    - Loving someone because they love you

- **Obsession**

    - Loving someone for their looks

- **Interest**

    - Loving someone for their money

- **Admiration**

    - Loving someone for their kindness

- **True Love**

    - Being unsure why you love them

#### 3. **Interrelation of Values and Love**

- **Empathy and Ethical Values**

    - Empathy aligns with ethical values such as caring, compassion, and kindness.

- **Admiration and Social Values**

    - Admiring someone for their kindness connects with social values like generosity and supporting others.

- **True Love and Personal Values**

    - True love, being unconditional, resonates with personal values like selflessness and tolerance.

- **Obsession and Economic Values**

    - Obsession with looks can sometimes be linked to economic values, such as status and monetary interests.

- **Interest and Organisational Values**

    - Interest in someone for their money can be related to organisational values like efficiency and practical returns.

#### 4. **Value Creation in Relationships**

- **Sustainable Value**

    - Building relationships that sustain both individuals and the community.

- **Shared Value**

    - Mutual benefits and health of the relationship and the community.

- **Value Engineering**

    - Modifying behaviors and actions to enhance the relationship's value.

- **Value Analysis**

    - Assessing the relationship to ensure it meets the needs and wants of both parties.

This integrated taxonomy provides a comprehensive framework for understanding how values and love intersect and influence each other in various contexts.
