### Definition:

**Soma** (सोम):

- **Mythological Context**: In Vedic literature, particularly the *Rigveda*, Soma is both a sacred plant and a deity associated with a divine elixir or ritual drink. The Soma plant was used in Vedic rituals to prepare a ceremonial beverage believed to confer immortality, divine inspiration, and communion with the gods. Soma is personified as a lunar deity, associated with the moon, nourishment, and rejuvenation.
- **Philosophical/Spiritual Context**: Beyond its ritualistic use, Soma symbolizes divine bliss, spiritual intoxication, or the nectar of immortality (*amṛta*). In yogic and Tantric traditions, Soma may refer to the subtle essence or energy within the body, often linked to the crown chakra (*sahasrāra*), where the "nectar of immortality" is said to reside, facilitating spiritual awakening.
- **Cosmic and Poetic Context**: Soma is associated with the moon, representing cooling, nourishing, and creative energies, in contrast to the fiery energy of the sun. It embodies qualities like peace, serenity, and divine inspiration.
- **Linguistic Note**: The word Soma is derived from the root **su** (to press or extract), referring to the process of extracting the juice from the Soma plant in Vedic rituals.
