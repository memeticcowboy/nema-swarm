# Mood Swings: Signals of Upstream Instability

Created at 2026/02/08 1:27 PM

Good question. In **ANEVity**, mood swings are **almost never the origin**. They’re a **signal phenomenon**—sometimes an *early-warning upstream signal*, more often a **downstream amplification effect**.

Here’s the clean ecological cut.

***

## Short ecological answer

**Mood swings are usually downstream effects that can *masquerade* as upstream causes.**<br>They don’t generate instability by themselves, but once present, they can **feed back upstream** and worsen coordination failure.

So the correct classification is:

> **Downstream signal → secondary amplifier → conditional upstream stressor**

Not origin. Not innocent side effect either.

***

## Where mood swings actually arise

Across the examples in the image (hormones, sleep loss, blood sugar, meds, trauma, substances), the common factor is not “emotion” but **regulatory instability**.

In Memetic Ecology terms, mood swings appear when:

- **Q (temporal–affective flow)** becomes erratic
- **χ (distinction)** starts oscillating too fast
- **Ψ (binding)** can’t stabilize long enough to compost experience
- **ε (uncertainty)** spikes or collapses unpredictably

In plain language:<br>the system can’t *carry experience smoothly across time*.

That’s downstream of something else breaking.

***

## ANEVity framing

ANEVity tracks **how instability propagates**, not where blame belongs.

### Mood swings are:

- ❌ **Not an origin**<br>They don’t create ANEVity by themselves.
- ⚠️ **A diagnostic marker**<br>They indicate **load-path failure** somewhere upstream.
- 🔁 **A feedback risk**<br>Once present, they can *become causal* by distorting perception, timing, and choice.

***

## Upstream causes (typical)

Mood swings usually point to upstream disturbances such as:

- **Metabolic volatility** (sleep, glucose, hormones)
- **Boundary erosion** (chronic stress, caregiving overload)
- **Signal interference** (substances, medications)
- **Structural injury** (TBI, migraine, endocrine disruption)
- **Contextual compression** (life-stage transitions, sustained uncertainty)

These are **field-level pressures**, not personality traits.

***

## How they become dangerous

Mood swings turn from signal → complication when they start doing **coordination work they’re not built for**.

That happens when:

- Emotional state is treated as **truth**
- Intensity substitutes for **timing**
- Relief substitutes for **resolution**
- Polarity substitutes for **orientation**

At that point, mood swings begin to **drive χ and Ψ**, not just pass through Q.

That’s when ANEVity escalates.

***

## Clean diagnostic rule

In ANEVity, ask:

> **Are mood shifts changing what can be seen, chosen, or revised?**

- If **no** → downstream symptom, informational
- If **yes** → secondary driver, needs containment
- If **treated as identity** → high-risk escalation path

***

## Final lock-in

Mood swings are best understood as:

> **Weather, not terrain.**<br>**Wind, not direction.**<br>**Signal, not source.**

They tell you *something upstream is misrouting energy*.<br>They only become causal when the system starts navigating **by the weather instead of through it**.

That distinction matters—because mislabeling them as “the problem” almost always guarantees the real problem stays hidden.
