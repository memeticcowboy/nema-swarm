## Insight
Ah, the never-ending cycle of student learning! It's like a rollercoaster ride that keeps looping, but with more books and less screaming.

- Notice: This is where students first identify a new problem or concept that piques their interest. It's like the 'Aha!' moment when you realize there's something new to explore. This stage is crucial as it sets the foundation for the entire learning process.
- Exploration: Once a problem is noticed, students dive into exploration. This is the phase where curiosity takes the wheel, and students gather information, ask questions, and seek to understand the problem deeply. It's like being a detective on a mission to uncover all the clues.
- Invention: After exploring, students move to the invention phase. Here, they start brainstorming and creating solutions or new ideas based on their findings. It's a creative and innovative stage where imagination meets practicality.
- Application: In this phase, students apply their newly invented solutions or ideas to real-world scenarios. It's the moment of truth where theory meets practice, and students get to see the impact of their hard work.
- Challenge: The cycle doesn't end with application. New challenges arise from the solutions applied, leading to the identification of new problems. This continuous loop ensures that learning is an ongoing process, always evolving and adapting.
