# मनस्-प्रसादनम्

Created at 2025/08/17 12:59 PM

The Sanskrit term **Manas Prasadanam** can be broken down into its components: **Manas** (मनस्) and **Prasadanam** (प्रसादनम्). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Manas** (मनस्): This term means "mind," "intellect," or "mental faculty." In Indian philosophy, particularly in Sāṃkhya, Yoga, and Vedanta, Manas refers to the faculty of the mind responsible for thought, perception, and processing sensory information. It is considered part of the subtle body and acts as a bridge between the senses and the higher consciousness (*Buddhi* or *Ātman*).
2. **Prasadanam** (प्रसादनम्): Derived from the root **sad** (to sit, settle, or purify) with the prefix **pra-** (indicating excellence or intensity), Prasadanam means "calming," "purification," "clarification," or "bringing to a state of serenity." It implies the act of making something clear, tranquil, or pure, often in a mental or spiritual context. In Patañjali’s *Yoga Sutras* (1.33), *Maitri-adi-Prasadanam* refers to cultivating a serene and pure mind through positive attitudes like friendliness, compassion, joy, and equanimity.
3. **Manas Prasadanam Combined**: The term Manas Prasadanam can be interpreted as "the calming of the mind," "the purification of the mind," or "bringing serenity to the mind." It likely refers to practices or states that lead to mental clarity, tranquility, or purification, such as meditation, yoga, or cultivating virtuous emotions to stabilize and purify the mind. In the context of *Yoga Sutras*, it aligns with techniques to quiet the fluctuations of the mind (*citta-vṛtti-nirodha*) to achieve a state conducive to spiritual growth or liberation (*mokṣa*). It could also suggest a state of mental grace or peace achieved through spiritual practices.
