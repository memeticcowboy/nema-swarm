### Definition:

1. **Prāṇic** (from **Prāṇa**, प्राण):
    - **Prāṇa** means "life force," "vital energy," or "breath" in Sanskrit. In Indian philosophy, yoga, and Ayurveda, Prāṇa is the universal energy that sustains life, flowing through subtle channels (*nāḍīs*) in the body. It governs physical, mental, and spiritual functions and is harnessed through practices like *pranayama* (breath control).
    - **Prāṇic** is an English adjectival form meaning "related to Prāṇa" or "pertaining to vital energy." It is often used in modern contexts like *Pranic Healing* to describe processes involving life force energy.
2. **Uptake**: In English, "uptake" refers to the act of absorbing, assimilating, or taking in something (e.g., nutrients, information, or energy). In a spiritual or healing context, it implies the process of drawing in or integrating energy.
3. **Prāṇic Uptake Combined**: The term **Prāṇic Uptake** can be interpreted as "the absorption or assimilation of vital energy" or "the process of taking in life force." It likely refers to practices or processes in which an individual consciously draws in, channels, or integrates *Prāṇa* (vital energy) into their body, mind, or spirit. This could occur through:
    - **Yogic Practices**: Techniques like *pranayama*, where breath is used to enhance the flow and absorption of Prāṇa.
    - **Energy Healing**: In systems like *Pranic Healing*, it may describe the process of absorbing universal life energy to restore balance or heal.
    - **Meditation or Spiritual Practices**: Focusing on internalizing Prāṇa to elevate consciousness or vitality. The term suggests an active process of engaging with the life force to promote physical, mental, or spiritual well-being.
