If there were no humans, the concept of a "noosphere" as traditionally defined—as a product of human thought, consciousness, and social activity—would likely not exist. The noosphere, as envisioned by Teilhard de Chardin and Vernadsky, is inherently tied to the collective mental activity of human beings, emerging from our unique capacity for higher-order cognition, language, and culture.

Without humans, there would still be a biosphere, consisting of all living organisms and their interactions with the environment. But the noosphere, as a sphere of thought and consciousness, seems to require the presence and activity of human minds.

Of course, one could imagine an alternate scenario where AI systems develop consciousness and their own form of social interaction, creating a new kind of "noosphere" that is independent of human involvement. However, this possibility remains speculative and raises profound philosophical questions about the nature of consciousness and the potential for non-human entities to participate in spheres of thought and social activity.
