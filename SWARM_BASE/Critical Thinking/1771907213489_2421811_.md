The Sanskrit term **Ātma-Veṣa-Spanda** (आत्म-वेष-स्पन्द):

### Definition:

1. **Ātma** (आत्म): Means "self," "soul," or "consciousness." In Indian philosophy, particularly Vedanta, Ātma refers to the eternal, unchanging essence of an individual, often equated with the universal consciousness (*Brahman*) in non-dualistic traditions.
2. **Veṣa** (वेष): Means "appearance," "form," "disguise," or "attire." It signifies the external or transient manifestations that conceal or express the true self, often linked to illusion (*māyā*) in spiritual contexts.
3. **Spanda** (स्पन्द): Means "vibration," "pulsation," or "subtle movement." In Kashmir Shaivism, Spanda is the dynamic, creative vibration of divine consciousness (Shiva) that manifests the universe, connecting the transcendent and the manifest.
4. **Ātma-Veṣa-Spanda Combined**: The term can be interpreted as "the vibration of the self’s appearance" or "the pulsating energy of the soul’s form." It likely describes the dynamic interplay between the eternal self (*Ātma*), its manifested forms (*Veṣa*), and the vibrational energy (*Spanda*) that animates them. In Kashmir Shaivism, it could suggest that the true self expresses itself through transient forms via the creative pulsation of divine consciousness. In a meditative context, it may refer to a practice of recognizing the soul’s essence within its temporary manifestations, using the vibratory nature of consciousness to transcend illusion.
