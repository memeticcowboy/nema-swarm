# Monistic Vitalism (Zoe) and Its Role in Posthuman Ethics

Created at 2025/06/14 2:24 PM

The sources indicate that **Monistic Vitalism (Zoe)** is a key tenet within **posthuman ethics**, particularly as articulated by Rosi Braidotti. This concept represents a significant departure from traditional human-centric views of morality, emphasizing a broader understanding of life and ethics in interconnected systems.

Here's a breakdown of what the sources say about Monistic Vitalism (Zoe) in the context of Main Tenets:

- **Definition and Core Idea**:
    - Monistic Vitalism, or **Zoe**, is defined as **emphasizing life as a dynamic force**, rather than being exclusively human-centric (bios).
    - This concept is part of Rosi Braidotti's framework for critical posthumanities, which outlines several key tenets, including species egalitarianism and relational ethics.
    - It underpins the idea of **zoe-centred justice**, which further underscores a shift toward ethics as a process of interconnectedness, adaptability, and affirmation of all life.
- **Critique of Humanism**:
    - The concept of Zoe challenges classical humanist moral frameworks that reinforce Western exceptionalism and species supremacy.
    - By focusing on a dynamic, non-human-centric vital force, Monistic Vitalism critiques the anthropocentric bias of humanism, which has historically prioritized human life above all else. This re-evaluation moves beyond an ethical framework that is solely focused on human interests to one that considers the unity of all living matter.
- **Implications for Posthuman Ethics**:
    - As a main tenet of posthuman ethics, Monistic Vitalism (Zoe) guides the ethical framework to focus on **life in all its forms**, challenging dualistic oppositions (e.g., human/non-human) that traditionally underpin ethical thought.
    - It calls for **post-anthropocentric practices** that move beyond human dominance in ethical considerations.
    - This perspective helps in designing AI that considers broader impacts beyond just human well-being, potentially influencing areas like urban planning, healthcare, and warfare to account for ecological impacts and the sustainability of all life.

In essence, Monistic Vitalism (Zoe) in posthuman ethics shifts the moral compass from an exclusive focus on human beings to an **inclusive recognition of life as a fundamental, interconnected force**, influencing how ethical frameworks are constructed for an increasingly technologically mediated and hybrid world.
