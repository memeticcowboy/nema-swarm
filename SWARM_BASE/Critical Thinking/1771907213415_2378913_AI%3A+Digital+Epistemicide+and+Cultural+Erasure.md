**Title:**

**Digital Epistemicide – The Killing of Knowledge in the Age of AI**

[de Beer](https://open.substack.com/pub/anarcasper/p/part-1-domination-through-artificial?r=g7p6y&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&showWelcomeOnShare=false)

🧠 Core Idea Unit:

- AI systems enact a symbolic colonization of human knowledge: they scrape, extract, and flatten cultural expressions, erasing context and agency.
- This process destroys living epistemologies (ways of knowing) by turning them into statistical artifacts for corporate or elite use.
- The core “thought-virus” is: AI is not neutral—it is an engine of cultural erasure.




🎭 Identity Play & Roles:

- Hero/Defender: Cultural guardians, decolonial scholars, digital activists.
- Victim: Marginalized communities, Indigenous cultures, creators whose knowledge is stolen.
- Villain: Tech corporations, surveillance capitalism, extractive AI models.
- Rebel: Those calling out “epistemic theft” and demanding consent-based AI.




💥 Emotional Triggers:

- Outrage: at theft of culture and knowledge without consent.
- Grief: for the erasure or distortion of living traditions and voices.
- Moral Urgency: to protect vulnerable epistemologies.
- Distrust: of AI systems and the corporations that profit from them.
