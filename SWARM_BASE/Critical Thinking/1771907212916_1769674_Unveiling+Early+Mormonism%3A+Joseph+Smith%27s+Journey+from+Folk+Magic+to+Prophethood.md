# Unveiling Early Mormonism: Joseph Smith's Journey from Folk Magic to Prophethood

Created at 2025/02/21 4:07 PM

D. Michael Quinn’s *Early Mormonism and the Magic World View* explores the deep connections between Joseph Smith’s early religious experiences and the folk magic traditions of the 19th century. The discussion highlights how Joseph Smith and his family were deeply engaged in treasure digging, using seer stones, magical daggers, talismans, and other occult practices. The podcast details Joseph's use of a Jupiter Talisman, divining rods, and magic circles—practices aligned with contemporary folk magic beliefs. The hosts emphasize how the Book of Mormon's origins are intertwined with treasure-seeking rituals, spirits guarding hidden treasures, and Masonic influence. The evolution of Joseph Smith from folk magician to prophet is explored, as well as the LDS Church's efforts to normalize these magical roots over time.

3. **Modern LDS Church Narratives** – Attempts to normalize Smith’s magical past, making it more palatable to modern believers.
