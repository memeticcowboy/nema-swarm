## The Communist Manifesto by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels

**Core Question:** "What if the history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles?"

**Alternate Principle:** The materialist conception of history, which posits that social, political, and intellectual life processes are rooted in the economic structure and development of society[1].

**Actionable Possibility:** Workers of the world should unite and overthrow the bourgeoisie to establish a classless society.

**Charged Symbolic Power:** The specter of communism haunting Europe, and the call for workers to break their chains[1].

## The Futurist Manifesto by Filippo Tommaso Marinetti

**Core Question:** "Why should we look back, when what we want is to break down the mysterious doors of the Impossible?"

**Alternate Principle:** The beauty of speed and modern technology surpasses traditional artistic values[2].

**Actionable Possibility:** Embrace noise, aggression, and the machine age in art and life.

**Charged Symbolic Power:** The racing car as more beautiful than the Victory of Samothrace, and the roaring motor car as a symbol of modernity[2].

## A Cyborg Manifesto by Donna Haraway

**Core Question:** "Why should our bodies end at the skin, or include at best other beings encapsulated by skin?"

**Alternate Principle:** The cyborg as a hybrid of machine and organism, blurring boundaries between human and animal, organism and machine, physical and non-physical[3].

**Actionable Possibility:** Embrace technological and biological hybridity to challenge traditional notions of identity and politics.

**Charged Symbolic Power:** The cyborg as a creature of social reality and fiction, representing a post-gender world[3].

## The SCUM Manifesto by Valerie Solanas

**Core Question:** "What if men, not women, were the biological accident?"

**Alternate Principle:** Male-dominated society is fundamentally flawed and destructive[4].

**Actionable Possibility:** Women should overthrow the government, eliminate the money system, and create a female-led society.

**Charged Symbolic Power:** SCUM (Society for Cutting Up Men) as a metaphor for radical feminist action[4].

These manifestos exemplify how a core question can challenge existing assumptions, propose an alternate principle that shifts perception, seed actionable possibilities for engagement, and utilize charged symbolic power to resonate deeply with readers. They demonstrate the power of manifestos to inspire, provoke, and potentially catalyze social change.
