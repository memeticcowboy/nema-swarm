# Foundational Thinkers and Concepts Supporting the Six Core Assumptions of The River of Leaving

Created at 2025/10/25 11:00 AM

Below is a “short-list” of thinkers whose vocabularies can be quoted almost phrase-for-phrase when you need to legitimate the six core assumptions of [The River of Leaving](https://memeticcowboy.substack.com/p/the-river-of-leaving) .  For each philosopher I give the single most useful passage or concept to cite, followed by the exact place it intersects your framework.<br>METAPHYSICS – reality as recursive, fluid, co-constructed<br>1.  Gilles Deleuze & Félix Guattari – “rhizome” (A Thousand Plateaus, 1980)<br>“A rhizome has no beginning or end; it is always in the middle, between things…”  ← direct warrant for “reality is recursive flow.”<br>2.  Henri Bergson – Creative Evolution (1907) élan vital & duration<br>Reality is “the continuous elaboration of the new” that cannot be sliced into metric frames.<br>3.  Alfred North Whitehead – Process & Reality (1929) “actual occasion”<br>Every entity is a momentary concrescence of prehensions; self is a society of occasions, not a substance.<br>EPISTEMOLOGY – knowledge as attunement, not extraction<br>4. Francisco Varela – “knowing as enaction” (The Embodied Mind, 1991)<br>Cognition is not representation but “a history of structural coupling” – “signal and echo both.”<br>5. Donna Haraway – “Situated Knowledges” (1988)<br>Calls “the god-trick of seeing everything from nowhere” the epistemic violence your text refuses.<br>6. Maurice Merleau-Ponty – Phenomenology of Perception (1945)<br>“The world is not what I think, but what I live through.”  Attunement precedes objectification.<br>LOGIC – multi-valent, narrative, anti-diagnostic<br>7. Bruno Latour – “irreductions” (The Pasteurization of France, 1984)<br>“Nothing is, by itself, either reducible or irreducible to anything else” → undercuts single-variable diagnostics.<br>8. Gregory Bateson – “ecology of mind” (Steps to an Ecology of Mind, 1972)<br>“The major problems in the world are the result of the difference between how nature works and the way people think.”  Causality is circular, story-laden.<br>9. Gianni Vattimo – “weak thought” (pensiero debole, 1983)<br>Truth is a “weakening” of rigid structures in favor of plural narrative trajectories.<br>ETHICS – emotional coherence as resistance to optimisation<br>10. Simone Weil – “attentive waiting” (Gravity & Grace, 1947)<br>“Attention is the rarest and purest form of generosity” – ethics starts with non-intervening receptivity.<br>11. Marshall Rosenberg – Non-Violent Communication (1999)<br>Provides a practised technique for the “allowance for dissonance” you name as imperative.<br>12. Byung-Chul Han – The Burnout Society (2010)<br>Calls the compulsion to optimise “the violence of positivity” – your “rewriting hearts into loops.”<br>AESTHETICS – meaning as harmonic flow<br>13. John Dewey – Art as Experience (1934)<br>“The live creature” restores “continuity between the refined and intensified forms of experience that are works of art and the everyday events…”  Mist, current, sound are primary data.<br>14. Hans-Georg Gadamer – Truth & Method (1960) “aesthetic non-differentiation”<br>A work of art is not an object but an event of disclosure – coherence without categorical collapse.<br>15. Susanne Langer – Feeling & Form (1953)<br>“Music is the sonorous image of emotive life” – her concept of “presentational symbolism” translates directly into your “harmonics, not categories.”<br>POLITICS – difference against sealed utopias<br>16. Chantal Mouffe – “agonistic pluralism” (The Democratic Paradox, 2000)<br>Democracy is not consensus but the institutionalisation of respectful conflict – “unassimilated voices.”<br>17. James C. Scott – Seeing Like a State (1998)<br>Shows how high-modernist “sealed cities” (his word: “authoritarian high-modernism”) fail precisely because they ignore local, tacit knowledges.<br>18. Isabelle Stengers – “cosmopolitical proposal” (Cosmopolitics I-II, 2003-2010)<br>Politics must make room for “the unknown, the aliens, the excluded” without translating them into existing metrics.<br>Quick citation package (one-liner for each domain)<br>Metaphysics: “Reality is rhizomatic, always in the middle” (Deleuze & Guattari).<br>Epistemology: “Cognition is enaction, a history of structural coupling” (Varela).<br>Logic: “Nothing is by itself reducible” (Latour).<br>Ethics: “The violence of positivity is optimisation without feeling” (Han).<br>Aesthetics: “Art restores continuity between intensified and everyday experience” (Dewey).<br>Politics: “Authoritarian high-modernism produces legibility at the cost of freedom” (Scott).<br>Use these names and tag-lines whenever you need an external authority that already speaks your system’s language.<br>
