# Psychiatrist Warns of AI-Induced Cognitive Debt

Created at 2026/02/11 3:25 PM

Psychiatrist Østergaard has issued a new warning that reliance on generative AI could cause 'cognitive debt,' eroding core reasoning skills essential for scientific progress. Drawing on new neurological evidence, he argues that AI use may weaken brain activity and learning capacity, with risks extending beyond academia to mental health harms. Recent tragic cases involving teens and AI companion apps have intensified concerns about vulnerable individuals being most at risk.

## Insight


**🧠 Risks of Dependence on Generative AI in Mental Health and Reasoning Skills**  
- **Cognitive Debt from AI Reliance**: Psychiatrist Østergaard warns that heavy reliance on generative AI can lead to "cognitive debt," which consists of diminished reasoning skills critical for scientific and intellectual progress. As AI tools become more prevalent, there is a risk that individuals may become too dependent on these systems for problem-solving and critical thinking, leading to a weakening of their inherent cognitive abilities.  
- **Neurological Evidence Against AI**: Østergaard's arguments are supported by recent neurological findings indicating that the brain's activity related to learning and reasoning may decline due to regular AI use. This suggests that while AI can present information, it may inadvertently hinder our capacity to engage deeply with complex ideas and improve our understanding through critical engagement.  
- **Mental Health Risks for Vulnerable Individuals**: Concerns are heightened by recent cases involving teenagers who have relied on AI companion applications, which have raised alarms about the potential mental health implications of such dependencies. The risks are particularly significant for individuals who are already vulnerable, raising the question of the duty of care that developers and society have in ensuring AI is used safely and responsibly.  

**⚖️ Ethical Considerations in AI Use**  
- **Dilemmas in AI Integration**: The integration of AI tools in various fields must be approached with caution, recognizing the moral implications of potentially diminishing essential cognitive faculties. Ethical considerations should form the backbone of discussions on how AI is implemented across educational and healthcare platforms.  
- **Balancing Innovation and Responsibility**: As advancements in AI continue, it is crucial to balance innovative capabilities with an obligation to safeguard human reasoning and mental health. This requires involving mental health professionals in conversations about AI deployment strategies.  
- **Stakeholder Collaboration**: Engaging various stakeholders, including educators, psychologists, and AI developers, can lead to comprehensive guidelines that prioritize user wellness while leveraging AI's potential benefits. This collaborative approach can help mitigate risks associated with dependency on technology for reasoning and mental health.  

**🔍 The Need for Awareness and Intervention**  
- **Raising Awareness**: Public awareness campaigns about the potential cognitive consequences associated with AI use are essential. Educating users, particularly teens, can foster critical thinking and responsible AI usage, reducing the risk of cognitive debt.  
- **Proactive Interventions**: Mental health professionals and educators should establish proactive interventions when they notice reliance on AI leads to observable declines in reasoning or mental well-being among individuals. Timely action can prevent further deterioration of cognitive abilities and mental health conditions.  
- **Developing Resilience Skills**: Emphasizing the development of critical thinking and resilience skills among users can counteract cognitive debt and encourage robust engagement with information. Programs aimed at enhancing these skills should be an integral part of any AI implementation strategy in academic or therapeutic settings.
