# Algebraic Geometry's Role in Understanding Universe's Structure

Created at 2025/11/19 7:18 PM

Mathematicians Claudia Fevola and Anna-Laura Sattelberger are using algebraic geometry to understand the behavior of elementary particles and the structure of the universe.


Their research connects to the field of positive geometry, which represents interactions as volumes of high-dimensional geometric objects and offers a simpler way to compute scattering amplitudes.


The approach has implications for both particle physics and cosmology, with potential to unify our understanding of nature at all scales, and is part of a growing international effort to bridge mathematics, particle physics, and cosmology.
