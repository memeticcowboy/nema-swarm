## HOLD BOTH: What “Why” Actually Does

Philosophics is right about something many people sense but don’t articulate:

- “Why” *feels* like a flashlight
- but often functions like a **summons**

It doesn’t just ask *how things unfolded*.<br>It quietly asks:

> *Who meant this?*<br>*Who is responsible?*<br>*Who owes me an answer?*

That works fine in a kitchen or a courtroom.<br>It breaks down in a landslide.

At the same time—this matters—“why” didn’t become dominant by accident. It *does* something useful:

- It coordinates blame and responsibility
- It enables planning and justification
- It stabilizes social worlds

So we **hold both**:

- “Why” is a powerful coordination tool
- “Why” is a terrible metaphysical probe

The problem isn’t the word.<br>It’s **using a wrench as a telescope**.
