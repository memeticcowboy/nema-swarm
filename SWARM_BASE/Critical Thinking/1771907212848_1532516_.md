1. How did Bergson view the connection between time and consciousness?
    - Henri Bergson believed that real understanding comes not from static analysis but through experiencing the flow of time, or 'duration'. He argued that consciousness is deeply intertwined with time, as our perception of time is inherently subjective and continuous, unlike the segmented, measurable time of clocks. This suggests that Bergson viewed time and consciousness as fundamentally connected, where consciousness is a flowing process that cannot be fully captured by static or discrete analysis.
