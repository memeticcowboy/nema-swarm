Being Critical?

The principle of bivalence is a concept in logic that states every declarative sentence must be either true or false, with no middle ground. This means each statement about something either definitely happens or it doesn’t. It’s different from the “law of excluded middle,” which just states that a statement and its negation cannot both be false. The principle of bivalence is useful in classical logic but can be problematic for statements about the future or vague concepts, where truth is not always clear-cut.
