Bryan Johnson posted about “mass psychosis” in a detailed thread on October 18, 2025, framing it as a potential civilizational risk driven by rapid technological change outpacing human cognition and biology. Here’s the key excerpt where he directly addresses it:<br>“When environments change too rapidly, when the map no longer matches the territory, the brain destabilizes. This can trigger a cognitive dissonance cascade, where our core beliefs are invalidated so rapidly that we lose narrative coherence. When feedback loops in an organism can’t stabilize, the system oscillates until it breaks. Civilization is entering that oscillatory phase.<br>At a collective level, this is the recipe for mass psychosis.
<br>Historians call it cultural disintegration and psychologists call it dissociation. It means the same thing: when the story breaks, the organism panics.”<br>The full post expands on this as part of a larger thesis for his “Don’t Die” moral framework to build societal resilience against AI-induced disruptions.
