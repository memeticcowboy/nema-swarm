**🧠 The Power of Words**
- **Words have psychological effects**: Words can inspire and motivate but also hurt, especially when directed at oneself.
- **Negative self-talk is harmful**: Negative words we pin on ourselves can sting and lead to emotional pain.
- **Rethinking self-talk is liberating**: Changing how we talk to ourselves can free us from self-imposed mental prisons.

**💬 Language Influences Thought**
- **Whorf's hypothesis**: Language shapes our perception of the world, influencing how we think and interpret experiences.
- **Gender-neutral terms**: Using gender-neutral occupational titles can change perceptions and open opportunities for all genders.
- **Impact on self-perception**: Words we use to describe ourselves can significantly affect our self-esteem and behavior.

**🔖 Label Thyself (Not)**
- **Cognitive distortions**: Negative labels on oneself are cognitive distortions that affect mood and self-perception.
- **Self-fulfilling prophecies**: Labeling oneself negatively can lead to behaviors that fulfill those negative expectations.
- **Constructive self-talk**: Avoiding negative labels and focusing on constructive self-talk can improve problem-solving and self-esteem.

**🗣️ Choose Your Words Carefully**
- **Words as motivators**: Words can inspire and motivate us to achieve great things.
- **Negative outcomes of self-talk**: Negative self-talk can lead to underperformance and failure.
- **Adopting a self-questioning style**: Using rational countering thoughts instead of negative labels can lead to healthier thinking.

**👂 Whose Words Are These, Anyway?**
- **Internalized negative self-talk**: Negative self-talk often reflects internalized comments from significant figures in our lives.
- **Mind control**: Negative thoughts cannot occupy your mind without your permission.
- **Self-compassion**: Remind yourself that you are doing your best and avoid self-criticism.
