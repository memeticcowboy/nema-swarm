The Sanskrit phrase **Caitanyam Ātmā** can be broken down into its components: **Caitanyam** (चैतन्यम्) and **Ātmā** (आत्मा). Below is the definition and the phrase written in Sanskrit.

**Definition:**

1.  **Caitanyam** (चैतन्यम्): This term means “consciousness,” “awareness,” or “sentience.” Derived from the root **cit** (to perceive or know), Caitanyam refers to the quality of pure consciousness or the state of being aware. In Indian philosophy, particularly in Advaita Vedanta, it denotes the fundamental, unchanging essence of awareness that underlies all experience.

2.  **Ātmā** (आत्मा): This term means “self,” “soul,” or “essence.” In Vedantic traditions, Ātmā refers to the individual self, which, in non-dualistic philosophies like Advaita Vedanta, is ultimately identical with *Brahman*, the universal consciousness. It is the eternal, unchanging core of a being, distinct from the body, mind, and ego.

3.  **Caitanyam Ātmā Combined**: The phrase Caitanyam Ātmā can be interpreted as “the self is consciousness” or “consciousness is the self.” It encapsulates a core teaching of Advaita Vedanta, asserting that the true nature of the self (*Ātmā*) is pure consciousness (*Caitanyam*). This phrase emphasizes that the essence of an individual is not the physical body or mind but the unchanging, infinite awareness that is identical with the ultimate reality (*Brahman*). It reflects the non-dualistic understanding that the individual self and universal consciousness are one and the same.
