# साक्षी-मिथुन

Created at 2025/07/16 3:35 PM

The Sanskrit term **Sākṣī-Mithuna** can be broken down into its components: **Sākṣī** (साक्षी) and **Mithuna** (मिथुन). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Sākṣī** (साक्षी): Derived from the root **sakṣ** (to see or witness), Sākṣī means "witness," "observer," or "spectator." In Indian philosophy, particularly in Advaita Vedanta, Sākṣī refers to the witnessing consciousness or the pure awareness that observes all experiences without being affected by them. It is often associated with the true self (Ātman) that remains detached from the fluctuations of the mind and body.
2. **Mithuna** (मिथुन): This term means "pair," "couple," or "union." It can refer to a pair of opposites, a romantic or conjugal couple, or the concept of duality. In Vedic and Tantric contexts, Mithuna may also symbolize the union of complementary forces, such as masculine and feminine energies (e.g., Shiva and Shakti in Tantra). In astrology, Mithuna is the Sanskrit name for the zodiac sign Gemini, which is associated with duality and communication.
3. **Sākṣī-Mithuna Combined**: The term Sākṣī-Mithuna could be interpreted as "the witnessing pair" or "the observer of duality." It likely refers to the witnessing consciousness (Sākṣī) that observes the interplay of dualities (Mithuna), such as subject and object, self and other, or masculine and feminine. In a metaphysical sense, it may point to the pure awareness that remains beyond the dualities of existence, observing them without being entangled. In a Tantric context, it could also suggest the witnessing consciousness that perceives the union of divine masculine and feminine energies.
