# सधुत्व-स्पन्द

Created at 2025/07/16 4:12 PM

The Sanskrit term **Sadhutva-Spanda** can be broken down into its components: **Sadhutva** (सधुत्व) and **Spanda** (स्पन्द). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Sadhutva** (सधुत्व): Derived from **Sadhu** (साधु), meaning "good," "virtuous," "holy," or "saintly," the suffix **-tva** indicates the quality or state of being. Thus, Sadhutva refers to the "state of being virtuous," "goodness," "holiness," or "saintliness." It denotes the quality of purity, righteousness, or spiritual integrity, often associated with a person or action aligned with dharma (cosmic order or moral duty).
2. **Spanda** (स्पन्द): This term means "vibration," "pulsation," or "subtle movement." In philosophical and spiritual traditions, particularly in Kashmir Shaivism, Spanda is a key concept referring to the dynamic, creative vibration of consciousness that underlies all existence. It represents the subtle pulsation of the divine (Shiva or universal consciousness) that manifests as the universe while remaining rooted in pure awareness.
3. **Sadhutva-Spanda Combined**: The term Sadhutva-Spanda can be interpreted as "the vibration of goodness" or "the pulsation of virtue." It may suggest the dynamic expression of virtuous or saintly qualities (Sadhutva) as a manifestation of the divine vibration (Spanda) of consciousness. In a spiritual context, it could imply that the state of goodness or holiness is not static but a living, pulsating energy that flows from the divine source. In Kashmir Shaivism, it might point to the interplay between the purity of consciousness and its creative expression in the world. Alternatively, it could poetically describe the vibrant quality of a virtuous life as an emanation of divine energy.
