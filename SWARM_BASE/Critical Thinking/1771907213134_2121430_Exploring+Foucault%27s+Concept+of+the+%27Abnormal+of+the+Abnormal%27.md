# Abnormal of the Abnormal

Created at 2025/05/16 10:56 AM

In Michel Foucault’s framework—particularly from his 1974–75 *Abnormal* lectures—the idea of the **“abnormal of the abnormal”** would likely point to:

> **A figure that not only violates the norm, but also transgresses the very categories used to define abnormality.**

### Foucault’s Key Categories of the “Abnormal”:

In *Abnormal*, Foucault explores three major figures:

1. **The Human Monster** – a juridico-biological anomaly (e.g. conjoined twins, hermaphrodites)
2. **The Incorrigible** – resistant to reform, repeat offenders
3. **The Masturbator** – a child seen as pathologically sexualized

Each of these reflects how society disciplines and defines danger by constructing a subject that deviates from norms of:

- Biology
- Morality
- Social behavior

***

### So what is the "Abnormal of the Abnormal"?

This would be:

- **A figure that escapes not only the normalizing gaze, but also the grids of intelligibility** applied to other deviants.
- A **meta-deviant**, or perhaps a **reflexive threat**—someone whose existence **undermines the taxonomic system itself**.

### Possible Examples:

- A person who seems to embody conflicting categories: e.g. an *incorrigible saint*, or *a moral monster*.
- Someone who **passes as normal**, yet harbors a radical subversion—suggesting that **abnormality isn’t detectable** by visible traits or behaviors.
- Perhaps a **“pure anomaly”** that defies prediction, correction, or even recognition by medical or juridical institutions.

***

### Foucaultian Framing:

- **“Abnormal of the abnormal”** is not just a more extreme deviant.
- It is the **limit case**—the point at which the system of classification breaks down.
- It may signal a **discontinuity**, much like what Foucault finds in epistemes: a rupture in how knowledge orders the world.

***

### TL;DR:

> The “abnormal of the abnormal” is not the most dangerous, but the most **illegible**—a figure that destabilizes the categories through which normal and abnormal are constructed and maintained.

It is a threat not just to the social body, but to the **order of knowledge itself**.
