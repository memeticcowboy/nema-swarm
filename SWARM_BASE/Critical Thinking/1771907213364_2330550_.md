**अधर्म**

The Sanskrit term **Adharma** (अधर्म) is a single word with significant meaning in Indian philosophy and spirituality. Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

**Definition:**

**Adharma** (अधर्म):

•  Derived from the prefix **a-** (negation) and **dharma** (धर्म), meaning “righteousness,” “duty,” “law,” or “cosmic order,” Adharma translates to “unrighteousness,” “injustice,” “immorality,” or “that which is contrary to dharma.”

•  In Indian philosophical and religious traditions, Adharma refers to actions, thoughts, or behaviors that violate the natural or moral order of the universe. It encompasses unethical conduct, chaos, or anything that disrupts harmony, truth, or righteousness.

•  In texts like the *Mahabharata* or *Bhagavad Gita*, Adharma is often depicted as the root cause of societal and personal discord, leading to suffering and the perpetuation of *saṃsāra* (the cycle of birth and death). It is contrasted with *Dharma*, which upholds order, virtue, and balance.

•  In a broader context, Adharma can also refer to the absence of spiritual or moral principles, such as dishonesty, violence, greed, or selfishness, which lead one away from liberation (*mokṣa*).
