# Rethinking Reality: Events versus Existence

Created at 2025/11/19 7:28 PM

The concept of space-time is a useful model for understanding reality, but it does not actually exist.


Events do not exist as objects or locations, they simply happen, and space-time is a map that records these happenings.


According to physicist Sean Carroll, philosophical debates about time, such as presentism and eternalism, rely on the assumption that events exist, but recognizing that events happen, not exist, resolves time-travel paradoxes and reframes the philosophy of time.

## Insight


**🌌 The Non-Existence of Space-Time: Rethinking Reality**

- **The Ontological Status of Space-Time**: Space-time is an observer-centred model used to describe phenomena but does not constitute an independent reality. It serves as a conceptual map, much like outdated beliefs that celestial spheres exist, demonstrating that models can effectively describe without being real entities.

- **Events as Temporal Occurrences, Not Existences**: Events should be understood as happenings that occur, rather than entities that exist independently. This distinction helps clarify the confusion surrounding temporal philosophy and aids in resolving time-travel paradoxes inherent in assumptions that events can be revisited as fixed locations.

- **Real Motion vs. Space-Time Diagrams**: Real-world motion is complex, yet simplified by space-time diagrams which allow for a visual representation of events over time. These diagrams frame the discussion of space-time as a mapping tool rather than a physical structure, emphasizing that while we can plot events, they do not "exist" in the same way physical objects do.

**⏳ Philosophical Implications of Time and Events**

- **Diverse Philosophical Perspectives**: Different theories of time—such as presentism, eternalism, and the growing block view—rely on the flawed assumption that events exist as tangible entities. Recognizing that events are occurrences helps clarify philosophical debates and mitigates complications arising from conceptualizing time.

- **Tense Properties and Their Misunderstandings**: Traditional philosophical discourse surrounding events has focused on their tense properties (past, present, future), yet this focus is misplaced if we recognize that events only happen. Such a shift in thinking prevents falling into the trap of treating time as an illusion or misunderstanding the reality of events.

- **Reassessment of Time-Travel Paradoxes**: Current debates over time travel are often rooted in the misconception that past events are tangible places; clarifying that events occur rather than exist allows for a more coherent understanding of time travel, thereby resolving many inherent paradoxes.

**📏 Space-Time and Its Measurements**

- **Defining Space-Time in Physics**: Space-time is not a concrete entity but a mathematical framework that organizes and describes events. Its utility lies in its capacity to map the relationships between occurrences rather than suggesting that it is a spatial entity that can exist.

- **The Role of Empirical Evidence**: The absence of empirical evidence confirming the "existence" of past, present, or future events underscores the distinction between physical entities and happenings. Confirming the existence of an event would necessitate observational capabilities akin to time travel, illustrating the issue with treating events as existing.

- **Complexity of Existence vs. Occurrence**: Objects and entities are bound to physical existence, whereas events are fleeting occurrences that do not have a persistent reality. This distinction allows for clearer discourse about physical laws and their implications without conflating events with objects.

**🧠 Enhancing Conceptual Clarity in Physics**

- **Shifting Language for Better Understanding**: By leaving behind the terminology that suggests space-time and events exist as physical entities, we can achieve greater clarity in discussions about the nature of reality. This shift does not eliminate predictions or practical applications within physics but refines our understanding of the underlying concepts.

- **Relativity as a Descriptive Framework**: General relativity provides a mathematical description of how events unfold within the framework of space-time rather than positing that space-time itself exists. This approach clarifies that physics describes occurrences instead of treating the medium of events as an existing phenomenon.

- **Restoration of Conceptual Foundations**: Recognizing that events happen and that neither space-time nor events exist leads to a conceptual re-establishment that fosters clearer analysis and discussions in both physics and philosophy. By disentangling existence from occurrence, we can engage with theories and models in a more meaningful and accurate manner.
