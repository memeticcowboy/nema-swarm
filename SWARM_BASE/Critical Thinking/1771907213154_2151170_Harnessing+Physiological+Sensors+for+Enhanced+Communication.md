### 🧠 Key Hypothesis

**Multimodal physiological sensor data** — particularly:

- **Increased inter-brain synchrony** (via fNIRS),
- **Enhanced gamma band coherence and alpha/theta shifts** (via EEG),
- **Increased parasympathetic activity** (via HRV),

→ will **predict**:

- **Constructive shared interpretations**, and
- **Minimization of negative emotional triggers**

in response to **specific word combinations**, reflecting enhanced cognitive alignment and emotional regulation between communicators.

***

## 🧪 Experimental Hypotheses (Simplified Versions)

1. **EEG gamma band coherence** increases during moments of shared understanding or "aha!" moments.
2. **High HRV** corresponds with emotionally regulated and productive conversation states.
3. **High inter-brain synchrony (fNIRS)** is a real-time neural correlate of successful interpersonal communication.
4. **Misunderstanding or stress** will be marked by:

    - Decreased HRV
    - EEG patterns showing increased beta (stress) and reduced alpha/theta
    - Decreased brain-to-brain coupling (fNIRS)
