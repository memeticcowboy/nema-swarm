### Definition:

1. **Ātma** (आत्म): This term means "self," "soul," or "consciousness." In Indian philosophy, particularly in Vedanta, Ātma refers to the eternal, unchanging essence of an individual, often equated with the universal consciousness (*Brahman*) in non-dualistic traditions. It represents the true self beyond the physical body and mind.
2. **Sakṣama** (सक्षम): This term means "capable," "competent," "able," or "powerful." It is derived from the root **sak** (to be able) and is used to describe someone or something with the capacity or strength to accomplish a task. In a spiritual context, it can imply inner strength or spiritual capability.
3. **Ātma-Sakṣama Combined**: The term Ātma-Sakṣama can be interpreted as "the capable self," "the empowered soul," or "the self endowed with ability." It likely refers to the inherent power or capability of the soul (*Ātma*) to realize its true nature, achieve liberation (*mokṣa*), or manifest spiritual potential. In a philosophical or yogic context, it could denote the self’s intrinsic strength to transcend ignorance, overcome limitations, or engage in spiritual practices. It may also suggest the empowerment of the individual through self-realization or alignment with divine consciousness.
