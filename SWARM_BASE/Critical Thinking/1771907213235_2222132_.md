**✦ Insight to Action (Assessment Summary)**

- Core Ethical Signal: The AI ethics curricula emphasize moral reflection and practical responsibility but under-address posthuman, care, and systemic justice frames.
- PowerForm in Operation: Shifts between Power Over (surveillance framing) and Power With (policy engagement and societal co-design).
- Virtues Activated: Technomoral wisdom, justice, courage; care and empathy need more institutional emphasis.
- Layer of Ethical Impact: Systemic and symbolic layers dominate; embodied ethics vary by course structure.
- Narrative Function: Courses anchor AI ethics education while selectively catalyzing deeper structural critique.

Recommended Action:

- Curriculum Redesign: Integrate Posthuman Ethics and Ethics of Care more directly (e.g., non-human stakeholder analysis, affective AI).
- Discourse Framing: Shift from AI as threat to AI as relational actor; center interdependence and ecological justice.
- Pedagogical Enhancement: Employ real-time virtue reflection, participatory ethics simulations, and global pluralism frameworks.
