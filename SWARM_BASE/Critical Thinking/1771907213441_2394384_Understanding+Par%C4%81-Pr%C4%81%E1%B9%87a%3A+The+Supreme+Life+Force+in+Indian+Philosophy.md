# परा-प्राण

Created at 2025/08/14 1:06 PM

The Sanskrit term **Parā-Prāṇa** can be broken down into its components: **Parā** (परा) and **Prāṇa** (प्राण). Below is the definition and the term written in Sanskrit.

### Definition:

1. **Parā** (परा): This term means "supreme," "highest," "transcendent," or "beyond." In Indian philosophy, particularly in Vedanta and Tantric traditions, Parā denotes the ultimate or absolute aspect of reality, often associated with the highest state of consciousness or the divine. In the context of speech (*Vāc*), *Parā* refers to the subtlest, transcendental level of sound or vibration, beyond ordinary perception.
2. **Prāṇa** (प्राण): This term means "life force," "vital energy," or "breath." In yoga, Ayurveda, and Indian philosophy, Prāṇa is the universal energy that sustains life, flowing through subtle channels (*nāḍīs*) and governing physical, mental, and spiritual functions. It is central to practices like *pranayama* (breath control) for enhancing vitality and spiritual awakening.
3. **Parā-Prāṇa Combined**: The term Parā-Prāṇa can be interpreted as "the supreme life force," "the transcendent vital energy," or "the ultimate breath." It likely refers to the highest or subtlest form of Prāṇa, the primordial life energy that transcends physical breath and connects to divine consciousness or the universal source. In a Tantric or yogic context, Parā-Prāṇa could denote the cosmic life force that underlies all existence, manifesting as the subtle vibration of consciousness. It may also suggest a meditative focus on the transcendent aspect of Prāṇa to achieve spiritual liberation or unity with the divine.
