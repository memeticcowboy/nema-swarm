**📜 The Authority of Legalese**
- **Legalese conveys authority.**: The convoluted language of legal documents serves to signal power and authority, akin to 'magic spells' that use archaic terms and structures.
- **Non-lawyers adopt legalese.**: Even individuals without legal training tend to use complex legal language when drafting laws, indicating a societal understanding of how legal language should be structured.
- **Implicit rules govern legal writing.**: There exists an implicit rule that legal documents should be written in a convoluted style, which influences both lawyers and non-lawyers alike.

**🔍 Complexity vs. Comprehension**
- **Legalese complicates understanding.**: The study reveals that legal documents are often difficult to comprehend, even for trained lawyers, due to their complex structure and language.
- **Preference for plain language.**: Lawyers express a preference for plain English versions of legal documents, finding them just as enforceable as traditional legalese.
- **Center-embedding increases difficulty.**: The use of center-embedded clauses in legal writing contributes significantly to its complexity, making it harder for readers to follow.

**🧠 Cognitive Insights**
- **Research on legal language began in 2020.**: The MIT research group has been investigating the characteristics of legalese since 2020, focusing on its unique linguistic features.
- **Center-embedding is a key feature.**: The study identified center-embedding as a common structural element in legal documents, which is not typical in everyday language.
- **Magic spell hypothesis proposed.**: The researchers suggest that the convoluted style of legal language may serve a similar purpose to magic spells, signaling authority and special status.

**✍️ Writing Experiments**
- **Participants wrote laws and stories.**: In experiments, participants were asked to draft laws and narratives, revealing a consistent use of center-embedding in legal writing but not in storytelling.
- **Editing did not change complexity.**: Regardless of whether participants wrote laws from scratch or edited them later, they consistently employed complex structures.
- **Plain language used in narratives.**: When writing stories related to the laws, participants opted for simpler language, highlighting the disparity between legal and narrative writing.

**📚 Historical Context**
- **Origins of legalese under investigation.**: The researchers are exploring the historical roots of center-embedding in legal documents, tracing back to British law and even ancient codes.
- **Potential stylistic legacy.**: There may be a stylistic tradition in legal writing that has persisted over centuries, influencing how laws are drafted today.
- **Hammurabi Code analysis planned.**: Future research aims to analyze the Hammurabi Code to determine if similar grammatical structures are present in ancient legal texts.

**🔄 Changing Legal Language**
- **Efforts to simplify legal documents.**: There have been ongoing efforts since the 1970s to write legal documents in simpler language, yet little progress has been made.
- **Optimism for future changes.**: The researchers express hope that understanding the complexities of legal language will lead to more comprehensible legal writing.
- **Historical resistance to change.**: Despite calls for simplification, traditional legal language has remained largely unchanged, indicating a deep-rooted resistance to reform.
