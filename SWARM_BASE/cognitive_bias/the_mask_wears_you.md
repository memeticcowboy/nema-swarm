The phrase "You don't wear the mask. The mask wears you" suggests that the mask, or whatever it represents (such as a false persona or disguise), becomes a dominant force that controls or consumes the wearer

. 

Here's a breakdown of the possible meanings:

- **Loss of Identity:** The mask becomes so integral to the person's identity that they lose touch with their true self.
- **Embodiment of the Persona:** The individual becomes defined by the role or character the mask represents, rather than being their own person.
- **Loss of Control:** The mask's influence takes over the individual's actions and decisions, leading to a loss of agency.
- **The Mask as a Burden:** The mask can be seen as a heavy burden that weighs down the wearer, impacting their life in significant ways.
- **Transformation:** In some cultural contexts, masks are believed to transform the wearer, allowing them to embody different personas or connect with spiritual forces. 

This quote can be interpreted in various contexts, including:

- **Psychology/Sociology:** Masking, or social camouflaging, is a defensive behavior where individuals conceal their natural personality or behavior due to social pressure or fear.
- **Literature:** The mask can symbolize deception, hidden identities, or the pressure to conform.
- **Cultural Practices:** Masks have been used in various cultures for rituals, ceremonies, and performances, where they can represent ancestral spirits, deities, or fictional characters. 

Essentially, the quote implies that the mask is not just a costume, but a powerful entity that can deeply affect the wearer's life and sense of self.
