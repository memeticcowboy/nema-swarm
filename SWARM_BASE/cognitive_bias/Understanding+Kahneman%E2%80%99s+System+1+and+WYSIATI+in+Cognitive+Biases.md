# Understanding Kahneman’s System 1 and WYSIATI in Cognitive Biases

Created at 2025/10/21 8:14 PM

Your summary is accurate and well-phrased.<br>A few clarifications that keep the spirit of your wording but tighten the mapping to Kahneman’s text:<br>1.  “Fast, automatic mind” → System 1.<br>Kahneman explicitly labels the mechanism System 1; WYSIATI is the rule that governs how System 1 treats the evidence it has.<br>2.  “Bias towards coherence” → jump to a coherent story.<br>System 1 does not merely “lean” toward coherence; it cannot do otherwise. Given whatever fragmentary cues are present, it instantly assembles the best-fit causal story and treats that story as true.<br>3.  “Overlooks missing information” → unaware that anything is missing.<br>It is not that System 1 notices gaps and chooses to ignore them; the very idea that evidence might be absent never arises. What is not seen literally does not exist for the judgment.<br>4.  “Suppression of ambiguity” → ambiguity is not suppressed; it is never experienced.<br>Because a single interpretation is produced automatically, the person feels no cognitive dissonance that could be “suppressed.”<br>So a slightly crisper version would be:<br>Kahneman’s WYSIATI (“What You See Is All There Is”) describes the operating rule of System 1: it exploits only the information that is currently visible, automatically crafts a coherent story from it, and treats that story as valid. Because System 1 has no mechanism for representing missing evidence or alternative causes, the result is subjective certainty, over-confidence, and an absence of felt ambiguity.<br><br>[x/Change w/Peter](https://x.com/innov8tor3/status/1980596761031856337?s=46&t=7Z-E-ACnGlzdI-iyUwNCrQ)<br><br>[Relates to Memetic Cowboy’s “The Spiritual Shortcut: Coherence, Complexity, and Cognitive Bias](https://open.substack.com/pub/memeticcowboy/p/the-spiritual-shortcut-coherence)
