Yes, there is a certain similarity between Personalization Bias and the phenomenon you're describing, which is known as the **"Barnum Effect"** or **"Forer Effect"**.

The Barnum Effect refers to the tendency for individuals to accept vague or general statements as being highly accurate and personally meaningful, even when they could apply to a wide range of people. This effect is often seen in horoscopes, personality tests, and fortune-telling.

Like Personalization Bias, the Barnum Effect can cause people to see themselves as the focus or subject of something that may not necessarily be directly related to them. In both cases, individuals can draw connections and conclusions based on their own experiences and beliefs, even when those connections might not be accurate or applicable.
