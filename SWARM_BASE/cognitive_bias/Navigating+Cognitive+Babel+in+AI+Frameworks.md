**🌐 Navigating Cognitive Babel in AI Frameworks**  
- **Incompatibility of Sensemaking Models**: AI-personalized frameworks often lead to a fragmented understanding among users, creating barriers to effective communication and collaboration. This incompatibility can hinder knowledge sharing and innovation within organizations, as individuals may interpret information differently based on their unique frameworks.
- **Need for Translation Layers**: To address the challenges posed by diverse sensemaking models, there is a pressing need for translation layers or shared epistemic protocols. These tools can facilitate better understanding and alignment among stakeholders, ensuring that everyone is on the same page when discussing complex ideas or projects.
- **Urgency of Lost Intelligibility**: The confusion and alienation stemming from incompatible frameworks highlight an urgent need for clarity in communication. As knowledge workers, designers, and strategists navigate this landscape, they must prioritize creating shared syntax and resonance loops to foster collaboration and reduce misunderstandings.
