**🔍 Overconfidence and Evidence Evaluation**

- **Subjective Confidence and Storytelling**: The confidence individuals have in their beliefs is largely influenced by the quality of the story they can tell about what they see, even if the evidence is limited. This often leads to overconfidence, as people fail to consider missing critical evidence. [_TIMESTAMP_](102-134)
- **Suppression of Doubt and Ambiguity**: Our associative system tends to form a coherent pattern of activation, which suppresses doubt and ambiguity, reinforcing the "what you see is all there is" mindset. [_TIMESTAMP_](138-148)

**🎭 Framing Effects and Perception**

- **Impact of Presentation on Emotions**: Different ways of presenting the same information can evoke different emotions, such as the difference in perception between survival rates and mortality rates, or fat-free versus fat content descriptions. - **Transparency and Limited Perspective**: Although alternative formulations are equivalent, individuals typically perceive only one formulation, reinforcing the "what you see is all there is" bias. [_TIMESTAMP_](181-194)

**📊 Base-Rate Neglect and Jumping to Conclusions**

- **Ignoring Statistical Information**: People often ignore statistical information in favor of vivid and specific case details, leading to base-rate neglect. - **Halo Effect and Independent Judgments**: Decisions are sometimes based on limited information, such as a good presentation, leading to errors like the halo effect.
