# Triality, Boycotts, and Obsolescence Theater: Four Arcs of Cultural Friction

*when complexity meets urgency, partner—how do we stay oriented?*

## Abstract

This reflection maps four converging investigations into the same fundamental question: **how do we navigate cultural complexity without collapsing into simplistic binaries?** Drawing on Jennifer Spicer's triality framework, Damian Milton's double empathy problem, Baudrillardian spectacle analysis, and the Cowboy's own memetic cartography, we trace a path from individual cognitive patterning through collective political theater to the manufactured urgency of AI discourse and the meta-philosophy of the Memetic Cowboy project itself. Through SIML notation and dimensional operator analysis, we examine how triality functions as navigation tool for wicked problems, how mythic warfare structures political spectacle, how obsolescence theater manufactures compliance through urgency, and how the Cowboy project offers a meta-framework for holding this complexity without dimensional collapse. The analysis reveals a central pattern: in each case, culture pressures toward binary simplification, but dimensional thinking—three perspectives before synthesis, embodiment before representation, refusal before compliance—offers paths of resistance.

**Keywords:** triality, neurodivergence, boycott, memeplex, No Kings Day, obsolescence theater, AI urgency, cultural friction, mythic warfare, memetic cowboy, wicked problems, double empathy, dimensional collapse, power over, power with

## 1. Introduction: The Pressure Toward Collapse

Four trails through the same territory. Four attempts to understand what happens when complexity meets urgency:

1. **Triality and Neurodivergence** — Three perspectives as navigation tool for wicked problems
2. **Boycott Walmart / No Kings Day** — Mythic warfare and the struggle over American identity
3. **AI Obsolescence Theater** — Urgency manufactured to enforce compliance
4. **The Memetic Cowboy Project** — Meta-framework for holding complexity without collapse

*spits into the dimensional fog*

This one's about the pressure to simplify. The cultural gravity that pulls three-dimensional thinking into flat binaries. The Cowboy rides through not to judge, but to understand: *What happens when we're asked to navigate wicked problems with tools designed for tame ones?*

### 1.1 The Dimensional Collapse Pattern

Every analysis carries its shadow. To advocate triality is to risk infinite regression—three perspectives becoming thirty, paralysis masquerading as wisdom. To map political spectacle is to risk participating in it. To critique urgency is to risk avoiding necessary adaptation. To build meta-frameworks is to risk abstraction eating action.

This is the fundamental recursion at the heart of dimensional thinking: any framework that successfully maps complexity becomes itself subject to complexity's pressure. The map faces the same gravity as the territory. The triality that sees duality risks becoming its own duality—triality vs. duality, another binary.

## 6. The Thread Between: Complexity, Urgency, and the Third Way

Four posts, one arc: **the struggle to maintain dimensional thinking when culture pressures collapse toward binaries.**

### 6.1 The Pattern

1. **Triality** — Three perspectives as navigation tool for wicked problems
2. **Boycott/King** — Binary political spectacle masking shared mythic substrate
3. **Obsolescence Theater** — Urgency manufactured to enforce compliance
4. **Cowboy Project** — Meta-framework for holding all of this complexity

### 6.2 The Risk

Each analysis carries its shadow:
- Triality can become paralysis—three perspectives becoming infinite regression
- Political spectacle can consume all attention, leaving no energy for systemic work
- Urgency resistance can become excuse for avoiding necessary adaptation
- Meta-frameworks can become meta-meta-frameworks, abstraction eating action

### 6.3 The Cowboy's Discipline

Perhaps the work is:
- **Expand before collapsing** — Three perspectives, then integration, not premature synthesis
- **See the shared substrate** — King and rebel drinking from the same well
- **Feel urgency without being governed by it** — Discomfort as signal, not command
- **Build frameworks that build** — Philosophy as scaffolding, not cage

### 6.4 The Cost and the Gift

What's striking across all four is the **pressure toward dimensional collapse**. Triality faces the default mode of duality. The boycott spectacle reduces complex political economy to "Walton = enemy." Obsolescence Theater manufactures urgency precisely to prevent reflection. Even the Cowboy project risks becoming just another intellectual system.

But the Cowboy also sees the crack: *triality as practice*, not just theory—actually holding three views. *The No Kings Day rupture*—bodies in space breaking the meme loop. *Slow tech as resistance*—refusing the tempo of manufactured urgency. *The elemental framework*—six modes, not one, preserving dimensionality.

The gift is recognition: to see the pressure toward collapse is the first step toward refusing it. To name the binary is to begin the work of triality.

**Expand first. Collapse carefully. Ride the spiral.**
