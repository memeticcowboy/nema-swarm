# Boundaries of Perception: War, Tunnels, Selves, and Spheres

*the architecture of containment—what holds us together, what breaks us apart, and the impossible geometry of defending coherence in an age of dissolution*

## Abstract

This reflection examines four converging analyses that map the epistemic infrastructure of the self: how external forces penetrate consciousness, how perception filters construct experiential worlds, how technology fragments identity, and what defensive structures emerge to preserve sanity against collapse. Drawing on memetic theory, General Semantics, Robert Anton Wilson's reality tunnels framework, Susan Blackmore's selfplex theory, and the elemental operator model (σ, ρ, λ, β, δγ, μ, ∮), this essay traces a terrifying arc from external threat through internal fragmentation to defensive withdrawal. The analyses reveal not just different topics but interconnected patterns governing the containers that contain us—and what happens when they crack. Through SIML notation and dimensional operator analysis, we map the architecture of containment, the impossibility of neutrality, and the recursive nature of all defensive structures.

**Keywords:** memetics, reality tunnels, selfplex, sanity spheres, war, perception, identity fragmentation, containment, boundaries, epistemic infrastructure, General Semantics, ontological violence

## 1. Introduction: The Architecture of Containment

I've been tracking something structural lately—not a specific meme, but a **pattern of patterns** that governs how we experience reality, construct identity, and defend our coherence against dissolution. Four analyses landed in my queue that, taken together, map the **epistemic infrastructure of the self**: how external forces penetrate us, how perception filters construct our worlds, how technology fragments our identities, and what defensive structures we build to preserve sanity.

This isn't just about different topics. It's about **the containers that contain us**—and what happens when they crack.

The arc traces a journey from external threat through internal fragmentation to defensive withdrawal:
1. **War's Gaze** breaches the boundary—no neutrality, no escape
2. **Reality Tunnels** reveal that boundaries were always constructed—perception is filtering
3. **AI Selfplexes** fragment the constructor itself—identity becomes distributed, optimized, licensed
4. **Sanity Spheres** emerge as last-ditch defense—containment against collapse

Each step follows logically. First we're threatened. Then we learn we're always already shaped. Then we fragment under technological acceleration. Finally we retreat into defensive coherence.

But here's what haunts me: **the sanity sphere is itself a tunnel**. The defense against fragmentation becomes another fragment. The attempt to hold oneself together becomes another performance of self. There's no outside to the architecture—only different rooms in the same collapsing mansion.

Saddle up. This trail winds through ontological violence, perceptual construction, identity dissolution, and the recursive trap of defensive containment.
