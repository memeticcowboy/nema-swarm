# Exaptation: Evolution of Feathers for Flight

Created at 2026/01/22 8:30 AM

Exaptation is an evolutionary process where a trait that evolved for one purpose is co-opted for a new, different function, like bird feathers evolving for insulation but then being used for flight. It differs from adaptation (a trait shaped for its current use) and highlights evolution's reliance on repurposing existing structures, often leading to innovation and novelty in biology, technology (like the printing press using wine press mechanics), and linguistics. The term was introduced by Stephen Jay Gould and Elisabeth Vrba in the 1980s, replacing Darwin's "pre-adaptation".
