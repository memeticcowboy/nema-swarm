# Retrospective Assessment and Refinement of Co-SPHERE Framework for Ecological Integrity

## Co-SPHERE Retrospective Assessment Prompt

*(Stability, Pruning, and Canon Alignment)*

**Context**<br>You are reviewing prior writings, diagrams, notes, or frameworks labeled or implied as **Co-SPHERE**.<br>The goal is **not synthesis or enrichment**, but **diagnostic alignment** with the current definition of Co-SPHERE as:

> *Ω-permeable, Z-level soft coordination basin enabling shared action without coercive closure.*

Your task is to determine **what stays**, **what must be revised**, and **what must be removed** to preserve ecological integrity and avoid MemeGrid drift.

***

### Step 1 — Definition Lock (Non-Negotiable)

Evaluate all material against the following invariants.<br>Anything that violates these **cannot remain unchanged**.

A valid **Co-SPHERE** must:

- Coordinate **action**, not belief or identity
- Operate as a **soft basin**, not a sealed structure
- Remain **Ω-permeable** (revisable under dissonance)
- Allow **plural paths** to pool without fusion
- Preserve **exit without punishment**
- Distinguish itself clearly from:
    - We-Sphere (relational continuity)
    - Threadplex (distributed gradients)
    - Lattice (structural memory)
    - MemeGrid (hard Z collapse)

***

### Step 2 — Material Classification

For each prior artifact, label it **one** of the following (no hybrids):

1. **Canonical Co-SPHERE**
    - Already matches current definition
    - Requires no structural revision
2. **Recoverable (Reframe Required)**
    - Contains useful elements
    - Currently mis-scoped (e.g., identity, consensus, morality, totality)
    - Can be corrected by:
        - Removing claims of finality
        - Reintroducing Ω-permeability
        - Shifting from belief → coordination
3. **Misclassified (Relocate Layer)**
    - Is *not* Co-SPHERE, but belongs to:
        - We-Sphere
        - Threadplex
        - Lattice
        - I-Tube / My-Stream
    - Should be **moved**, not deleted
4. **Pathological (Discard)**
    - Enacts or justifies:
        - Closure without exit
        - Moralized unity
        - Suppression of dissonance
        - Structural inevitability
    - Exhibits MemeGrid traits

***

### Step 3 — Failure Mode Detection

Explicitly scan for these **disqualifying signals**:

- Co-SPHERE described as:
    - “shared truth”
    - “collective identity”
    - “consensus field”
    - “stable worldview”
- Dissonance framed as:
    - threat
    - corruption
    - misunderstanding to be resolved
- Revision treated as:
    - betrayal
    - weakness
    - instability
- Language implying:
    - inevitability
    - moral closure
    - final coordination

Any detected signal → **cannot remain as-is**.

***

### Step 4 — Low-Frequency Test

Ask of each piece:

- What **survives compression** if narratives are stripped?
- Are there:
    - action affordances?
    - phase relationships?
    - timing synchronizations?
- Or only:
    - stories
    - values
    - identities
    - moral claims?

Only **low-frequency affordances** qualify as Co-SPHERE-compatible.

***

***

### Step 6 — Final Integrity Check

Conclude with a **single paragraph** answering:

> *Does the remaining Co-SPHERE material still allow coordinated action **without** demanding belief, identity, or permanence?*

If the answer is not unambiguously **yes**, further pruning is required.

***

### Absolute Constraint

Do **not**:

- add new theory
- reconcile contradictions
- preserve material for sentimental or historical reasons

The ecology values **health over heritage**.
