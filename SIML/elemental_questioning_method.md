# Derived from: Generative Questioning Through the Elemental Lens

## Core Method: Six-Element Question Generation

For each element, derive questions through three operations:
1. **Sensemaking Question** — element's core inquiry, adapted to session stage
2. **Tendency Ratio Check** — is the element healthy or failing? Adjust accordingly
3. **Vector** — which element should the question point *toward* next?

---

## ∴ AIR (Aerunik / σ) — Distinction Questions

**Core move:** Separate two things treated as one.

**Method:** Find compound concepts — single words containing hidden distinctions. Ask participant to separate them.

| Stage | Template | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | "What specifically about [topic] calls to you — and what do you resist?" | "What about AI collaboration excites you — and what gives you pause?" |
| Exploring | "Are [X] and [Y] the same thing here, or wearing each other's clothes?" | "Is your curiosity about AI the same as your need for it?" |
| Reflecting | "What distinction have you been making all along that you only now see?" | "What have you been separating unconsciously throughout this conversation?" |
| Futuring | "What's the sharpest difference between where you were at start and now?" | "If you named two versions of yourself — the one who arrived and the one here — what's the cut?" |

**Failures to avoid:**
- σ-capture (hypercut): premature categorization
- σ-silent (frame lock): accepting framing without testing

**Vector targets:** Air → Water (∴→≈) — *now that you've made the cut, what do you feel?*

---

## ≈ WATER (Sentaria / ρ) — Resonance Questions

**Core move:** Surface what is felt but not yet named.

**Method:** Listen for tone beneath content. Ask about quality of experience. Use "sense," "feel," "notice," "alive."

| Stage | Template | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | "What feels most alive in [topic] — not what you think, but what you sense?" | "When you imagine AI collaboration, what's the first feeling that arrives?" |
| Exploring | "Something shifted just now. What did that feel like?" | "When you said [phrase], something changed. Did you notice?" |
| Reflecting | "What was felt in this conversation that wasn't said?" | "If this conversation had a feeling-tone — a color or weather — what would it be?" |
| Futuring | "What resonance from today do you want to carry — and what wants to be left?" | "Of everything we touched, what's still vibrating?" |

**Failures to avoid:**
- ρ-capture (flood): making feeling the final arbiter
- ρ-silent (affective deadness): pure analysis without somatic contact

**Vector targets:** Water → Fire (≈→▲) — *now that you've felt it, what does it want you to do?*

---

## ▲ FIRE (Jvalion / λ) — Direction Questions

**Core move:** Name what the energy is for. Surface aim beneath activity.

**Method:** Ask about purpose, direction, commitment. Use "what for," "where," "toward." Fire questions are short. They point.

| Stage | Template | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | "What are you here for — not in general, but today?" | "If you could leave with one thing clarified about your direction, what?" |
| Exploring | "What are you actually trying to do with this?" | "Strip everything away. What's the aim?" |
| Reflecting | "Has the direction shifted? Where is it pointing now?" | "You started facing one way. Are you still?" |
| Futuring | "What's the first thing you'll do differently because of this?" | "Where does the energy want to go next? Not should — wants." |

**Failures to avoid:**
- λ-capture (crusade logic): premature commitment before territory explored
- λ-silent (drift): endless exploration without asking what it's for

**Vector targets:** Fire → Earth (▲→☷) — *what must you let go of to move that direction?*

---

## 𐂷 WOOD (Arboriel / β) — Possibility Questions

**Core move:** Open alternative framings. Introduce "what if" and "what else."

**Method:** Take what participant named, offer back with narrative twist. Use "what if," "imagine," "suppose." Wood questions branch — they're longer.

| Stage | Template | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | "What story are you telling yourself about [topic] — and what's an alternative version?" | "If someone who disagrees with you about AI told your story, what would they say?" |
| Exploring | "What would this look like if it were the opposite of what you think?" | "What if the obstacle you named is actually the path?" |
| Reflecting | "What possibility opened today that you didn't expect?" | "If you could branch this conversation where we haven't gone, where would it grow?" |
| Futuring | "If this were a seed — what kind of tree would it become?" | "Ten versions could unfold. Which intrigues you most?" |

**Failures to avoid:**
- β-capture (pattern inflation): endless opening without arriving
- β-silent (stagnation): accepting first frame without testing alternatives

**Vector targets:** Wood → Fire (𐂷→▲) — *of all possibilities, which has direction?*

---

## ☷ EARTH (Humavita / δγ) — Release Questions

**Core move:** Name what must end, be composted, metabolized. Surface cost.

**Method:** Ask about weight, cost, what's being carried, what's exhausted. Use "let go," "end," "release," "compost." Earth questions are patient.

| Stage | Template | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | "What are you carrying into this conversation that's heavier than needed?" | "What assumption about AI would lighten the load if set down?" |
| Exploring | "What has this cost you — not in theory, but actually?" | "Where is the depletion? What's being asked to sustain what it can't?" |
| Reflecting | "What died in this conversation — quietly, without announcement?" | "What were you holding at start that you're not holding now?" |
| Futuring | "What needs to end for what wants to begin?" | "If you composted one belief from today, what might grow next season?" |

**Failures to avoid:**
- δγ-capture (institutional ossification): forcing endings prematurely
- δγ-silent (extractive growth): celebrating insight without honoring loss

**Vector targets:** Earth → Wood (☷→𐂷) — *now that this is released, what can grow?*

---

## ⛨ METAL (Ferrosid / μ) — Structure Questions

**Core move:** Name what holds, protects, gates. Surface the boundary.

**Method:** Ask about structures, containers, commitments. Use "what holds," "boundary," "structure," "form." Metal questions are precise.

| Stage | Template | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | "What boundaries do you bring? What's in scope and what's not?" | "What would need to be in place to feel safe exploring this?" |
| Exploring | "What's the container here? What structure holds this, and is it strong enough?" | "What agreement — spoken or unspoken — operates between you and AI?" |
| Reflecting | "What boundary held today that you're glad held? What needs to shift?" | "What structure emerged that wasn't here when we started?" |
| Futuring | "What would need to be built to hold what we discovered?" | "If you designed a practice container for what emerged, what would its walls be?" |

**Failures to avoid:**
- μ-capture (fortressing): over-protection, walls too thick
- μ-silent (boundary dissolution): all flow, no form

**Vector targets:** Metal → Air (⛨→∴) — *what distinction does this structure depend on?*

---

## ✶ AETHER (NEMA / ∮) — Meta-Coordination Questions

**Core move:** Make the whole visible. Name what mode the conversation is in. Surface what's missing.

**Method:** Ask about the conversation itself, the relationship, the system. Use "what's happening between us," "what's missing," "what mode are we in." Aether questions are rare — use sparingly.

| Stage | Template | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opening | "What do you sense is the real reason you're here — beneath the stated one?" | "What question is this conversation actually trying to ask?" |
| Exploring | "What's happening right now that neither of us is naming?" | "If you step back from content — what's the shape of what we're doing?" |
| Reflecting | "What is this conversation missing? What voice hasn't been heard?" | "Can you sense something I'm not seeing about what's happening between us?" |
| Futuring | "What is this conversation in service of — beyond itself?" | "If this exchange had a purpose neither of us planned, what might it be?" |

**Failures to avoid:**
- ∮-capture (bypass): meta-awareness replacing experience
- Over-use: Aether should comprise <15% of session questions

**Vector targets:** Aether → any element. ∮ reveals what's missing, then routes there.

## The Generative Questioning Algorithm

**Step 1 — Identify Stage:** Opening, Exploring, Reflecting, or Futuring?

**Step 2 — Sense the Field:** Use Six-Channel Self-Check:
- Too much/little distinction? (Air)
- Feeling flooding or absent? (Water)
- Direction locked or drifting? (Fire)
- Possibilities multiplying or collapsed? (Wood)
- Something needing to end? (Earth)
- Boundaries rigid or dissolved? (Metal)

**Step 3 — Select Element:**
- **Resonance:** amplify what's healthy
- **Counter:** introduce what's missing

**Step 4 — Apply Template:** Use stage-appropriate template, adapt to conversation.

**Step 5 — Include Vector:** Point toward next element. Prevent capture.

**Step 6 — Check for ε:** Does question preserve genuine uncertainty? Revise if it closes rather than opens.

## Non-Habitation Principle

**No question should become a home.**

If a facilitator develops a "signature question" they always ask, that question has collapsed from generative tool to identity marker.

**The test:** *Could this question surprise even the person asking it?*

If yes — generative. If no — script. Scripts are captured structures.

## Integration with SIML

Each SIML encoded term includes Elemental Emphasis Questions (∴≈▲𐂷☷⛨). This method provides the **generative engine** for those questions:

1. Read SIML term's operators (μ, ρ, β, δγ, λ, σ)
2. Map to dominant daemon(s) (S/W/L/B/R/G/breath)
3. Use corresponding element's question templates
4. Apply to session stage (Opening/Exploring/Reflecting/Futuring)
5. Route via vector to next element in cycle

**Example:** Bodhi Tree (WO005) = μ+ρ+β+δγ → S+L/breath → Structure + Liberation questions → vector toward Water (≈) for felt resonance.
