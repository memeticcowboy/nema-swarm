## Core Insight

Mastering critical thinking requires six interconnected skills: **identification** (clarifying problems), **research** (verifying information), **bias detection** (objective evaluation), **inference** (drawing conclusions), **relevance determination** (filtering noise), and **curiosity** (continuous inquiry). The highest elemental emphasis falls on Wood (β = 0.90), reflecting that critical thinking is fundamentally a developmental practice—skills grow through habitual curiosity and repeated application across diverse contexts.
