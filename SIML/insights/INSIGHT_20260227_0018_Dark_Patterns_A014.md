## DEFINITION

Dark patterns are deceptive user interface designs that manipulate users into actions they might not otherwise take, primarily benefiting the company rather than the user. They exploit cognitive biases—particularly FOMO (fear of missing out), loss aversion, and urgency heuristics—to bypass rational decision-making.
