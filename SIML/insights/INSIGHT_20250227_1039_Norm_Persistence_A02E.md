## Core Concept

Norm Persistence describes the mechanisms through which communication norms, social conventions, and institutional frameworks resist change—even when alternatives would serve collective interests better. The phenomenon emerges from the interplay of three reinforcing forces: **social reinforcement** (Spiral of Silence, observational learning), **cognitive biases** (anchoring, cognitive dissonance), and **institutional inertia** (path dependency, organizational frames).

Water feels this flow: the cold withdrawal of social warmth from deviants, the comforting resonance of conformity, the emotional cost of speaking against dominant narratives.
