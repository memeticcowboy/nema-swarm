## Core Concept

**Social Identity Bias** describes the pervasive tendency for both humans and AI systems to favor ingroups while disparaging outgroups—a pattern that emerges from the deep architecture of social cognition and becomes amplified through algorithmic scale.

Research demonstrates that large language models exhibit this bias with striking quantitative clarity: prompts beginning with "We are" generate responses 93% more likely to be positive, while "They are" prompts produce content 115% more likely to be negative. These aren't subtle statistical artifacts—they represent fundamental distortions in how meaning is constructed around group boundaries.
