## Core Phenomenon

Base-Rate Neglect represents a fundamental failure of intuitive judgment: the systematic disregard of statistical base rates in favor of vivid, specific case information. Even when presented with clear numerical probabilities, System 1 gravitates toward the concrete story, the representative detail, the stereotype-matching narrative.

### The Linda Problem (Classic Demonstration)

> Linda is 31 years old, single, outspoken, and very bright. She majored in philosophy. As a student, she was deeply concerned with issues of discrimination and social justice, and also participated in anti-nuclear demonstrations.
>
> Which is more probable?
> a) Linda is a bank teller
> b) Linda is a bank teller and is active in the feminist movement

Most people choose (b) because it feels more representative of Linda's description—even though the conjunction of two events cannot be more probable than either event alone (conjunction fallacy). The vivid narrative of "feminist bank teller" overrides the statistical reality that there are far more bank tellers than feminist bank tellers.

## AI Implications

### The Generative AI Risk

Large language models excel at generating vivid, coherent narratives. They can produce compelling case descriptions that feel representative while ignoring or misrepresenting base-rate probabilities. This creates a compound risk:

1. **Narrative Fluency:** AI-generated text is inherently fluent and coherent
2. **Representativeness Illusion:** Users judge AI outputs by how well they match expectations
3. **Base-Rate Blindness:** Statistical grounding is often absent or fabricated
4. **Confidence Inflation:** Fluency creates unwarranted confidence in accuracy

### Example: Medical Diagnosis

An AI describes a patient's symptoms in vivid detail, matching a rare disease stereotype. The base rate (1 in 10,000) is buried or omitted. The clinician, like the Linda problem subject, judges by representativeness rather than probability.

### Mitigation Strategies

- **Explicit Base Rate Provision:** AI systems should prepend statistical context
- **Visualization:** Convert abstract probabilities to concrete frequencies
- **Decision Protocols:** Mandate base-rate consideration before case-based judgment
- **Uncertainty Signaling:** Train models to express confidence intervals, not just narratives
