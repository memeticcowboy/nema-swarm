# SIML Insight: Motivated Reasoning

## Core Definition

**Motivated Reasoning** is the cognitive process by which individuals seek out, interpret, and remember information in ways that confirm their pre-existing beliefs while avoiding contradictory evidence. It represents a defensive mechanism where the drive for cognitive coherence and identity protection overrides the pursuit of objective truth.

## Source Context

From the Atlantic article "This Article Won't Change Your Mind" (2017), drawing on Leon Festinger's theory of cognitive dissonance:

> "People seek out information that supports their beliefs and avoid contradictory evidence."

The case study of Dorothy Martin's doomsday cult illustrates the phenomenon: when prophecy failed, believers didn't abandon their worldview—they doubled down. The social and psychological investment in the belief had become too great to permit revision.

Daniel Shaw's experience with a guru accused of misconduct demonstrates the identity-fusion aspect: he "chose to believe" despite evidence because the belief was inseparable from his sense of self, community, and meaning.
