## CONTEXT NOTE

Dark patterns are deceptive UX designs that manipulate users into actions benefiting companies, exploiting cognitive biases like FOMO and loss aversion. Common types include:

- **Forced Continuity**: Hard-to-cancel subscriptions, hidden cancellation flows
- **Hidden Fees**: Costs revealed only at final checkout
- **Privacy Zuckering**: Deceptive privacy settings that encourage data sharing
- **Urgency Timers**: Manufactured scarcity ("Only 2 left!", countdown timers)
- **Confirmshaming**: Guilt-tripping language for opting out ("No, I don't want to save money")
- **Roach Motel**: Easy to get in, hard to get out

Regulatory responses include GDPR (data protection) and the Digital Services Act (explicitly banning dark patterns in EU, effective May 2024).

**AI Amplification Risk:** AI-driven personalization threatens to evolve dark patterns into individually tailored manipulation. When exploitation is personalized, collective recognition and resistance become harder. The pattern that works on you may not work on your neighbor—making it invisible to collective scrutiny.

**Normalization Risk:** As dark patterns become standard practice, users develop learned helplessness. The expectation of manipulation erodes trust in all digital interfaces, even those that are genuinely transparent.
