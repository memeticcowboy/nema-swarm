## CONTEXTUAL NOTES

### Historical Trajectory
- **400 A.D.:** St. Augustine refers to *falsae memoriae* (false memories)
- **Victorian Era:** Linked to paranormal beliefs and psychopathy
- **1896:** François-Léon Arnaud coins "déjà vu" as scientific term
- **Present:** Neuroscience reveals neurological basis in entorhinal cortex, hippocampus

### Neurological Mechanisms
1. **Hemispheric Delay:** Information arrives at different times to each hemisphere
2. **Unconscious Processing:** Distraction leads to unconscious environment registration
3. **Familiarity Without Retrieval:** Recognition systems activate without memory recall
4. **Prediction Error:** Brain's predictive coding generates false familiarity signals

### Clinical Relevance
- **Epilepsy:** Temporal lobe seizures often preceded by déjà vu auras
- **Schizophrenia:** Reality monitoring deficits amplify déjà vu experiences
- **ADHD:** Attention fluctuations create processing gaps
- **Pathological Déjà Vu:** Persistent, debilitating false familiarity leading to confabulation

### AI-Related Parallels
- **Synthetic Familiarity:** AI-generated content may trigger similar "I've seen this before" feelings
- **Reality Monitoring Challenges:** Distinguishing AI-generated from genuine human content mirrors déjà vu's source confusion
- **Memory Contamination:** Exposure to AI content may alter or fabricate memory traces
