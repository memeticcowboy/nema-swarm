## CONTEXT NOTE

Dark patterns are deceptive UX designs that manipulate users into actions benefiting companies, exploiting cognitive biases like FOMO and loss aversion. Common tactics include:

- **Forced Continuity** — Hard-to-cancel subscriptions (#A04A)
- **Hidden Fees** — Costs revealed only at final checkout
- **Misleading Privacy Settings** — Confusing controls that default to data sharing
- **Guilt-Tripping Language** — Emotional manipulation to prevent cancellation
- **Urgency Triggers** — Countdown timers creating false scarcity

These designs prioritize corporate gain over user autonomy, eroding trust and making users feel deceived. Major companies like Google and Amazon have been cited for using these manipulative tactics.

Regulatory responses include:
- **GDPR** — Data protection and consent requirements
- **Digital Services Act** — Explicitly bans dark patterns (effective May 2024)
- **EU Unfair Commercial Practices Directive** — Prohibits misleading practices

AI-driven personalization threatens to evolve dark patterns into hyper-targeted manipulation tailored to individual cognitive vulnerabilities.
