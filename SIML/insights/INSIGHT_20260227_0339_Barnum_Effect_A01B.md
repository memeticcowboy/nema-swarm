## CONTEXT & APPLICATION

**Origin:** Named after P.T. Barnum's "something for everyone" showmanship; formally identified by Bertram Forer (1949) who gave students identical personality descriptions allegedly tailored to them—rated 4.26/5 accuracy despite being identical and generic.

**Modern Exploitation:**
- AI "personalized" content that adapts vocabulary but not substance
- Personality tests that return Barnum statements as "insights"
- Horoscopes and fortune-telling that leverage subjective validation
- Marketing that feels targeted while being broadly deployed

**Mitigation:**
- Demand specificity: "What exactly do you mean?"
- Test counterfactuals: Would this apply to my opposite?
- Seek falsifiability: What would prove this wrong?
- Notice the feeling: Felt accuracy ≠ actual accuracy

**Related Terms:**
- Subjective Validation (confirming vague statements with personal details)
- Personalization Bias (seeing oneself as focus of general content)
- Confirmation Bias (seeking confirming evidence)
