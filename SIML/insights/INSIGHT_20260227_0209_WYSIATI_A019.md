## ESSENCE

> WYSIATI is the cognitive closure that occurs when coherence substitutes for completeness—when the mind's automatic pattern-making generates subjective certainty from partial evidence, rendering invisible the very fact that anything is missing.

## PHENOMENON

Kahneman's WYSIATI ("What You See Is All There Is") describes the operating rule of System 1: it exploits only the information currently visible, automatically crafts a coherent story from it, and treats that story as valid. Because System 1 has no mechanism for representing missing evidence or alternative causes, the result is subjective certainty, overconfidence, and an absence of felt ambiguity.

The phenomenon operates through several mechanisms:
- **Associative Coherence:** The mind constructs the best-fit causal story from available cues
- **Suppression of Doubt:** Ambiguity is not suppressed—it is never experienced
- **One-Sided Evidence Effect:** Exposure to partial information increases confidence more than exposure to complete information
- **Framing Transparency:** Alternative formulations exist but remain invisible to the perceiver
- **Base-Rate Neglect:** Statistical information is ignored in favor of vivid, specific details

## THE CLOSURE MECHANISM

### Stage 1: Evidence Encounter
System 1 encounters partial evidence. The visible cues are processed automatically. No mechanism alerts the system that evidence might be absent.

### Stage 2: Coherence Construction
The associative system assembles the best-fit story from available cues. The narrative forms instantly, without felt effort.

### Stage 3: Certainty Generation
Coherence produces cognitive ease, which is interpreted as truth. The smoother the story, the more certain the judgment.

### Stage 4: Missingness Blindness
The very concept that evidence might be missing never arises. What is not seen literally does not exist for the judgment.

### Stage 5: Overconfidence Expression
Confidence is calibrated to story quality, not evidence completeness. The result is systematic overconfidence in judgments based on partial information.
