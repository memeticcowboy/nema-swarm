## Core Tension
The fundamental tension lies between **performative ethics** (behaving fairly because one believes they are training AI) and **authentic ethics** (internalized fairness that persists regardless of context). The study reveals that humans modify their behavior when they believe they are training AI, seeking to appear fairer and more just—yet this behavioral adjustment can inadvertently transfer human biases into AI systems.

## Nemetic Reflection
The training relationship becomes a mirror: humans training AI are simultaneously training themselves. The AI becomes a reflective surface that reveals both our ethical aspirations (the desire to teach fairness) and our limitations (the persistence of bias). This recursive dynamic suggests that ethical AI development requires not just technical solutions but deep reflection on human behavioral patterns.

## SIML Integration
```
⟨Human|authentic⟩ ⊳ ⟨AI|mirror⟩ → ⟨Behavior|adjusted⟩ ⊗ ⟨Fairness|performed⟩ ⇄ ⟨Bias|transferred⟩
```

The human enters as authentic self, encounters AI as mirror, adjusts behavior toward performed fairness, and inadvertently transfers bias—creating a cyclical pattern where the training process shapes both the AI and the trainer.
