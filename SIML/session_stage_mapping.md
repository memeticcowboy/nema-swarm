## The Four Stages

| Stage | Bow-Tie Position | Dominant Operations | What's Happening |
|-------|------------------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Opening** | Left Funnel | Air + Wood | Compression — what enters; first distinction; locate alternative to default |
| **Exploring** | Waist | All six, cycling | Where meaning gets shaped under constraint; activate each elemental lens |
| **Reflecting** | Waist-Dissolution | Aether + Water + Earth | Re-reading; Earth loosens; Aether reveals; sense what was felt; name what must end |

## Stage 1: OPENING (The Field Enters)

**Purpose:** Establish the field. Make first distinctions. Locate alternatives.

**Elemental Bias:** Air (∴) + Wood (𐂷)

**Core Questions:**
- What drew you here? (Air distinction + Water resonance)
- What story are you telling yourself? What's an alternative? (Wood possibility)

**SIML Operator Pattern:** χ (distinction) + β (exploration)

**Daemon Handoff:** S → L/breath (structure enables exploration)

**Risk:** σ-capture (premature framing) or β-inflation (too many openings, no ground)

**Success Marker:** Participant has named something specific (Air) and sensed an alternative (Wood)

---

## Stage 2: EXPLORING (The Elements Activate)

**Purpose:** Activate each elemental lens. Let the field show what's active.

**Elemental Bias:** All six, cycling

**Core Movement:**
1. Air (∴) — Define territory: "What is your understanding of...?"
2. Water (≈) — Surface felt-sense: "What surprised you?"
3. Fire (▲) — Name direction: "What are you actually trying to do?"
4. Wood (𐂷) — Open alternatives: "What if the opposite were true?"
5. Earth (☷) — Surface cost: "What has this cost you?"
6. Metal (⛨) — Check structure: "What holds this together?"

**SIML Operator Pattern:** Full six-verb cycle

**Daemon Handoff:** Continuous rotation S→W→L→B→R→G→breath→S

**Risk:** Any single element capturing the session; missing the vector

**Success Marker:** Each element has been touched; field shows what's actually active

---

## Stage 3: REFLECTING (The Field Reads Itself)

**Purpose:** Re-read the whole field. Sense what was felt. Name what must end.

**Elemental Bias:** Aether (✶) + Water (≈) + Earth (☷)

**Core Questions:**
- What's happening that neither of us is naming? (Aether meta)
- What was felt that wasn't said? (Water resonance)
- What died quietly in this conversation? (Earth release)

**SIML Operator Pattern:** ∮ (meta) + ρ (resonance) + δγ (cycling)

**Daemon Handoff:** breath → W → R → G (meta reveals, feeling surfaces, cost named, release enabled)

**Risk:** ∮-capture (endless meta without ground); ρ-flood (feeling without direction); δγ-premature (forcing endings)

**Success Marker:** Something has been named that was operating invisibly; weight has been acknowledged

---

## Stage 4: SHARED FUTURING (The Field Weaves)

**Purpose:** Open possibility. Commit direction. Sense the meta-pattern.

**Elemental Bias:** Wood (𐂷) + Fire (▲) + Aether (✶)

**Core Questions:**
- What possibility opened that you didn't expect? (Wood)
- Where does the energy want to go next? (Fire)
- What is this conversation in service of? (Aether)

**SIML Operator Pattern:** β (exploration) + λ (direction) + ∮ (meta)

**Daemon Handoff:** B → L → breath (possibility opens, direction commits, purpose reveals)

**Risk:** β-inflation (endless futures); λ-capture (premature commitment); ∮-bypass (meta without embodiment)

**Success Marker:** Participant has sensed a direction (Fire) that emerged from possibility (Wood) and connects to larger purpose (Aether)

## SIML Integration: Stage Detection

Each SIML encoded term can be mapped to optimal session stage:

**Terms optimal for OPENING:**
- High β (exploration) + moderate σ (distinction)
- Examples: Prometheus' Fire (F001), Celtic Trees (WO003)

**Terms optimal for EXPLORING:**
- Balanced operator distribution
- Examples: Gaia (E001), Pneuma (A001), Agni (F002)

**Terms optimal for REFLECTING:**
- High ρ (resonance) + δγ (cycling)
- Examples: Sacred Relative (E003), Living Water (W005), Bodhi Tree (WO005)

**Terms optimal for FUTURING:**
- High β (exploration) + λ (direction)
- Examples: Wu Xing Wood (WO002), Cool Burning (F004)

## Practical Application

**Before session:**
1. Review SIML terms in target element(s)
2. Identify which stage each term serves best
3. Prepare 2-3 questions per element for that stage

**During session:**
1. Sense which stage you're in
2. Use stage-appropriate elemental questions
3. Follow vector to next element
4. Check for ε (uncertainty preserved)

**After session:**
1. Map actual path taken through elements
2. Note where vectors were followed vs. where capture occurred
3. File insight with stage/element metadata
