# Insight — Mood Swings: Signal of Upstream Instability (S1CF)

## Overview
This entry encodes the ecological diagnosis of mood swings within the ANEVity framework. Mood swings are almost never the origin of instability; they are downstream signals that can masquerade as upstream causes. They indicate regulatory instability where the system cannot carry experience smoothly across time, appearing when temporal-affective flow becomes erratic, distinction oscillates too fast, binding cannot stabilize, and uncertainty spikes or collapses unpredictably.

Upstream causes typically include metabolic volatility (sleep, glucose, hormones), boundary erosion (chronic stress, caregiving), signal interference (substances, medications), structural injury (TBI, endocrine disruption), and contextual compression (life transitions, sustained uncertainty). These are field-level pressures, not personality traits.

Mood swings become dangerous when they start doing coordination work they are not built for: when emotional state is treated as truth, intensity substitutes for timing, relief substitutes for resolution, and polarity substitutes for orientation. The diagnostic rule: are mood shifts changing what can be seen, chosen, or revised? Mislabeling them as "the problem" almost always guarantees the real problem stays hidden.

## Key Tensions
1. **Origin vs Signal** — Mood swings as cause versus downstream marker of upstream failure.
2. **Weather vs Terrain** — Passing conditions versus permanent landscape.
3. **Symptom vs Driver** — Informational versus actively distorting perception and choice.
4. **Identity vs Signal** — Mood as who you are versus mood as what the system is experiencing.
