# Insight — Follow Your Bliss (S0C7)

## Overview

Joseph Campbell's famous injunction to "follow your bliss" is often trivialized as motivational optimism, but its roots run far deeper. Campbell grounded the idea in the Sanskrit concepts of sat (being), chit (consciousness), and ananda (rapture or bliss). He observed that while he was unsure whether his life fully expressed being or consciousness, he could recognize moments of rapture — and those moments became his compass. Bliss, in this framework, is not pleasure or happiness but a signal from the deepest self indicating alignment with one's authentic path.

The practical dimension of Campbell's philosophy was forged through decades of teaching at Sarah Lawrence College, where he held one-on-one student conferences rather than delivering lectures. This pedagogical method — deeply personal, attentive to each student's unique trajectory — embodied the principle that bliss cannot be taught in the abstract but must be discovered through individual encounter. Campbell's observation that "hidden hands" open doors for those who follow their bliss was not mysticism but a phenomenological report: when one commits fully to a path of genuine resonance, previously invisible opportunities and support structures become apparent.

The deeper insight connects individual bliss to universal mythological patterns. The hero's journey is not merely a narrative structure but a map of the bliss-following process: departure from the mundane, descent into uncertainty, and return with renewed alignment. Each person's bliss connects them to the archetypal patterns that all mythological traditions describe, making the personal journey simultaneously universal.

- **Arboriel (Wood/β)**: Traces the growth from mundane existence to mythic alignment, where commitment to bliss opens doors through Campbell's "hidden hands."
- **Humavita (Earth/δγ)**: The cycling between following and finding as the bliss compass recalibrates through life stages, requiring continual re-discovery and re-commitment.
- **Ferrosid (Metal/μ)**: The structural frameworks of the Sanskrit sat-chit-ananda triad and Campbell's pedagogical method at Sarah Lawrence as scaffolding for bliss navigation.

## Key Tensions

1. **Uncertainty vs. rapture** — Existential anxiety vs. the clarity of bliss as compass
2. **Individual path vs. universal wisdom** — Personal uniqueness vs. archetypal mythological patterns
3. **Intellectual knowledge vs. felt bliss** — Knowing about meaning vs. experiencing ananda in the body
4. **Mundane existence vs. mythic alignment** — Convention-bound life vs. vocation as hero's journey
5. **Following vs. finding** — Active pursuit of bliss vs. receptivity to its unexpected appearances
