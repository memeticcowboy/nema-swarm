## Context Note

Sleep Paralysis is a REM sleep phenomenon where individuals experience temporary motor atonia (muscle paralysis) while maintaining conscious awareness, often accompanied by vivid hallucinations. During normal REM sleep, the brain inhibits voluntary muscle movement to prevent acting out dreams; sleep paralysis occurs when this atonia persists into wakefulness.

### Common Hallucination Types
- **Sense of presence:** Feeling watched or not alone
- **Visual figures:** Shadow beings, entities, intruders
- **Auditory sensations:** Voices, footsteps, breathing
- **Tactile experiences:** Pressure on chest, being touched, held down
- **Vestibular sensations:** Floating, falling, out-of-body experiences

### Cultural Interpretation Shapes Narrative
| Culture | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|
| Western modern | Alien abduction |
| Historical European | Demonic visitation (incubus/succubus) |
| East Asian | Ghost oppression |
| Some indigenous | Spiritual visitation or test |

### Prevalence and Risk Factors
- **General population:** ~8% lifetime prevalence
- **Higher rates:** Narcolepsy, anxiety disorders, PTSD
- **Triggers:** Sleep deprivation, supine position, stress, irregular sleep schedules, abrupt awakenings

The phenomenon is physiologically benign but often terrifying; education about mechanisms reduces distress and prevents misinterpretation as supernatural or pathological.
