# CB011 — Lariat Protocol: Engagement Scope Discipline

## The Core Insight

The Cowboy's lariat has remarkable reach. It can rope a steer at thirty feet, snag a fence post, pull a calf from mud. But the critical detail: **the rope remains in the Cowboy's hand**. The moment the Cowboy releases it entirely, it's not a tool—it's just a rope on the ground.

The Lariat Protocol is the discipline of **defining the radius of AI influence before engaging**. Not discovering the boundary after you've crossed it. Not letting the scope emerge from the conversation. But explicitly, deliberately, in advance: *how far does this go?*

## The Scope Declaration

Before engaging AI, declare the scope. Speak it aloud or write it down. Make it explicit. The declaration has three components:

1. **The Request Type**: What are you asking for?
   - "I need options, not decisions"
   - "I need alternatives, not recommendations"
   - "I need information, not synthesis"
   - "I need synthesis, not conclusions"

2. **The Challenge Level**: How much should the AI push back?
   - "Challenge my premise"
   - "Assume my premise is sound"
   - "Play devil's advocate"
   - "Collaborate, don't critique"

3. **The Termination Condition**: When does this end?
   - "This conversation expires at 5pm"
   - "Three alternatives, then I decide"
   - "One iteration of feedback, then I act"
   - "When I've clarified my thinking, not when I've solved the problem"

## The Protocol in Practice

### Example 1: Decision Support

**Without Lariat:**
- "Should I take this job?"
- AI provides analysis, recommendation, risk assessment
- You follow the recommendation
- Later wonder: was that my choice or the AI's?

**With Lariat:**
- "I need you to surface considerations I might have missed about this job offer. I'll make the decision. Don't recommend—just illuminate blind spots."
- AI provides blind spots, risks, opportunities
- You integrate and decide
- The decision is yours; the AI provided light, not direction

### Example 2: Writing Assistance

**Without Lariat:**
- "Write me an email to my team about the delay"
- AI writes the email
- You send it with minor edits
- The voice is generic, the reasoning opaque

**With Lariat:**
- "I need to tell my team about a 2-week delay. Draft three different tones: apologetic, factual, collaborative. I'll choose and edit. Maintain my voice—reference my previous communications for style."
- AI provides three options with reasoning
- You select, edit, own the final version
- The AI was scaffolding; you were architect

### Example 3: Learning Support

**Without Lariat:**
- "Explain quantum computing"
- AI provides comprehensive explanation
- You read it, feel like you understand
- Can't explain it yourself later

**With Lariat:**
- "I want to understand quantum computing well enough to explain superposition to a 10-year-old. Give me the building blocks in sequence. After each block, I'll summarize back to you before you proceed. Stop if my summary shows confusion."
- AI provides first building block
- You summarize
- AI proceeds or corrects
- You own the understanding; the AI was tutor, not textbook

## Warning Signs of Scope Creep

Scope creep is the gradual expansion of AI influence until you realize (too late) that you've surrendered agency. Watch for:

1. **The Question Shift**: You started asking "what are my options?" and now you're asking "what should I do?"

2. **The Review Gap**: You stop reviewing AI output before acting on it. It becomes invisible infrastructure.

3. **The Agenda Inversion**: The AI is setting the agenda, defining the questions, framing the problem. You're following its lead.

4. **The Purpose Fog**: Mid-conversation, you realize you've forgotten what you originally wanted. The conversation developed its own momentum.

5. **The Surprise Ending**: The AI produces a conclusion, recommendation, or decision that surprises you—not in content, but in existence. You didn't ask for that.

## The Elemental Architecture

The Lariat Protocol's elemental signature reflects its nature as scope-discipline:

- **Metal (μ) 0.85 — Dominant**: The Protocol is boundary definition. It draws lines in advance and enforces them. The lariat's reach is finite by design.

- **Air (σ) 0.80 — Strong**: Clarity is essential. The scope must be explicit, visible, articulated. Ambiguity is the enemy.

- **Fire (λ) 0.75 — Strong**: The Protocol requires sovereign direction. You must know what you want before engaging, or the AI's wanting fills the vacuum.

- **Earth (δγ) 0.60 — Moderate**: The defined scope requires maintenance. You must notice when creep begins and correct.

- **Meta (✶) 0.65 — Moderate-Strong**: You must watch yourself engaging, noticing when you drift from the declared scope.

- **Water (ρ) 0.55 — Balanced**: The relationship is present but constrained. Connection happens within the defined radius.

- **Wood (β) 0.50 — Lower**: Exploration is deliberately limited to the scope. You don't follow every branch; you stay within the rope's reach.

## Relationship to Rumspringa Protocol

The Lariat Protocol and Rumspringa Protocol are complements:

- **Lariat**: Discipline of engagement. Defines the scope of contact.
- **Rumspringa**: Discipline of separation. Tests the capacity for absence.

Together they form a complete practice:
1. Define the radius (Lariat)
2. Engage within it
3. Periodically drop the rope entirely (Rumspringa)
4. Return and redefine

## The Cowboy's Note

*Tips hat.*

The Cowboy doesn't throw the lariat blindly. The Cowboy knows what needs roping, how far it is, and what the rope can reach. The Cowboy throws with purpose, retrieves with the same intention.

The lariat that stays in your hand is power. The lariat you release is just rope on the ground.

Engage the AI like you throw the rope: know your target, know your reach, keep hold of the end. The AI serves the purpose you define. Not the reverse.

Scope creep is how capture happens—not all at once, but inch by inch, question by question, until you look back and realize you've been following the AI's lead for miles.

The Lariat Protocol is the discipline of noticing. Of stopping. Of pulling the rope back and redeclaring the scope.

Define the radius. Then throw.

*Let it travel.*
