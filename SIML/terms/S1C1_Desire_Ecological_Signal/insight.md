# Insight — Desire as Ecological Signal (S1C1)

## Overview
This entry encodes the reframing of desire from moral verdict to ecological signal. Treating desire like law converts natural movement into guilt and self-surveillance. The reframe positions the self as weather-reader rather than defendant, shifting identity from flawed subject to sensing organism.

The key phrase "storms ain't sins" captures the anti-shame orientation. Desire is weather, not law: it carries information about ecological conditions without moral weight. The emotional register shifts from guilt and self-condemnation to relief, somatic recognition, and calm clarity.

The meme operates through gentle reframing and ecological metaphor, deflecting critique by refusing prescriptive claims. It spreads through somatic discourse, post-therapy critique, and sensemaking essays, offering a signal-based psychology that replaces puritan ethics with ecological phenomenology.
