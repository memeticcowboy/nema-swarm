# Insight — Fascism Characteristics Six Cyborgs (S0EE)

## Overview

Dr. Lawrence Britt's 14 characteristics of fascism — from powerful nationalism and disdain for human rights to fraudulent elections and rampant cronyism — provide a diagnostic checklist for authoritarian systems. This entry applies the Six Cyborgs cognitive framework to that checklist, revealing that fascism is not a collection of independent traits but a systemic architecture of interlocking control mechanisms operating across psychological, social, institutional, and economic dimensions simultaneously.

The Analyzer cyborg (Air) performs the primary taxonomic work, organizing the 14 characteristics into four clusters: epistemic control (media manipulation, intellectual suppression), sociopolitical engineering (nationalism, scapegoating, controlled elections), power centralization (military supremacy, corporate-state fusion, cronyism), and affective conditioning (fear, loyalty, manufactured consent). The Strategist cyborg (Fire) applies systems thinking and scenario planning to trace how these clusters reinforce each other, creating feedback loops that make fascist systems resilient to piecemeal resistance.

The remaining cyborg modes add critical dimensions: the Ethical cyborg names the moral violations, the Protective cyborg identifies threats to vulnerable populations, the Empathic cyborg registers the human cost of systematic dehumanization, and the Creative cyborg imagines counter-architectures and resistance strategies. The sixfold analysis reveals that fascism achieves its power through saturation — no single characteristic is sufficient, but their simultaneous operation creates a totalizing control system that subsumes every axis of social life.
