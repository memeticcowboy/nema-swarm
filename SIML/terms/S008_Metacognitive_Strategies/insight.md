# Metacognitive Strategies
## SIML Term Entry S008
### Source: 1771907212356_235901_Mastering+MetaCognitive+Strategies.md

## Overview

This framework presents ten metacognitive strategy domains for enhancing thinking, learning, and collaboration. Unlike simple study techniques, these strategies operate at the meta-level—they are strategies *about* how to think, not just what to think about. They range from individual cognitive practices (Complementary Thinking, Conceptual Clarity) to interpersonal competencies (Collaborative Teamwork, Caring Empathy) to dispositional qualities (Courageous Action, Continuous Learning).

The ten domains are: Complementary Thinking, Connected Seeing, Collaborative Teamwork, Constructing Meaning, Conceptual Clarity, Communicating Effectively, Courageous Action, Caring Empathy, Conversational Reflection, and Continuous Learning. Each has three sub-strategies creating a 30-strategy matrix. The framework integrates intellect, emotion, and imagination rather than treating cognition as purely rational.

## Key Tensions

- **Thinking ↔ Awareness**: Cognition vs. metacognition
- **Explicit ↔ Tacit**: Articulated knowledge vs. embodied knowing
- **Courage ↔ Commitment**: Risk-taking vs. sustained dedication
- **Collaboration ↔ Dialogue**: Working together vs. communicating together
- **Believing ↔ Doubting**: Openness to new ideas vs. critical evaluation
