## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does expertise develop in complex, ill-structured domains where simple rules fail?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Cognitive flexibility emerges from **exploratory multiplicity** — the capacity to navigate conceptual landscapes through multiple pathways, encountering diverse cases that challenge rigid schemas, and developing the ability to reassemble knowledge components in context-sensitive ways. Unlike well-structured domains where expertise means knowing more rules, ill-structured domains require knowing more ways of seeing, more connections between concepts, and more adaptive responses to novelty.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Cognitive Flexibility Theory demonstrates that expertise in complex domains develops through criss-crossing conceptual landscapes via multiple representations and diverse cases, enabling learners to assemble knowledge flexibly in response to novel, ill-structured situations.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A theory of advanced knowledge acquisition emphasizing that complex, ill-structured domains require multiple representations, diverse cases, and non-linear traversal of conceptual material to develop flexible, adaptive expertise
- **How it works:** Knowledge decomposition → Multiple representation construction → Case diversity exposure → Criss-crossing the landscape → Schema assembly → Context-sensitive application, with learners revisiting concepts from different angles rather than following linear sequences
- **Where it aims:** Toward cognitive flexibility — the capacity to restructure knowledge spontaneously in response to novel situations, assembling appropriate schemas from component knowledge structures in context-sensitive ways
