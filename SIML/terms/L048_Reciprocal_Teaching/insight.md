## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does taking on the teacher role transform the learner's relationship to comprehension?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Reciprocal Teaching works not merely by providing practice with comprehension strategies but by **repositioning the learner** — shifting from passive recipient to active meaning-maker, from student to teacher. This positional shift creates a water/ρ resonance field where understanding must be articulated, questioned, clarified, and defended — making comprehension visible and therefore improvable.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Reciprocal Teaching reveals that comprehension develops through the water/ρ space of collaborative dialogue, where the gradual transfer of the teacher role creates a crucible for internalizing expert reading practices — demonstrating that the path to independent expertise runs through structured interdependence.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** An instructional approach where teachers and students engage in collaborative dialogues using four metacognitive strategies (summarizing, questioning, clarifying, predicting), with students gradually taking leadership roles
- **How it works:** Structured social interaction creates conditions for comprehension strategy practice; the positional shift from student to teacher activates deeper processing; repeated cycles build internalized competence
- **Where it aims:** Toward the development of strategic, self-regulated readers who can monitor and repair their own comprehension through internalized dialogue
