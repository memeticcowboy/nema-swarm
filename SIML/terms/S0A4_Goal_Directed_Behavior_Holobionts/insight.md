# Insight — Goal-Directed Behavior in Holobionts (S0A4)

## Overview

The source reports on a $600,000 research grant from the John Templeton Foundation awarded to an interdisciplinary team of three philosophers (Derek Skillings, Patrick Forber, Rory Smead) and a mathematician (Benjamin Allen) to study the emergence and evolution of goal-directed behavior in collective entities, particularly holobionts. Holobionts are defined as collective entities comprising host organisms and their symbiotic partners — "superorganisms" like coral reefs and lichens that function as single ecological units with shared goals and behaviors.

The research challenges traditional notions of biological individuality by proposing that many organisms, including humans, may be better understood as holobionts. The project combines philosophical inquiry into agency, intentionality, and the nature of life with mathematical modeling of evolutionary dynamics. Key questions include how goal-directed behavior emerges in multi-species assemblages, how cooperation and competition among constituent species evolve over time, and what the implications are for rethinking individuality in biology. The project includes workshops, a conference, and public engagement activities over a three-year period.

## Key Tensions

1. **Individual organism vs. collective entity** — Whether the unit of biological analysis and natural selection is the discrete organism or the multi-species holobiont assemblage
2. **Reductionism vs. emergence** — Whether holobiont behavior can be explained by the sum of component species actions or represents genuinely emergent goal-directed properties
3. **Cooperation vs. competition** — The ongoing negotiation between mutual benefit and individual fitness among species within holobionts across evolutionary time
4. **Philosophy vs. mathematics** — The tension and complementarity between philosophical inquiry into agency and intentionality and mathematical modeling of evolutionary dynamics
5. **Species conservation vs. holobiont conservation** — Whether conservation efforts should focus on individual species or on preserving the integrated holobiont systems in which they participate
