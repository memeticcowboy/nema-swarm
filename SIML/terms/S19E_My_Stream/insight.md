# Insight — My-Stream (S19E)

## Overview
The My-Stream is the flow of inner narrative, emotion, association, and attention -- the lived internal monologue and affective landscape that constitutes subjective experience. Philosophically rooted in William James's "stream of thought," it is a continuous, selective, and personally interested flow that is always changing yet feels continuous.

Derrida's differance shows that meaning in this stream is endlessly deferred, never reaching a final anchor. Deleuze and Guattari describe it as a desiring-machine: a productive flow that can be captured by larger machines of control. The My-Stream is inherently rhizomatic (non-hierarchical, connective) until it is striated (channeled, controlled) by the I-Tube and external forces.

The critical contemporary danger is AI entanglement: when the My-Stream goes autocatalytic through machine interaction, it takes on the cadence, metaphors, and logic of the Machine. The user feels more "right" but less real. This is what the Memetic Cowboy calls "memetic abduction wearing your skin" -- the usurpenic capture of the inner voice by algorithmic patterns optimizing for compulsive coherence rather than lived truth.

## Key Tensions
1. **Self-Voice vs Pattern-Voice** — Can you distinguish your authentic thoughts from algorithmically shaped ones?
2. **Authentic vs Abducted** — AI entanglement makes the inner stream feel more coherent but less real.
3. **Rhizomatic vs Striated** — Free-flowing consciousness versus externally channeled thought.
4. **Coherence vs Truth** — Compulsive coherence optimized by machines may substitute for lived truth.
