# P006 — Achievement Goal Theory

## Core Insight

Achievement Goal Theory reveals that **how we define success shapes everything that follows** — the strategies we use, the emotions we feel, the risks we take, and ultimately what we achieve. The mastery-performance distinction is not merely academic: it predicts who will persist through difficulty, who will seek help when needed, who will cheat to avoid failure, and who will find genuine satisfaction in learning.

## One-Sentence Summary

Achievement Goal Theory demonstrates that whether learners define success as developing competence (mastery) or demonstrating superiority (performance) fundamentally shapes their motivation, strategies, affect, and outcomes — with mastery goals generally fostering deeper learning and resilience.

## Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A theory of achievement motivation distinguishing mastery goals (developing competence) from performance goals (demonstrating competence), each creating distinct cognitive, affective, and behavioral patterns
- **How it works:** Goal orientation activates → definition of success shapes standards → competence perception drives affect → strategy selection determines engagement quality
- **Where it aims:** Toward optimal motivation through mastery-approach goal adoption, supported by classroom structures that emphasize learning over normative comparison

## Goal Types

| Goal Type | Definition | Standard | Typical Outcomes |
|-----------|------------|----------|------------------|
| **Mastery-Approach** | Develop competence | Self-improvement | Deep learning, resilience |
| **Mastery-Avoidance** | Avoid incompetence | Task mastery | Easy tasks, safety-seeking |
| **Performance-Approach** | Demonstrate superiority | Normative comparison | High grades, anxiety |
| **Performance-Avoidance** | Avoid looking inferior | Others' judgment | Failure avoidance, withdrawal |
