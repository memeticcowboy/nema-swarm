## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does a crisis of meaning become an opportunity for transformative learning?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Transformative learning works by **frame-breaking and frame-reforming** — when experience cannot be interpreted within existing meaning perspectives, a disorienting dilemma occurs. Critical reflection on the premises (not just content) of these perspectives, tested through rational discourse, enables a fundamental shift to more inclusive, discriminating, and integrative meaning-making.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Mezirow reveals that the deepest learning occurs when a disorienting dilemma triggers critical examination of our meaning-making assumptions, leading to perspective transformation and more autonomous, authentic engagement with life.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A theory describing how meaning perspectives — the broad assumptions through which we interpret experience — can be transformed through critical reflection on premises
- **How it works:** Disorienting dilemma → critical reflection → rational discourse → perspective transformation → action; changes the *frame* of interpretation, not just its content
- **Where it aims:** Toward emancipatory learning — liberation from forces that limit awareness and choice, enabling more autonomous meaning-making
