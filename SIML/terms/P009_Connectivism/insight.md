## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does network structure enable learning in an age of information abundance?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Connectivism emerges from **distributed cognition** — when knowledge is too vast for any individual to contain, learning shifts from internal storage to external navigation. The network becomes the knowledge repository, and the learner's competency is wayfinding: the ability to form connections, recognize patterns, and traverse the information landscape effectively. This is not a diminishment of individual learning but an expansion — the individual becomes part of a larger cognitive system.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Connectivism posits that learning is the formation of connections in a network — where knowledge exists distributed across nodes and relationships, and the core competency is wayfinding through an ever-expanding information landscape.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A learning theory for the digital age where knowledge is distributed across networks of people, resources, and technology — learning is the capacity to construct, navigate, and traverse these connections
- **How it works:** Node formation → connection establishment → pattern recognition → wayfinding → network maintenance → emergent knowledge; the learner builds a personal learning network and develops wayfinding competencies
- **Where it aims:** Toward networked literacy and distributed cognition — where individuals can effectively navigate information abundance, recognize patterns across diverse sources, and contribute to collective intelligence
