## 8. Core Insight

> Concept Mapping transforms the invisible architecture of knowledge into visible, manipulable structures. The hierarchy is not arbitrary — it mirrors how the mind actually organizes information, with inclusive concepts serving as retrieval anchors for more specific knowledge. But the true power lies in the cross-links: these integrative connections, which require the mapper to see relationships across different branches of the hierarchy, are where deep understanding is revealed. A concept map without cross-links is merely a taxonomy; with cross-links, it becomes a theory.

## 10. Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A visual knowledge representation tool using hierarchical node-link diagrams where concepts are connected by labeled relationships, developed by Novak and Gowin (1984) based on Ausubel's Subsumption Theory, making abstract cognitive structures concrete and examinable
- **How it works:** Learners identify concepts, arrange them hierarchically with most general at top, connect with relationship-defining phrases, and add cross-links between branches — the process of constructing the map is itself a learning activity that reveals and shapes understanding
- **Where it aims:** Toward meaningful learning through knowledge externalization — by making thinking visible, concept maps enable diagnosis of misconceptions, identification of knowledge gaps, integration of new learning with prior knowledge, and development of increasingly sophisticated mental models
