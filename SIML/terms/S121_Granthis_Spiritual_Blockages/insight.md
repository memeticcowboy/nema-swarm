# Insight — Granthis Spiritual Blockages (S121)

## Overview

The three granthis (literally "knots") are pranic blockages in the Sushumna nadi — the central energy channel of yogic anatomy — that hinder the ascent of Kundalini shakti from base to crown. Each granthi is named after a deity whose domain metaphorically corresponds to the realm of attachment the knot represents.

**Brahma Granthi** sits at the base and sacral chakras (Muladhara and Svadhisthana), governing survival instinct and sexual energy. It roots consciousness in material existence — the fear of death, the drive to reproduce, the body's most primal imperatives. This is the first knot to address because it represents the deepest, most ancient layer of attachment.

**Vishnu Granthi** occupies the heart and solar plexus chakras (Anahata and Manipura), governing emotional attachment and relational bonds. It binds consciousness to others — love that clings, meaning that depends on external validation, identity constructed through relationship. This is often the most humanly challenging granthi because its domain is felt as the most valuable.

**Rudra/Shiva Granthi** guards the third eye and crown chakras (Ajna and Sahasrara), governing ego-identification itself. It represents the final threshold: the attachment to being a separate self, the identification with individual consciousness as distinct from universal awareness. This is the subtlest and most paradoxical knot because the very entity trying to untie it is what the knot binds.

## Key Tensions

- **Blockage vs. flow**: The granthis are simultaneously obstacles and protective structures. They prevent kundalini ascent but also prevent premature access to states the practitioner is not prepared to integrate. The knot is both the problem and the safeguard.
- **Material attachment vs. spiritual liberation**: The fundamental axis of the system. Each granthi represents a form of attachment — to survival, to relationship, to selfhood — that must be dissolved for liberation but that also serves essential functions in ordinary human existence.
- **Sequential order vs. forced awakening**: The tradition insists on bottom-up progression: survival attachments before emotional attachments before ego attachments. Attempting to bypass lower granthis through intense practice targeting higher centers risks destabilization — spiritual emergence without adequate psychological foundation.
- **Metaphorical deity vs. literal energy**: The naming after Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva is both metaphorical (representing realms of existence) and, within the yogic framework, experientially precise. The tension between metaphorical reading and literal energetic interpretation remains productive rather than resolvable.
