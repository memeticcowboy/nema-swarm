| Condition | Outcome |

## 5. The Embodiment Principle

**Principle:** People learn more deeply when agents display human-like gesturing, movement, eye contact, facial expressions.

**Why IVR > Video:** Higher **presence** (σ) makes embodiment (μ) more believable, enabling generative methods (λ).

**Elemental chain:** σ (presence) → μ (embodiment) → λ (generative method)

## 6. Core Insight

> The media-method interaction reveals that IVR is not inherently superior — it becomes superior when instructional methods are designed to exploit its unique affordances of presence and agency. The medium is not the message; the medium-method synergy is the message.

## 7. One-Sentence Insight

> CAMIL's key finding is that IVR learning depends not on the medium itself but on the interaction between medium affordances (presence, agency) and instructional methods (generative strategies) — method enables medium.
