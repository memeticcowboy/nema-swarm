## Laws of Form

Spencer-Brown's radical claim:
- "Draw a distinction."
- "Call it the first distinction."
- The space is severed.
- Inside and outside emerge.
- All else follows.

The distinction is **not** drawn in space. Space is drawn by the distinction.

This is not metaphor. This is **arithmetic**.

## The Epistemological Priority

We do not perceive a world of differences and then distinguish them.

We **make distinctions** and perceive a world of differences.

The distinction is prior. The difference is consequence.

## Information as Distinction

Bateson: "Information is a difference that makes a difference."

Three requirements:
1. **Difference exists** (ontological)
2. **Distinction is made** (epistemological)
3. **Difference makes difference** (consequential)

Information is not difference. Information is **distinction with impact**.

## The Cost of Clarity

Every distinction:
- Creates clarity (this vs. that)
- Excludes (what is neither this nor that)
- Enables action (based on distinction)
- Limits perception (cannot see across the cut)
- Becomes invisible (the cut is forgotten)

The distinction that is most powerful is the distinction that is **not seen as distinction**.

## Pathologies of Distinction

- **Reification:** The distinction mistaken for reality
- **Exclusion:** What is cut off is forgotten
- **Rigidity:** The distinction that cannot be revised
- **Violence:** The cut that wounds
- **Obsession:** Endless distinction-making (analysis paralysis)

## The Recovery

- **Meta-distinction:** Distinguishing the distinctions
- **Permeability:** Flow across the cut
- **Multiplicity:** Many distinctions, none absolute
- **Integration:** Holding the cut and the continuity
