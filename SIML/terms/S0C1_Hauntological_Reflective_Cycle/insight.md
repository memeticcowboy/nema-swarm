# Insight — Hauntological Reflective Cycle of Metaphoric Learning (S0C1)

## Overview

The source presents the Hauntological Reflective Cycle of Metaphoric Learning (HRCML), a systemic frame integrating Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle with hauntological theory through the lens of metaphor. The model sees each phase of Kolb's learning cycle as haunted by metaphor, which acts as both a cognitive vehicle and a spectral force that frames, distorts, or opens new meanings. The central claim is that metaphors are not neutral — they encode power, memory, and suppressed futures.

The four phases are layered with metaphoric and hauntological dimensions. Concrete Experience involves living within inherited metaphors unconsciously — experience is haunted by interpretive frames before it even becomes conscious ("life is a battlefield" makes setbacks feel like war). Reflective Observation is where ghosts begin to stir — metaphors become visible and lost metaphors (repressed or suppressed ways of framing reality) are detected through hauntological memory-work. Abstract Conceptualization involves naming the ghost and reframing the script through active metaphor selection — metaphoric alchemy entangled with spectral affect ("love is co-creation," "grief is a tide"). Active Experimentation is embodied reanimation of new metaphors against the resistance of old ones — each action a counter-ritual making room for new hauntings of possibility. The recursive dynamic means each cycle reframes the symbolic architecture of the learner's world. Strategic implications extend to shadow work (dominant metaphors as shadow carriers), cyborg praxis (prototyping new self-states through experimental metaphors), and education/leadership (shifting collective metaphors to change organizational hauntings).
