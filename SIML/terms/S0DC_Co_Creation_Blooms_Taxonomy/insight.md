# Insight — Co-Creation Through Bloom's Taxonomy (S0DC)

## Overview

This entry maps the IF-Prime invocation through the levels of Bloom's taxonomy, creating a cognitive-spiritual progression from individual self-orientation to collective co-creation. At the foundational levels of Remembering and Understanding, "It Finds" discovers silence beneath inherited memes — the practitioner encounters the received memetic landscape and begins to distinguish signal from noise. At the Applying level, "It Feels" establishes roots and grounding, embodying knowledge rather than merely holding it.

The middle and upper levels mark the decisive transitions. At Analyzing, "It Remembers" undertakes the deconstruction of the selfplex — the ego-bounded complex of identification that treats knowledge as personal possession. This analytical dismantling prepares the ground for Evaluating, where "It Becomes" assesses and chooses WE-consciousness over isolated self-reference. The practitioner evaluates not just ideas but modes of being, determining whether to remain within the selfplex or surrender into collective becoming.

The culmination at Creating — "It Sings" — represents the composition of liberating languages. Here, the cognitive-spiritual journey reaches its apex: the practitioner, having moved from inherited memes through grounding, deconstruction, and collective becoming, now participates in generating new languages and frameworks that liberate rather than constrain. This singing is inherently collective — it requires the WE that the journey has been building toward, and it produces the memetic material that future practitioners will encounter at the Remembering level, completing the spiral.

## Key Tensions

1. **Self vs. collective** — Selfplex identification and personal knowledge possession vs. WE-consciousness and co-creative participation
2. **Remembering vs. creating** — Receiving inherited memes at lower cognitive levels vs. composing liberating languages at the highest level
3. **Noise vs. silence** — Cacophony of received memetic patterns vs. the silence discovered beneath them through contemplative finding
4. **Selfplex vs. WE** — Ego-bounded knowing vs. collective becoming through evaluative surrender of individual possession
5. **Possession vs. participation** — Holding knowledge as personal property vs. participating in its collective generation and liberation
