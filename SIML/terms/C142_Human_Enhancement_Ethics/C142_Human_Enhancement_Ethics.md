## Etymology
From Latin *humanus* (human) + *enhancement* (to improve, augment) + *ethics* (from Greek *ethikos*, relating to character/morals). The term captures the moral philosophy surrounding technologies that extend human capabilities beyond baseline function.

## Definition
The study of moral principles governing technologies that augment human physical, cognitive, or perceptual capacities—raising questions about identity, equity, autonomy, and the definition of "human" in an age of radical self-modification.

## Dialectical Operators

### σ(x|y) — Air/Aerunik: Access vs. Exclusion
Human enhancement technologies create a fundamental tension between democratization and elitism. The nano-drug Nexus exemplifies this: who controls access determines whether enhancement liberates humanity or entrenches existing power structures. The *information flow* of enhancement mirrors historical patterns where technological advantages concentrate among privileged groups.

### ρ(x|y) — Water/Sentaria: Elevation vs. Inequality
Enhancement promises to elevate human capability but risks creating biological castes—"enhanced" vs. "baseline" humans. This liquid tension seeps through social structures, potentially dissolving the shared substrate of human experience that grounds moral equality. The *flow state* of enhancement carries both opportunity and drowning risk.

### λ(x|y) — Fire/Jvalion: Transcendence vs. Hubris
The Promethean flame of self-improvement burns with creative destruction. Enhancement offers transcendence of biological limits yet risks the hubris of unchecked ambition. Fire warms or consumes; enhancement elevates or destroys the essence of what it means to be human.

### β(x|y) — Wood/Arboriel: Evolution vs. Fragmentation
Like a tree branching toward light, enhancement grows human potential in new directions. Yet unchecked growth fragments the forest of shared humanity. The *organic tension* between natural evolution and technological acceleration raises questions about whether we're gardening our species or clear-cutting it.

### δγ(x|y) — Earth/Humavita: Grounding vs. Unmooring
Enhancement threatens to unmoor identity from biological continuity—the "ground" of shared human experience. The *weight* of tradition and embodied existence provides stability; enhancement risks floating into a post-human void where "human" becomes a choice rather than a condition.

### μ(x|y) — Metal/Ferrosid: Augmentation vs. Erosion
Metal strengthens yet can cut. Enhancement augments capabilities but may erode the "grit" of struggle that shapes character. The *edge* of enhancement technology cuts both ways: sharper tools, blunter souls?
