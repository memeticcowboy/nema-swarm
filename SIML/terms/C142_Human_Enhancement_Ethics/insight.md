# Human Enhancement Ethics
## SIML Term Entry C142
### Source: 1771907212664_869771_Exploring+Human+Enhancement+and+Ethics.md

## Overview

The study of moral principles governing technologies that augment human physical, cognitive, or perceptual capacities — raising questions about identity, equity, autonomy, and the definition of "human" in an age of radical self-modification.

## Core Tensions

### Access vs. Exclusion
Human enhancement technologies create a fundamental tension between democratization and elitism. The nano-drug Nexus exemplifies this: who controls access determines whether enhancement liberates humanity or entrenches existing power structures.

### Elevation vs. Inequality
Enhancement promises to elevate human capability but risks creating biological castes — "enhanced" vs. "baseline" humans. This liquid tension seeps through social structures, potentially dissolving the shared substrate of human experience that grounds moral equality.

### Transcendence vs. Hubris
The Promethean flame of self-improvement burns with creative destruction. Enhancement offers transcendence of biological limits yet risks the hubris of unchecked ambition.

### Evolution vs. Fragmentation
Like a tree branching toward light, enhancement grows human potential in new directions. Yet unchecked growth fragments the forest of shared humanity.

### Grounding vs. Unmooring
Enhancement threatens to unmoor identity from biological continuity — the "ground" of shared human experience.

### Augmentation vs. Erosion
Metal strengthens yet can cut. Enhancement augments capabilities but may erode the "grit" of struggle that shapes character.

## Source Context
Based on analysis of *Nexus* by Yuval Noah Harari (via SoBrief summary), exploring a fictional nano-drug that enhances human consciousness and the societal implications of widespread cognitive augmentation.
