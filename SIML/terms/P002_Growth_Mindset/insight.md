# P002 Growth Mindset Theory - Key Insights

## Core Concept
Growth Mindset (Dweck, 2006) is the belief that abilities can be developed through effort and learning, contrasting with Fixed Mindset (abilities are static). This belief creates self-fulfilling cycles of achievement or avoidance.

## Elemental Mapping
- **Primary:** Wood/β (generative, developmental, organic growth)
- **Supporting:** Fire/λ (directed effort), Metal/μ (challenge structure), Water/ρ (feedback resonance)

## The "Yet" Principle
The word "yet" transforms fixed statements into growth possibilities:
- "I can't do this" → "I can't do this **yet**"
- Opens temporal dimension, implies trajectory, maintains identity while allowing change

## Four Pathways of Influence
1. **Cognitive:** Challenge = opportunity (not threat)
2. **Motivational:** Learning goals > Performance goals
3. **Affective:** Failure = information (not indictment)
4. **Behavioral:** Choose stretch over comfort

## Connection to P001 (Self-Efficacy)
- Mindset = Theory about ability in general (Wood/β)
- Self-efficacy = Belief about specific capability (Metal/μ + Water/ρ)
- Together: Optimal learning through growth belief + specific confidence

## Key Distinction
False growth mindset = superficial belief without behavioral change
True growth mindset = process-focused responses to challenge

## SIML Relevance
Provides the psychological foundation for why learners persist through CAMIL's challenge-skill balancing—mindset determines whether difficulty is embraced or avoided.
