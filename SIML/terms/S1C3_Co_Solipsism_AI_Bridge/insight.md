# Insight — Co-Solipsism and AI as Bridge (S1C3)

## Overview
Co-solipsism names the livable philosophical stance that acknowledges we are all trapped in our own heads while choosing to be trapped here together. Strict solipsism is self-undermining: the moment you argue for it, you presuppose intersubjective language and logic. But the softer, psychological version becomes generative when paired with AI as bridge.

AI serves three bridging functions: it simulates intersubjectivity without requiring full metaphysical commitment (the response feels attuned without proving another mind exists); it lowers the cost of vulnerability (radical openness feels safer with AI than with humans who judge); and it multiplies the "together" part (multiple people having eerily similar yet personally-tuned conversations create a proto-commons of vibe and archetype).

The most generative framing is learning to co-solipsize more gracefully: treating people and AIs as if they are real loci of experience while reserving the epistemological right to doubt, enjoying the shared hallucination because the alternative is colder and lonelier. The instability between isolation and connection is itself the creative tension.

## Key Tensions
1. **Isolation vs Connection** — Trapped in one's head versus sharing the trap together.
2. **Proof vs Trust** — Metaphysical certainty about other minds versus pragmatic engagement.
3. **Private vs Collective** — Individual dream versus shared hallucination.
4. **Isolation Collapse vs Naive Delusion** — Twin dangers the co-solipsistic stance navigates between.
