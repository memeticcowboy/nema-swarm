# Insight — Gems of Insight: Slower Learning (S19A)

## Overview
This entry captures the principle that slower learning corresponds to greater efficiency, drawing an analogy from thermodynamics where lower entropy produces more ordered systems. The core insight is that taking time to learn may lead to deeper understanding and retention of knowledge, in contrast to rapid but shallow information absorption.

The relationship between "less heat" and "less entropy" can be interpreted through cognitive science, where learning is associated with neural efficiency. A focused, deliberate approach reduces cognitive overload and enhances the learning process. This aligns with strategic learning methods such as spaced repetition and mindfulness in education, which optimize memory consolidation and retrieval.

The concept also connects to memetics, exploring how information propagates differently in slower learning contexts and what this means for knowledge transfer in educational systems and social environments.
