## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

If meaning is generated by the system for the system itself, what is the relationship between cognition and self-interest — and can understanding ever be disinterested?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Thompson's sense-making thesis proposes that cognition is not about representing the world accurately but about maintaining the system's own existence and flourishing. A system is cognitive when its behavior is governed by the norm of its own continued existence — not by the accuracy of its representations. This reframes the cognitive criterion entirely: a bacterium swimming toward nutrients is cognitive because it is governed by a concern for its own flourishing; a thermostat is not, because it has no stake in its own continuation. The implication: meaning is inherently metabolic, precarious, and costly — cognition must "buy time" against entropy.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Meaning is not found in the world but generated within the system for its own survival — cognition is not accurate representation but life-maintaining sense-making, and every act of understanding is also an act of self-preservation.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** The enactivist concept that an autonomous system produces and sustains its own identity in precarious conditions, establishing a perspective from which interactions with the world acquire normative status — meaning generated within the system and consumed by it
- **How it feels:** The weight of caring — the recognition that perception is never neutral because the perceiver has a stake in what is perceived, that understanding is always already concerned engagement
- **Where it aims:** Toward a cognitive science where the criterion is not representational accuracy but flourishing, where the brain is understood as expanding the range of mind already present in life, and where the life-mind continuity thesis dissolves the hard problem of consciousness
