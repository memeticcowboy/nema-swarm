## Nemetic Insight

The Radical Theory of Consciousness challenges us to embrace the paradox: consciousness is both virtual and causally powerful, both simulated and fundamentally real to the experiencer. Bach's framework suggests that understanding consciousness as a learning algorithm doesn't diminish its significance—it reveals the profound nature of self-organizing systems that construct their own reality.

The path forward requires holding the tension between viewing consciousness as software-like (virtual, constructed) while honoring its lived reality (causal, meaningful). This is the essence of the simulated mind: not a dismissal of consciousness, but a deeper appreciation for how virtual constructs can shape physical existence.
