# L056 — Cognitive Flexibility Theory

## Core Insight

Cognitive Flexibility Theory reveals that expertise in complex domains requires not just knowing more, but knowing **differently** — holding multiple representations, traversing knowledge webs non-linearly, and revising schemas continuously. The goal is not simplified understanding but **flexible understanding**: the capacity to adapt knowledge to the unique demands of each new situation.

## One-Sentence Summary

Cognitive Flexibility Theory demonstrates that advanced learning in ill-structured domains requires criss-crossing conceptual landscapes through multiple cases and representations, building web-like knowledge structures that enable adaptive expertise rather than rigid rule-following.

## Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A theory of advanced knowledge acquisition for ill-structured domains, using multiple representations, case variation, and non-linear "criss-crossing" of conceptual landscapes to build flexible, transferable understanding
- **How it works:** Complex concepts encountered in diverse contexts → schema revision through re-representation → web-like knowledge structures → adaptive application to novel situations
- **Where it aims:** Toward adaptive expertise — the capacity to apply knowledge flexibly across contexts, recognizing that ill-structured domains defy simple rules and require context-sensitive judgment
