## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How do external events affect our natural, intrinsic motivation to engage?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

External events are not neutral interventions—they carry signals about control and competence. When we perceive external events as controlling (threatening our autonomy), our intrinsic motivation withers even as we comply. When we perceive them as informational (affirming our competence while supporting our autonomy), our intrinsic motivation flourishes. The same reward can undermine or enhance motivation depending on how it is experienced.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Cognitive Evaluation Theory demonstrates that external events affect intrinsic motivation through their impact on perceived autonomy and competence—controlling aspects undermine natural engagement while informational aspects that affirm competence without threatening self-determination enhance it.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A theory explaining how external events (rewards, feedback, deadlines, surveillance) affect intrinsic motivation through two functional aspects—controlling (threatens autonomy) and informational (enhances competence)
- **How it works:** External events are cognitively evaluated for their impact on autonomy and competence; perceived control shifts locus of causality external (undermining intrinsic motivation), while competence-affirming information maintains or enhances internal locus and intrinsic interest
- **Where it aims:** Toward autonomy-supportive environments that maintain intrinsic motivation while providing necessary structure—enabling self-determined behavior, internalization of values, and integrated regulation
