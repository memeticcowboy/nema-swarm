## CONTEXT & APPLICATION

### Core Definition
Cognitive Babel describes the fragmentation of sensemaking that occurs when AI-personalized frameworks create incompatible models of understanding among users. Each person's AI-mediated experience generates unique "priority vectors" and "resonance loops" that become increasingly untranslatable to others.

### Key Symbols & Phrases
- **Priority vectors**: The unique weightings of importance that personalized AI systems develop for each user
- **Resonance loops**: Feedback cycles where AI responses reinforce user frameworks, creating self-referential sensemaking
- **Shared syntax**: The aspiration for common language that Babel conditions undermine
- **Epistemic drift**: The gradual divergence of meaning systems over time

### Target Identity Roles
- Knowledge workers navigating AI-mediated collaboration
- Designers creating systems for collective sensemaking
- Strategists planning for organizational communication
- Educators preparing students for fragmented information landscapes
- System architects building translation infrastructure

### Transmission Vectors
- Thought leadership on AI and organizational culture
- Organizational consulting on communication frameworks
- Academic discourse on post-structuralist concerns with meaning
- Online forums discussing AI use and its social implications

### Ideological Frame
Post-structuralist concern with meaning fragmentation meets technoskeptical views of AI hyper-individualization. The phenomenon reflects broader anxieties about whether shared reality is possible in an age of algorithmic personalization.
