## Coordination Dynamics

**High-Absorption + Cultural Narrative Availability → False Memory Risk**

The combination of individual trait (absorption) and environmental factor (cultural narrative templates) creates conditions for false memory formation. This is not a pathology but a predictable cognitive phenomenon—human memory is reconstructive, not reproductive.

**Source Monitoring Failure + Emotional Need → Belief Entrenchment**

When source monitoring fails and the resulting narrative satisfies emotional needs (for meaning, specialness, validation), belief becomes resistant to counter-evidence. The belief is not held despite evidence but because of the coherence and emotional satisfaction it provides.

**Z-State Assessment: :open**

The system remains permeable—false memories can be corrected through source monitoring training, exposure to counter-evidence presented non-confrontationally, and development of metacognitive awareness. However, if the belief becomes central to identity and social belonging, Z may shift toward :sealing.
