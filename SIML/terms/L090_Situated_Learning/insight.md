# L090 Situated Learning — Core Insight

## The Essential Understanding

Situated Learning reveals that **knowledge is not a substance to be transferred but a mode of engagement to be developed**. We do not learn abstract concepts in classrooms and then apply them in the world; we learn by participating in the world, gradually becoming the kind of person who knows. The apprentice tailor does not learn tailoring theory and then practice — learning to tailor and becoming a tailor are inseparable processes.

## Why This Matters

### For Education
- **Authenticity is essential:** Learning requires genuine participation, not simulated exercises
- **Identity matters:** Education should support becoming, not just knowing
- **Community is the curriculum:** The social context shapes what is learned
- **Peripherality is valuable:** Beginners need low-risk entry points to real practice

### For Organizations
- **Knowledge lives in relationships:** Expertise is distributed through communities, not concentrated in individuals
- **Communities of practice are strategic assets:** They are where organizational knowledge develops
- **Newcomers need legitimate access:** Onboarding should grant real participation, not just observation
- **Generational continuity requires investment:** Today's experts must mentor tomorrow's

### For Understanding Human Development
- **We are our practices:** Identity emerges from sustained participation
- **Learning is social:** Even "individual" learning is shaped by social context
- **Context is not noise:** The specific situation is essential to what is known
- **Becoming is ongoing:** We are always in trajectories of participation

## The Core Mechanism

Legitimate Peripheral Participation (LPP) is the engine:

1. **Legitimate** — The learner has real standing in the community, not outsider status (Water/ρ belonging)
2. **Peripheral** — The learner participates at the edges, with limited but genuine tasks (Metal/μ structured entry)
3. **Participation** — Learning happens through doing, not receiving (Water/ρ + Earth/δγ engagement)

Over time, the trajectory moves from periphery to center, from novice to expert, from one identity to another.

## Key Distinction

Situated Learning differs from apprenticeship models:
- **Apprenticeship** often implies one-to-one expert-novice transmission
- **Situated Learning** emphasizes community participation and identity transformation
- **LPP** is not just observation but genuine, if limited, contribution
- The focus is on **becoming a practitioner**, not just acquiring skills

## The Paradox of Continuity

Communities of practice face a fundamental tension:
- They must reproduce themselves across generations
- This requires newcomers to eventually replace old-timers
- Full participation means becoming the people who will be displaced
- The community continues by transforming its members

## In One Line

> We do not learn about the world from outside it; we learn by becoming part of it, gradually moving from the edges of communities toward their centers, transforming who we are in the process.

## Connection to NEMA

Situated Learning maps to NEMA's elemental framework:
- **Water/ρ** — The social flow, participation, belonging, relational resonance
- **Metal/μ** — Community structure, boundaries, legitimate access, roles
- **Earth/δγ** — Identity transformation, generational cycling, practice accumulation
- **Fire/λ** — Joint enterprise, purpose, aspiration toward full participation
- **Air/σ** — Meaning negotiation, parsing complex practice situations

The theory is fundamentally about **relational becoming** — knowledge as a state of engagement between person and community.
