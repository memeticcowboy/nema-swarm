## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How do the explanations we create for our successes and failures shape our future?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Attributions function as **interpretive lenses** that transform objective outcomes into subjective meaning. The same success or failure can trigger vastly different emotional and motivational consequences depending on how it is explained. Mastery-oriented individuals attribute success to internal, stable, controllable causes (building confidence) and failure to internal, unstable, controllable causes (maintaining hope). Helpless-oriented individuals show the reverse pattern, creating self-fulfilling cycles of either growth or stagnation.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Attribution Theory demonstrates that how learners explain their successes and failures—along dimensions of locus, stability, and controllability—shapes their emotions, motivation, and future behavior more profoundly than the outcomes themselves.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A theory examining how individuals explain the causes of events and behaviors, particularly their own successes and failures, along three dimensions: locus (internal/external), stability (stable/unstable), and controllability (controllable/uncontrollable)
- **How it works:** Outcomes trigger causal search → attributions form along three dimensions → emotional consequences (pride, shame, guilt, anxiety) → motivational consequences (effort, persistence) → future behavior patterns (mastery vs. helpless orientation)
- **Where it aims:** Toward adaptive attribution patterns that promote resilience, persistence, and growth—where success is attributed to internal, stable, controllable causes and failure to internal, unstable, controllable causes (effort/strategy rather than ability)
