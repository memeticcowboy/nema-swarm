# Memetic Mediation for Online Tribes
## SIML Term Entry S010
### Source: 1771907212395_257803_Memetic+Mediation+for+Online+Tribes.md

## Overview

Online tribes form around shared memeplexes—clusters of mutually reinforcing ideas that define group identity. When tribes encounter each other, the result is often cognitive dissonance, psychological pain, and entrenchment rather than dialogue. Memetic mediation proposes a framework for building bridges between these tribes through deliberate cross-pollination of ideas, not by eliminating conflict but by creating conditions where conflict becomes generative.

The critical insight is that the pain of encountering opposing worldviews should not be mediated away. Rather than smoothing over disagreements or finding premature compromise, the mediator's role is to help participants stay present with the discomfort of cognitive dissonance. This requires creating intentional virtual spaces with explicit rules of engagement—containers strong enough to hold genuine conflict without collapse into trolling, ghosting, or tribal reinforcement. Growth comes from the clash itself, not from its resolution.
