## Source Insight

Peter Godfrey-Smith's exploration of animal consciousness reveals that **movement is the catalyst for mind**. Consciousness did not emerge as an abstract property but as a practical tool for organisms that needed to navigate, choose, and act within their environments.

The framework challenges anthropocentric views by showing how:
- Cyanobacteria transformed Earth's atmosphere before consciousness existed
- Animal minds evolved through the demands of controlled action
- Organisms actively shape their environments, creating feedback loops of co-evolution
- The transition from habit to deliberation marks increasing cognitive sophistication

This has profound implications for AI development, environmental ethics, and understanding our place within the web of conscious and pre-conscious life.
