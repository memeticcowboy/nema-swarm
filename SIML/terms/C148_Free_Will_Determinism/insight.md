# Free Will and Determinism
## SIML Term Entry C148
### Source: 1771907212690_960953_Exploring+Free+Will+and+Determinism.md

## Overview

The philosophical tension between the experience of autonomous choice (free will) and the causal necessity of physical laws (determinism), resolved through Dennett's compatibilist framework where freedom evolves as an emergent property of complex biological and cultural systems.

## Core Arguments

### Compatibilism
Neither pure libertarian free will nor hard determinism, but a middle path where freedom is understood as the capacity to act according to one's desires and values, shaped by but not reducible to underlying causes.

### Freedom Evolves
What evolves is not just biological form but the *space of possible actions* — freedom itself evolves, becoming more sophisticated over time. Cultural tools (language, institutions, norms) extend biological capabilities.

### Moral Responsibility
Responsibility emerges from communities of praise and blame, evolving as our understanding of behavior changes. Grounded in social practices rather than metaphysical truths.

### Self-Control
Metal-like discipline enables agents to bind themselves against hyperbolic discounting, aligning short-term actions with long-term goals through pre-commitment devices.

## Source
Daniel C. Dennett, *Freedom Evolves*
