## Source Context
This insight derives from Huw Green's exploration of how social context shapes cognition, challenging the machine analogy of brain-as-hardware. Key concepts include:

- **Social Model of Disability**: Societal barriers, not individual impairments, define disability
- **Vygotsky's Theory**: Individual thought emerges from internalizing social interactions
- **Extended Mind**: Cognition distributed across social networks, not confined to individual brains
- **Clinical Evidence**: Case studies showing social support masking cognitive impairment
