## ESSENCE

---

## PHENOMENON

WYSIATI—"What You See Is All There Is"—describes the fundamental operating constraint of Kahneman's System 1: the fast, automatic mind that cannot represent missing information. Given any fragmentary cues, System 1 instantly assembles a coherent causal narrative and treats that narrative as true. Not probably true. Not provisionally true. Simply true.

The critical feature: **what is not seen literally does not exist for the judgment.**

This is not a choice to ignore gaps. The very idea that evidence might be absent never arises. Ambiguity is not suppressed—it is never experienced. A single interpretation is produced automatically, and because it is the only interpretation available, it feels like direct perception of reality.

Manifestations include:
- **Overconfidence from One-Sided Evidence:** Those exposed to one-sided evidence are more confident than those who saw both sides—because coherence, not completeness, drives certainty
- **Framing Effects:** Survival rates vs mortality rates evoke different emotions despite mathematical equivalence; fat-free vs fat content shift perception through presentation alone
- **Base-Rate Neglect:** Statistical likelihood ignored in favor of vivid, specific case details
- **Halo Effect:** Limited information (a good presentation) driving broad conclusions about character or capability
- **Jumping to Conclusions:** Premature closure based on associative coherence rather than systematic evaluation

## THE COHERENCE MECHANISM

### Stage 1: Fragment Reception (Q_in)
System 1 receives whatever evidence is currently available. No mechanism exists to tag evidence as incomplete or partial. The input is simply what is.

### Stage 2: Associative Coherence (ρ)
Given the fragments, System 1 automatically assembles the best-fit causal story. This is not deliberation—it is instantaneous pattern-completion. The story that emerges feels discovered, not constructed.

### Stage 3: Confidence Generation (λ)
Confidence is not calibrated to evidence quality but to narrative coherence. A coherent story from limited evidence produces higher confidence than an incomplete understanding of comprehensive evidence. The quality of the story, not its basis, determines felt certainty.

### Stage 4: Reality Collapse (Z-sealing)
The constructed narrative becomes transparent—experienced as direct perception of reality rather than interpretation. What was assembled becomes what simply is. The μ-boundary prevents questioning because questioning requires awareness of construction, and construction is invisible.
