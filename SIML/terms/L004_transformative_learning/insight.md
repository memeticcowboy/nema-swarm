## 5. Core Insight

> Transformative learning is not about delivering better content but about *structuring experiences* where the learner's epistemology itself shifts — from "I know this" to "I am someone who knows."

## 6. One-Sentence Insight

> Bateson's "difference that makes a difference" reveals that learning transformation requires second-order change — not improving the content but transforming the structure of the learning experience itself.

## 7. Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A learning design principle from Bateson's epistemology — true transformation comes from structural change (second-order), not content improvement (first-order)
- **How it feels:** Initially disorienting (second-order disruption), then exploratory (pattern recognition), finally integrated (new way of being)
- **Where it aims:** Toward recursive transformation — each learning cycle changes the learner's structure, not just their content
