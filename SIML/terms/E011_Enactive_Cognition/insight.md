## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

What changes when we recognize that the mind does not represent the world but enacts it — that cognition is not about the world but of it?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Evan Thompson's enactive cognition thesis dissolves the subject-object split at its root. If cognitive agents actively bring forth their own cognitive domains, then what counts as "world" for an agent is constituted through ongoing activities and sensitivities, not passively received. The key insight: cognition is not neutral information processing but life-maintaining sense-making. This means every act of perception is already an act of creation, and the boundary between self and environment is not a wall but a dynamic surface continuously produced through enactment.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> The organism does not process information about the environment — it enacts an environment, and in doing so, enacts itself.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A cognitive science framework proposing that cognition is enactment — bringing forth a world of significance through embodied action rather than internally manipulating representations of an independent world
- **How it feels:** The dissolution of the gap between perceiver and perceived, the recognition that thinking is already doing, that every perception is simultaneously an act of world-creation
- **Where it aims:** Toward a post-representational cognitive science where cognition is continuous with life, where the brain expands the range of mind already present in living systems, and where AI research must grapple with embodiment
