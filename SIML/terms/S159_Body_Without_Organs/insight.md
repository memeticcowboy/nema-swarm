# Insight — Body Without Organs (S159)

## Overview

The Body without Organs (BwO), one of Deleuze and Guattari's most provocative and essential concepts, names a state of potential and freedom beyond traditional organic organization. Borrowed from Antonin Artaud's declaration of war against the organs, the BwO is not a body lacking organs but a body freed from the organism — from the particular hierarchical organization that assigns organs their functions and desire its channels.

The BwO is what remains when you take everything away — not a void but a field of raw potential where desire and affect operate without pre-set channels. It is about becoming rather than being: continuous experimentation and transformation rather than fixed identity and predetermined function. On the BwO, desire does not flow through Oedipal channels or capitalist circuits but creates its own connections, its own intensities, its own zones of experience.

Practices of "dismantling the organism" — loosening the grip of habitual organization without destroying the body — open to new modes of experience and expression. But these practices require caution. Deleuze and Guattari identify three types of BwO: the empty BwO (chaotic dissolution — catatonia, drug-addled collapse, the destruction of all capacity for connection), the full BwO (creative experimental intensity — productive desire flowing through novel connections, new capacities emerging), and the cancerous BwO (over-stratified rigidity — fascistic organization that mimics liberation while imposing totalitarian control). The full BwO is the achievement of careful, patient, incremental experimentation at the boundary between organization and chaos, related to the broader distinction between smooth spaces of open possibility and striated spaces of fixed organization.

## Elemental Analysis

- **Aerunik (Air/sigma)**: The discernment between the organized organism and the destratified potential field — dissolving the assumption that organic organization is natural or necessary and revealing the plane of potential beneath all stratification.
- **Sentaria (Water/rho)**: The relational flow of free desire and affect across the BwO — desire creating its own connections and intensities without predetermined pathways, circulating freely rather than being captured by organic functions.
- **Jvalion (Fire/lambda)**: The catalytic transformation from fixed being to radical becoming — experimental practices of dismantling the organism that open to new modes of experience, expression, and connection at the edge of organization and chaos.
- **Arboriel (Wood/beta)**: The organic growth from rigid stratification to expanding experimental practice — careful, incremental destratification avoiding the dangers of chaotic dissolution and fascistic rigidity alike.
- **Humavita (Earth/delta-gamma)**: The grounding oscillation between stratification and destratification — cycling between striated organization and smooth possibility, continuously experimenting at the boundary without achieving pure destratification.
- **Ferrosid (Metal/mu)**: The structural architecture of triadic BwO types — empty (chaotic dissolution), full (creative intensity), and cancerous (over-stratified rigidity) — providing the map for navigating practices of destratification.

## Key Tensions

1. **Organized organism vs. destratified potential** — The body as hierarchically organized with predetermined functions versus the BwO as raw potential freed from organic stratification, revealing what remains when you take everything away
2. **Channeled desire vs. free affective flow** — Desire routed through pre-set Oedipal, capitalist, and institutional channels versus desire and affect operating freely, creating novel connections and intensities without predetermined pathways
3. **Fixed being vs. radical becoming** — Static identity maintained by organic stratification versus continuous experimentation and transformation through practices of dismantling the organism
4. **Empty BwO vs. full BwO** — Chaotic dissolution through too-rapid destratification (catatonia, drug collapse) versus creative experimental intensity through careful, patient practice at the boundary of organization and chaos
5. **Smooth space vs. striated space** — Open possibility and free movement across the BwO versus fixed organization and hierarchical control of the organism, with the cycling between them constituting the rhythm of experimental practice
