# Insight — The Lattice: Curvature in Structural Behavior (S1BF)

## Overview
The Lattice describes how behavior is governed less by explicit commands than by architectures of repeatability that create curvature in the field of possible action. Drawing on Gibson's affordances, Giddens' structuration, Bourdieu's habitus, Meadows' systems thinking, memetics, and Winner's politics of artifacts, it reveals a convergent insight: structure drives behavior through curvature, not instruction.

History does not tell you what to do; it shapes what feels doable. Affordances are relational and asymmetric, felt before conceptualized. Repetition reproduces constraint without explicit commands. Systems feel fated because paths of least resistance dominate. Memes don't win; curvature does. Infrastructure governs silently, embedding power relations before legislation occurs.

The Lattice adds a phenomenology of curvature to abstract systems diagrams, asking how inevitability feels from the inside. The outcome is biased probability rather than determinism: structural curvature shapes what is likely without making anything certain.

## Key Tensions
1. **Command vs Curvature** — Explicit governance versus structural bias through repeatability.
2. **Instruction vs Affordance** — Following rules versus navigating felt possibilities.
3. **Determinism vs Biased Probability** — Fated outcomes versus weighted likelihoods.
4. **Design vs Legislation** — Infrastructure preceding formal governance.
5. **Agency vs Constraint** — Acting within curvature, not outside it.
