## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does understanding arrive suddenly as complete comprehension rather than accumulating gradually?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Learning is not always incremental. When a learner perceives the deep structure of a problem — the relationships between elements rather than the elements themselves — comprehension can arrive suddenly as a complete gestalt. This "aha!" moment represents a cognitive restructuring where the mental field reorganizes to reveal the solution. Because insight involves seeing underlying principles rather than memorizing procedures, it produces immediate, flexible transfer to novel situations.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Köhler's Insight Learning Theory demonstrates that understanding arrives suddenly through perceptual reorganization — not through incremental conditioning but through seeing relationships within the total field, producing complete comprehension that transfers immediately to novel situations.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A Gestalt theory (Köhler, Wertheimer, Koffka) that learning occurs through sudden perceptual reorganization — the "aha!" moment — where the learner sees relationships and structures within the total field, producing complete comprehension that is immediately transferable
- **How it works:** Problem situation → incubation/perceptual exploration → cognitive restructuring → sudden insight (complete solution) → immediate transfer to novel problems — contrasted with gradual trial-and-error learning
- **Where it aims:** Toward productive thinking (Wertheimer) — not applying learned procedures but seeing deep structures that enable creative problem-solving, emphasizing that the whole is greater than the sum of its parts
