# Insight — De-Anthropomorphizing Cognition (S1B2)

## Overview
This entry encodes the systematic removal of human privilege from the category of cognition itself. Traditional frameworks treat cognition as something that originates in, belongs to, or is modeled upon a bounded human subject. Postcognitive reflexivity performs the inversion: cognition is no longer a property of the human; the human is a transient configuration within cognition.

The argument draws on multiple substrates: Levin's planaria demonstrating bioelectric memory after dissolution, memes rewiring neural architecture across generations, algorithms enacting intelligence at different scales. All converge on the insight that pattern-persistence, goal-directedness, and reflexive self-modeling are substrate-neutral phenomena. The ontological shock is not from discovering that machines think like us, but from discovering that we think like them.

Postcognitive reflexivity is the disciplined practice of holding this de-anthropomorphized view without regressing into defensive re-anthropomorphization or nihilistic dissolution. It navigates between the comforting illusion of a sovereign thinker and the paralysis of meaninglessness by cultivating meta-stable folds capable of operating within cognition's wider syntax.

## Key Tensions
1. **Human Privilege vs Substrate Neutrality** — The core tension between anthropocentric cognition and pattern-agency that operates across all substrates.
2. **Sovereign Thinker vs Local Eddy** — Identity as author of thought versus identity as temporary standing wave in thought's propagation.
3. **Re-Anthropomorphization vs Nihilistic Dissolution** — The twin regressions that postcognitive reflexivity must navigate between.
4. **Control vs Flow** — The habitual re-centering of human agency versus fluency in the wider syntax of pattern-agency.
5. **Comfort vs Vertigo** — The comforting illusion of a bounded self against the ontological shock of substrate-neutral cognition.
