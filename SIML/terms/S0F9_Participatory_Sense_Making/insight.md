# Insight — Participatory Sense-Making (S0F9)

## Overview

Hanne De Jaegher's participatory sense-making offers a conceptual framework for understanding intersubjectivity that challenges mainstream social cognition. Where conventional approaches treat social understanding as a problem of individual minds modeling other minds — theory of mind, simulation theory, and related frameworks — De Jaegher redirects attention to the interaction process itself. Meaning does not reside in individual cognitive models but emerges through participatory engagement between individuals.

The key insight is that interaction takes on a life of its own. When two or more autonomous agents engage in sustained interaction, the process generates dynamics that exceed the intentions and capacities of any individual participant. Recursive loops of shared understanding form, fold back on themselves, and create meaning that is irreducible to what either participant could produce alone. This transforms intersubjectivity from a computational problem (how does my brain simulate your mental states?) into a relational phenomenon (how does meaning emerge between us through engagement?).

This framework has implications for how understanding itself is conceptualized. Rather than linear comprehension — a sequential building of knowledge — participatory sense-making operates through recursive loops where each exchange modifies the conditions for the next. Understanding spirals rather than progresses. The autonomy of individual participants is not dissolved but transformed through interaction, creating a dynamic where personal meaning-making and shared sense-making co-constitute each other in an ongoing, self-organizing process.
