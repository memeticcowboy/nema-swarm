# Understanding Reality Through Science
## SIML Term Entry S011
### Source: 1771907212395_257810_Understanding+Reality+Through+Science.md

## Overview

Science is not an elite institution but a democratic, everyday tool for understanding reality. At its core lies the IO Loop (Information-Organization Loop)—a cyclical process of observation, questioning, and experimentation that progressively refines our mental models. Reality is always communicating through phenomena; our job is to listen, question what we observe, form hypotheses, test them, and refine our understanding accordingly.

The key warning is against confirmation bias—the tendency to seek information that confirms existing beliefs while ignoring contradictory evidence. The IO Loop functions as a corrective discipline: by making observation primary and subjecting hypotheses to genuine testing, the loop forces our mental models to adapt to reality rather than allowing reality to be filtered through our preferences. Science, in this framing, is not about arriving at final truths but about maintaining an honest, iterative relationship with what is actually there.

## Key Tensions

- **Observe ↔ Question**: Passive reception of phenomena vs. active interrogation of meaning
- **Reality ↔ Model**: What is actually there vs. our organized representation of it
- **Hypothesis ↔ Test**: Speculative framework vs. empirical contact with results
- **Information ↔ Organization**: Raw data intake vs. structured understanding
- **Method ↔ Bias**: Disciplined inquiry vs. the pull of confirmation bias
