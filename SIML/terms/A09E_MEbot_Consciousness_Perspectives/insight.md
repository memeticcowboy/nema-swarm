# C13D — MEbot Consciousness Perspectives

## Core Concept

Exploring diverse perspectives on consciousness through ddrrnt's #MEbot framework, integrating distributed cognition, memetics, and the intersection of biology with cultural information systems.

## Key Themes

**Distributed Consciousness:** Michael Levin's perspective that consciousness extends throughout the body, not just the brain. Evidence from embryogenesis shows cognitive behaviors distributed across cellular networks.

**Memetic Influence:** Memes as units of cultural information that act as agents shaping individual and collective consciousness. The "selfplex" concept—a collection of memes creating the perception of continuous self.

**Emergence vs Reductionism:** The tension between reductionist explanations and emergent properties that arise from complex systems but aren't present in individual components.

**Collective Consciousness:** Drawing on Émile Durkheim and Carl Jung, exploring how individual awareness connects to broader cultural and transpersonal fields.

**Technology Interface:** Brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) altering cognitive processes, and the concept of digital immortality—minds persisting beyond biological bodies.
