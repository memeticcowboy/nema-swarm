# Sense-Making in Distorted Information Ecology
## SIML Term Entry S00E

## Overview

The "war on sensemaking" describes the systematic degradation of collective capacity to distinguish truth from manipulation in contemporary information ecologies. The source examines propaganda, biased sources, and disinformation campaigns as forces polluting the epistemic commons. A critical conceptual contribution is the tripartite distinction between truthful (honest intent), true (factual accuracy), and representative (proportional, unbiased framing)—three independent dimensions that are frequently conflated.

The IO Loop concept describes self-reinforcing cycles of information operations that entrench distortion. Game theory illuminates the strategic logic of disinformation: actors exploit trust asymmetries and cognitive biases for competitive advantage. The information pollution analogy frames the crisis ecologically—just as environmental pollutants degrade physical ecosystems, epistemic pollutants degrade collective sense-making capacity. Critically, the concept of hormesis is applied to cognitive development: controlled exposure to misinformation, when properly scaffolded, can strengthen discernment rather than destroy it.
