## Contextual Notes

The Spiral of Silence theory, developed by Elisabeth Noelle-Neumann in 1974, emerged from observation of German public opinion dynamics. The theory has been applied to contexts ranging from Nazi Germany's suppression of dissent to contemporary "cancel culture" debates. Critics note that the theory may overstate fear of isolation and understate other motivations for silence (disinterest, strategic restraint). In digital contexts, the spiral interacts complexly with filter bubbles, algorithmic amplification, and the visibility of dissent in online spaces.
