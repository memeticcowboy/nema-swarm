# System 1 and System 2 Thinking
## SIML Term Entry S002
### Source: 1771907212331_227833_Understanding+System+1+and+System+2+Thinking.md

## Overview

Daniel Kahneman's dual process theory identifies two fundamentally different modes of thinking. System 1 operates fast, automatically, and intuitively—handling familiar patterns, snap judgments, and emotional reactions with minimal effort. System 2 engages slowly and deliberately, applying logical analysis, careful evaluation, and conscious reasoning to complex problems.

The source draws an embodied analogy between these cognitive modes and muscle fiber types: fast-twitch fibers for explosive bursts mirror System 1's rapid processing, while slow-twitch endurance fibers parallel System 2's sustained analytical effort. This somatic grounding suggests that cognitive duality is not merely abstract but reflects deeper biological patterns of energy allocation and performance optimization.

## Key Tensions

- **Intuitive ↔ Analytical**: The core polarity defining when each system leads
- **Efficiency ↔ Accuracy**: Speed gains from System 1 risk error; System 2's precision costs energy
- **Bias ↔ Correction**: System 1 generates biases that System 2 must detect and correct
- **Fast ↔ Slow**: The metabolic rhythm of cognitive effort mirrors physical energy systems
