# Insight — Nonaka Takeuchi Knowledge Spiral (S08B)

## Overview

This entry encodes the Nonaka and Takeuchi Knowledge Conversion Model, which illustrates how knowledge is dynamically created and transformed through four modes forming the SECI spiral: Socialization (tacit-to-tacit, sharing embodied knowledge through shared experience), Externalization (tacit-to-explicit, articulating intuitive understanding into concepts and metaphors), Combination (explicit-to-explicit, synthesizing documented knowledge into more complex systems), and Internalization (explicit-to-tacit, embodying codified knowledge back into practice through learning-by-doing).

The model's central contribution is reframing knowledge from a static stock to be managed into a dynamic process of continuous creation. The SECI modes do not form a closed loop but an ascending spiral — each cycle builds upon the previous one, expanding both tacit depth and explicit breadth. The fundamental distinction between tacit knowledge (embodied, experiential, difficult to articulate) and explicit knowledge (codified, systematic, readily communicable) drives the entire model, with all four modes operating across this boundary. The model's principles apply across business, education, and AI contexts, demonstrating that innovation emerges from the friction and flow between what we know tacitly and what we can articulate explicitly.

## Key Tensions

1. **Tacit vs. explicit knowledge** — The fundamental tension between embodied understanding that resists articulation and codified knowledge that can be readily shared but may lose experiential depth
2. **Individual vs. organizational knowledge** — The challenge of converting personal insights into collective assets without losing the contextual richness of individual understanding
3. **Static stock vs. dynamic process** — Whether knowledge should be managed as an asset to be stored or cultivated as a process of continuous creation
4. **Articulation vs. embodiment** — The tension between Externalization (making tacit knowledge explicit) and Internalization (making explicit knowledge tacit), each involving translation losses
5. **Spiral expansion vs. knowledge entropy** — Whether the spiral continues to generate genuinely new knowledge or eventually recycles existing understanding in increasingly elaborate forms
