## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does grappling with authentic problems create deeper understanding than traditional instruction?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

PBL works by **productive difficulty** — the struggle to understand an ill-structured problem creates cognitive dissonance that drives inquiry. The collaborative structure ensures multiple perspectives, the self-directed study ensures individual ownership, and the synthesis phase ensures integration. The problem is not a test of learning but its catalyst.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Problem-Based Learning reveals that understanding emerges not from receiving information but from struggling with authentic challenges—where collaborative inquiry, self-directed research, and synthesis create integrated knowledge that transfers to real-world contexts.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** An instructional method where learners collaboratively investigate complex, authentic problems—using inquiry, self-directed research, and peer dialogue to construct understanding
- **How it works:** Ill-structured problem triggers collaborative analysis → learning issues identified → self-directed study → knowledge synthesis → solution application, with facilitator scaffolding throughout
- **Where it aims:** Toward integrated, transferable understanding and problem-solving capability—developing learners who can analyze complex situations and apply knowledge authentically
