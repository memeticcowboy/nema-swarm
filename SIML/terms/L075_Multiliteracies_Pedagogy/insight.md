## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does recognizing multiple modes of meaning and multiple cultural contexts transform our understanding of what it means to be "literate"?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Multiliteracies Pedagogy works by **expanding the design space** — recognizing that meaning is made not just through language but through visual, audio, gestural, and spatial modes, and that these modes operate differently across cultural contexts. The learner becomes a flexible transformer of available designs rather than a consumer of standardized forms.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Multiliteracies Pedagogy reveals that literacy in the 21st century is not about mastering a single standardized form of language but about becoming a **flexible designer of meaning** across multiple modes, cultural contexts, and digital platforms.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A pedagogical framework recognizing that literacy now requires meaning-making across multiple modes (visual, audio, spatial, gestural, linguistic) in culturally and linguistically diverse contexts
- **How it works:** Four-component pedagogy — Situated Practice + Overt Instruction + Critical Framing + Transformed Practice — organized around Design as transformation of available resources
- **Where it aims:** Toward learners as flexible designers who can navigate plural cultural contexts, critique how texts position people, and create new meanings across multimodal environments
