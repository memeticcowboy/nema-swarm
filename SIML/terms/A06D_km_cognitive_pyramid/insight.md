## Core Concept

The KM Cognitive Pyramid (DIKW: Data-Information-Knowledge-Wisdom) represents a hierarchical model of knowledge transformation, where raw data ascends through progressive stages of contextualization and interpretation to become actionable wisdom.

**The Four Levels:**
- **Data**: Raw, unprocessed facts and figures (the base)
- **Information**: Data organized with context and meaning
- **Knowledge**: Information applied, interpreted, and embodied
- **Wisdom**: The capacity to use knowledge for sound judgment

## Critical Considerations

The DIKW pyramid, while pedagogically useful, risks:

1. **Oversimplification**: Real knowledge work is recursive, not linear
2. **Hierarchy bias**: Privileging "wisdom" over other forms of knowing
3. **Context blindness**: Assuming transformations are neutral and automatic
4. **Individual focus**: Neglecting collective and distributed knowledge processes

Alternative frameworks (Cynefin, SECI model, rhizomatic knowledge) may better capture the complexity of actual knowledge practices.
