## CONTEXT NOTE

Peter Godfrey-Smith's *Living on Earth* presents a philosophical and scientific case for expanding moral consideration to other species. Key concepts include:

1. **Consciousness Evolution:** Sentience and subjective experience evolved independently across the tree of life—not just in vertebrates but in cephalopods and potentially other lineages

2. **Animal Agency:** Animals transform their environments through purposeful action—whales redistribute nutrients, beavers reshape rivers, birds engineer ecosystems

3. **Cultural Evolution:** Human cultural learning enables greater environmental impact, amplifying both our capacity for harm and for stewardship

4. **Factory Farming:** Industrial animal agriculture represents a moral crisis given what we know about animal consciousness; cessation is an ethical imperative

5. **Moral Intuitions:** Our innate senses of harm, fairness, and loyalty can guide ethical frameworks beyond anthropocentrism

6. **Interconnectedness:** Life has always transformed Earth; the question is whether human transformation will sustain or destroy the diversity of minds that evolved here

The framework emphasizes:
- Scientific understanding of consciousness as foundation for ethics
- Recognition of evolutionary continuity between human and animal minds
- Active stewardship rather than passive preservation
- The moral cost of convenient ignorance about animal suffering
- Collective responsibility for planetary conditions that support consciousness diversity
