## The Consumption Society

We live in an age of unprecedented consumption:
- Information abundance
- Content saturation
- Experience economy
- Instant gratification

But consumption without metabolism is **malnutrition**. The body starves while the stomach is full. The mind atrophies while the screen is active.

## The Biological Archetype

Metabolism in the cell:
1. **Intake:** Nutrients cross the membrane (permeability)
2. **Catabolism:** Breaking down (fire)
3. **Anabolism:** Building up (wood)
4. **Integration:** New molecules join the structure (earth)
5. **Waste elimination:** Byproducts removed (flow)

The cell that consumes without metabolizing dies. The organism that eats without digesting sickens.

## The Cognitive Parallel

Mental metabolism:
1. **Intake:** Information, experience, sensation
2. **Processing:** Reflection, analysis, questioning
3. **Integration:** Connection to existing knowledge
4. **Ground-building:** Cumulative wisdom
5. **Expression:** Output from integrated ground

The mind that consumes without metabolizing becomes **shallow**. Information passes through without becoming knowledge. Knowledge passes through without becoming wisdom.

## The AI Accelerant

AI removes the friction that enables metabolism:
- **No struggle:** Answers without effort
- **No retention:** Retrieval replaces memory
- **No synthesis:** Generation replaces thinking
- **No integration:** Outsourcing replaces processing

The result: **abundance without digestion**. We consume more than ever and metabolize less than ever.

Metabolism is **ground through fire**:
- Earth (δγ): The ground that is built—dominant
- Fire (λ): The transformation that enables—elevated
- Meta (∮): The intelligence of processing—elevated

## The Pathologies

**Indigestion:** Too much input, too little processing
- Information overload
- Overwhelm
- Shallow engagement

**Malabsorption:** Processing without integration
- Knowledge that doesn't connect
- Skills that don't build
- Experience without learning

**Constipation:** Input without output
- Accumulation without expression
- Hoarding without use
- Stagnation

## The Recovery

Restoring metabolism:
1. **Slow down:** Reduce input to match processing capacity
2. **Reflect:** Build processing time into consumption
3. **Connect:** Explicitly link new to existing knowledge
4. **Express:** Output as integration (teaching, writing, making)
5. **Rest:** Metabolism requires cycles—activity and recovery
