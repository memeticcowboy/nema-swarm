### 6.2 Hypothesis

Whitehead's concrescence dissolves the problem of change by making change fundamental. If actual occasions are the basic units of reality — momentary acts of becoming that incorporate the past through prehension and achieve objective immortality through structural transmission — then nothing endures in the traditional sense. What we call "objects" are just stable patterns of occasion-succession. The famous declaration — "the fundamental facts are organisms, not matter" — means that experience, not substance, is the ground of reality. Creativity is "the universal of universals," exemplified in all actualities.
