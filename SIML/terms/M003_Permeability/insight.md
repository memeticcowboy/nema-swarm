## The Membrane as Model

The cell membrane is the archetype of permeability:
- **Phospholipid bilayer:** Structure that maintains separation
- **Channel proteins:** Regulated passage for specific molecules
- **Transport mechanisms:** Active and selective exchange
- **Receptors:** Sensing and responding to environment

The cell that is impermeable dies (starvation). The cell that is fully permeable dies (dissolution). Life is permeability—regulated, dynamic, selective.

## The Social Membrane

Organizations, societies, selves—all have membranes:
- **Information boundaries:** What enters, what leaves
- **Cultural membranes:** What practices transmit, which don't
- **Personal boundaries:** Self/other distinction with connection
- **Knowledge systems:** Disciplinary borders with interdisciplinary flow

The healthy social membrane:
- Permits nutrient flow (useful information)
- Blocks toxins (misinformation, manipulation)
- Adapts to conditions (dynamic permeability)
- Maintains identity (structure persists)
