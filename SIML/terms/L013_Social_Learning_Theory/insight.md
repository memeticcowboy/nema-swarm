## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does recognizing learning as fundamentally social transform educational practice?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Social Learning Theory works by **relational resonance** — learning emerges not from individual information processing but from attunement to others, participation in shared practices, and gradual identity transformation. The community is not a context for learning; it **is** the curriculum.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Bandura and Lave & Wenger reveal that learning is fundamentally social — we acquire knowledge not through isolated study but through observing models, participating in communities, and transforming our identities through relational practice.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A family of theories (Social Learning, Communities of Practice) arguing that learning occurs through observation, modeling, and participation in social communities — not through individual information processing alone
- **How it works:** Attention to models → retention of observed behaviors → reproduction attempts → motivational reinforcement → legitimate peripheral participation → identity transformation → full community membership
- **Where it aims:** Toward learning as social becoming — the transformation of identity through participation in communities of shared practice
