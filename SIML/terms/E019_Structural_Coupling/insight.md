## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How can two systems shape each other without either losing its identity — and what does this tell us about the nature of relationship?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Structural coupling resolves the false dichotomy between autonomy and connection. The organism is not a closed system (that would be death) nor an open one (that would be dissolution) but an operationally closed, structurally coupled system — self-organizing internally while materially engaged with its environment. The key insight: the body is an autonomous system that actively generates and maintains distinction between itself and environment through metabolism. This is not a wall but a living process — the organism must incessantly "buy time" against entropy through continuous, materially costly coupling. Precariousness is not a threat to coupling but its condition.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Structural coupling is the basic pattern of life — mutual specification without fusion, autonomy without isolation, the precarious art of being changed by what you do not become.
