# Insight — Multifaceted Sensemaking Theory (S09F)

## Overview

The source explores Multifaceted Sensemaking Theory through systematic literature review and content analysis, examining how individuals bridge gaps in understanding by constructing bridges of ideas, thoughts, emotions, and memories. Sensemaking is characterized as a cognitive process encompassing acquisition (gathering information), interpretation (making sense of what is gathered), understanding (integrating into existing knowledge), and action (responding based on new understanding).

The theory identifies multiple factors shaping sensemaking: situational factors (immediate circumstances), contextual factors (history, constraints, power structures), and personal factors (lived experience, emotional state, cognitive frameworks). Information, data, narratives, and communication play central roles in the construction of meaning. Foundational concepts include time (temporal context of understanding), space (physical and conceptual environment), movement (directionality of sense construction), and gaps (the discontinuities that trigger sensemaking). The Multifaceted Sensemaking Theory synthesizes existing frameworks from prominent theorists in the field, integrating individual and social ontologies to demonstrate coverage and coherence across sensemaking characteristics. The practical goal is fostering new ways of organizing information and behaviors that lead to positive outcomes.

## Elemental Analysis

- **Aerunik (Air/sigma)**: The distinction between the understanding gap (confusion, disorientation, insufficient knowledge) and the constructed bridge of meaning (assembled ideas, thoughts, emotions, and memories).
- **Sentaria (Water/rho)**: The relational coupling of individual experience, contextual factors, and information environment, integrating personal and social ontologies.
- **Jvalion (Fire/lambda)**: The transformation of raw experience into interpreted meaningful action through the cognitive processes of acquisition, interpretation, understanding, and action.
- **Arboriel (Wood/beta)**: The developmental trajectory from initial gap awareness to comprehensive sensemaking capacity for navigating complex, ambiguous situations.
- **Humavita (Earth/delta-gamma)**: The cycling through the four-phase sensemaking process alongside iterative synthesis and refinement of sensemaking theory across frameworks.
- **Ferrosid (Metal/mu)**: Cognitive process as structural framework for individual sensemaking alongside narrative and communication as architectural mechanisms for collective meaning construction.

## Key Tensions

1. **Understanding gap vs. constructed bridge** — The fundamental tension between not-knowing and the active construction of meaning that spans it, which is the engine of all sensemaking
2. **Individual vs. social ontology** — Whether meaning is primarily constructed by individuals from personal experience or emerges through social interaction, shared narratives, and collective norms
3. **Contextual constraint vs. personal agency** — How history, power structures, and institutional constraints shape sensemaking versus the individual's capacity to construct meaning despite or against these constraints
4. **Information as resource vs. information as shaping force** — Whether data and narratives are neutral materials for meaning construction or actively shape and constrain the meanings that can be constructed
5. **Theory integration vs. framework proliferation** — The tension between synthesizing diverse sensemaking frameworks into a coherent multifaceted theory and preserving the distinct contributions of each theoretical tradition
