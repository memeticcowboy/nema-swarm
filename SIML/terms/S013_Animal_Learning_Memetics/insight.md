# Animal Learning and Memetics
## SIML Term Entry S013
### Source: 1771907212400_259972_Animal+Learning+and+Memetics.md

## Overview

The relationship between hardwired behaviors and learning in animals and humans reveals a deep co-evolutionary dynamic between genes and memes. Memes function as "hardwired software" — patterns implanted in brain circuitry that remain dormant until triggered by specific environmental conditions. This framing collapses the strict nature/nurture divide: memes are neither purely cultural nor purely biological, but occupy an intermediate zone where cultural transmission becomes neurally instantiated.

Precepts (gene-driven behavioral templates) and concepts (meme-driven cultural constructs) represent two distinct but interlocking inheritance systems. Precepts carry the ancient neural connections refined over millions of years of evolution, while concepts — memetic structures — emerged approximately 200,000 years ago with the advent of symbolic cognition. Darwin's dog memory test illustrates this intersection: the dog's recognition of Darwin after years of absence reflects hardwired bonding instincts activated by learned associative memory, demonstrating how both systems operate simultaneously in shaping behavior.
