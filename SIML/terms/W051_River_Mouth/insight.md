## Cross-Elemental Synthesis

- **Operator pattern analysis:** λ (fire) pushes a directed flow of water and sediment toward the mouth; σ (air) sharpens the fresh‑salt transition, creating the mixing zone; δγ (earth) receives and cycles sediment into landforms. The sequence is energetic input → boundary activation → metabolic deposit. The tension arises because δγ’s cycle is being drained by a broken λ input (dams blocking flow), leaving σ to define a boundary that can no longer build outward.
- **Cross‑cultural bridges:** The Mekong Delta, starved of sediment by upstream dams, shows the same dynamic; the Mississippi river mouth’s land loss after levee and dam construction; the Nile Delta’s retreat after the Aswan High Dam. Each case is a local instance of the river‑mouth tension between engineered stability and the metabolic, land‑building agency of the more‑than‑human.
