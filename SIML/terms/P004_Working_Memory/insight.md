## 7. Synthesis Statement

Working Memory Theory reveals that conscious cognition operates within severe constraints that are not merely limitations but defining features. The multi-component architecture — with a controlling executive, specialized slave systems, and an integrative buffer — shows how the mind manages the fundamental problem of limited resources. This is not a passive storage system but an active workspace where information is maintained, manipulated, and transformed. The capacity limit (~4 chunks, not Miller's 7) forces the mind to compress, chunk, and prioritize — processes that are essential for intelligent behavior. Understanding WM transforms educational design: instruction must respect these limits, minimize extraneous load, and scaffold the development of chunking strategies that effectively expand functional capacity.
