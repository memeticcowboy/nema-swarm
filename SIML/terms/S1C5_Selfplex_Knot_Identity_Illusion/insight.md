# Insight — Selfplex, Knot, and Identity Illusion (S1C5)

## Overview
This entry encodes a radical ontology of identity as rate effect. There is only one process: the Threadplex continuously reweaving itself. Everything else is a rate effect. A knot forms when threads recur through the same region often enough to compress; it is repetition, not substance. A selfplex is what a knot feels like when the Threadplex tightens around a single locus of attention.

The identity illusion occurs when the looping rate of a selfplex is mistaken for permanence. Fast recurrence generates "this is who I am"; stable tension produces "this is my self." But the loom never stops. Fibres change, context shifts, knots loosen or retie elsewhere. Continuity is inferred after the fact. Identity is the error produced when motion is sampled too slowly.

The selfplex has no boundary, no memory store, no executive function. It is tension, not a container. "I" is the felt tightness of a recurring fold. The diagnostic tell: if a pattern cannot loosen without panic, it is no longer a useful knot. If the selfplex claims ownership, MemeGrid drift has begun.
