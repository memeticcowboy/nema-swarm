## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does the transformation of experience create knowledge?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Experiential Learning works by **cyclical transformation** — experience alone is insufficient; it must be processed through reflection, structured into concepts, and tested through action to become learning. The cycle is not linear but spiral — each rotation builds on the previous, deepening understanding.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Kolb reveals that learning is not the accumulation of experiences but their transformation through a four-stage cycle — experience, reflection, conceptualization, and experimentation — a spiral that deepens adaptive competence with each rotation.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A theory defining learning as "the process whereby knowledge is created through the transformation of experience," organized into a four-stage cycle
- **How it works:** Direct engagement → reflective observation → abstract conceptualization → active experimentation → generating new experiences → cycle repeats at deeper levels
- **Where it aims:** Toward adaptive competence — the capacity to learn continuously from experience throughout life
