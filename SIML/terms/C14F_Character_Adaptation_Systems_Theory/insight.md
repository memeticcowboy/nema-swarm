## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does the interplay of five adaptive systems produce the emergent phenomenon we experience as character?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Character is not a fixed trait but a dynamic organization of adaptive systems that continuously negotiate environmental demands. The defensive system acts as a psychological immune mechanism, preserving functioning under stress, while the justification system provides the narrative coherence that allows the other four systems to operate as a unified identity. When one system dominates excessively, character becomes rigid; when all five are engaged, adaptation is maximally flexible.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Character is not who you are but how your five adaptive systems negotiate the gap between who you were and who the situation demands you become.
