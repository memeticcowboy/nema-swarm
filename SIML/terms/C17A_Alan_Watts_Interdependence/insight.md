# Alan Watts on Reality: Interdependence vs. Separation
## SIML Term Entry C17A
### Source: 1771907212930_1798385_Alan+Watts+on+Reality%3A+Interdependence+vs.+Separation.md

## Overview

Alan Watts' philosophical critique of the "ceramic" and "fully automatic" models of reality, advocating for a holistic understanding that emphasizes interdependence over separation between humans and nature.

## Core Models

### The Ceramic Model
Reality as shaped by an external creator — humans as artifacts made by a divine potter. This model implies separation between creator and creation, and between individual entities.

### The Fully Automatic Model
Reality as a mechanical process with no inherent meaning — the universe as a blind machine. This model strips away purpose and connection, leaving alienation.

### Watts' Alternative
Neither ceramic nor automatic, but an interconnected field where self and universe are not separate. The "prickles and goo" dialectic — between those who want hard boundaries and those who want fluid merger.

## Key Insight
The experience of separation is itself a cultural construct. Direct experience reveals interdependence as the fundamental nature of reality. Models are maps, not territories — and the map of separation has been mistaken for the territory.
