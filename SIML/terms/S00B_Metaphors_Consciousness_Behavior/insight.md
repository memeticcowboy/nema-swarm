# Metaphors, Consciousness, and Behavior
## SIML Term Entry S00B
### Source: 1771907212373_244360_The+Influence+of+Metaphors+on+Consciousness+and+Behavior.md

## Overview

Lakoff and Johnson's conceptual metaphor theory reveals that metaphors are not mere linguistic decoration but fundamental cognitive structures shaping thought, perception, and action. Three primary types operate across human experience: orientational metaphors (HAPPY IS UP, SAD IS DOWN) grounded in spatial bodily experience, ontological metaphors (MIND IS A MACHINE) that treat abstract phenomena as bounded entities, and structural metaphors (ARGUMENT IS WAR) that map the logic of one domain onto another.

These metaphors are rooted in embodied experience rather than abstract logic, uniting reason and imagination in a way that challenges the objectivist tradition. Meaning does not correspond to an independent reality—it emerges from the ongoing interaction between embodied beings and their world. The metaphors we live by constrain what we can think, what we notice, and how we act.
