# The Hard Question of Memetics
## SIML Term Entry S00F
### Source: 1771907212394_257622_The+Hard+Question+of+Memetics%3A+A+Critical+Analysis.md

## Overview

Andrej Drapal's defense of memetics as a legitimate science confronts the "hard question" head-on: how are memes different from ideas, myths, PR, or other cultural units? If the distinction cannot be made, memetics collapses into mere rebranding of existing disciplines. Drapal's answer operates on three levels: consilience (memetics uniquely links natural and social sciences under a single evolutionary framework), evolutionary necessity (memes are not arbitrary cultural artifacts but units motivated by evolutionary forces analogous to genetic selection), and a philosophical bridge across Platonic/Cartesian dualism through quantum-like acceptance of unity within duality.

The core insight is that memetics is not just another cultural theory but a consilient framework that resolves the traditional split between natural science (genes, evolution, biology) and social science (culture, meaning, communication). By grounding cultural transmission in evolutionary dynamics, memetics provides explanatory power that ideas-as-ideas cannot. The quantum analogy is particularly striking: just as quantum mechanics accepts wave-particle duality without resolving it into one or the other, memetics accepts that cultural units are simultaneously material (brain states, behaviors) and immaterial (meanings, intentions) without collapsing to either pole.
