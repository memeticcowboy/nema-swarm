# Faceted Classification of Knowledge: Themes, Attributes, and Influencers

## Core Insight

The faceted classification framework reveals knowledge as a multi-dimensional ecosystem rather than a linear hierarchy. By organizing information across eight interconnected facets—from theoretical foundations to spatiotemporal context—this approach enables more nuanced navigation of complex knowledge domains.

## The Eight Facets

1. **Themes & Theoretical Foundations**: The conceptual bedrock (philosophy, memetics, systems thinking)
2. **Physical Attributes**: Information density, complexity levels, media types
3. **Cultural & Technological Influences**: Historical periods, cultural movements, technological epochs
4. **Agents**: Thinkers, organizations, communities that shape knowledge
5. **Activities**: Processes, methods, thinking models applied to knowledge
6. **Materials**: Containers and structures that hold knowledge
7. **Objects**: Frameworks, tools, theories as knowledge artifacts
8. **Space & Time**: Historical evolution and contextual situatedness

## Critical Implications

- **Knowledge is not neutral**: Classification systems embody power relations and cultural assumptions
- **Multiplicity over hierarchy**: Networked associations often reveal more than rigid taxonomies
- **Context matters**: The same information transforms across different facets and contexts
- **Emergence**: Knowledge systems evolve; fixed classifications become obsolete

## Nemetic Resonance

This framework exemplifies the σ (Air) tension between unity and multiplicity—seeking coherent organization while honoring diverse perspectives. The ρ (Water) flow between structure and fluidity mirrors how knowledge must be both navigable and adaptable.
