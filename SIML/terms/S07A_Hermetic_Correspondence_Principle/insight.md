# Insight — Hermetic Correspondence Principle (S07A)

## Overview

The phrase "As Above, So Below" originates from the Hermetic tradition, most notably the Emerald Tablet of Hermes Trismegistus, a cornerstone text in Western esoteric thought. Though brief, it encapsulates an entire philosophical worldview asserting correspondence and resonance between different levels of reality, where patterns and truths found in one domain reflect and inform those in another.

In its original Hermetic context, the principle articulates that the celestial and terrestrial realms mirror each other — the macrocosm of the vast universe and the microcosm of individual human experience are intimately connected through underlying unity. In alchemy, this became a conceptual pivot: turning base metals into gold symbolically aligned with transmuting one's raw psyche into purified understanding, establishing recursive self-similarity between physical refinement and spiritual development. Beyond its esoteric origins, the principle suggests a general metaphysical claim that the patterns of the universe are fractal, repeating across scales and dimensions — a notion resonating with contemporary complexity theory, fractal geometry, and systems thinking. Spiritually, the phrase encourages seekers to look both inward and outward, creating a bootstrapping mechanism where growth of insight in one domain fuels deeper comprehension in the other.
