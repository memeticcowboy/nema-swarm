## Core Insight

This consolidated synthesis reveals democracy undergoing a paradigm metamorphosis—from mechanical counting to ritualized sense-making, from static representation to fluid resonance. The networked age doesn't just modify democracy; it recodes its fundamental ontology.

The ancient Agora was a physical space of discourse. The Enlightenment Parliament was a symbolic one. Today's agora is distributed, datafied, and emotionally charged—where legitimacy flows not from procedural regularity but from visibility, virality, and affective alignment.

## Synthesis Warning

This synthesis reveals both promise and peril:

**The Promise:** Democracy as living, semiotic system—alive, reflexive, sacred, symbolically metabolizing its own myths in real-time.

**The Peril:** Without epistemic justice and attention equity, this shift risks becoming "a beautiful trap: more noise, more agency theater—but no shared ground."

The bridge isn't just technical—it's cultural, symbolic, and incremental. These new models must first "haunt" legacy systems, interleaving with them through hybrid protocols, until the shift from representation to resonance becomes embodied and inevitable.
