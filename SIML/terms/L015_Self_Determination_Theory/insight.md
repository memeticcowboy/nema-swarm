## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does supporting basic psychological needs transform motivation from controlled to autonomous?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Self-Determination Theory works by **need satisfaction** — humans have innate growth tendencies that flourish when three basic needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness) are satisfied. External control undermines intrinsic motivation, while autonomy support enables volitional engagement and internalization.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Deci & Ryan reveal that human flourishing emerges when autonomy, competence, and relatedness are satisfied — shifting motivation from external control to internal purpose and self-regulation.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A macro-theory arguing that humans have three innate psychological needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness) that must be satisfied for intrinsic motivation, well-being, and optimal development
- **How it works:** Need satisfaction → intrinsic motivation and internalization → self-regulated engagement → integrated self; need thwarting → controlled motivation → alienation
- **Where it aims:** Toward understanding motivation as growth-oriented — learning and development as natural tendencies requiring nurturing (not controlling) contexts
