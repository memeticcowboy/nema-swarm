## CONTEXT & APPLICATION

**Manifestations:**
- **Memory Offloading:** Using devices to store information we once memorized
- **Analysis Delegation:** Asking AI to interpret data rather than reasoning independently
- **Writing Automation:** Generating text through prompts rather than composition
- **Decision Support:** Outsourcing judgment to algorithmic recommendations
- **Navigation Dependency:** GPS replacing spatial memory and wayfinding

**Modern Risks:**
- Students using AI for essays lose writing and reasoning skills
- Professionals delegating analysis to AI lose critical judgment capacity
- Creatives using generative AI may lose imaginative capacity
- Memory offloading reduces ability to form long-term memories
- Dependency creates vulnerability when AI systems fail or are unavailable

**Mitigation:**
- Deliberate practice of cognitive skills without AI assistance
- Memory training and active recall exercises
- Independent analysis before consulting AI
- Digital fasting periods to reclaim cognitive autonomy
- Clear boundaries: AI as tool, not replacement

**Related Terms:**
- Cognitive Atrophy (skill loss from disuse)
- Extended Mind (legitimate tool use vs. replacement)
- Digital Amnesia (forgetting information stored digitally)
- Automation Bias (over-reliance on automated systems)
