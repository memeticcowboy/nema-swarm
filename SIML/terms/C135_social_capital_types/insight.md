# C135: Social Capital Types — Elemental Emphasis Questions

## ∴ (Air/Sigma — Distinction/Classification)
- How do we classify the difference between bonding ties among similar people and bridging ties across diverse groups?
- What categorical cuts separate inward-looking from outward-looking social connections?
- How do we distinguish thick trust (ascribed) from thin trust (earned) in network analysis?
- What logical framework best captures the continuum vs. binary debate in social capital typologies?

## ≈ (Water/Rho — Flow/Relation)
- What flows between horizontal peer relationships and vertical power-spanning connections?
- How does the relational dynamic link community members to institutional resources?
- What channels enable the conversion of social capital into economic or political advantage?
- How do resources circulate differently through bonding versus bridging network structures?

## ▲ (Fire/Lambda — Transformation/Catalyst)
- Where does the spark of transformation occur when weak ties convert to strong ties?
- What catalyzes the expansion of social networks across social boundaries?
- How does the strength of weak ties enable innovation and opportunity discovery?
- What ignites the shift from isolated community to interconnected ecosystem?

## 𐂷 (Wood/Beta — Growth/Development)
- What forms of growth emerge from the tension between social inclusion and exclusion?
- What developmental potential exists in cultivating diverse network ties?
- How do social networks organically evolve from dense clusters to expansive webs?
- What growth patterns characterize the maturation of community social capital?

## ☷ (Earth/Delta-Gamma — Ground/Cycle)
- What sustains the ground between community integration and fragmentation?
- What are the cyclical returns between collective solidarity and individual mobility?
- How do communities maintain cohesion while remaining open to external connections?
- What cyclical rhythms characterize the renewal and depletion of social capital?

## ⛨ (Metal/Mu — Structure/Boundary)
- What structures contain the boundary between in-group and out-group?
- What thresholds separate social capital as resource from social capital as liability?
- How do network boundaries determine who benefits from social capital and who is excluded?
- What structural holes create opportunities for brokerage and innovation?
