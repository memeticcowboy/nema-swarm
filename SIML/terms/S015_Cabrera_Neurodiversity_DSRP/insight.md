# Cabrera's Neurodiversity and DSRP
## SIML Term Entry S015
### Source: 1771907212404_262767_Dr.+Cabrera%27s+Neurodiversity+Insights.md

## Overview

Derek Cabrera's personal journey with neurodiversity became the crucible from which DSRP theory emerged. Rather than experiencing neurodiversity as a deficit — the dominant framing in clinical and educational contexts — Cabrera recognized it as a difference in cognitive processing that conferred unique strengths, particularly the ability to see more connections between ideas, patterns, and systems than neurotypical cognition typically surfaces.

This reframing is not merely personal but theoretical: DSRP (Distinctions, Systems, Relationships, Perspectives) identifies four universal cognitive operations that apply equally to neurotypical and neurodiverse thinking. The theory does not privilege one neurological profile over another but reveals the shared structural operations underlying all human cognition. What neurodiversity changes is not the operations themselves but their relative emphasis, speed, and combinatorial patterns — a neurodiverse mind may draw distinctions differently, perceive relationships more densely, or shift perspectives more fluidly. The struggle that accompanied Cabrera's own neurodiversity became the experiential ground from which a universal theory of thinking grew.
