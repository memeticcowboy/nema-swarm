## CONTEXT NOTE

Adam Grant's "Think Again" presents a framework for intellectual flexibility in a rapidly changing world. Key concepts include:

1. **The Scientist Mindset:** Approaching beliefs as hypotheses to be tested rather than truths to be defended
2. **Binary Bias:** The tendency to simplify complex issues into false dichotomies; antidote is embracing nuance
3. **Confident Humility:** The balance of believing in one's capabilities while acknowledging limitations
4. **Constructive Conflict:** Differentiating task conflict (productive disagreement about ideas) from relationship conflict (personal attacks)
5. **Identity Foreclosure:** Premature commitment to a fixed identity that prevents growth and adaptation
6. **Psychological Safety:** Creating environments where people feel safe to express doubts and admit mistakes

The framework emphasizes:
- Questioning assumptions over defending positions
- Seeking disconfirming evidence rather than confirmation
- Viewing disagreement as opportunity rather than threat
- Separating identity from beliefs ("I was wrong" ≠ "I am wrong")
