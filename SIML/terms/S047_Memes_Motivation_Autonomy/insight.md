## Overview

Self-Determination Theory (SDT) identifies three basic psychological needs — autonomy, competence, and relatedness — as the foundations of intrinsic motivation. When reinterpreted through memetic philosophy, these needs become the channels through which memes exert their most profound influence on human behavior. Memes do not merely transmit information; they shape the very motivational landscape by influencing what individuals internalize as their own goals, values, and social norms.

The dance of autonomy emerges from the tension between memetic absorption and self-determined action. Cultural memes carry expectations about how to behave, what to value, and where to belong. When these memes align with intrinsic needs, internalization feels authentic and autonomous. When they conflict, individuals experience the friction between cultural conformity and genuine self-expression. SDT's continuum from external regulation to integrated regulation maps directly onto the degree of memetic internalization — from superficial compliance with cultural memes to deep integration where external norms become genuinely one's own.

Competence and relatedness provide additional memetic pathways. Skill-promoting memes spread through mastery resonance: demonstrations of competence create memetic attractors that draw others toward similar development. Social belonging memes foster community by providing shared identity markers and relational scripts. Together, these three need-satisfying memetic channels create the motivational ecology within which individuals navigate their relationship to culture, negotiating the perpetual dance between autonomous self-expression and meaningful social participation.

## Elemental Analysis

### Air (σ) — Aerunik
The distinction between intrinsic and extrinsic motivation provides the foundational analytical axis. SDT's framework depends on clearly separating internally generated drive from externally imposed pressure, and memetic philosophy sharpens this distinction by revealing how cultural transmission can blur the boundary — making extrinsic memes feel intrinsic through deep internalization.

### Water (ρ) — Sentaria
Relatedness operates as the relational substrate of memetic motivation. Belonging memes create the social bonds that satisfy the need for connection, while memetic exclusion — being outside the meme-group — produces isolation. The relational dimension is particularly strong here because SDT positions relatedness as a basic need, making social memes powerful motivational forces.

### Fire (λ) — Jvalion
The transformative process of internalization is where memes reshape motivation most dramatically. External norms undergo a progressive transformation from mere compliance through identification to full integration, where cultural values become authentically self-endorsed. This memetic internalization is the fire that forges external cultural material into personal motivational structure.

### Wood (β) — Arboriel
Competence grows through mastery resonance — skill-promoting memes spread as individuals observe and absorb demonstrated expertise. This growth dimension captures how memetic environments can either cultivate competence through supportive scaffolding or suppress it through rigid performance expectations that undermine intrinsic interest.

### Earth (δγ) — Humavita
The cycling between autonomy and conformity is the dominant dynamic, reflected in the tendency score. Individuals do not simply choose autonomy or conformity but oscillate between them as cultural contexts shift, new memes arrive, and psychological needs fluctuate. This cycling is the dance referenced in the title — an ongoing negotiation rather than a final resolution.

### Metal (μ) — Ferrosid
Social norms provide the structural framework within which motivational dynamics unfold. These memetic architectures — encoded in institutions, traditions, and cultural expectations — create the channels through which motivation flows, constraining and enabling different forms of self-expression.

## Key Tensions
- **Autonomy vs Conformity**: Whether authentic self-determination can coexist with deep memetic internalization of cultural norms, or whether all internalization represents a subtle form of conformity
- **Intrinsic vs Extrinsic**: The blurred boundary between genuinely self-generated motivation and externally originated memes that have been so thoroughly internalized they feel intrinsic
- **Individual Mastery vs Social Belonging**: The potential conflict between competence-driven memes that promote individual excellence and relatedness-driven memes that prioritize group cohesion over individual distinction
