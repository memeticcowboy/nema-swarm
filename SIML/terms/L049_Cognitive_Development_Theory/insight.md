## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does recognizing developmental stages change our approach to education?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Piaget's theory works not by prescribing what to teach but by **revealing the possible** — showing what forms of thinking are developmentally accessible. The educator's task is not to force early development but to create conditions where stage-appropriate challenges drive natural equilibration.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Piaget's Cognitive Development Theory reveals that **cognitive growth is not additive but transformative** — each stage represents a fundamentally different way of knowing, not merely more knowledge, and understanding these structural transformations enables educators to meet learners where they actually are.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A stage theory proposing four universal, invariant sequential stages through which intelligence develops via assimilation, accommodation, and equilibration
- **How it works:** Biological maturation + experience → cognitive conflict → schema accommodation → new equilibrium at higher structural level
- **Where it aims:** Toward operational intelligence — systematic, logical, abstract reasoning
