# Orchestrated Objective Reduction

## Etymology
From Latin *orchestrare* (to arrange, organize) + *objective* (existing independently of perception) + *reduction* (return to simpler state). Roger Penrose's term for consciousness arising from orchestrated quantum wave collapse in neuronal microtubules.

## Definition
A theory of quantum consciousness proposing that conscious awareness arises from orchestrated objective reduction (Orch OR) of quantum wave functions in microtubules within neurons—collapses orchestrated by scale quantum processes and non-computable influences in spacetime geometry.

## Commentary
Orchestrated Objective Reduction represents a radical hypothesis at the intersection of quantum physics, neuroscience, and philosophy of mind. Unlike computational theories of consciousness that view mind as information processing, Orch OR posits that consciousness arises from specific quantum mechanical processes occurring within the brain's microtubular structures.

The theory challenges materialist reductionism by invoking quantum gravity effects—specifically, Penrose's objective reduction (OR), where spacetime geometry itself reaches a threshold that causes wave function collapse. This collapse is "orchestrated" by cellular structures (microtubules), suggesting consciousness is not merely an emergent property of complex computation but a fundamental feature of reality accessed through specific biological architectures.

Critically, Orch OR implies that consciousness is non-computable in the Turing sense, potentially explaining why human understanding (particularly mathematical insight) differs fundamentally from algorithmic processing. The theory remains controversial, with critics questioning whether quantum coherence can be maintained in the warm, wet environment of the brain.
