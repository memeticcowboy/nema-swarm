# C169: Bergson Duration Consciousness

## Etymology
**Duration**: From Old French *duracion* (lasting, continuance), from Latin *duratio* (a lasting, continuing), from *durare* (to harden, last, endure). Bergson's *durée* refers to the subjective, continuous flow of time as experienced by consciousness.  
**Consciousness**: From Latin *conscientia* (knowledge within oneself), from *con-* (together) + *scire* (to know). Bergson viewed consciousness as fundamentally temporal—a continuous flow inseparable from duration.  
**Free Will**: From Old English *freo* (free) + *willa* (will). Bergson argued for genuine novelty and spontaneity in human action, rejecting deterministic frameworks.

## Core Definition
Henri Bergson's philosophy posits that true understanding emerges not from static analysis but through experiencing the subjective flow of time (*duration*). Consciousness is inherently temporal—continuous, qualitative, and indivisible—resisting the spatialized, measurable time of clocks. This perspective offers fresh insights into free will (as creative evolution), Neoplatonism (dynamic emanation), and simulation theory (consciousness as temporal process rather than static computation).
