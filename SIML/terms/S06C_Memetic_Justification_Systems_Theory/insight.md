## Overview

Memetic Justification Systems Theory (mJUST) integrates Justification Systems Theory — the study of how beliefs are warranted, legitimated, and organized into coherent systems — into Memetic Philosophy. The core insight is that justification is not merely an individual epistemic activity but a cultural transmission process. When a belief becomes justified within a cultural context, it gains memetic fitness: it becomes more transmissible, more resistant to challenge, and more likely to consolidate into a cultural norm.

The framework aligns with the DIKUW+ model (Data, Information, Knowledge, Understanding, Wisdom, and beyond), mapping how raw data passes through justification processes to become cultural knowledge and ultimately wisdom. At each level of the hierarchy, justification systems operate as filters — determining which information is elevated, which is discarded, and which is transformed. Memetic transmission dynamics explain how these justified packages propagate through populations, compete with alternative justification systems, and evolve over time.

Cultural evolution, in this view, is driven not simply by the replication of memes but by the competition between justification systems. Norms persist not because they are simply repeated but because they carry justificatory weight — reasons, arguments, evidence, and narrative structures that make them compelling within their cultural context. Understanding how justification systems shape cultural norms and behaviors through transmission patterns provides a more nuanced account of cultural evolution than simple memetic replication models.

Distinguishing justified from unjustified content is the analytical starting point for understanding differential memetic success.

The transformation of unjustified memes into legitimated cultural norms is the catalytic process. Justification systems act as transformative filters, conferring legitimacy on some memetic content and withholding it from others. This transformation is not passive but involves active processes of argumentation, evidence gathering, narrative construction, and institutional endorsement.

Growth from naive memetic adoption to critical justification awareness represents memetic literacy. As individuals and cultures mature, they develop the capacity to recognize justification systems as constructed, contingent, and subject to evaluation. This critical awareness enables more deliberate engagement with cultural transmission.

Earth is co-dominant. The justification-transmission cycle is the engine of cultural evolution. Justifications are constructed, transmitted, encountered by new audiences, tested against local conditions and competing justifications, revised, and retransmitted. This iterative cycling drives the ongoing evolution of cultural norms and behaviors.

### Metal (μ) — Ferrosid

Metal is co-dominant. The DIKUW+ framework and memetic architecture provide the structural scaffolding within which justification systems operate. These are not informal processes but structured hierarchies — institutional, epistemic, and cultural architectures that organize how information is justified, elevated, and transmitted at scale.

## Key Tensions

- **Individual Justification vs Cultural Transmission**: The tension between justification as a personal epistemic activity (evaluating evidence, constructing arguments) and justification as a cultural transmission process (norms propagating through social networks regardless of individual evaluation).
- **Justified Persistence vs Memetic Fitness**: The question of whether cultural norms persist because they are genuinely well-justified or because their justification systems are rhetorically effective — raising the possibility that memetically fit justifications may not be epistemically sound.
- **DIKUW+ Hierarchy vs Flat Memetic Replication**: The tension between the ordered hierarchy of the DIKUW+ model (data must become information, then knowledge, then wisdom) and the reality of memetic transmission, where content at any level can propagate without passing through the full justificatory hierarchy.
