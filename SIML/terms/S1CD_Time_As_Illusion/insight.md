# Insight — Time as Illusion: Quantum Proposals (S1CD)

## Overview
Time remains one of the deepest mysteries in physics. Neither Einstein's relativity nor quantum mechanics fully explains its nature. Some physicists propose time is an illusion, an emergent property from a fundamentally timeless universe.

The Page-Wootters mechanism (1983) suggests time emerges through quantum entanglement. The universe as a giant quantum wave function encodes everything atemporally; time appears when subsystems become entangled and observe each other. Physicist Paola Verrucchi suggests measurement collapse, which collapses quantum possibilities into definite values, may be key to understanding time's flow. The arrow of time becomes a record of what has been measured.

Black holes are proposed as potential universal quantum clocks due to their entanglement with the outside world. Research directions include studying quantum clock limitations, connecting quantum physics with cosmology, and exploring implications for neuropsychiatric disorders and the second law of thermodynamics.
