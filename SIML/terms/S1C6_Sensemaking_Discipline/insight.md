# Insight — The Sensemaking Discipline (S1C6)

## Overview
This entry encodes a four-stage discipline for navigating complexity without freezing meaning into dogma. The discipline centers on the lifecycle of a frame: born from honest confusion, used as a temporary coordination tool, acted upon with monitoring, and eventually dissolved through mandatory metabolic reopening.

Stage 1 (Confusion) treats uncertainty as starting condition rather than problem to solve. Stage 2 (Working Frame) creates a temporary handle that narrows attention enough to move while leaving visible seams. Stage 3 (Action) moves with the frame rather than inside it, taking small reversible bets with active monitoring for surprise. Stage 4 (Mandatory Return) reopens the loop, preventing momentum from hardening into dogma.

The success metric is not the correctness of any frame but its revisability without shame or collapse. Treating coherence as scaffolding and revision as competence marks the difference between healthy sensemaking and dogmatic capture.

## Key Tensions
1. **Confusion vs Compression** — Honest uncertainty versus panic framing.
2. **Frame Loyalty vs Frame Literacy** — Inhabiting versus operating frames.
3. **Freezing vs Moving** — Being held by versus holding the frame.
4. **Momentum vs Reopening** — Productive inertia versus mandatory revision.
5. **Scaffolding vs Cage** — Temporary support versus permanent capture.
