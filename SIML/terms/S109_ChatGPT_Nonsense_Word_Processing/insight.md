# Insight — ChatGPT Nonsense Word Processing (S109)

## Overview

Psycholinguist Michael Vitevitch, whose career has centered on phonological neighborhood effects in human language processing, turned his experimental apparatus toward ChatGPT to investigate whether large language models process language through the same mechanisms as human speakers. By deploying nonsense words — phonological forms stripped of semantic content — Vitevitch created a controlled probe that forces any language-processing system to reveal its raw pattern-matching substrate rather than relying on memorized associations.

The results were striking and consistently pointed toward fundamental divergence. ChatGPT defined words that have been extinct for centuries, suggesting access to archival text corpora rather than living language knowledge. When asked to perform phonological neighborhood tasks (listing words that differ by one phoneme), ChatGPT spontaneously switched languages — treating English and Spanish phonological space as a continuous statistical manifold rather than discrete systems. Most remarkably, when asked to generate new nonsense words, ChatGPT produced forms by visibly recombining existing morphemes, a strategy humans rarely employ in comparable tasks.

These findings position nonsense words as uniquely powerful diagnostic tools for distinguishing human cognitive architecture from AI processing strategies. Where humans rely on embodied articulatory memory, emotional associations, and phonological intuitions shaped by developmental experience, ChatGPT relies on distributional co-occurrence patterns across massive text corpora. The outputs may overlap, but the generative processes are fundamentally incommensurable — a fact that only becomes visible when semantic content is deliberately removed from the experimental stimulus.

## Key Tensions

- **Human pattern recognition vs. AI statistical inference:** Humans process phonology through embodied articulatory-acoustic circuits; AI processes it through distributional co-occurrence matrices. Same input, incommensurable mechanisms.

- **Semantic understanding vs. statistical proximity:** ChatGPT's ability to define extinct words reveals access without comprehension — proximity in embedding space masquerading as knowledge.

- **Real words vs. nonsense words as diagnostics:** Real words allow human and AI processing to converge in output; nonsense words strip away this convergence to expose the underlying divergence.

- **Human language grounding vs. AI probabilistic embedding:** Human words are grounded in sensorimotor experience, social interaction, and developmental history; AI words are points in a high-dimensional probability space trained on text alone.

- **Experimental rigor vs. interpretive overreach:** The temptation to anthropomorphize AI language behaviors must be disciplined by clear falsifiability criteria and mechanistic specificity.
