# Insight — Whitehead Actual Occasions (S158)

## Overview

Alfred North Whitehead's actual occasions represent a radical reconception of reality's fundamental units. An actual occasion is one indivisible pulse of becoming: it receives influence from the whole past through prehension, decides how to unify those influences into a unique character, and then becomes new influence for everything following. There is no enduring "thing" underlying the act of becoming — process replaces substance as the fundamental ontological category.

Experience is central to all actuality in Whitehead's scheme, not exclusive to conscious minds. Even the most elementary events — quantum interactions at the subatomic level — are actual occasions possessing a proto-experiential character. This panexperientialism (sometimes called panpsychism, though Whitehead's version is more nuanced) places feeling, not matter, at the base of reality. Each occasion feels its past, unifies those feelings into a novel synthesis, and contributes that synthesis to the future.

Reality is understood as a series of interconnected moments of creative advance into novelty. Each actual occasion undergoes concrescence — the process of growing together into a unified experience — then perishes as subjective immediacy. But this perishing is not annihilation; the occasion achieves objective immortality as datum for all subsequent occasions. The rhythm of becoming and perishing, concrescence and transition, constitutes the pulse of reality. Creativity — the universal of universals in Whitehead's metaphysics — is the principle by which the many become one and are increased by one, an endless advance into novelty.

## Key Tensions

1. **Enduring substance vs. momentary becoming** — The traditional assumption of permanent substances underlying change versus Whitehead's recognition that reality consists of indivisible pulses of becoming with no persistent thing beneath the process
2. **Isolated entity vs. constitutive relation** — Self-contained objects existing independently versus actual occasions constituted by their prehensive relations, each inheriting the whole past and contributing to the whole future
3. **Static being vs. creative advance** — Reality as fixed and determined versus reality as creative advance into genuine novelty, with each occasion's decision introducing something unprecedented into the world
4. **Material mechanism vs. panexperientialism** — Matter as inert substance moved by external forces versus experience as central to all actuality, with even quantum events possessing proto-experiential character
5. **Subjective becoming vs. objective immortality** — The occasion's living immediacy in concrescence versus its perishing into objective immortality as datum for the future, creating the rhythmic pulse of reality's advance
