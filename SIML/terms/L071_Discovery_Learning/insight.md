## 6. Insight Generation

### 6.1 Core Question

How does the process of discovery create understanding that differs from direct instruction?

### 6.2 Hypothesis

Discovery Learning works not by delivering knowledge but by **architecting insight** — creating conditions where learners must generate hypotheses, test possibilities, and recognize patterns for themselves. The cognitive effort of discovery encodes knowledge more deeply and creates transferable inquiry skills that pure reception cannot develop.

### 6.3 One-Sentence Insight

> Bruner's Discovery Learning reveals that learners construct deeper, more transferable understanding when they actively discover principles through hypothesis generation and exploration — transforming learning from passive reception into generative inquiry.

### 6.4 Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A constructivist learning approach where learners actively discover principles and relationships through structured problem-solving, hypothesis generation, and self-directed exploration rather than direct instruction
- **How it works:** Structured problems activate curiosity → learners generate and test hypotheses → exploration reveals patterns → insight moments lead to knowledge construction → understanding integrates into cognitive structure
- **Where it aims:** Toward autonomous inquiry capability — developing learners who can investigate, discover, and construct knowledge independently, with understanding that transfers across contexts
