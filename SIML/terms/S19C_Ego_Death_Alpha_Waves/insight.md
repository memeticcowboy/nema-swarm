# Insight — Ego Death and Alpha Waves (S19C)

## Overview
Research published in the Journal of Neuroscience reveals that DMT significantly suppresses alpha wave activity in the brain, with this suppression strongly correlating with subjective reports of ego dissolution. Alpha waves normally maintain the critical balance between chaos and order necessary for coherent self-related thoughts; DMT shifts the brain toward subcritical states, disrupting narrative continuity and fragmenting the sense of self.

Under DMT influence, individuals experience intense present-moment focus, loss of self-referential thought, and dissolution of the boundary between self and world. The increased neural entropy and reduced complexity provide measurable correlates of subjective self-dissolution, offering a novel approach to measuring consciousness states. These findings position alpha dynamics as the neural scaffolding of personal identity.

The therapeutic implications are significant: understanding how psychedelics affect self-perception could advance treatment for depression, PTSD, and other conditions where rigid self-narratives contribute to suffering. The research also opens pathways for neurotechnology development aimed at assessing and modulating brainwave patterns.
