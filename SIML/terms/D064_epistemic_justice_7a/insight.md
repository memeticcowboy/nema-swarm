## Design Implications

### For Civic Platforms
- **Multimodal Expression Layers**: Enable voice through symbol, video, metaphor — not just analytical text
- **Contextual Credentials**: Rewire legitimacy through social trust, care labor, affective proximity
- **Epistemic Audits**: Institutional reflexivity — systematically ask whose knowledge is missing

### For DAOs
- **Co-Governed Data Commons**: Communities steward and interpret their own informational artifacts
- **Civic Meme Labs**: Enable narrative co-creation and counter-hegemonic symbolic tools
- **Reflexivity Membranes**: Self-assess knowledge flows through ritualized reflection

### Risk Vectors
- **Performative Pluralism**: Inclusion becomes aesthetic; marginalized voices invited but not centered
- **Over-Symbolization**: Excessive focus on narrative occludes practical constraints
- **Platform Lock-in**: Inclusion tools deployed inside infrastructures still coded by dominant epistemologies
- **Cognitive Overload**: Over-democratization without scaffolding creates incoherence

## Three-Bullet Expansion

- **What:** A framework recognizing testimonial injustice (being heard) and hermeneutical injustice (having concepts to express experience) — centering lived experience, narrative, and symbolic expression as valid epistemic modes
- **How it feels:** Validating yet vulnerable, empowering yet exhausting — the relief of being understood mixed with the labor of educating others about one's reality
- **Where it aims:** Toward epistemic liberation where marginalized voices co-author the frames of knowing — but with risks of performative pluralism, over-symbolization, and platform lock-in
