# DSRP Theory of Thinking
## SIML Term Entry S005
### Source: 1771907212350_232644_DSRP+Theory+of+Thinking.md

## Overview

DSRP theory, developed by Derek Cabrera, identifies four universal patterns underlying all cognition: **Distinctions** (identifying what something is and is not), **Systems** (understanding parts and wholes), **Relationships** (identifying connections between elements), and **Perspectives** (viewing from different angles). These four operations are proposed as the fundamental cognitive building blocks that, when applied deliberately, enhance clarity of thought and problem-solving capacity.

The framework is notable for its simplicity and universality—each pattern applies recursively and can be combined with others. Making a Distinction creates a System (identity and other), which has Relationships (between parts), each viewable from multiple Perspectives. This recursive nesting enables DSRP to scale from simple classification tasks to complex systems analysis.

## Key Tensions

- **Identity ↔ Other**: The foundational distinction that creates all further structure
- **Part ↔ Whole**: The systems tension between component and composite
- **Point ↔ View**: Each perspective both reveals and conceals
- **Simple ↔ Complex**: Recursive application deepens understanding but risks over-analysis
