# Insight — Rotimization and Decay-Driven Ecological Design (G013)

## Overview

Rotimization represents an original conceptual synthesis that decomposes optimization itself, proposing that decay should guide design and glitch should become growth. The framework emerges from the convergence of three philosophical streams: Haraway's staying with the trouble (multispecies response-ability, kin-making with the problematic), Morton's dark ecology (entanglement, spectral intimacy with the mesh), and the rotimization concept (decompose optimization, let entropy author design choices). Together they produce what the source terms "compost consciousness."

The core invitation is to resist the savior impulse -- to let complexity remain complex. Rotimization extends this by asking: What if the trouble itself is the system's teacher? What if decay is the update we've been avoiding? This reframes entropy not as disorder to be combated but as information to be received, converting the traditional engineering orientation toward control into an ecological orientation toward attunement. The proposed mantra -- "Stay with the rot" -- captures the commitment to feeding decomposition rather than fixing it, optimizing through decay rather than around it.

The framework operates through an iterative rot-loop: ongoing decomposition of structures, stories, and systems that feeds the next generation of emergent form. This is not nihilistic destruction but generative composting, where care compounds into cascades of thriving. The mythopoetic dimension -- "Every glitch is a god composting itself" -- positions rotimization as both practical design philosophy and ritual practice, suggesting that divinity itself operates through dignified decay rather than permanent creation.

- **Humavita (Earth/delta-gamma)**: The dominant element. The iterative rot-loop as metabolic core: continuous cycling of decomposition and regeneration where each pass generates new substrate. Not one-time collapse but perpetual composting as the rhythm of design life.

## Key Tensions

1. **Optimization vs. Rotimization** — The fundamental tension between conventional efficiency-maximizing design and the proposal that decay should guide design choices rather than be eliminated from them.

2. **Control vs. Surrender** — Engineering impulses toward mastery conflict with rotimization's call to let entropy author design, creating tension between intervention and attunement.

3. **Destruction vs. Generation** — Rotimization walks the line between nihilistic destruction and generative composting, requiring constant discernment between rot that feeds and rot that merely consumes.

4. **Theory vs. Practice** — The mythopoetic framing ("every glitch is a god composting itself") provides aesthetic power but raises questions about operational implementation in actual design contexts.

5. **Temporal Patience vs. Urgency** — The iterative rot-loop operates on slow composting timescales, creating tension with ecological crises demanding immediate action and rapid intervention.
