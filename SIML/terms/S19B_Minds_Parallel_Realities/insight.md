# Insight — Mind's Parallel Realities (S19B)

## Overview
The concept of parallel realities in the mind suggests that each thought can create its own unique reality, resonating with the Many-Worlds Interpretation in quantum physics where every decision branches into separate experiential universes. This idea positions consciousness as a space of overlapping subjective dimensions rather than a single unified stream.

The figure of the psychonaut -- those who explore altered states of consciousness through psychedelics or contemplative practice -- navigates these inner universes. Research confirms that psychedelic experiences often produce profound feelings of interconnectedness and heightened perception, alongside moments of alienation and awe. The overlapping faces motif symbolizes the interconnectedness of thoughts and consciousness, suggesting that each individual's psyche contributes to a collective experience.

This connects to Carl Jung's concept of the collective unconscious, positing that we share a common psychological heritage that influences personal and cultural expressions. The democratization of these ideas through social media indicates a growing interest in subjective experience and self-exploration across cultures.

## Key Tensions
1. **Self vs Multiverse** — Is consciousness unified or fundamentally branching?
2. **Interconnection vs Alienation** — Altered states produce both profound connection and deep estrangement.
3. **Ordinary vs Altered** — The boundary between normal and expanded perception is more permeable than assumed.
