# Insight — Sensemaking Experts Across Disciplines (S0A0)

## Overview

The source surveys key experts and concepts in sensemaking across multiple disciplines, mapping the field's interdisciplinary landscape. Karl E. Weick introduced sensemaking to organizational studies, emphasizing retrospective meaning-making and the construction of plausible images to rationalize action. Brenda Dervin pioneered sensemaking methodology in information science, focusing on how individuals bridge cognitive gaps. Christian Madsbjerg advocates for humanities-based thinking in business, positioning human intuition against algorithmic approaches. Dave Snowden developed the Cynefin framework and SenseMaker tools for navigating complex systems. Gary A. Klein explored naturalistic decision-making in high-stakes environments. Daniel M. Russell contributed to sensemaking in human-computer interaction through interactive system design.

The survey identifies emerging contributors including R.J. Cordes (memetic warfare and pandemic sensemaking) and Thomas Aston (organizational learning and evidence integration). A comparative analysis reveals the field spanning foundational theorists, applied practitioners, and contemporary commentators, with ongoing debates about expert qualification given sensemaking's breadth across psychology, sociology, computer science, and organizational theory.

## Key Tensions

1. **Disciplinary ownership vs. interdisciplinary convergence** — Whether sensemaking belongs to any single field or is inherently a convergence point where multiple disciplines meet
2. **Foundational theory vs. applied practice** — The tension between theoretical purity (Weick's academic framework) and practical application (Snowden's SenseMaker tools, Madsbjerg's business consulting)
3. **Individual cognition vs. social construction** — Whether sensemaking is primarily an individual cognitive process (Klein, Dervin) or a socially constructed phenomenon (Weick's emphasis on organizational retrospection)
4. **Human intuition vs. algorithmic approaches** — Madsbjerg's explicit positioning of humanities-based sensemaking against data-driven algorithmic decision-making
5. **Expert qualification vs. field breadth** — The unresolved debate about who qualifies as a sensemaking expert given the field's interdisciplinary sprawl and the distinction between core researchers and commentators
