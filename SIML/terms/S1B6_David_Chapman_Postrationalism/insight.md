# Insight — David Chapman: Postrationalist Meaning-Making (S1B6)

## Overview
David Chapman represents a key figure in the postrationalist intellectual landscape, bridging Buddhist philosophy, cognitive science, and critiques of strict rationalism. His major project Meaningness explores how meaning operates as fluid process rather than fixed property, proposing that people adopt "stances" toward meaning that can be constructive or confused.

Chapman's work critiques both nihilism (meaning is impossible) and eternalism (meaning is fixed and knowable), aiming for a "complete stance" that acknowledges uncertainty while remaining participatory. His concept of meta-systematicity describes the developmental capacity to operate across and between systems rather than within any single one.

His interdisciplinary background in AI and cognitive science at MIT informs his philosophical approach, blending technical analytical clarity with phenomenological and cultural depth. Within the rationalist diaspora, Chapman is regarded as a leading postrationalist voice bridging scientific rationality and existential philosophy.

## Key Tensions
1. **Rationalism vs Postrationalism** — Algorithmic reduction of meaning versus participatory fluid engagement.
2. **Nihilism vs Eternalism** — Meaning-denial versus meaning-fixation as twin traps.
3. **System vs Meta-System** — Operating within frameworks versus operating across them.
4. **Clarity vs Uncertainty** — Analytical precision alongside honest acknowledgment of not-knowing.
