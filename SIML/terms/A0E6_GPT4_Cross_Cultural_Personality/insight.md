## Overview

GPT-4 Cross Cultural Personality examines the capacity of large language models to replicate cross-cultural personality differences, specifically comparing simulated US and South Korean personality profiles using the Big Five framework (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism). The research reveals that GPT-4 can capture directional differences between cultural groups — scoring US profiles higher on certain traits and Korean profiles higher on others in ways that roughly align with empirical human data.

However, the simulation exhibits systematic distortions that reveal fundamental limitations. GPT-4 shows an upward bias, consistently producing elevated personality scores compared to human norms. It demonstrates lower variance, compressing the range of responses into a narrower band than human populations exhibit. Most critically, the structural validity of the simulated personality profiles does not fully replicate human factor structures — the internal relationships between personality dimensions differ from what psychometric research consistently finds in human populations.

These findings illuminate a broader question about AI and psychological simulation. The ability to mimic directional differences between cultural groups does not constitute understanding of those differences. GPT-4 appears to be reproducing statistical regularities from its training data rather than generating personality profiles from any model of cultural psychology. The gap between pattern reproduction and genuine simulation marks a boundary that current AI architecture may not be able to cross without fundamentally different approaches to representing cultural and psychological knowledge.

## Key Tensions

- **Pattern Mimicry vs Genuine Simulation**: Whether GPT-4's ability to reproduce directional cultural differences constitutes meaningful simulation or merely reflects statistical regularities in training data.
- **Upward Bias vs Calibrated Output**: The systematic tendency toward elevated scores versus the need for accurate calibration against human population norms.
- **Structural Validity vs Surface Accuracy**: The model can approximate mean differences between cultures while failing to replicate the internal factor structure that gives those differences their psychological meaning.
