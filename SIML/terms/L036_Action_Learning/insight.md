# L036: Action Learning — Core Insight

## The Essential Understanding

Action Learning, developed by Reg Revans, represents a profound shift in how we understand the relationship between knowledge and action. The simple formula **L = P + Q** (Learning = Programmed Knowledge + Questioning Insight) encodes a radical critique of traditional education: that knowledge alone is insufficient, and that wisdom emerges only when we commit to action in the face of uncertainty, supported by peers who help us see what we cannot see alone.

## Why This Matters

### The Problem with "Learning First, Acting Later"

Traditional approaches assume we must understand before we do. Action Learning inverts this: **we learn through doing, and we do while still uncertain**. This is not recklessness — it is the recognition that complex problems cannot be solved by analysis alone. They require **committed experimentation**.

### The Power of the Set

The small group (set) is not incidental — it is essential. We are blind to our own assumptions. We need others to:
- Ask questions we wouldn't ask ourselves
- Reflect back what we cannot see
- Hold us accountable to our commitments
- Provide emotional support for the risk of action

### The Discipline of Questioning

Revans insisted on **questions before statements**. This is harder than it sounds. Our instinct is to give advice, share experience, solve problems. Action Learning requires the discipline of **inquiry over telling** — because only through our own questions do we truly learn.

## The Double Gift

Action Learning gives us two things simultaneously:

1. **Solutions to real problems** — The immediate pragmatic value
2. **Capacity for future learning** — The development of wisdom

This is what Revans meant by "double-loop learning" — we don't just solve the problem; we learn how to learn from problems.

## The Risk of Capture

Action Learning can fail when:
- **It becomes therapy** — Losing the fire of action in endless reflection
- **It becomes advice** — Set members telling rather than questioning
- **It becomes academic** — Working on hypothetical rather than real problems
- **It becomes rigid** — Following the method without attending to the field

## The Core Practice

```
THE ACTION LEARNING DISCIPLINE

1. Bring a REAL problem — not a puzzle, not a case study, but something
   that matters to you, that you don't know how to solve

2. Commit to ACTION — between sessions, you will do something, try
   something, step into the unknown

3. Submit to QUESTIONING — the set asks; you answer. No advice. No
   "have you tried..." Just questions that deepen your inquiry.

4. Engage in REFLECTION — what happened? So what? Now what?

5. Return to the SET — share results, receive fresh questions, commit
   to next action

6. Repeat — until the problem transforms or you transform
```

## The Ultimate Insight

> **We do not learn from experience. We learn from reflecting on experience.**

Action Learning makes reflection social and accountable. It creates a structure where we cannot hide from our commitments, where our peers help us see our blind spots, and where the fire of action keeps us from getting lost in analysis.

This is learning as **courageous practice** — the willingness to act without certainty, to be seen in our struggles, and to grow through the disciplined cycle of commitment and reflection.

## One Question to Carry

*What problem am I avoiding because I don't feel ready — and who could help me take the first step?*
