# Insight — Reframing Johari: I-Tube and Ecosystems (S1BB)

## Overview
This entry encodes a fundamental reframe of the Johari Window through the I-Tube model. Johari collapses three distinct domains into two axes and assumes a single self, a single other, and knowledge as binary state. The I-Tube model asks instead: where is the signal currently resident, and which interfaces can it traverse?

The five-layer topology replaces Johari's four panes: It-Field (undifferentiated potential), My-Stream (felt but unspeakable, accessible somatically not semantically), I-Tube (articulated and filtered through translation, throttling, knotting, untwisting), We-Sphere (relationally transformed, acquiring social meaning independent of intent), and Other-Sphere (abstracted, indexed, scored, enforced).

The key insight is that Johari tells you who knows what, while the I-Tube tells you what can become knowable without breaking coherence. This preserves agency, timing, and somatic truth against the premature disclosure, identity capture, and governance without grounding that Johari logic invites.

## Key Tensions
1. **Static vs Dynamic** — Johari's fixed panes versus the I-Tube's flowing topology.
2. **Binary vs Gradient** — Known/unknown binary versus articulation gradients.
3. **Hidden vs Unarticulated** — Johari's Hidden pane conflates deliberate withholding with pre-verbal somatic content.
4. **Blind vs Emergent** — Johari's Blind Spot versus emergent attribution that never existed as stable internal object.
5. **Legibility vs Grounding** — Other-Sphere metrics that exist about you but not in you.
