# Insight — Posthumanism Human Agency (S129)

## Overview

Posthumanism challenges the Western Enlightenment framework that positions humans as uniquely rational, autonomous agents separate from the natural world. By emphasizing entanglement with nonhuman entities — animals, technologies, environments, material systems — posthumanist thought demands a fundamental rethinking of discourse boundaries. The concept reframes agency not as an individual human property but as a distributed, relational capacity that emerges through networks of interaction. For discourse studies, this means examining how language and materiality co-produce social realities rather than treating language as a purely human symbolic system.

## Key Tensions

- **Human exceptionalism vs. posthuman entanglement**: The core tension between maintaining human distinctiveness and recognizing radical interdependence with nonhuman systems.
- **Subject vs. object**: Posthumanism dissolves the clean separation between knowing subject and known object, raising questions about who or what can be an agent.
- **Language vs. materiality**: Discourse analysis traditionally privileges language, but posthumanism insists that material forces shape meaning as much as symbolic systems do.
- **Individual agency vs. relational networks**: If agency is distributed across networks, what happens to ethical responsibility and political accountability?
