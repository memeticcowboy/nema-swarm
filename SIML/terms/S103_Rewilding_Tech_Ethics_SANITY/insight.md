# Insight — Rewilding Tech Ethics via SANITY (S103)

## Overview

The SANITY framework — Soundness, Alignment, Nurturance, Integrity, Transparency, Yielding — represents a comprehensive attempt to rewild technology ethics by replacing the extractive attention economy with an ecology of mindful engagement. Rather than treating technology ethics as a set of constraints imposed on otherwise profit-maximizing systems, SANITY reimagines the foundational purposes of technology design. The framework asks not "how do we make this platform less harmful?" but "what would technology look like if it were grown from ethical soil?"

Central to this rewilding is the integration of slow media philosophy with user-centric design principles. Slow media rejects the assumption that faster dissemination, broader reach, and more frequent engagement are inherently good. Instead, it proposes that meaningful human-technology interaction requires temporal spaciousness — time for reflection, discernment, and genuine connection. When combined with decentralized architectures, slow media design distributes both power and attention across networks rather than concentrating them in extractive platforms.

The SANITY framework maps six functional capacities onto technology design. Soundness ensures epistemic grounding — that information architectures support clarity rather than confusion. Alignment creates value coherence between users, developers, and communities. Nurturance builds relational trust into interaction patterns. Integrity maintains ethical consistency even when market pressures push toward compromise. Transparency opens algorithmic and design processes to examination. Yielding allows adaptive responsiveness, making space for emergence and organic growth rather than rigid optimization.

## Elemental Analysis

- **Aerunik (Air/sigma)**: The discerning perception that distinguishes extractive attention capture from mindful engagement — seeing through the normalized patterns of addictive design, dark patterns, and attention monetization to recognize them as domestication of human attention. The air element clears the fog of techno-optimism that obscures extractive dynamics.
- **Sentaria (Water/rho)**: The relational flow between centralized platforms and decentralized trust networks — sensing how power, data, and agency move through technological architectures. Water reveals that trust cannot be extracted and stored; it must flow through relationships, and decentralized design honors this flow.
- **Jvalion (Fire/lambda)**: The catalytic transformation from speed optimization to slow media ethics — the revelatory burning away of the assumption that faster, bigger, and more frequent are always better. Fire transforms the temporal logic of technology from velocity to depth, from virality to meaning.
- **Arboriel (Wood/beta)**: The organic growth from monoculture UI to rewilded design — expanding interface ecology from A/B-tested uniformity to diverse, adaptive interaction patterns that allow users genuine agency. Wood reminds that design should grow like forests, not like factory farms.
- **Humavita (Earth/delta-gamma)**: The grounding oscillation between engagement metrics and ethical coherence — cycling between quantitative measurement (clicks, impressions, conversion) and qualitative assessment (nourishment, alignment, transparency). Earth holds the tension between what can be counted and what counts.
- **Ferrosid (Metal/mu)**: The structural architecture of the SANITY framework itself — providing the durable, principled scaffolding that holds rewilded ethics in coherent form across the six dimensions. Metal ensures that ethical principles are not merely aspirational but architecturally embedded.

## Key Tensions

1. **Speed vs. mindfulness** — Optimization for rapid engagement and viral spread versus slow media principles that prioritize reflection, depth, and intentional interaction
2. **Centralized vs. decentralized** — Platform architectures that concentrate power and data versus distributed systems that spread trust and agency across networks
3. **Engagement metrics vs. ethical coherence** — Measuring success through clicks, time-on-site, and conversion versus measuring it through alignment, nurturance, and integrity
4. **Technological growth vs. organic adaptability** — Scaling through optimization and uniformity versus growing through ecological diversity and adaptive responsiveness
5. **Corporate architecture vs. SANITY framework** — Profit-driven design logic versus the six-principle ethical framework that regrounds technology in relational and ecological values
