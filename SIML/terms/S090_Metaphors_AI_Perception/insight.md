# Insight — Metaphors AI Perception (S090)

## Overview

Lance Eliot's article explores how metaphors used to describe AI profoundly influence public understanding, interaction dynamics, and the development and regulation of AI systems. Common metaphors for AI — "helpful assistant," "monster," "child," "god," and "mirror" — each carry distinct implications for how AI is perceived and treated. The "friend" metaphor can lead to misplaced trust, while "monster" evokes fear and skepticism. The "child" metaphor may lead to leniency in expectations, while "god" can result in dangerous over-reliance.

The analysis reveals that metaphors are not neutral descriptions but active constructors of reality. Developers may unconsciously embed these metaphors into AI systems, shaping technological behavior in ways that reflect metaphorical assumptions rather than technical necessity. AI itself can adopt metaphors that influence its responses, creating a self-referential feedback loop. A recent study identified 20 dominant metaphors shaping public understanding with significant demographic differences, highlighting the need for AI practitioners to be mindful of language. Cultural variations in AI metaphors across different countries offer insights into global attitudes and suggest that responsible AI development requires deliberate attention to metaphorical framing.

## Key Tensions

1. **Description vs. construction** — Whether metaphors merely describe AI or actively construct the reality of how AI is perceived and built
2. **Anthropomorphism vs. accuracy** — The tendency to attribute human-like qualities to AI through metaphor, leading to unrealistic expectations versus the need for accurate representation
3. **Cultural universality vs. variation** — Whether AI metaphors are universal or culturally specific, and how global AI governance can account for diverse metaphorical frameworks
4. **Developer intent vs. unconscious embedding** — The gap between intentional AI design and the unconscious embedding of metaphorical assumptions into system behavior
5. **Public engagement vs. manipulation** — The fine line between using metaphors to make AI accessible to the public and using them to manipulate perception for commercial or political ends
