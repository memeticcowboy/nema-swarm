# Insight — IIT vs Computational-Functionalism (S0A1)

## Overview

The source examines Integrated Information Theory (IIT) as a challenge to the dominant computational-functionalist paradigm in neuroscience. IIT begins with subjective experience as its starting point, aiming to define the presence and quality of consciousness in objective, testable terms through a mathematical framework. This consciousness-first approach contrasts sharply with computational-functionalism, which treats consciousness as an emergent byproduct of information processing.

The theory, primarily associated with Giulio Tononi, is backed by decades of conceptual, mathematical, and empirical development. IIT quantifies consciousness through integrated information (phi), proposing that consciousness arises from systems whose information integration cannot be decomposed into independent parts. The source highlights a paradigm crisis in neuroscience where computational models fail to address the hard problem of consciousness, positions IIT as a serious contender despite critics labeling it pseudoscientific, and explores interdisciplinary implications for philosophy, psychology, and artificial intelligence, including ethical considerations around machine consciousness.

## Key Tensions

1. **Consciousness-first vs. computation-first** — The core paradigmatic tension between IIT's insistence that subjective experience is primary and functionalism's treatment of consciousness as emergent from computational processes
2. **Subjective experience vs. objective testability** — How IIT bridges the gap between the inherently subjective nature of consciousness and the demand for objective, empirically testable scientific theories
3. **Scientific rigor vs. pseudoscience accusation** — The contested status of IIT between proponents citing decades of mathematical and empirical development and critics dismissing it as unfalsifiable
4. **Integrated information vs. computational simulation** — Whether consciousness requires genuine information integration or can be replicated through computational simulation, with profound implications for AI consciousness
5. **Paradigm maintenance vs. paradigm revolution** — The tension between maintaining the productive computational-functionalist research program and acknowledging its failure to address the hard problem of consciousness
