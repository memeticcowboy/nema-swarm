## ELEMENT-SPECIFIC PROMPT VARIATIONS

### For Narrative Questions
```
Explore the story of [TOPIC]:
- What narrative is currently dominant?
- Where is that narrative failing?
- What alternative story wants to be told?
- What would a generative reframing look like?
```

### For Possibility Questions
```
Investigate the possibility space of [TOPIC]:
- What is possible beyond current constraints?
- What new forms want to emerge?
- What would breakthrough look like?
- What evolution is being prevented?
```

### For Constraint/Creativity Questions
```
Examine the creative constraints of [TOPIC]:
- What constraints enable generativity?
- What constraints block evolution?
- Where is there too much freedom (chaos)?
- Where is there too little freedom (stagnation)?
```

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Personal Growth
```
Research the narrative dimensions of personal development:

Focus on:
- What growth story are you currently living?
- Where is that narrative limiting vs. enabling?
- What alternative story of becoming wants to emerge?
- What would a generative reframing of your path look like?
- What constraints are protecting you? What are constraining you?

Key terms: #8C4D (narrative), #E2A7 (possibility), #F1B3 (constraint)

Return: Insights about what story wants to be told — generative possibilities for who you could become.
```

### Example 2: Organizational Innovation
```
Explore the evolutionary dimensions of [organization/field]:

Focus on:
- What innovation narrative is dominant? Where is it failing?
- What new forms of organizing want to emerge?
- What would breakthrough look like beyond current models?
- What constraints enable creativity vs. prevent it?
- What evolution is the system trying to make?

Key terms: #A3F7 (innovation), #D8E4 (emergence), #B9C2 (structure)

Return: Understanding of what could become — generative possibilities for the organization's evolution.
```
