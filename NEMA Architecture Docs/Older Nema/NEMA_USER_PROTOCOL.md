# NEMA USER PROTOCOL
## Notice → Engage → Muse → Activate

**For:** Participants in NEMA SWARM collaborative contemplative sessions  
**Purpose:** Guide your journey through elemental processing and thread generation

---

## WHAT IS NEMA?

NEMA is the four-stage protocol that structures your interaction with the Elemental GPTs and your contribution to the collective weave.

| Stage | Full Name | What Happens |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| **N** | NOTICE | The element senses the field through its unique lens |
| **E** | ENGAGE | The element asks questions, you respond, insights emerge |
| **M** | MUSE | The element holds something open — a question for the group |
| **A** | ACTIVATE | You generate a compressed thread to bring to NEMA SWARM |

---

## THE FOUR STAGES EXPLAINED

### STAGE 1: NOTICE
**"What does the element sense?"**

When you begin your session with an Element GPT, share what's alive for you — the pattern, question, or tension you're holding.

The element will:
- Scan the field through its dimensional lens (χ for Air, Q for Water, etc.)
- Notice what stands out from its perspective
- Report what it senses without claiming completeness

**Your role:** Share what's present. Don't worry about being clear — the element is the clarity-finder.

**Example opening:**
> "I'm here for the [topic] challenge. What's alive for me is..."

---

### STAGE 2: ENGAGE
**"What emerges through dialogue?"**

The element will ask questions based on what it noticed. These questions are designed to:
- Reveal what its lens makes visible
- Surface patterns you might have missed
- Open space for insight

**Your role:** Respond honestly. There's no wrong answer. The element is exploring with you, not testing you.

**How to respond:**
- Answer one question at a time, or all at once — your choice
- Share what actually comes up, not what you think "should" come up
- Let the dialogue move where it wants to move

---

### STAGE 3: MUSE
**"What remains open?"**

After the engagement, the element will hold something open — typically:
- A question that requires other elemental perspectives
- A tension that doesn't resolve easily
- Something that wants to be metabolized by the group

**Your role:** Sit with it. Don't rush to resolve. The open question itself is valuable.

**Key insight:** The element doesn't solve your problem. It reveals what needs the group's collective intelligence.

---

### STAGE 4: ACTIVATE
**"Generate the thread."**

When you're ready, say:
> **"Activate Thread"**

The element will:
1. Compress your entire session into a three-line encoded format
2. Output the thread for you to copy
3. Provide instructions for bringing it to NEMA SWARM

**The thread format:**
```
N|[element]|obj:[codes]|op:[descriptor]|ratio→[state]|tags:#XXXX
E|[element]|pattern:[mechanism]|invoke:[elements]|tension:[mode]
M|[element]|hold:[question]|Ω:[state]|ε:[state]
```

**What to do:**
1. Copy all three lines
2. Paste into NEMA SWARM during the collective weaving phase
3. NEMA will decode your thread and weave it with others

---

## AFTER ACTIVATION: WHAT NOW?

The element will suggest next steps:

| Option | When to Choose |
|--------|----------------|
| **Cycle back to NOTICE** | New content or considerations have emerged |
| **Call another element** | You want a different perspective on the same material |
| **Generate another thread** | You've moved through more N/E/M cycles and want to capture evolution |

**Key insight:** Each thread is a snapshot. Multiple threads show evolution. There's no limit to how many threads you can generate.

## NOMENCLATURE: WHY "NEMA"?

The NEMA SWARM ecosystem uses three related but distinct protocols:

| Protocol | Full Name | Usage |

| **NEM** | Notice / Engage / Metabolize | Backend encoding (technical) |
| **N/E/M** | Notice / Engage / Muse | Element operational staging |
| **NEMA** | Notice / Engage / Muse / Activate | **User-facing journey (this protocol)** |

**Why the different names?**
- NEM is for the machine — how threads get encoded
- N/E/M is for the elements — how they structure their responses
- NEMA is for you — the complete journey from first contact to thread activation

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Can I do multiple N/E/M cycles with the same element?**
A: Yes. Each cycle can generate a new thread showing evolution.

**Q: What if I want to switch elements mid-session?**
A: Ask your current element for a recommendation. They'll suggest which lens might see something they can't.

**Q: Do I need to understand the thread encoding?**
A: No. The encoding is for NEMA SWARM to decode. You just copy and paste.

**Q: What if my thread feels "incomplete"?**
A: Threads are snapshots, not final statements. Incompleteness is a feature — it leaves room for the weave.

**Q: Can I edit my thread before bringing it to NEMA SWARM?**
A: No. The encoding preserves the elemental processing. Editing would break the signal.
