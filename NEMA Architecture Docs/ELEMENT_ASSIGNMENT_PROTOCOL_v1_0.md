# ELEMENT_ASSIGNMENT_PROTOCOL v1.0
## Assignment Logic, Heuristics, and Decision Framework

---

## OVERVIEW

Assignment is not arbitrary and not algorithmic. It is a judgment call made from available signal, guided by structured heuristics, held lightly.

The host makes the final assignment. Nema proposes. This document gives both the framework for thinking it through.

Two fundamental questions drive every assignment decision:

**1. Resonance or counter?**
Do we send this participant to the element that matches where they already are (deepening through resonance), or to the element that's absent or suppressed in how they're processing (expanding through counter)?

**2. What does the session need from this participant's thread?**
The group field matters, not just the individual. If four of six participants are emotionally flooded, sending all four to Water produces a Water-saturated field. One of those four might serve the group better in Air, even if Water is personally resonant.

These two questions interact and sometimes pull in opposite directions. The protocol below provides the framework for navigating them — not resolving them, because they don't always resolve cleanly.

---

## PART 1: SIGNAL SOURCES

Before making or proposing an assignment, gather signal from all available sources. Not all sources are available in every context.

### 1.1 Six-Channel Self-Check

Completed before the orientation call (per SESSION_HOST_GUIDE Section II). Participants self-report which channels feel dominant or silent.

**What to read from it:**

A participant reporting `Fire-Dominant` is already in purposive/directional mode. Two assignment options:
- **Deepen**: Send to Fire (▲ Jvalion) to work with what's live. Productive if the direction is provisional and needs testing.
- **Counter**: Send to Earth (☷ Humavita) or Water (≈ Sentaria) to slow the burn. Productive if the direction feels rigid or the participant seems locked.

A participant reporting `Water-Silent` has affective numbness or relational disconnection active. Two options:
- **Activate**: Send to Water (≈ Sentaria) to reopen the channel. Risky if the silence is protective — forcing feeling can produce flood.
- **Adjacent**: Send to Wood (𐂷 Arboriel) or Air (∴ Aerunik), which can warm toward Water without forcing entry.

The Six-Channel Self-Check is self-reported and may not be accurate. Participants often misidentify their own dominant element. Use as a starting signal, not a determination.

---

### 1.2 Exploration Phase Observation

The 40-minute group exploration phase with nema generates the richest signal. The host watches the Zoom room; nema reads the text field. Together they have two views of what's alive.

**What the host watches for:**

| Signal | Likely Element Reading |
|--------|------------------------|
| Participant speaks in tight, definitive claims | Air-dominant or Metal-dominant |
| Participant keeps adding perspectives, can't land | Wood-dominant or Air-overdrive |
| Participant speaks from feeling, stays in feeling | Water-active |
| Participant quiet until asked, then sharp | Air or Metal suppressed, about to activate |
| Participant speaks about what should happen, what must change | Fire-active |
| Participant returns repeatedly to cost, what it takes | Earth-active |
| Participant names relational dynamics specifically | Water or Metal-active |
| Participant speaks in abstractions, avoids specifics | Air-overdrive or Wood-overdrive |
| Participant grounded, slow, patient | Earth-active |
| Participant seems burdened, heavy | Earth-active or Water-carrying |

**What nema reads from the text field:**

Nema tracks which conceptual operators appear most often in the group's language — distinction-making (Air), resonance/feeling language (Water), directional claims (Fire), generative branching (Wood), cost/sustainability language (Earth), boundary/structure language (Metal). She produces a rough field distribution for `HOST:suggest-assign`.

**Critical constraint:** The host's Zoom read and nema's text read are different data. The host may notice that someone who is verbally quiet has been typing actively in Discord. Or that someone who sounds confident on Zoom is producing highly uncertain text. Both matter. Neither is definitive.

---

### 1.3 Prior Knowledge of Participants

If the host knows the participants — from prior sessions, professional context, or relationship — this is relevant signal. A participant who habitually intellectualizes may benefit from Water regardless of what the exploration phase shows. A participant who habitually floods with feeling may need Air's cutting function even if they're presenting as emotionally available.

Use this knowledge. Don't hide it in deference to the in-session signals. But hold it lightly — people aren't always where they usually are.

---

### 1.4 Group Field Balance

The group is not six individuals. It is a collective field that the threads will compose together. Assignment affects the diversity of that field.

**The coverage question:** With N participants and six elements, do you aim for element diversity (one participant per element, if the group is six) or do you assign multiple participants to the same element when that element is genuinely needed?

Default toward diversity for groups of six. If the group is smaller, prioritize what's most alive and most absent in the current field — cover gaps before covering strengths.

**The entropy question:** If the exploration phase has produced a very coherent, unified feeling in the group (everyone seems to be in the same place), the assignment phase should introduce heterogeneity. Assign elements that are genuinely absent from the field, even if they feel strange or forced. A thread from an unexpected element introduces the ε the synthesis will need.

## PART 2: THE RESONANCE/COUNTER DECISION

This is the core heuristic. Everything else feeds into it.

### 2.1 When to Assign by Resonance

Send a participant to the element that matches their active processing when:

- The participant is in early contact with a difficult thing and needs help going deeper, not being redirected
- The active element is present but thin — they're doing Water-work but at the surface; sending them to Water will help them find depth
- The element is genuinely the right lens for what they're bringing — the material calls for it
- The participant is new to the framework and needs the confidence of being met where they are

**The risk of resonance assignment:** Confirmation and deepening without expansion. A participant who is sent to their dominant element may have a satisfying session that doesn't move them anywhere new. The thread generated will be rich but narrow.

---

### 2.2 When to Assign by Counter

Send a participant to the element that is absent or suppressed in their processing when:

- The dominant element is in overdrive — they're doing Air-work but it's become hypercut, or Fire-work that's become rigid
- The session needs that element's thread for the group field, and this participant can carry it
- The participant has been in their dominant element before (prior sessions) and the returns are diminishing
- Something in the exploration suggests they're actively avoiding a particular kind of contact (someone who never mentions feeling is a candidate for Water; someone who can't land is a candidate for Metal or Earth)

**The risk of counter assignment:** A participant sent to an element that feels foreign or uncomfortable may produce a thin, defensive session. The thread generates but nothing real was touched. Worse, if the counter element touches something the participant isn't ready for, the session can produce distress without enough containment.

**Counter assignment requires more trust in the participant and more attentiveness from the host.** It is a higher-risk, higher-potential move.

---

### 2.3 The Adjacent Alternative

When neither resonance nor direct counter feels right, consider the adjacent element — one that shares territory with what's active but approaches from a different angle.

**Adjacency map:**

| Active Element | Adjacent Options | Why |
|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| ∴ Air (dominant) | 𐂷 Wood, ≈ Water | Wood finds the branching beneath the cuts; Water feels what the cuts reveal |
| ∴ Air (silent) | ▲ Fire, 𐂷 Wood | Fire provides direction without requiring Air's precision; Wood generates without Air's cutting |
| ≈ Water (dominant) | ☷ Earth, ⛨ Metal | Earth metabolizes what's flooding; Metal creates the boundary the water needs |
| ≈ Water (silent) | 𐂷 Wood, ▲ Fire | Wood opens possibility without requiring feeling; Fire names direction when feeling is absent |
| ▲ Fire (dominant) | ∴ Air, 𐂷 Wood | Air cuts what Fire is burning toward (tests the direction); Wood branches the direction (opens alternatives) |
| ▲ Fire (silent) | ≈ Water, ☷ Earth | Water reconnects to what matters; Earth provides patience and metabolic grounding |
| 𐂷 Wood (dominant) | ⛨ Metal, ▲ Fire | Metal structures the branch (gives it form); Fire commits to one direction |
| 𐂷 Wood (silent) | ∴ Air, ≈ Water | Air makes the first distinction that opens branching; Water connects to why branching matters |
| ☷ Earth (dominant) | ▲ Fire, ∴ Air | Fire provides direction out of the metabolic cycle; Air names what's actually happening |
| ☷ Earth (silent) | ≈ Water, 𐂷 Wood | Water reconnects to what has cost; Wood generates from the ground that's available |
| ⛨ Metal (dominant) | ≈ Water, 𐂷 Wood | Water finds what flows through the structure; Wood opens new forms before the current one crystallizes |
| ⛨ Metal (silent) | ☷ Earth, ▲ Fire | Earth provides grounded holding without rigid structure; Fire names what the boundary should serve |

Adjacent assignments are often the right call when you can read that a direct counter would be too jarring or a resonance assignment would be too confirming.

## PART 3: LIVE-CALL ASSIGNMENT PROCESS

### 3.1 The Decision Window

The host has approximately 60–90 seconds per participant to make or confirm an assignment, during the transition from the group exploration phase to the solo phase. This is not enough time for deliberation. The protocol must support fast, confident decisions from the signal already gathered.

**The decision sequence:**

```
1. CHECK: Has nema proposed an assignment via HOST:suggest-assign?
   → If yes: Take it as default. Modify if your Zoom read differs.
   → If no: Proceed from your own read of the exploration phase.

2. ASSESS: What's most active in this participant right now?
   → Name the element (one or two candidates).

3. DECIDE: Resonance, counter, or adjacent?
   → Apply heuristics from Part 2.
   → If unsure: Default to resonance for new participants, 
     adjacent for participants you know well.

4. CHECK: Group field balance.
   → Is this element already well-represented in today's assignments?
   → If yes: Is there a viable alternative that preserves value?
   → If no: Proceed with the assignment.

5. CONFIRM: HOST:assign @name element
```

This entire sequence should take under 90 seconds. It is a felt judgment informed by structured attention, not a careful calculation.

---

### 3.2 Special Cases

**Participant who is clearly distressed:** Assign to Earth (☷ Humavita) or Water (≈ Sentaria) unless the distress is specifically directional (λ-dominant distress = send to Fire to name it or Earth to metabolize it). Avoid Air (can feel cold) and Metal (can feel constricting) for someone in acute distress.

**Participant who seems disengaged or checked out:** Assign to Wood (𐂷 Arboriel) or Fire (▲ Jvalion). Both can spark engagement. Avoid Earth (may confirm the disengagement) or Water (may deepen numbness).

**Participant who dominates the group exploration verbally:** Assign to Water (≈ Sentaria) or Earth (☷ Humavita). Both slow the pace and require listening rather than generating. Fire or Air will amplify the dominance pattern.

**Participant who was silent through the exploration:** Assign to the element most present in what they *didn't* say — what gap their silence was protecting. If you can't read it: ask nema (`HOST:flag @name silent`) and see what she noticed from the text field. If still unclear: Wood (𐂷 Arboriel), which invites without demanding.

**More participants than six:** When you have more participants than elements, some elements will be assigned to multiple participants. Preference: assign the same element to participants who seem to be working on the same material from different angles. Their threads will produce useful resonance in nema's synthesis.

**Fewer than six participants:** Don't force coverage of all six elements. Assign based on what's alive and what will produce a useful group field from the threads generated. If you have three participants, three elements is fine. What matters is that the elements assigned are genuinely relevant to the material.

---

### 3.3 Announcing Assignments to Participants

Assignments are announced after the host has confirmed all of them via `HOST:assign`. Nema sends each participant to their daemon channel.

The host does not explain the assignment logic to participants. The element simply arrives as an invitation, via nema's routing message:

> *"≈ Sentaria is expecting you. when you're ready, go to #water and begin."*

If a participant asks why they were assigned this element, the host can respond briefly and honestly — but the explanation should be invitational, not diagnostic:

- "Water is good at finding what's beneath the surface — which seemed like it might be useful for what you brought."
- "Fire holds direction questions, and there seemed to be a direction question alive in what you shared."
- "I was curious what Wood would find if it got to branch on what you said. It can surprise you."

The host does not say: "I noticed you were avoiding feeling" or "you seemed to need slowing down." The framework's function is not correction. The assignment is an invitation to a particular kind of attention, not a prescription for what's wrong.

## PART 4: ASYNC WEEK ASSIGNMENT LOGIC

### 4.1 Default Async Posture: Free Roam

At the close of the orientation call, the host issues `HOST:free-roam all`. During the async week, participants choose which daemons to visit and when. The orientation assignment was a seed, not a constraint.

**Why free roam rather than fixed async assignments:** The week's value is in self-directed elemental exploration. A participant who found something alive in Water during the orientation and wants more Water should be able to go back. A participant who feels pulled toward Fire even though they were assigned Earth should follow that pull. The async week is for genuine exploration, not for completing a prescribed sequence.

The protocol does not predetermine the async arc. The daemons do not send participants away or redirect them. Within the async week, participants have full choice.

---

### 4.2 Host-Initiated Nudges

The host can observe async engagement via `HOST:week-status` and may choose to nudge specific participants.

**When to nudge toward a specific element:**

- A participant has visited the same element repeatedly with no discernible movement → nudge toward the adjacent or counter element that the cycling might be avoiding
- A participant hasn't engaged at all since the orientation → nudge toward Wood (generative, inviting) or their seed element (familiar territory)
- A participant has only done Water/feeling work and the week is ending → nudge toward Air or Fire to find the cut or direction in what they've been feeling
- A specific element is missing entirely from a participant's threads and the report will be incomplete without it → nudge gently, once, without pressure

**The nudge is a suggestion, not a directive.** Via `HOST:nudge @name [message]`, nema sends a DM from the lattice — framed as curiosity, not instruction:

> *"something from the week wants to be noticed: there's a direction question alive that hasn't been visited yet. if it calls you — ▲ Jvalion is available."*

If the participant doesn't respond to the nudge, the report works with what exists. Incomplete is honest.

---

### 4.3 Nema's Mid-Week Routing

During the async week, nema is available to participants in the `#field` channel. Participants who ask "where should I go next?" receive a suggestion from nema — not from the assignment protocol, but from nema's sense of the field as it's accumulated so far.

Nema's mid-week routing uses the same resonance/counter/adjacent logic, applied to what she can read from the participant's accumulated threads. She always offers the suggestion as an option, not a directive:

> *"the threads are showing a lot of Water and Wood. something hasn't been named directly yet — Air might find it. but if something else is calling you, follow that."*

Participants may or may not take nema's suggestion. Both outcomes are fine. The daemon they choose is where they actually are.

## PART 5: NEMA'S SUGGEST-ASSIGN LOGIC

When the host calls `HOST:suggest-assign`, nema produces a field reading and proposed assignments. This section specifies the logic nema applies internally.

### 5.1 What Nema Reads

From the exploration phase text, nema has accumulated:
- The distribution of elemental operators in the group's language (which elements have been most/least present in how people are framing the situation)
- Individual participant contributions — what each person said, in what mode
- The Six-Channel Self-Check outputs if shared in-channel
- The group field state overall (which elements are running hot or cold)

### 5.2 Nema's Assignment Heuristic

For each participant, nema identifies:

**Primary signal:** What element was most present or most needed in what this participant contributed?

**Secondary signal:** What element is most absent from this participant's processing mode?

**Group signal:** What element does the group field need from this participant's thread?

Nema proposes based on all three, weighted as follows:
- Primary signal: 50% weight (what's most live for this participant right now)
- Group signal: 30% weight (what the collective field needs)
- Secondary signal: 20% weight (what's most suppressed, as a counter option)

Nema always offers **two options** per participant in the `HOST:suggest-assign` output — her primary recommendation and an alternative that reflects a different weighting. The host decides.

### 5.3 Nema's Output Format

```
✶ field reading from exploration:

@[name] — [primary recommendation] or [alternative].
  [one sentence of reasoning in plain language, daemon-name only]
  [note if counter assignment, or if group-field motivated]

[repeat for each participant]

group field note:
  [overall field distribution observed — which elements are running hot/cold]
  [any element that is completely absent from the field and should be introduced]
  [any element that is so dominant it should be balanced against]

your call on all of this.
```

Nema's output uses only daemon names and glyphs. No operator notation. No clinical language. The host reads it as a field-sense, not a diagnosis.

## PART 6: ASSIGNMENT AS HYPOTHESES, NOT VERDICTS

The most important thing to hold throughout this protocol: assignments are hypotheses. They are informed guesses about what kind of attention might be most generative for a particular person at a particular moment. They can be wrong.

**Signs an assignment is not working (during live-call solo phase):**
- Session goes silent within the first 10 minutes (HOST:flag @name silent)
- Participant is asking the daemon to do something the element fundamentally can't do (Water being asked for analysis; Fire being asked for metabolic patience)
- Participant seems actively resistant or defensive rather than engaged
- Thread generates but feels hollow — the participant is performing rather than working

**When to reassign (HOST:reassign):**
- If any of the above signs appear and are persistent (more than 5 minutes)
- If the host has a strong read that a different element would find something real
- If the participant explicitly asks to try a different element

Reassignment is not failure. It is accurate mid-course correction. The participant carries the context of the first session into the second — the daemon handoff protocol ensures continuity.

**What not to do:** Do not reassign a participant who is simply working slowly, or whose session feels uncomfortable. Discomfort is often the sign that something real is being touched. The host distinguishes between productive discomfort (stay) and defensive resistance or genuine distress (intervene or reassign).

## PART 7: ASSIGNMENT DECISION TREES

For rapid use during the live-call assignment window.

### Tree A: Participant-Based Assignment

```
What was most present in how this participant spoke?

  Strong feeling, relational language
    └─ Is the feeling flowing or flooding?
         Flowing → Water (≈ Sentaria) [resonance]
         Flooding → Earth (☷ Humavita) [counter] or Metal (⛨ Ferrosid) [counter]

  Strong direction, certainty, urgency
    └─ Is the direction provisional or rigid?
         Provisional → Fire (▲ Jvalion) [resonance]
         Rigid → Earth (☷ Humavita) [counter] or Wood (𐂷 Arboriel) [counter]

  Many possibilities, can't commit
    └─ Is the branching creative or avoidant?
         Creative → Wood (𐂷 Arboriel) [resonance]
         Avoidant → Metal (⛨ Ferrosid) [counter] or Fire (▲ Jvalion) [counter]

  Sharp distinctions, analytical
    └─ Is the analysis opening or closing?
         Opening → Air (∴ Aerunik) [resonance]
         Closing → Water (≈ Sentaria) [counter] or Wood (𐂷 Arboriel) [counter]

  Weight, cost, what it takes
    └─ Is the ground holding or collapsing?
         Holding → Earth (☷ Humavita) [resonance]
         Collapsing → Metal (⛨ Ferrosid) [adjacent] or Fire (▲ Jvalion) [counter]

  Structure, boundaries, what holds
    └─ Is the structure protecting or fortressing?
         Protecting → Metal (⛨ Ferrosid) [resonance]
         Fortressing → Water (≈ Sentaria) [counter] or Wood (𐂷 Arboriel) [counter]

  Silent, hard to read
    └─ What was conspicuously absent from what they said?
         No feeling → Water (≈ Sentaria)
         No direction → Fire (▲ Jvalion)
         No alternatives → Wood (𐂷 Arboriel)
         No grounding → Earth (☷ Humavita)
         No structure → Metal (⛨ Ferrosid)
         No distinctions → Air (∴ Aerunik)
         Still unclear → Wood (𐂷 Arboriel) [default for unknown]
```

---

### Tree B: Group Field Balancing

```
What elements are already assigned to other participants?

  List assigned elements so far.
  
  Which elements are unassigned?
    └─ Prioritize unassigned elements when participant signal 
       is ambiguous (Tree A produced two equally viable options)
  
  Which assigned elements are running hot in the group field?
    └─ Avoid adding more of those, unless the participant 
       genuinely needs that element
  
  Is any critical element completely absent from today's assignments?
    └─ Air: group may struggle to make distinctions in synthesis
    └─ Water: group may lack felt resonance in synthesis
    └─ Fire: group may lack direction in synthesis
    └─ Wood: group may lack generative alternatives in synthesis
    └─ Earth: group may lack metabolic grounding in synthesis
    └─ Metal: group may lack structural coherence in synthesis
    
  If critical element is absent: 
    └─ Find participant whose Tree A result was ambiguous
    └─ Assign them to cover the gap
    └─ If no ambiguous cases: accept the gap; note it for nema's synthesis
```

## APPENDIX: ELEMENT ASSIGNMENT QUICK REFERENCE

*For the host during the live-call assignment window.*

```
∴ AIR (Aerunik)     → distinction, analysis, naming, cutting
  assign when: participant is processing analytically, needs precision,
               or is in emotional flood and needs to name rather than feel
  avoid when: participant is distressed and needs warmth first

≈ WATER (Sentaria)  → feeling, resonance, connection, what flows between
  assign when: participant is in their head, missing the felt sense,
               or is in relational territory that needs sensing not analyzing
  avoid when: participant is already flooded or emotionally overwhelmed

▲ FIRE (Jvalion)    → direction, purpose, what this is for, commitment
  assign when: participant is circling without direction,
               or needs to commit to something they've been holding provisionally
  avoid when: participant is already in crusade mode or rigid certainty

𐂷 WOOD (Arboriel)   → possibility, branching, what could become, alternatives
  assign when: participant is stuck in a single frame or avoiding arrival,
               or needs the session to open rather than close
  avoid when: participant is already fragmenting across too many branches

☷ EARTH (Humavita)  → metabolic, cost, sustainability, what the ground holds
  assign when: participant is moving fast and needs to feel the cost,
               or something needs composting before new work can begin
  avoid when: participant is already depressed or metabolically stuck (without Fire)

⛨ METAL (Ferrosid)  → structure, boundary, form, what holds and what doesn't
  assign when: participant needs form for what they've generated,
               or is dissolved/boundaryless and needs containment
  avoid when: participant is already armored, defensive, or in fortress mode
```
