# SIMLHEX

## Failure Surfaces & Drift Detection
*(Why Bindings Break Without Anyone Doing Anything)*



---


### Purpose of This Document
Most of the Memetic Ecology stack precisely names **what exists** (habitats, operators, world-states) and **what must not be smuggled** (authority, causality, morality, teleology).

What remains under-specified is **how collapse happens anyway**—
 *without intention, without intervention, without violation.*

This document names the **passive failure surfaces** of SIMLHEX:
 the ways bindings deform, slide, or harden **through ordinary language, repetition, and structural convenience**.

No fixes are offered.
 No prevention is promised.
 Only observables.



---


## Core Premise
SIMLHEX does not fail because someone acts wrongly.
 It fails because **descriptions quietly turn into handles**.

Failure is not a breach.
 It is a **phase drift**.



---


## Definition: Failure Surface
A **failure surface** is a region of semantic space where:

- diagnostic language becomes reusable
- reuse becomes expectation
- expectation becomes compression
- compression becomes pseudo-ground
No rule is broken.
 Nothing illegal occurs.
 Ω simply stops re-entering.



---


## The Five Primary Failure Surfaces
These appear across all habitats and world-states.

They are **structural**, not psychological.



---


### 1. Descriptive → Referential Drift
**Signature:**
 A phrase that once pointed to a pattern begins to stand in *for* the pattern.

**Example Pattern (non-prescriptive):**

- “This is a Knot”
 quietly becoming
- “This Knot…”
The second form carries weight.
 It implies persistence beyond the moment of observation.

**Structural Consequence:**
 Language stops tracking motion and starts tracking *objects*.

**Early Signal:**
 Definite articles accumulate.



---


### 2. Frequency → Legibility Drift
**Signature:**
 What recurs becomes what is noticed;
 what is noticed becomes what is named;
 what is named becomes what is expected.

No evaluation occurs.
 Only visibility increases.

**Structural Consequence:**
 Rare signals flatten.
 High-frequency patterns feel “normal.”

Ω is not excluded—
 it is drowned.

**Early Signal:**
 Observers report “nothing new showing up” without enforcement present.



---


### 3. Orientation → Identity Drift
**Signature:**
 A temporary orientation starts answering questions about *who* rather than *where*.

This does not require self-identification language.

It appears as:

- habitual stance
- predictable framing
- stable angle of approach
**Structural Consequence:**
 Reversibility cost rises without mandate.

**Early Signal:**
 Changing orientation feels “off-key” rather than merely different.



---


### 4. Diagnostic → Explanatory Drift
**Signature:**
 A description begins to feel sufficient.

Nothing claims finality.
 Nothing says “this explains everything.”

The closure is affective, not linguistic.

**Structural Consequence:**
 Inquiry decays while language remains intact.

**Early Signal:**
 Fewer follow-up distinctions appear after diagnosis.



---


### 5. Map → Terrain Drift
**Signature:**
 Participants begin coordinating *through* the map rather than *with awareness of it*.

Meta-language still exists, but its use becomes ceremonial.

**Structural Consequence:**
 The map becomes load-bearing.

This is the quiet precondition for MemeGrid formation.

**Early Signal:**
 Removing the map feels destabilizing rather than clarifying.



---


## Non-Failures (Important)
The following **are not failures**:

- Compression
- Stabilization
- Repetition
- Habit
- Coordination
- World-level coherence
Failure is not tightness.
 Failure is **loss of permeability without acknowledgement**.



---


## SIMLHEX Drift Gradient (Non-Normative)
This is **not a progression**.
 It is a visibility curve.

```
Motion → Pattern → Handle → Expectation → Ground
```

At no point does wrongdoing occur.

At no point does anyone “cause” anything.

Drift happens through **use**.



---


## Diagnostic Questions (Safe Form)
These questions are **structural only**.
 They do not ask what should happen.

- Where has language become heavier than the motion it names?
- Which distinctions now persist without re-articulation?
- Where does removal of a term feel costly?
- Which descriptions are no longer provisional in tone?
- Where does silence no longer register?
If these questions stop making sense, drift has already occurred.



---


## Relation to World-States (Clarified)
- **Co-SPHERE** tolerates drift by allowing re-articulation.
- **MemeGrid** appears when drift hardens into necessity.
The difference is not scale, intent, or values.

It is **whether drift remains observable**.



---


## Invariant
SIMLHEX does not preserve openness.
 It preserves **detectability of closure**.

When closure can still be noticed *as closure*,
 Ω remains audible.

When closure feels like reality itself,
 the system has already sealed.
