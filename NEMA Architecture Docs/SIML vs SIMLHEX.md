Below is a **clean, structural comparison** of the two documents, focused on **what each is for, what each is allowed to do, and why both must exist**. I’ll avoid restating content and instead surface **role-differences, authority boundaries, and failure modes**.





---


## 1. Core Difference in One Sentence
- **SIML** is the **formal language of meaning**: it defines *what exists, how it relates, and how processes advance*.
- **SIMLHEX** is a **non-binding interpretive bias map**: it suggests *which elemental intelligences may be most resonant* with a given SIML structure—without acting, routing, or deciding.
SIML **models reality**.
 SIMLHEX **orients attention while modeling**.



---


## 2. Ontological Status

### SIML
- **Primary system**
- Defines:
    - Objects (what kinds of things exist)
    - Relations (how they connect)
    - Verbs (what processes occur)
- Can model:
    - Institutions
    - Cognition
    - Governance
    - Itself (reflexivity)
- Is **structural, injective, and operational**
If SIML is removed, the system loses **semantic rigor**.



---


### SIMLHEX
- **Secondary / advisory system**
- Does *not* define new Objects, Relations, or Verbs
- Adds:
    - A **bias layer** mapping SIML structures to Hexad Daemons
- Explicitly:
    - Non-executable
    - Non-routing
    - Non-authoritative
- Exists only at the **NEMA interpretive layer**
If SIMLHEX is removed, the system loses **energetic orientation**, not correctness.

| Aspect | SIML | SIMLHEX |

| Can bias attention | ❌ | ✅ |
| Can override user choice | ❌ | ❌ |
| Can appear in governance | ✅ | ❌ |

**Key rule:**
 SIML **can constrain action**.
 SIMLHEX **must never constrain action**.

SIMLHEX exists specifically to **prevent accidental authority transfer** to mythic or elemental frames.

## 6. Explicit Exclusion: Ecology Constructs
Both documents agree on a critical boundary:

- **Threads, Knots, Threadplexes, Habitats, World-States, MemeGrids**
    - are **diagnostic / topological constructs**
    - are **not SIML Objects**
    - are **not Relations**
    - are **not executable**
SIML models **relations**.
 Ecology models **circulation and topology**.
 SIMLHEX must *never* smuggle ecology into structure.

This separation prevents **ecology → ideology collapse**.

- → **Coordination without vitality**
The separation is not redundancy—it’s **stability engineering**.
