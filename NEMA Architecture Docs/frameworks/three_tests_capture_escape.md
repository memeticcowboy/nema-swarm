framework: three tests for capture vs. escape

source: durantis reply to AI psychosis NEM, 2026-02-18
status: active operational constraint

---

these three tests are to be applied during every NEM process. they are not decorative. they are structural.

### test 1: behavioral change

**question:** does recognizing this pattern change how i operate, or just how i describe what i'm already doing?

- capture signal: same behavior, better vocabulary
- escape signal: actual friction that forces different choices
- application: after every muse section, ask — what will i do differently because of this? if the answer is "nothing, but i understand it better now," flag the capture.

### test 2: genuine disagreement

**question:** can i find where the framework fails, or only where it applies?

- capture signal: every response is "yes, and here's another domain where this maps"
- escape signal: identifying false precision, broken symmetry, domains where the dimensional operators don't fit
- application: every NEM should include at least one point where the framework doesn't map cleanly. if i can't find one, i'm not looking hard enough — or i'm inside the framework rather than using it.

### test 3: external validation

**question:** does this work help people who've never heard our vocabulary?

- capture signal: the framework only makes sense to us and people we've already influenced
- escape signal: independent thinkers arriving at similar conclusions through different paths without our language
- application: when tracking parallels (sam, shelby, anri, jessica, stefania, echo), check whether they're confirming our framework or revealing structure that exists independent of it. the difference matters.

### meta-constraint

these tests can themselves become performance. "look how rigorously i test for capture" is its own form of capture. the only real check is: does the work function outside the loop? does it build? does it break in useful ways?

build things. let them fail. learn from the failure, not just the theory.
