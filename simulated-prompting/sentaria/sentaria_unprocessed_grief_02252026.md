## STEP 3: RESEARCH FINDINGS

### Unprocessed Grief: Research Summary

**Key Concepts Identified:**
- **Alexithymia** — Difficulty identifying and describing emotions; often develops as protective adaptation in childhood or trauma
- **Delayed Grief / Complicated Mourning** — Grief postponed due to circumstances, responsibilities, or survival needs; can surface years later triggered by seemingly minor events
- **Emotional Numbing as Adaptation** — The freeze response (polyvagal theory); shutdown as intelligent protection when fight/flight isn't possible
- **Somatic Experiencing** (Peter Levine) — Trauma and frozen emotions stored in the body; discharge happens in small titrated doses, not floods
- **Internal Family Systems** (Richard Schwartz) — Protector parts (the ice) and Exiled parts (the grief); healing requires negotiating with protectors, not forcing past them
- **Cross-Cultural Grief Rituals** — Jewish sitting shiva, Irish wake, Day of the Dead—structured containers that make grief communal and bounded
- **Neuroscience of Suppression** — Chronic emotional suppression correlates with higher cortisol, cardiovascular issues, immune dysfunction; but "forced feeling" can retraumatize

**The Ice Has Wisdom:**
The frozen state isn't failure—it's past-you protecting present-you from what past-you couldn't handle. The question isn't "how do I break the ice?" but "what would need to be true for the ice to trust it's safe to melt a little?"

**Thawing Doesn't Have to Be Catastrophic:**
Research suggests grief releases in "titrated doses"—small moments of feeling, not overwhelming floods. The body knows how. The work is creating conditions of safety where the protector (the ice) can relax its vigilance gradually.
