## STEP 3: RESEARCH FINDINGS

### Framework Collapse: Research Summary

**Key Concepts Identified:**
- **Kuhn's "Aristotle Experience"** — The lived phenomenology of paradigm shift as Gestalt conversion, not intellectual update
- **The Dark Night of the Soul** (St. John of the Cross) — Purification through felt abandonment; "the soul makes greater progress when it imagines it is losing"
- **Buddhist Insight Stages** — Dissolution → Fear → Misery → Disgust → Desire for Deliverance; breakdown as sign of practice working
- **Deleuze's Deterritorialization** — Breakdown as opening, not destruction; "lines of flight" as escape from rigid systems
- **Nietzsche's Revaluation** — Active vs. passive nihilism; the Three Metamorphoses (Camel → Lion → Child)
- **Heidegger's Equipment Breakdown** — Ready-to-hand becoming unready-to-hand, revealing the world that was hidden
- **Victor Turner's Liminality** — The charged threshold space; communitas among fellow liminals

**The Meta-Question:** Can any framework hold its own breakdown? The research suggests: no framework can fully contain its own collapse—but paradoxical holding (using frameworks lightly, expecting failure) is possible.
