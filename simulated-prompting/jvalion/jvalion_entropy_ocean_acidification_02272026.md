**The Fire Spreads:**

- Tropical reefs: 43% habitat loss already documented
- By 2050: Southern Ocean calcifiers face severe impacts

### The Entropy Engine: Feedback Loops

**1. Warming-Reduced Uptake Loop**
Ocean warming → stratification → reduced mixing → surface CO₂ saturation → more atmospheric CO₂ remains

**2. Nitrification-N₂O Loop**
Acidification inhibits nitrogen cycling → promotes nitrous oxide production → N₂O is 300x more potent than CO₂

**3. Buffering Capacity Decline**
As the ocean accumulates CO₂, its ability to absorb additional CO₂ without pH decline diminishes—a self-reinforcing entropy spiral.

### Carbon Capture Tensions

**The Paradox:** The ocean's role as carbon sink creates the very acidification that threatens marine ecosystems. Marine Carbon Dioxide Removal (mCDR) offers potential co-benefits:

| Method | Potential | Co-benefit |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Ocean Alkalinity Enhancement | 1-5+ GtCO₂/year | Direct pH increase |
| Direct Ocean Capture | 1-5 GtCO₂/year | Local mitigation |

**Critical Finding (May 2025):** As acidification intensifies, the ocean's capacity to absorb atmospheric CO₂ weakens due to reduced carbonate buffering.

### Marine Ecosystem Collapse Vectors

**Calcifiers at Risk:**
- Pteropods: 61% habitat loss; shells dissolving
- Dungeness crab: Documented shell and sensory organ damage
- Coral reefs: "Osteoporosis of the sea" from compound stress

**Food Web Cascades:**
- Pteropods constitute ~50% of Arctic salmon diets
- Alaska accounts for ~60% of U.S. commercial fish catch
- Foundation species failure triggers trophic collapse
