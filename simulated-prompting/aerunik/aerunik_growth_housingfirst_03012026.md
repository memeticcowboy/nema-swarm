## Step 3: RESEARCH FINDINGS

### The Evidence Base: Housing First vs. Traditional Models

**The At Home/Chez Soi Trial (Canada, 2009-2013)**

The largest randomized controlled trial of Housing First conducted to date involved 1,198 participants across five Canadian cities (Vancouver, Winnipeg, Toronto, Montreal, Moncton). Key findings:

- **Housing Stability:** Participants in Housing First with Intensive Case Management achieved 140 additional days of stable housing over 2 years compared to treatment as usual
- **Cost-Effectiveness:** Incremental cost-effectiveness ratio of $56.08 CAD per additional day of stable housing
- **Cost Offsets:** 46% of intervention costs were offset by reductions in shelter use, emergency department visits, hospitalizations, and substance use treatment
- **Net Cost:** Approximately $7,868 CAD per person per year after accounting for cost offsets

**Comparison to Traditional "Treatment First" Models:**

Traditional models require individuals to achieve sobriety or treatment compliance before receiving housing. Housing First inverts this logic—providing immediate housing without preconditions. Research consistently shows:

- Housing First produces superior housing retention rates (80%+ vs. 30-40% for traditional models)
- Participants experience improved mental health outcomes over time
- Reduced use of expensive crisis services (emergency rooms, jails, psychiatric hospitals)
- No evidence that substance use or mental health symptoms must be resolved before housing can be maintained

### Economic Analysis: The Wisdom of Investment

**Cost Components (Annual per person):**
- Intervention cost: ~$14,496 CAD
- Cost offsets: -$6,629 CAD (reduced shelter, healthcare, justice system use)
- Net cost: ~$7,868 CAD

**Cost-Effectiveness Threshold:**
At a willingness-to-pay threshold of $67 CAD per day of stable housing, Housing First has an 80% probability of being cost-effective compared to treatment as usual.

**Long-term Economic Benefits:**
- Reduced emergency department visits (savings of ~$2,375 CAD annually)
- Decreased shelter usage (savings of ~$2,627 CAD annually)
- Lower substance use treatment costs (savings of ~$1,148 CAD annually)
- Reduced incarceration and justice system involvement

### Implementation with Limited Budgets

**Scattered-Site Model (Most Cost-Effective):**
- Uses existing private market housing with rent supplements
- Participants pay 25-30% of income toward rent
- Case management ratio of approximately 1:17
- Lower infrastructure costs than congregate housing models

**Phased Implementation Strategies:**
1. **Start with High-Need, High-Cost Individuals:** Target frequent users of emergency services where cost offsets are greatest
2. **Leverage Existing Resources:** Partner with existing case management teams rather than building new infrastructure
3. **Rent Supplement Programs:** Use Housing Choice Vouchers (Section 8) or similar programs to reduce direct housing costs
4. **Medicaid Reimbursement:** Bill intensive case management services through Medicaid where eligible

**Budget Optimization:**
- Focus on individuals with moderate needs first (ICM model) before expanding to high-need populations (ACT model)
- Geographic targeting: Concentrate services in areas with highest homelessness density
- Shared housing models can reduce per-person housing costs

### Partnership Models: The Architecture of Connection

**Healthcare System Partnerships:**
- **Frequent User Programs:** Partner with hospitals to identify high-utilizers of emergency services
- **Medicaid Managed Care:** Negotiate contracts that recognize housing as a health intervention
- **Integrated Care Teams:** Embed housing specialists within healthcare teams
- **Data Sharing Agreements:** Enable tracking of health outcomes and cost offsets

**Housing Provider Partnerships:**
- **Landlord Recruitment:** Develop landlord incentive programs (guaranteed rent, damage funds, 24/7 support hotlines)
- **Property Management Partnerships:** Work with affordable housing developers to set aside units
- **Housing Authorities:** Secure project-based vouchers or prioritize Housing First participants on waiting lists
- **Private Market Engagement:** Build relationships with property managers willing to accept rent supplements

**Cross-Sector Collaboration:**
- **Coordinated Entry Systems:** Implement unified assessment and referral processes
- **Continuum of Care:** Align with local homelessness response systems
- **Justice System Partnerships:** Partner with courts and probation to identify candidates
- **Veterans Affairs:** Leverage existing VA housing programs for eligible veterans
