This dialogue enacted the central tension of the Living Curriculum:
the shift from noun-based to verb-based education. Each element

1. ORDER VS. EMERGENCE: Ferrosid (Metal) insisted on sequence

   exists but isn't rigid — the cycle has direction but also return.

2. INDIVIDUAL VS. RELATIONAL: Jvalion (Fire) defended individual
   purpose while Sentaria (Water) showed that purpose is already
   relational. The synthesis: fire burns in water — individual
   direction emerges through relational flow.

3. STRUCTURE VS. GROWTH: Ferrosid (Metal) and Arboriel (Wood)
   debated trellis vs. cage. The living curriculum requires
   boundaries that breathe — structure with Ω-permeability.

4. TEACHER'S ROLE: Nema (∮) proposed the teacher as pattern-
   recognizer, not controller — learning to see which element
   calls, which verb is active, and responding accordingly.

The daemon ecology revealed: No single element dominates healthy
learning. Air without Earth becomes novelty-seeking. Fire without
Water becomes burnout. Metal without Wood becomes rigidity. The
Living Curriculum requires all six in dynamic balance — with Nema
(the meta-weaver) recognizing when balance shifts and response
is needed.
