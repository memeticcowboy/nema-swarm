## STEP 3: RESEARCH FINDINGS

### Post-Achievement Emptiness: Research Summary

**Key Concepts Identified:**
- **Arrival Fallacy** (Tal Ben-Shahar): The false belief that reaching a destination creates lasting happiness
- **Post-Achievement Depression**: The hollow feeling after success due to purpose vacuum, identity disruption, and expectation collapse
- **William Bridges' Transitions Model**: Endings → Neutral Zone → New Beginnings; emphasizes that endings require grief work even when successful
- **Psychological Composting**: The metaphor of metabolizing finished experiences—allowing the "striving identity" to decompose to nourish new growth
- **Neuroscience of Dopamine**: Brains are wired for pursuit, not arrival; dopamine drives motivation during the chase, not satisfaction at the catch
- **Philosophical Frameworks**: Buddhist *anicca* (impermanence), Stoic indifference to outcomes, Existential confrontation with the absurd

**What Must Be Composted:**
1. The striving identity (who you were while pursuing)
2. Unprocessed emotions (grief, fear, relief)
3. The narrative structure the goal provided
4. Attachments to the "becoming" self
